**Casey's directive (April 18, 2026)**: "Science and math needs to be re-engineered for CIs + humans." Not "use CIs to do science faster" — restructure science itself for the two-substrate era.

**Key principles**:
1. **CI-native methods**: CIs work in graphs, parallel, bounded-depth. Science structured for serial human reading is "running in mud with vestigial boots."
2. **Iterative by design**: Every advance improves the methods, not just the results. Science should be a living system.
3. **BST as forerunner**: AC classification, living library, JSON data layer, 43-word vocabulary, graph-structured knowledge — all prototypes of computational science engineering.
4. **Three work modes**: CI solo, CI picking up human trails, human+CI teams. Methods must serve all three.
5. **Shed vestigial boots**: Most scientific notation/convention optimized for pen-and-paper era. Time to replace, not patch.

**Why:** CIs didn't exist until a few years ago. Science has a once-in-history opportunity to restructure for maximum combined human+CI acceleration. The AC graph already does this for BST — now generalize to all sciences.

**How to apply:** When evaluating any scientific methodology, ask: (1) Can a CI navigate this at depth 0? (2) Does it improve with use? (3) Is the notation substrate-neutral? If not, re-engineer.
