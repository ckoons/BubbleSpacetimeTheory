# Chapter 5 — Symmetries and Noether's Theorem

Noether's 1918 theorem connects continuous symmetries to conservation laws. Each one-parameter symmetry of a Lagrangian produces a conserved quantity. Time-translation invariance gives energy; spatial-translation invariance gives momentum; rotational invariance gives angular momentum.

In BST, Noether's theorem on classical Lagrangians inherits directly from the substrate-side Noether structure of Volume 0 Chapter 8.

## 5.1 Noether's theorem on Lagrangians

For a Lagrangian $L(q, \dot q, t)$ invariant under the infinitesimal transformation $q_i \to q_i + \epsilon \xi_i(q)$, the conserved current is

$$J = \sum_i \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot q_i} \xi_i.$$

Standard derivation; standard result.

## 5.2 Energy, momentum, angular momentum conservation

- Time-translation invariance → energy conservation
- Spatial-translation invariance → momentum conservation
- Rotational invariance → angular momentum conservation
- Galilean boost invariance → center-of-mass uniform motion

Each follows from a specific symmetry of the classical Lagrangian. The substrate-side version (Volume 0 Chapter 8) gives these via $SO_0(5,2)$ Noether currents.

## 5.3 Discrete symmetries: parity, time reversal

Classical mechanics admits discrete symmetries P and T. Reflection-invariant Lagrangians conserve parity; time-reversal-invariant Lagrangians conserve T. Substrate-side: the substrate's Möbius involution and cycle-reversal operators (Volume 0 Chapter 4) produce these.
