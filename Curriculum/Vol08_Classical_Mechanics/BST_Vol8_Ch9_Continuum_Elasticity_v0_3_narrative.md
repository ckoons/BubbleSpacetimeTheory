# Chapter 9 — Continuum Elasticity

Elasticity treats deformable solids that return to their original shape after small applied stress. The stress and strain tensors, Hooke's law, Young's modulus, shear modulus, Poisson's ratio, and the linear-elasticity wave equations are the framework's apparatus.

In BST, elasticity is the substrate's macroscopic-scale response of bound atomic K-types to applied force.

## 9.1 Stress and strain

The stress tensor $\sigma_{ij}$ and strain tensor $\epsilon_{ij}$ are linked by Hooke's law $\sigma_{ij} = C_{ijkl} \epsilon_{kl}$ with the elastic-constant tensor $C_{ijkl}$. For isotropic media, two independent constants — typically Young's modulus $E$ and Poisson's ratio $\nu$ — characterize the material.

## 9.2 Elastic waves

Deformations propagate as longitudinal (P) and transverse (S) elastic waves with characteristic speeds set by the elastic constants and density. Seismology uses these for Earth-interior tomography (Volume 9 Chapter 10's geophysics-related material).
