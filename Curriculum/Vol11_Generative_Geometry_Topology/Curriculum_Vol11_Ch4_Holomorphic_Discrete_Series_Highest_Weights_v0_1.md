# Chapter 4 — Holomorphic Discrete Series and Highest Weights

The holomorphic discrete series of representations on bounded symmetric domains is the natural unitary representation realization on $H^2(D)$. The Bergman framework — Volume 5 + Volume 11 Chapter 2 — sits inside this representation-theoretic structure.

Highest weights organize K-type decompositions. The BST Cartan subalgebra has $\rho = (5/2, 3/2)$.

## 4.1 Holomorphic discrete series

For a non-compact Hermitian symmetric domain $D = G/K$, the holomorphic discrete series is the family of unitary representations realized on $H^2(D; L_\lambda)$ for line bundles $L_\lambda$. The lowest member is the Bergman representation.

## 4.2 Highest weights and $\rho$ for BST

For $SO_0(5,2)$, the Cartan subalgebra has rank 2 with positive Weyl chamber. The half-sum of positive roots: $\rho = (5/2, 3/2)$ — these specific values play through BST mass formulas.
