# Chapter 5 — D_IV⁵ Geometry: Coset and Boundary Coordinates

This chapter develops the explicit geometry of $D_{IV}^5$: the coset realization, boundary structure, Shilov boundary, and the coordinates used throughout BST.

## 5.1 The coset realization

$D_{IV}^5 = SO_0(5,2)/[SO(5) \times SO(2)]$ is the quotient by the maximal compact subgroup. As a real manifold, its real dimension is $\dim SO_0(5,2) - \dim[SO(5) \times SO(2)] = 21 - 11 = 10 = 2 \cdot \dim_\mathbb{C}$.

## 5.2 Bounded realization

The Lie ball realization gives $D_{IV}^5$ as a specific bounded open subset of $\mathbb{C}^5$ characterized by quadratic-form inequalities.

## 5.3 The Shilov boundary

The Shilov boundary of $D_{IV}^5$ is the smallest closed subset of the topological boundary on which a holomorphic function on the closure attains its maximum modulus. For BST, the Shilov boundary plays a specific role: per Casey's Saturday work, the electron is identified as a "Shilov-boundary primitive cycle" (BST catalog T-numbered).
