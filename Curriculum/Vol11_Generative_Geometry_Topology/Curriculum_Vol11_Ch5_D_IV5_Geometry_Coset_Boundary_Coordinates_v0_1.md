## Level 2 — graduate-physicist precision

### 5.1 Lie ball realization

$D_{IV}^5$ in Lie ball realization: $z = (z_1, \ldots, z_5) \in \mathbb{C}^5$ satisfies

$$1 - 2 z^* z + |z^T z|^2 > 0, \quad |z^T z| < 1$$

Open bounded subset of $\mathbb{C}^5$. Real dim 10.

### 5.2 Coset structure

$D_{IV}^5 = SO_0(5, 2)/[SO(5) \times SO(2)]$.

$SO_0(5, 2)$ acts transitively on $D_{IV}^5$ by biholomorphisms (combined with cocycle).

Base point $z = 0$: stabilizer = $SO(5) \times SO(2)$.

### 5.3 Topological boundary

Topological boundary $\partial D_{IV}^5 = \overline{D_{IV}^5} \setminus D_{IV}^5$ has 9-real-dimensional interior. Not all of it is "boundary" in the function-theoretic sense.

### 5.4 Shilov boundary

The **Shilov boundary** $S \subset \partial D_{IV}^5$ is the smallest closed set on which holomorphic functions attain maximum modulus.

For $D_{IV}^5$: Shilov boundary is a 5-real-dimensional submanifold, the unique closed orbit of $SO_0(5,2)$ acting on $\partial D_{IV}^5$.

The Shilov boundary $S \cong U(2) \cdot S^4$ or similar (depending on convention) — explicit identification depends on coordinate choice.
