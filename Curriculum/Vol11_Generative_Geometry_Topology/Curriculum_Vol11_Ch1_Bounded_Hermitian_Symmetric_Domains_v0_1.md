# Chapter 1 — Bounded Hermitian Symmetric Domains

Volume 11 develops the deep mathematical foundations that BST's generative geometry rests on. Bounded Hermitian symmetric domains, Bergman kernels, Wallach representations, K3 surfaces, Heegner numbers, Monster moonshine, cyclotomic Reed-Solomon, and Mersenne primes — these are the structural pieces. Volume 12 develops chemistry; this volume develops the math behind the substrate.

This chapter introduces bounded Hermitian symmetric domains, of which $D_{IV}^5$ is one specific example.

## 1.1 Symmetric spaces

A symmetric space is a Riemannian manifold with an involutive isometry at every point. Cartan's 1926 classification gives four series and seven exceptional examples. Type IV is the relevant series for BST.

## 1.2 Bounded realizations

Hermitian symmetric spaces of non-compact type admit bounded realizations as open subsets of $\mathbb{C}^n$. The four classical series have these:
- Type I: $D_{p,q}$ — generalized unit ball
- Type II: $D_p^{II}$ — symmetric tube domain
- Type III: $D_p^{III}$ — Siegel upper half-space
- Type IV: $D_p^{IV}$ — Lie ball / generalized Cayley domain

## 1.3 $D_{IV}^5$ specifically

$D_{IV}^5 = SO_0(5,2)/[SO(5)\times SO(2)]$ is the Type IV bounded symmetric domain of dimension 5. Its specific structure — Lie ball realization, Bergman kernel, Wallach representations — anchors the BST framework.
