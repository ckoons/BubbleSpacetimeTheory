## Level 2 — graduate-physicist precision

### 6.1 K3 surfaces

A K3 surface is a compact complex surface with:
- Trivial canonical bundle: $K_X = \omega_X = 0$
- $h^{0,1}(X) = h^{1,0}(X) = 0$
- Holomorphic 2-form $\omega \in H^{0,2}$ exists (unique up to scale)
- Hodge numbers: $h^{0,0} = h^{2,2} = 1$, $h^{2,0} = h^{0,2} = 1$, $h^{1,1} = 20$

Euler characteristic: $\chi(K_3) = 24$. Signature: $\sigma = 16$.

All K3 surfaces are diffeomorphic but not all biholomorphic (60-real-dim moduli space).

Examples: quartic in $\mathbb{P}^3$, Kummer surface, elliptic K3, etc.

### 6.3 Niemeier lattices

The 24 **Niemeier lattices** are the 24-dimensional even unimodular lattices (Niemeier 1973). Classified by their root system.

23 of them have root systems (A, D, E type). The 24th has **no roots** — the Leech lattice $\Lambda_{24}$.

### 6.4 Leech lattice Λ_24

Leech lattice (Leech 1967): unique even unimodular 24-dimensional lattice with no vectors of squared length 2.

Properties:
- Densest sphere packing in 24D (Cohn et al. 2017 proof)
- Kissing number: 196560 = $2 \cdot 196883 / 2$ (related to Monster representation)
- Automorphism group $\text{Co}_0$ has order $\sim 8 \times 10^{18}$
- Contains Mathieu group $M_{24}$ as subgroup
