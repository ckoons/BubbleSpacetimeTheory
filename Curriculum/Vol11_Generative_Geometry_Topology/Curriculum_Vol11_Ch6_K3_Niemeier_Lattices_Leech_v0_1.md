# Chapter 6 — K3 Surfaces, Niemeier Lattices, and the Leech Lattice

K3 surfaces (Kummer 1864, Kähler 1933, Kodaira 1964) are compact complex surfaces with trivial canonical bundle. Their Hodge structure is specific. For BST, K3 is one of three RATIFIED Bridge Objects — it has 7 L1 connections in the BST audit chain and is load-bearing.

The 24 Niemeier lattices are the 24-dimensional even unimodular lattices; the Leech lattice $\Lambda_{24}$ is the unique one with no root vectors. The Leech lattice provides BST's χ=24 Bridge Object family-member (K76 audit).

## 6.1 K3 surfaces and Hodge structure

A K3 surface has Hodge numbers $h^{0,0} = h^{2,2} = 1$, $h^{2,0} = h^{0,2} = 1$, $h^{1,1} = 20$, all others zero. Its Hodge structure is the key data.

## 6.2 K3 in BST

K3 is a load-bearing Bridge Object in BST (K57 RATIFIED, 7 L1 connections). The K3 Hodge decomposition reads quark mass ratios $m_c/m_s$, $m_b/m_c$, $m_t/m_b$ (Task #119 completed).

## 6.3 Niemeier lattices and Leech

The 24 Niemeier lattices were classified Niemeier 1973. The Leech lattice — the unique no-root Niemeier — has automorphism group $\text{Co}_0$ containing the Mathieu group $M_{24}$ and many other sporadic-group structure.

In BST: Leech $\Lambda_{24}$ is K76 audit-partial-ready (multi-family Bridge Object family-member with χ=24 anchor).
