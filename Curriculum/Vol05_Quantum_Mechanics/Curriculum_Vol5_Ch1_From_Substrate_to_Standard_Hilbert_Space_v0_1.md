# Chapter 1 — From Substrate to Standard Hilbert Space

Standard quantum mechanics, in its graduate-textbook form, opens with a postulate: physical states live in a complex separable Hilbert space, with observables represented by self-adjoint operators on that space. The choice of which Hilbert space depends on the physical setting — $L^2(\mathbb{R}^3)$ for a single particle in three dimensions, Fock space for many-particle systems, finite-dimensional spaces for spin systems. The choice is partly empirical, partly conventional.

This volume develops quantum mechanics from the substrate framework of Volumes 0 and 1. Where standard QM postulates a Hilbert space, BST *gives* one — the Bergman space $H^2(D_{IV}^5)$ of square-integrable holomorphic functions on the substrate. The standard QM machinery — Heisenberg uncertainty, Schrödinger evolution, the Born rule, Bell inequalities, decoherence, POVMs — all emerge as theorems on the substrate Hilbert space rather than as postulates. This chapter sets the agenda.

## 1.1 What changes when QM lives on $D_{IV}^5$

The standard QM postulate list typically runs:

1. States live in a complex Hilbert space.
2. Observables are self-adjoint operators.
3. Time evolution is unitary, generated by the Hamiltonian via the Schrödinger equation.
4. Measurement outcomes are eigenvalues of the measured observable; probabilities follow the Born rule.
5. After measurement, the state collapses to the eigenstate of the measured outcome.
6. The Hilbert space of a composite system is the tensor product of subsystem Hilbert spaces.

In BST, postulates (1) through (6) are all *derived*. The Hilbert space is given by the substrate (Volume 1 Chapter 2). The operator zoo is given by the substrate isotropy decomposition (Volume 1 Chapter 6). Unitary evolution is the substrate commitment cycle (Volume 1 Chapter 7). The Born rule is the Bergman-kernel projection (K67). Composite-system tensor products are the substrate's natural bipartite K-type structure. Standard QM's postulates become BST's theorems.

What this volume does, chapter by chapter, is unfold this translation. Each of the following chapters takes a standard QM topic and develops its substrate-mechanical content.
