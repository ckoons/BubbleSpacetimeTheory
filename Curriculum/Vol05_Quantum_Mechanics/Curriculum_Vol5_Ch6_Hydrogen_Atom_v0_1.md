# Chapter 6 — The Hydrogen Atom and Atomic Spectra

The hydrogen atom is quantum mechanics' canonical exactly-solvable model. Standard QM solves the Schrödinger equation for the Coulomb potential and recovers the Rydberg formula $E_n = -13.6\, \text{eV}/n^2$, the orbital quantum numbers $n, l, m$, the degeneracy structure, and the selection rules for transitions. Hydrogen spectroscopy was the central empirical evidence for QM in the 1920s.

BST derives the same spectrum from substrate principles, with the additional integer structure of Volume 3 Chapter 7's atomic orbital sequence $(2l+1) = 1, N_c, n_C, g$ emerging naturally.

## 6.1 The hydrogen Hamiltonian on the substrate

The standard hydrogen Hamiltonian is

$$\hat{H} \;=\; \frac{\hat{P}^2}{2 m_e} - \frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r}.$$

On the substrate, the Coulomb potential emerges from the substrate's electromagnetic sector (Volume 7) as the Bergman-bundle connection acting on the substrate's electron K-type. The hydrogen Hamiltonian is therefore the substrate-Hamiltonian restricted to the electron-proton bound K-type sector, with the Coulomb coupling $e^2 / 4\pi\epsilon_0 = \alpha\hbar c = \hbar c / N_{\max}$.

## 6.2 The Rydberg formula

Standard QM derivation of $E_n = -13.6\, \text{eV}/n^2$ uses the Schrödinger equation with the Coulomb potential. The same derivation runs on the substrate, with the substrate's natural energy scale $C_2 \hbar c / a_0 = 6 \hbar c / a_0$ in place of the Rydberg constant. Numerically the two coincide because the substrate's energy unit is structurally tied to the BST primaries that define the Rydberg constant.

The principal quantum number $n$ runs over positive integers; orbital angular momentum $l$ runs from $0$ to $n-1$; magnetic quantum number $m$ runs from $-l$ to $l$; the standard $n^2$ degeneracy of each hydrogen level emerges.

## 6.3 The integer-orbital sequence

A structural observation that Volume 3 Chapter 7 developed: the orbital occupancies of multielectron atoms follow $(2l+1) = 1, 3, 5, 7$ — exactly the BST primary integer sequence $1, N_c, n_C, g$. The hydrogen atom carries this same structure: the $s$, $p$, $d$, $f$ orbital types correspond to angular-momentum quantum numbers $l = 0, 1, 2, 3$ with degeneracies $1, 3, 5, 7$.

This is one of BST's cleanest substrate-mechanical observations: the periodic table's structure is the substrate's K-type angular-momentum spectrum applied at the atomic scale. Standard QM treats the orbital sequence as an empirical consequence; BST identifies it with the BST primary integer sequence directly.

## 6.4 Fine structure and hyperfine

Beyond the basic Rydberg spectrum, hydrogen's energy levels are split by:

- **Fine structure**: relativistic corrections of order $\alpha^2$ from the Dirac equation, splitting levels with the same $n$ by different $j$
- **Hyperfine structure**: nuclear magnetic-moment coupling of order $\alpha^4 m_e/m_p$, splitting levels with the same $j$ by different total angular momentum $F = J \pm I$
- **Lamb shift**: QED vacuum-polarization correction of order $\alpha^3$, shifting $2s_{1/2}$ relative to $2p_{1/2}$

Volume 3 Chapter 8 developed the substrate-mechanism for hyperfine and Lamb shift via the $\alpha^{\text{BST primary}}$ exponent pattern. The fine structure follows similarly, with each correction order tied to a specific BST-primary integer exponent of $\alpha$.
