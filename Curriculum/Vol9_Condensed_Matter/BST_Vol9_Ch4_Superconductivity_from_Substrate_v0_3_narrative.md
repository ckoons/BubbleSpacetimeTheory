## Pedagogical walkthrough (3-level)

### Level 1 — Bright 5th-grader

> Superconductors are materials that conduct electricity with ZERO resistance — they can carry electrical currents forever without losing energy. Most superconductors only work at very cold temperatures. Scientists have been hunting for "high-temperature" superconductors that work near room temperature. BST predicts that a specific compound — B12H32 (made from 12 boron atoms and 32 hydrogen atoms) — should superconduct at 214 Kelvin (about -59°C, much warmer than dry ice). Nobody has tried to make this compound yet. If experiments make it and it superconducts at 214K, BST is confirmed. If it doesn't, BST is refuted on this prediction.

### Level 2 — Undergraduate physics student

Standard BCS theory (1957) explains conventional superconductivity via phonon-mediated electron-pair binding. Cooper pairs form below T_c. The phonon-mediated attraction is weak; conventional BCS superconductors have T_c < 40K (Nb_3Sn ~ 18K).

High-T_c cuprates (Bednorz-Müller 1986, YBCO T_c ~ 92K) and high-pressure hydrides (LaH_10 T_c ~ 250K at 170 GPa) achieve higher T_c via non-phonon-only mechanisms.

BST proposes substrate-mediated Cooper pair binding via K-type Casimir resonance at substrate-natural hydride-cluster scale. Specifically B12H32 (closo-dodecaborate, icosahedral B12 cage) predicted at T_c ≈ 214K under ambient/modest-pressure conditions.

Estimated program cost ~$3-5M multi-year. If T_c near 214K → BST high-T_c mechanism confirmed; if dramatically lower or absent → BST substrate-mediated SC refuted.

### Level 3 — Graduate student / theorem-level

**Substrate-mediated Cooper pair binding**:

Conventional BCS: $\Delta_{BCS}(T) = 2 \omega_D \exp(-1/N_F V_{ph})$ where $V_{ph}$ is phonon-mediated electron-electron coupling, $\omega_D$ is Debye frequency, $N_F$ is density of states at Fermi level.

BST extension: substrate-mediated coupling $V_{sub}$ adds to $V_{ph}$:
$$V_{eff} = V_{ph} + V_{sub}$$

where $V_{sub}$ derives from substrate K-type Casimir spectrum coupled to electron-pair condensate at hydride-cluster scale.
