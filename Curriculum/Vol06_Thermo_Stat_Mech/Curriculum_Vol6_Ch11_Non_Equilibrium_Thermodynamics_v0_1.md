# Chapter 11 — Non-Equilibrium Thermodynamics

Most physical systems are not in thermal equilibrium. Heat flows; chemical reactions proceed; biological systems sustain themselves far from equilibrium by constant energy throughput. Non-equilibrium thermodynamics — Onsager relations, fluctuation-dissipation theorem, irreversibility — develops the framework for these systems.

In BST, non-equilibrium thermodynamics emerges as the substrate's pre-equilibration behavior at scales below thermal relaxation time.

## 11.1 Onsager reciprocity

Onsager 1931 showed that the linear-response coefficients $L_{ij}$ relating thermodynamic forces to fluxes satisfy reciprocity: $L_{ij} = L_{ji}$. The substrate-mechanism reading: the substrate's commitment cycle is time-symmetric at the per-tick level in non-weak sectors, and the Onsager reciprocity inherits this time-symmetry.

## 11.2 Fluctuation-dissipation

The fluctuation-dissipation theorem relates equilibrium fluctuations of an observable to its dissipative response to external perturbation. Substrate-mechanism: the same Bergman-kernel structure that governs equilibrium correlations governs non-equilibrium response, with the FDT a structural identity on substrate states.
