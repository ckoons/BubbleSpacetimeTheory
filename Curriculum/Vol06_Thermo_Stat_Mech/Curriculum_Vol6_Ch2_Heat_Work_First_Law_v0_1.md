# Chapter 2 — Heat, Work, and the First Law

The first law of thermodynamics states that energy is conserved: the change in a system's internal energy equals the heat added minus the work done by the system. Symbolically, $dU = \delta Q - \delta W$. Standard treatments define heat and work operationally — heat as energy transferred by thermal contact, work as energy transferred by macroscopic forces — and the first law as the conservation accounting.

In BST, both heat and work have direct substrate-mechanical interpretations via the four-phase commitment cycle of Volume 0 Chapter 3.

## 2.1 Heat as Zone-1 substrate absorption

Heat transfer is the substrate's **Zone 1 absorption**: substrate state receiving energy from environment in the form of incoherent (thermal) excitations spread across many K-type eigenstates. The amount of heat $Q$ transferred corresponds to the substrate-natural energy summed over the absorbed K-type quanta weighted by their Casimir eigenvalues.

## 2.2 Work as Zone-4 substrate emission

Work transfer is the substrate's **Zone 4 emission**: substrate state broadcasting energy to environment in the form of coherent (directed) excitations concentrated on specific K-type modes. The amount of work $W$ corresponds to the substrate-natural energy emitted at the Zone-4 active edge in directed modes.

The distinction between heat and work is structural: incoherent versus coherent emission, distributed K-type versus concentrated K-type. Standard thermo's intuitive "heat is disordered energy, work is ordered energy" is the substrate's structural distinction between incoherent absorption/emission and directed absorption/emission.

## 2.3 The first law as substrate cycle closure

The first law $dU = \delta Q - \delta W$ becomes, on the substrate, a per-tick energy-conservation statement: the substrate's internal energy after Zone 4 equals the energy at Zone 1 plus heat absorbed minus work done. Conservation follows automatically from the unitarity of substrate commitment dynamics (Volume 0 Chapter 8).

## 2.4 Enthalpy and the laboratory framework

Enthalpy $H = U + PV$ — the energy plus pressure-volume — is the substrate's relevant energy quantity when pressure rather than volume is held constant. The substrate-mechanism follows from the Zone-1-to-Zone-4 cycle in pressure-constrained settings.
