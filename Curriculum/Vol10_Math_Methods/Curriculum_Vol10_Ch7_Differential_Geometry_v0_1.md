# Chapter 7 — Differential Geometry

Differential geometry treats smooth manifolds, tangent bundles, connections, curvature. Riemannian geometry underlies general relativity; Kähler geometry underlies bounded symmetric domains; bundle connections underlie gauge theory.

In BST, $D_{IV}^5$ is a Kähler manifold (bounded symmetric domain) with Bergman metric. The Bergman curvature underlies Newton's G derivation (Volume 4 Chapter 1).

## 7.1 Smooth manifolds

A smooth manifold is a topological space locally homeomorphic to $\mathbb{R}^n$ with smooth transition functions. Tangent and cotangent bundles provide the linear-algebra structure at each point.

## 7.2 Riemannian and Kähler geometry

Riemannian metric gives infinitesimal length; Levi-Civita connection gives parallel transport; Riemann curvature characterizes intrinsic bending. Kähler metric on a complex manifold is a Hermitian metric satisfying $d\omega = 0$.

## 7.3 $D_{IV}^5$ as Kähler manifold

The bounded symmetric domain $D_{IV}^5$ is Kähler with Bergman metric — the metric induced by the Bergman kernel. Its Bergman curvature is BST-derivable; in the BST framework it equals the substrate's natural gravitational coupling source for Newton's G derivation.
