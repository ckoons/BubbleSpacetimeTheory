# Chapter 9 — Special Functions

Special functions — Gamma, zeta, Bessel, hypergeometric, Fox H, Aleph, Heckman-Opdam, mock theta — recur throughout physics. BST's special-functions investigation program (May 2026) sought to identify substrate-natural special function structure underlying the $\zeta_B(s)$ functional equation; partial results were obtained on Fox H, and new candidates on Aleph / Heckman-Opdam / mock theta directions.

## 9.1 Gamma and zeta functions

The Gamma function $\Gamma(s)$ extends factorial to complex arguments. The Riemann zeta function $\zeta(s) = \sum n^{-s}$ has critical strip $0 < \Re s < 1$ where the Riemann Hypothesis lives (BST RH CONDITIONAL April 21, 2026 per the team's analysis).

## 9.2 Hypergeometric and generalized hypergeometric

Hypergeometric ${}_2F_1(a,b;c;z)$ and generalized ${}_pF_q$ functions cover many physically-relevant special functions as special cases. The Fox H function generalizes further.

## 9.3 Substrate-natural special functions

BST identifies certain combinations as substrate-natural. The Bergman kernel on $D_{IV}^5$ has explicit hypergeometric-type representation. The arithmetic triangle structure (Paper #9) gives substrate-specific evaluation of heat-kernel coefficients.
