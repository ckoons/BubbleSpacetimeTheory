# Chapter 5 — Fourier Analysis and Distributions

Fourier analysis decomposes functions into oscillatory modes. Schwartz distributions extend differentiation and Fourier transformation to objects like the Dirac delta. Both are foundational for quantum mechanics, signal processing, and PDEs.

## 5.1 Fourier series and Fourier transform

Periodic functions decompose into Fourier series; non-periodic functions decompose into Fourier transforms. Plancherel's theorem: the Fourier transform is an isometry on $L^2$.

## 5.2 Distributions

The Schwartz space $\mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R}^n)$ of rapidly decreasing smooth functions has dual space — tempered distributions — including $\delta$, $\delta'$, principal value $1/x$. Distributions extend differentiation and Fourier transformation to non-functions.

## 5.3 Substrate connection

The substrate's Wirtinger momentum operator (Volume 5 Chapter 2) is the holomorphic-Fourier analog on $D_{IV}^5$. The substrate's Bergman kernel decomposition is the Fourier-like expansion for the substrate.
