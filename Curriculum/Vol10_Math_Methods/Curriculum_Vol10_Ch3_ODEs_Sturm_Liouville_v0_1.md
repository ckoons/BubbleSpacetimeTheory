# Chapter 3 — ODEs and Sturm-Liouville Theory

Ordinary differential equations describe physical systems with one independent variable. Sturm-Liouville theory provides the spectral framework for boundary-value problems: eigenfunctions form complete orthogonal bases, eigenvalues are real, and many problems in quantum mechanics (radial Schrödinger, harmonic oscillator) reduce to Sturm-Liouville form.

## 3.1 First-order and second-order ODEs

Standard first-order $y' = f(x,y)$ and second-order $y'' = f(x,y,y')$ ODEs. Existence and uniqueness via Picard-Lindelöf.

## 3.2 Sturm-Liouville problems

A Sturm-Liouville operator $L = -d/dx(p(x)d/dx) + q(x)$ with weight $w(x)$ has discrete real spectrum $\lambda_n$ and orthogonal eigenfunctions $\phi_n$. Completeness gives Fourier-like expansions in the eigenbasis.

## 3.3 Connection to substrate framework

The substrate's Casimir operator on $L^2(D_{IV}^5; L_\lambda)$ is a Sturm-Liouville-type spectral problem. Eigenvalues organize substrate K-types; eigenfunctions are the substrate's natural state basis.
