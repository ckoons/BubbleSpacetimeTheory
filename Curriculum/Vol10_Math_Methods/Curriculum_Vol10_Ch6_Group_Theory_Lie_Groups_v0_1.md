# Chapter 6 — Group Theory and Lie Groups

Groups capture symmetry. Lie groups — smooth manifolds with compatible group structure — describe continuous symmetries: rotations, Lorentz transformations, gauge symmetries. The Standard Model is built on the gauge Lie group $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$. BST is built on $SO_0(5,2)$.

This chapter is load-bearing for the BST framework.

## 6.1 Groups and subgroups

A group is a set with an associative binary operation, identity, and inverses. Standard examples: $\mathbb{Z}$, $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, symmetric group $S_n$, orthogonal group $O(n)$, special orthogonal group $SO(n)$.

## 6.2 Lie groups and Lie algebras

A Lie group is a smooth manifold with smooth group operations. Its Lie algebra is the tangent space at the identity with the Lie bracket. The exponential map connects Lie algebra to Lie group: $\exp: \mathfrak{g} \to G$.

## 6.3 $SO_0(5,2)$ and its isotropy decomposition

The BST framework's central group is $SO_0(5,2)$ — the identity component of the orthogonal group preserving a signature $(5,2)$ quadratic form. Its maximal compact subgroup is $SO(5) \times SO(2)$. The quotient $D_{IV}^5 = SO_0(5,2)/[SO(5) \times SO(2)]$ is the Type IV bounded symmetric domain of dimension 5 — the unique Autogenic Proto-Geometry (APG) of BST.

The Lie algebra $\mathfrak{so}(5,2)$ has rank 2; the Cartan subalgebra has $\rho = (5/2, 3/2)$.
