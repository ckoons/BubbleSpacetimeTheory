# Chapter 2 — Complex Analysis and Holomorphic Methods

Complex analysis — holomorphic functions, Cauchy's theorem, residue calculus, analytic continuation, conformal maps — is foundational for quantum field theory (propagators, Wick rotation), special functions (Gamma, zeta), and the Bergman framework (holomorphic L² functions on bounded symmetric domains).

## 2.1 Holomorphic functions

A function $f: U \to \mathbb{C}$ is holomorphic if its complex derivative exists. The Cauchy-Riemann equations characterize holomorphy locally; holomorphic functions are infinitely differentiable and locally given by power series.

## 2.2 Cauchy's theorem and residues

Cauchy's theorem: $\oint_\gamma f(z)\,dz = 0$ for holomorphic $f$ over a simply connected region. Residues at poles allow evaluation of integrals via the residue theorem. Standard apparatus.

## 2.3 Holomorphic functions on $D_{IV}^5$

Bergman space $H^2(D_{IV}^5)$ consists of $L^2$-integrable holomorphic functions on $D_{IV}^5$. The Bergman kernel is the reproducing kernel for this space and the structural anchor for BST's substrate framework.
