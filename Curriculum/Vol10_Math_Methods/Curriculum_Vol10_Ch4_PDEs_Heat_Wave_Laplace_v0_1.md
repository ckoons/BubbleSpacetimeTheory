# Chapter 4 — PDEs: Heat, Wave, Laplace

The three canonical second-order PDEs — heat (parabolic), wave (hyperbolic), Laplace (elliptic) — span the classification of linear second-order PDEs and underlie much of mathematical physics.

In BST, the substrate's natural heat kernel on $D_{IV}^5$ — the kernel of the Laplace-Beltrami semigroup — anchors the partition function and the arithmetic triangle structure (Paper #9).

## 4.1 The three canonical PDEs

- Heat equation $\partial_t u = \Delta u$: parabolic; smoothing; diffusion-like
- Wave equation $\partial_t^2 u = \Delta u$: hyperbolic; finite propagation speed
- Laplace equation $\Delta u = 0$: elliptic; harmonic functions; mean-value property

## 4.2 Heat kernel on $D_{IV}^5$
