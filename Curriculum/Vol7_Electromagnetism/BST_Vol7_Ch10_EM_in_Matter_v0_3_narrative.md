## Optical phenomena

**Reflection + transmission**: Fresnel equations at boundary; reflection coefficient depends on (n_1 - n_2)/(n_1 + n_2).

**Snell's law**: n_1 sin θ_1 = n_2 sin θ_2 from boundary conditions.

**Dispersion**: n(ω) varies → prism + rainbow spectral separation.

**Absorption + extinction**: Im(n) gives absorption coefficient α_abs = 2ω Im(n)/c.

**Anomalous dispersion**: near resonance, n(ω) decreases with ω (vs normal increasing); related to Kramers-Kronig dispersion relations.

### Level 2 — Undergraduate physics student

**Dielectric function from Lorentz oscillator model**:

Driven damped oscillator (electron in atom):
$$m\ddot{x} + m\gamma\dot{x} + m\omega_0^2 x = -eE_0 e^{-i\omega t}$$

Solution: x(t) = -eE_0/(m(ω_0² - ω² - iγω)) e^{-iωt}.

Polarization P = -nex (n electrons per volume) → dielectric function:
$$\varepsilon(\omega) = 1 + \frac{ne^2/(m\varepsilon_0)}{\omega_0^2 - \omega^2 - i\gamma\omega}$$

**Refractive index**: n(ω) = √(ε(ω)μ(ω)). For non-magnetic (μ = 1): n = √ε.

**Real + imaginary parts**:
- Re(ε): refraction (phase velocity c/n)
- Im(ε): absorption

**Kramers-Kronig relations**: dispersion relations connect Re(ε) and Im(ε) via Hilbert transform.

**Examples**:
- Water visible: n ≈ 1.33
- Glass visible: n ≈ 1.5
- Diamond visible: n ≈ 2.4
- Silicon visible: n ≈ 3.5 (highly dispersive near band-edge)

**Drude model** (free-electron contribution):
$$\varepsilon(\omega) = 1 - \frac{\omega_p^2}{\omega(\omega + i/\tau)}$$

where ω_p = √(ne²/(mε_0)) is plasma frequency, τ is relaxation time. Below ω_p: metal (Re(ε) < 0, reflects light). Above ω_p: transparent.

**Kramers-Kronig dispersion relations**:
$$\text{Re}\,\varepsilon(\omega) - 1 = \frac{2}{\pi} \mathcal{P} \int_0^\infty \frac{\omega' \text{Im}\,\varepsilon(\omega')}{\omega'^2 - \omega^2} d\omega'$$
$$\text{Im}\,\varepsilon(\omega) = -\frac{2\omega}{\pi} \mathcal{P} \int_0^\infty \frac{\text{Re}\,\varepsilon(\omega') - 1}{\omega'^2 - \omega^2} d\omega'$$
