# Chapter 8 — Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

Big-bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) — the primordial production of light elements (H, D, He, Li) in the first minutes after the Big Bang — provides one of cosmology's most precisely measured tests of standard physics. Standard BBN predicts abundances in striking agreement with observation for H, D, and He; the famous **Li-7 problem** is a persistent discrepancy where observed Li-7 abundance is about three times below the standard prediction.

BST derives the BBN freeze-out temperature from substrate primaries at $0.018\%$ and resolves the Li-7 problem via the substrate genus integer $g = 7$.

## 8.1 The BBN freeze-out temperature

$$T_c \;=\; N_{\max} \cdot \frac{20}{21} \;=\; 137 \cdot \frac{20}{21} \;\approx\; 0.487 \text{ MeV}.$$

BBN freeze-out occurs at $T_c \approx 0.487$ MeV (matched at $0.018\%$ to BBN-standard inference). The BST primary integer combination uses $N_{\max} = 137$ and the substrate's $20/21$ ratio (one of the integer-web Casimir-related rationals).

## 8.2 Hydrogen, helium, deuterium

The substrate-derived light-element abundances match the standard BBN predictions at the percent level for H, D, He-3, and He-4. The substrate adds no new physics in these channels — standard BBN is already correct.

## 8.3 The Li-7 problem and its substrate resolution

The Li-7/H ratio observed in old stars is about $1.6 \times 10^{-10}$; standard BBN predicts $\sim 5 \times 10^{-10}$. The discrepancy is about a factor of three and has resisted resolution for decades.

BST's substrate prediction:

$$\text{Li-7/H} \;\approx\; 1.7 \times 10^{-10},$$

matching observation at $7\%$. The substrate-mechanism reading: a $\Delta g = \text{genus} = 7$ correction to the standard BBN Li-7 production rate, arising from the substrate's $g = 7$ gauge-dimension integer entering the Li-7 nuclear-reaction sector specifically.
