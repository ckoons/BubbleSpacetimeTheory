# Chapter 11 — Spin Liquids

Spin liquids are magnetic systems where geometric frustration prevents conventional magnetic ordering down to the lowest temperatures. The Kagome and pyrochlore lattices, herbertsmithite (ZnCu₃(OH)₆Cl₂), and Kitaev honeycomb model are the framework's prototypes.

In BST, spin liquids are substrate K-type configurations where lattice geometry forces the substrate into entangled non-ordered states.

## 11.1 Geometric frustration

Triangular and Kagome lattices: nearest-neighbor antiferromagnetic interactions cannot be simultaneously satisfied. Pyrochlore: the same in 3D.

## 11.2 Quantum spin liquid order parameters

BST has produced spin liquid order parameter predictions (Task #107). These extend the BST framework's predictive reach into the most challenging magnetic-correlation regime.

## 11.3 The Kitaev model

The Kitaev honeycomb model is exactly solvable: spins fractionalize into Majorana fermions plus a static Z₂ gauge field. The resulting state is a quantum spin liquid with topological order.
