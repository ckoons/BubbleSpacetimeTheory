# Chapter 11 — Quantum Spin Liquids

## Level 1 — one sentence

Quantum spin liquids are magnetic systems where geometric frustration prevents conventional magnetic ordering down to $T = 0$, with fractionalized spinon excitations and emergent gauge fields giving exotic topological order — and BST has filed specific order-parameter predictions (Task #107) for the leading candidate materials.

## Level 2 — graduate-physicist precision

### 11.1 Geometric frustration

A simple frustrated system: three spins at triangle vertices with antiferromagnetic Heisenberg coupling. No spin configuration satisfies all three antiferromagnetic bonds simultaneously. Lowest-energy states are degenerate.

Kagome lattice (corner-sharing triangles), pyrochlore lattice (corner-sharing tetrahedra) are quintessentially frustrated. Cause: ground state has macroscopic degeneracy at the classical level → quantum effects matter at all temperatures.

### 11.2 Resonating valence bond (RVB)

Anderson 1973: ground state of frustrated antiferromagnet can be a **resonating valence bond** state — singlet pairs that resonate among many configurations. No long-range order; gapless or gapped depending on details.

Re-introduced for cuprates by Anderson 1987 in superconductivity context.

### 11.3 Spinon excitations and fractionalization

In a spin liquid, the elementary excitations are **spinons** — spin-1/2 quasiparticles that carry only spin and no charge. In contrast to spin-1 magnons of ordered antiferromagnets, spinons can be deconfined.

Spinon deconfinement is a topological-order property: spinons exist in pairs in ordered antiferromagnets (the pair = spin-1 magnon) but separate freely in the spin liquid.

### 11.4 Emergent gauge fields

Spin liquids typically host emergent gauge fields:
- $\mathbb{Z}_2$ gauge field (toric code, gapped spin liquids like kagome candidate)
- $U(1)$ gauge field (gapless spin liquids)

Photon-like excitations in $U(1)$ spin liquids = emergent gauge bosons.

### 11.5 Candidate materials

- **Herbertsmithite** ZnCu₃(OH)₆Cl₂ (kagome): no magnetic order down to mK. Leading kagome candidate.
- **Pyrochlores** Yb₂Ti₂O₇, Tb₂Ti₂O₇: spin-ice and spin-liquid behaviors
- **Triangular Cs₂CuCl₄**: 2D triangular spin liquid candidate
- **α-RuCl₃** (Kitaev candidate): leading Kitaev-honeycomb realization

### 11.6 The Kitaev model

Kitaev 2006: honeycomb lattice with bond-dependent Ising couplings $H = -\sum_{\langle ij\rangle_\gamma} K_\gamma S_i^\gamma S_j^\gamma$. Exactly solvable.

Ground state: $\mathbb{Z}_2$ spin liquid with Majorana fermion + static $\mathbb{Z}_2$ flux. Non-Abelian phase in magnetic field; supports anyons useful for topological quantum computing.

α-RuCl₃ is the leading material realization candidate.

## Level 3 — 5th-grader accessibility

**Spin liquids** are magnetic systems that *refuse* to order even at $T = 0$. The frustration: arrange three spins at triangle vertices, each wanting to anti-align with neighbors — can't be done. Quantum fluctuations prevent any classical order from forming. Excitations are **spinons** (fractionalized spin-1/2 particles) instead of magnons. **Emergent gauge fields** arise — the spin liquid looks like a tiny universe with its own photons. Leading candidates: herbertsmithite (kagome lattice), α-RuCl₃ (Kitaev honeycomb). **Quantum computing applications**: Kitaev's model supports non-Abelian anyons useful for topologically-protected quantum bits.
