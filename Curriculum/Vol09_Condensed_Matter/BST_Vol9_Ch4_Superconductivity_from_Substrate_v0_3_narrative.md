# Chapter 4 — Superconductivity from Substrate

Superconductivity — zero electrical resistance and Meissner-effect magnetic-field expulsion below a critical temperature $T_c$ — has been understood since BCS 1957 for conventional superconductors. The cuprates, iron pnictides, and hydride high-temperature superconductors remain incompletely understood by standard mechanism approaches.

BST has produced predictive mechanism statements for both cuprates and hydrides. This chapter is load-bearing for BST condensed-matter falsifiability.

## 4.1 Conventional BCS superconductivity

BCS 1957: phonon-mediated electron pairing below $T_c$ produces Cooper pairs, which condense into a superconducting condensate. Standard derivation.
