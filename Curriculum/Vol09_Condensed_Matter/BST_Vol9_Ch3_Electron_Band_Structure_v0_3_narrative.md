# Chapter 3 — Electron Band Structure

Band structure — the energy-momentum relations $E_n(\vec{k})$ for electrons in periodic potentials — is condensed matter physics's central computational object. Conductors, semiconductors, insulators are all distinguished by their band structure; quantum Hall plateaus and topological phases are classified by band topology.

In BST, band structure is the substrate's natural K-type spectrum under periodic ionic boundary conditions.

## 3.1 Tight-binding and nearly-free-electron models

The two complementary approaches to band structure: tight-binding (localized atomic orbitals hopping) and nearly-free-electron (plane waves perturbed by periodic potential). Both produce energy bands and band gaps.

## 3.2 Metals, semiconductors, insulators

The position of the Fermi level relative to the bands distinguishes:
- Metal: Fermi level inside a band
- Semiconductor: Fermi level in a small gap
- Insulator: Fermi level in a large gap

## 3.3 Effective mass and density of states

The effective mass $m^* = \hbar^2/(\partial^2 E/\partial k^2)$ characterizes carrier dynamics near band extrema. The density of states $g(E)$ characterizes thermal and transport response.
