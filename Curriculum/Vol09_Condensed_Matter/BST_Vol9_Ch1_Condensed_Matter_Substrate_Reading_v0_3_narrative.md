# Chapter 1 — Condensed Matter as Substrate Many-Electron Behavior

Condensed matter physics is where BST is most experimentally falsifiable. The cuprate superconductor mechanism, B12H32 hydride T_c ~214 K prediction, BaTiO3 137-plane experiment (~$25K), photonic crystal eigentone test (~$10K), topological-insulator classification — all are BST-falsifiable at modest cost in working laboratories.

This volume develops the apparatus.

## 1.1 What condensed matter is in BST

Condensed matter — solids, liquids, electronic phases, magnetism, superconductivity — is the substrate's behavior in macroscopic many-electron systems. Electrons are substrate K-types; ions provide periodic boundary conditions; the resulting many-body behavior is what condensed matter physics studies.

The substrate's natural integer N_max = 137 figures load-bearingly: BaTiO3's 137-plane configuration is a substrate-natural target (Chapter 8).
