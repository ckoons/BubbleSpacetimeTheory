# Chapter 5 — Topological Phases

Topological phases of matter — topological insulators, quantum Hall states, Weyl semimetals, topological superconductors — are characterized by quantized topological invariants rather than local order parameters. The classification of topological phases via K-theory and bulk-boundary correspondence is a major theoretical achievement of the 2000s-2010s.

In BST, topological phases are substrate-K-type configurations classified by their substrate-bundle topology over the Brillouin zone.

## 5.1 Quantum Hall effect

The integer quantum Hall effect — quantized Hall conductivity $\sigma_{xy} = ne^2/h$ at integer $n$ — is the prototype topological phase. The Hall conductivity is exactly quantized because it equals the topological Chern number of the occupied bands.

BST quantum Hall plateau predictions (Task #108) verify the BST framework's reach into precision quantum Hall measurements.

## 5.2 Topological insulators

Topological insulators are insulating in the bulk but have conducting surface states protected by time-reversal symmetry. Bi₂Se₃, Bi₂Te₃ are textbook examples.

BST classification of topological insulators (Task #106): the substrate framework provides a natural classification scheme via substrate K-type representation theory on the substrate band-structure.

## 5.3 Weyl and Dirac semimetals

Weyl semimetals have band-touching points with linear dispersion. BST identifies these as substrate K-type degeneracies under specific boundary conditions.
