# Chapter 9 — Photonic Crystals: The ~$10K BST Falsifier

Photonic crystals — periodic dielectric structures that produce optical band gaps — provide BST's cheapest substrate-eigentone falsifier. Estimated cost ~$10K; standard optics-laboratory apparatus; substrate eigentone resonance prediction.

## 9.1 Photonic crystal physics

Photonic crystals (1D periodic stacks, 2D photonic-crystal slabs, 3D opal-like structures) produce optical band gaps analogous to electron band gaps in solid-state crystals. Light at frequencies inside the gap cannot propagate through the crystal.

## 9.2 Substrate eigentone prediction in photonic crystals

A specifically-designed photonic crystal with substrate-natural geometry (BST-primary-anchored lattice parameters) is predicted to ring at a substrate-natural eigentone frequency observable in transmission/reflection spectroscopy.

The cost — ~$10K — makes this the most accessible BST falsifier. Any well-equipped optics laboratory could perform the test.
