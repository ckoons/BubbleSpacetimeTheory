# Chapter 6 — Magnetism

Magnetism — diamagnetism, paramagnetism, ferromagnetism, antiferromagnetism, ferrimagnetism — arises from electron spin and orbital angular momentum in solids. The Curie temperature for ferromagnets, Néel temperature for antiferromagnets, and Bohr magneton are the framework's foundational quantities.

In BST, magnetism is the substrate's K-type spin alignment under exchange-coupling boundary conditions.

## 6.1 Magnetic order parameters

Magnetic order parameters (magnetization for ferromagnets, staggered magnetization for antiferromagnets) characterize the broken-symmetry magnetic phases.

## 6.2 Exchange interactions

Heisenberg exchange $J\vec{S}_i\cdot\vec{S}_j$, Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya $\vec{D}\cdot(\vec{S}_i \times \vec{S}_j)$, and anisotropy interactions characterize magnetic Hamiltonians.

## 6.3 Curie-Weiss law

Above the Curie temperature, ferromagnetic susceptibility follows $\chi = C/(T - T_c)$. The mean-field treatment is standard.
