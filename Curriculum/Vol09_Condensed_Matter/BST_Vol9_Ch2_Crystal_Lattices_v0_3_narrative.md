# Chapter 2 — Crystal Lattices

## Level 1 — one sentence

Crystalline solids have periodic atomic arrangements classified by the 14 Bravais lattices and 230 space groups, giving Bloch's theorem $\psi(\vec r) = e^{i\vec k\cdot\vec r} u(\vec r)$ as the fundamental solution-form for electrons in periodic potentials, with reciprocal lattices and Brillouin zones structuring all of solid-state band theory.

## Level 2 — graduate-physicist precision

### 2.1 The 14 Bravais lattices

A **Bravais lattice** is an infinite array of points generated by primitive translation vectors $\vec a_1, \vec a_2, \vec a_3$:

$$\vec R = n_1 \vec a_1 + n_2 \vec a_2 + n_3 \vec a_3, \quad n_i \in \mathbb{Z}$$

In 3D, 14 distinct Bravais lattices (Bravais 1849):
- 7 crystal systems (triclinic, monoclinic, orthorhombic, tetragonal, cubic, hexagonal, trigonal)
- Plus various centerings (P primitive, I body-centered, F face-centered, C base-centered)

Specific examples:
- Simple cubic (SC): primitive vectors along $\hat x, \hat y, \hat z$
- Body-centered cubic (BCC): atoms at corners + cube center (Fe, Na)
- Face-centered cubic (FCC): atoms at corners + face centers (Cu, Al, Au)

### 2.2 The 230 space groups

Adding rotation, reflection, glide, screw symmetries to the 14 Bravais lattices gives 230 space groups in 3D — the complete classification of crystallographic symmetry (Schönflies 1891, Fedorov 1891). All real crystals belong to one of these.

### 2.3 Reciprocal lattice and Brillouin zones

The **reciprocal lattice** has primitive vectors $\vec b_i$ satisfying $\vec a_i \cdot \vec b_j = 2\pi \delta_{ij}$. Explicit: $\vec b_1 = 2\pi(\vec a_2 \times \vec a_3)/[\vec a_1 \cdot (\vec a_2 \times \vec a_3)]$, cyclic.

Reciprocal lattice points $\vec G = m_1 \vec b_1 + m_2 \vec b_2 + m_3 \vec b_3$ satisfy $e^{i\vec G \cdot \vec R} = 1$ for all direct-lattice vectors $\vec R$.

**Brillouin zone** = Wigner-Seitz cell of reciprocal lattice — the natural domain for crystal momentum $\vec k$.

### 2.4 Bloch's theorem

For a single electron in a periodic potential $V(\vec r + \vec R) = V(\vec r)$:

$$\psi_{n\vec k}(\vec r) = e^{i\vec k \cdot \vec r} u_{n\vec k}(\vec r)$$

with $u_{n\vec k}(\vec r + \vec R) = u_{n\vec k}(\vec r)$ periodic. The wave function has plane-wave envelope $e^{i\vec k \cdot \vec r}$ modulated by a lattice-periodic function $u$.

$n$ is the band index; $\vec k$ is crystal momentum in first Brillouin zone.

Substrate reading: Bloch's theorem expresses the substrate's K-type structure on a periodic lattice — the K-type is labeled by band $n$ and Brillouin-zone-restricted momentum $\vec k$.

### 2.5 X-ray diffraction and Bragg's law

X-rays diffract from crystals at angles satisfying $2d\sin\theta = n\lambda$ (Bragg's law, 1912). The reciprocal-lattice formulation: scattering vector $\vec q = \vec k_{\text{out}} - \vec k_{\text{in}}$ must equal a reciprocal-lattice vector $\vec G$ for constructive interference.

Crystallography techniques: X-ray, neutron, electron diffraction. Determine atomic positions to <0.01 Å precision. Foundation for materials science, structural biology (DNA structure, protein structures all from crystallography).

### 2.6 BaTiO3 137-plane (preview Ch 8)

BaTiO3 has a perovskite structure (Pm$\bar 3$m space group at high T, P4mm at room T after ferroelectric transition). The "137-plane" crystallographic configuration is the specific plane orientation where the substrate's $N_{\max} = 137$ engages. Ch 8 develops the BST eigentone prediction.
