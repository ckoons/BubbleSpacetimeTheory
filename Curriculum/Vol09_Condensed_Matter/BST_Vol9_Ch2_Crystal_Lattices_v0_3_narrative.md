# Chapter 2 — Crystal Lattices

Crystallography classifies periodic arrangements of atoms. The 14 Bravais lattices, 230 space groups, point groups, Brillouin zones, reciprocal lattices, Bloch's theorem — the framework is foundational.

In BST, crystal lattices are macroscopic-scale substrate boundary conditions: periodic substrate K-type arrangements that constrain electron substrate dynamics.

## 2.1 The Bravais lattices

14 Bravais lattices classify all possible periodic arrangements in 3D. K50 in the BST audit chain investigates Bravais lattices as a candidate L1 ESTABLISHED source.

## 2.2 Reciprocal lattice and Brillouin zones

The reciprocal lattice $\vec{G}$ and Brillouin zone are dual structures used for band-structure analysis. Standard apparatus.

## 2.3 Bloch's theorem

For a periodic potential, electron wave functions take the form $\psi(\vec{r}) = e^{i\vec{k}\cdot\vec{r}} u_{\vec{k}}(\vec{r})$ with $u$ periodic. This is Bloch's theorem (1928), the foundation of solid-state band theory.

Substrate-mechanism: Bloch's theorem reflects the substrate's natural K-type periodicity under translational symmetry of the ionic boundary conditions.
