# Chapter 10 — Strongly Correlated Systems

Strongly correlated electron systems — heavy fermions, Mott insulators, doped cuprates, fractional quantum Hall — are systems where electron-electron interactions dominate over kinetic energy and standard band-theory approximations fail. The framework is one of the most computationally challenging areas of condensed matter.

In BST, strongly correlated systems are substrate K-type many-body configurations where boundary conditions force the substrate into non-trivial entangled states.

## 10.1 Mott insulators and Hubbard model

The Hubbard model $H = -t\sum c^\dagger c + U\sum n_\uparrow n_\downarrow$ is the simplest model of strong correlations. At large $U/t$, half-filled band becomes a Mott insulator despite naive band theory predicting metallic behavior.

## 10.2 Heavy fermion materials

Heavy fermion materials (CeAl₃, UPt₃, etc.) have effective electron masses 100-1000× the free electron mass due to Kondo coupling between conduction electrons and localized f-electron moments.

## 10.3 Fractional quantum Hall

The fractional quantum Hall effect — quantization at $\sigma_{xy} = (1/3)e^2/h$, $(2/5)e^2/h$, etc. — arises from strongly-correlated electron states in 2D under high magnetic field. Laughlin wave functions describe the basic states.
