# Chapter 7 — Phonons

Phonons — quantized lattice vibration modes — mediate the BCS superconductor pairing interaction, carry thermal energy in insulators, and provide the kinetic-scattering channel for electrons in metals. The Debye temperature $\Theta_D$ characterizes the highest phonon energy and sets the thermal-properties scale of materials.

In BST, phonons are substrate vibrational excitations of the periodic ionic lattice boundary condition — quantized substrate K-types of the lattice vibration spectrum.

## 7.1 Acoustic and optical phonons

Acoustic phonons (low-frequency, vanish at $k=0$) and optical phonons (high-frequency, finite at $k=0$) are the two phonon-mode families. Their dispersion relations follow from lattice dynamics.

## 7.2 Debye and Einstein models

The Debye model treats acoustic phonons up to a cutoff $\Theta_D$; the Einstein model treats optical phonons at a single frequency. Both give heat capacity predictions matching low-T and high-T experimental behavior.

## 7.3 Phonon-mediated interactions

Phonons mediate the BCS pairing interaction (Chapter 4). The strength of phonon-electron coupling controls T_c in conventional superconductors.
