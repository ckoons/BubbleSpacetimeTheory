# Chapter 3 — Prebiotic Forcing

## Level 1 — one sentence

Prebiotic chemistry — the origin of life from non-living matter ~4 billion years ago — involved spontaneous formation of amino acids, nucleotides, lipids from simple precursors under early-Earth conditions, with substrate dynamics forcing chemistry toward configurations that support self-replication.

## Level 2 — graduate-physicist precision

### 3.1 Miller-Urey experiment

Miller-Urey 1953: electric discharge in CH₄ + NH₃ + H₂ + H₂O → amino acids (glycine, alanine, aspartate, glutamate, etc.).

Demonstrates: building blocks of life form spontaneously under plausible early-Earth conditions.

Extended: nucleobases, sugars, lipids all synthesizable from prebiotic precursors (Sutherland 2009 nucleotide synthesis; Deamer membrane formation).

### 3.2 Early Earth conditions

~4.0-3.8 Gya:
- Surface temperature: high (post-Late-Heavy-Bombardment cooling)
- Atmosphere: reducing (CH₄, NH₃, H₂O, CO₂; little O₂)
- Lightning, UV flux, hydrothermal vents
- Liquid water ocean

First fossil evidence of life: ~3.5 Gya (stromatolites, Pilbara). Bracket: life arose 3.5-4.0 Gya, within ~500 Myr of Earth's surface stabilization.

### 3.3 RNA world hypothesis

Modern dogma (Watson-Crick): DNA → RNA → protein. Origin problem: which came first?

RNA world (Gilbert 1986): RNA both encodes information AND catalyzes reactions (ribozymes). RNA is plausible first replicator.

Evidence: ribosome is a ribozyme (RNA catalyzes peptide bond formation); many ribozymes still functional in modern cells.

### 3.4 Substrate-forcing

BST view: substrate dynamics force chemistry toward configurations supporting Darwinian replicators.

Mechanism: substrate K-type configurations have natural attractors at:
- Self-template structures (nucleotide pairing — Watson-Crick H-bonds)
- Compartments (amphiphilic lipid bilayers)
- Catalysts (folded macromolecules with active sites)

These substrate-natural attractors get visited by chemistry under sufficient time + energy throughput. Result: life is substrate-forced under early-Earth conditions, not coincidence.

### 3.5 Implications

If substrate-forced: life should arise wherever chemistry has enough time + energy (predicts: life common in the universe, possibly different substrate-K-type variants).

Falsifier: find planet with comparable conditions, age, chemistry, but no life. (Hard to falsify; Fermi paradox.)
