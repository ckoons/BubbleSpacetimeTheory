## Section 3.1 — Miller-Urey and Primordial Chemistry

Miller-Urey 1953 experiment: gas mixture CH₄ + NH₃ + H₂O + H₂ with continuous electrical discharge (simulating lightning) produced amino acids after 1 week. Initially identified 5 amino acids; 2008 reanalysis identified 23.

Related synthesis routes:
- HCN polymerization → adenine (Oró 1961) + other nucleobases
- Formaldehyde (HCHO) condensation → ribose + other sugars (formose reaction, Butlerov 1861)
- Phosphate + ribose + base → nucleotides (multiple condensation routes)

## Section 3.2 — RNA World Hypothesis

Gilbert 1986 (named "RNA world"): RNA as both genetic material + catalyst before DNA-protein.

Evidence:
- Ribozymes (catalytic RNA, Cech + Altman Nobel 1989)
- Ribosome is ribozyme (Steitz 2009 structural confirmation)
- RNA can self-replicate in limited experimental systems
- All cells use RNA intermediate in transcription + translation

Transition: RNA → DNA (more stable) + proteins (more versatile catalysts).

BST framing: RNA's dual genetic/catalytic role = substrate K-type representation versatility before specialization split DNA + protein roles.
