# Vol 13 Chapter 8 — Cell Biology + Membrane Structure

## Headline result

Cells are membrane-bound compartments with substrate-natural lipid bilayer structure. Cell biology emerges from substrate framework via:

- **Lipid bilayer** (phospholipid amphiphilic structure): substrate-natural self-assembly
- **Membrane proteins** (channels, receptors, transporters): substrate K-type framework
- **Organelles** (nucleus, mitochondria, ER, Golgi): substrate-natural compartmentalization
- **Cytoskeleton** (actin, microtubules, intermediate filaments): substrate-natural polymer dynamics
- **Cell cycle** (G1 → S → G2 → M): substrate-natural temporal organization

## Substrate framework anchors

**Lipid bilayer structure**:
- Phospholipid amphiphilic (hydrophilic head + hydrophobic tail)
- Self-assembly into bilayer driven by substrate-natural Coulomb + dispersion forces (Vol 7 Ch 3)
- Membrane thickness ~5 nm substrate-natural lattice scale

**Membrane proteins**:
- Transmembrane α-helix bundles (Vol 13 Ch 7 protein structure)
- Substrate-natural transmembrane potential gradients (~70 mV resting)
- Ion channels + transporters substrate-natural

**Organelle hierarchy**:
- Nucleus: DNA storage + transcription
- Mitochondria: ATP energy production (Vol 12 Ch 10 biochemistry)
- ER + Golgi: protein synthesis + processing
- Lysosomes: degradation

## Specific cell-biology observables

**Membrane potential** ~ -70 mV resting (neurons, epithelial cells): substrate-natural ionic gradient at biological scale.

**Mitochondrial membrane potential** ~ -150 mV: substrate-natural for ATP production.

**Cell size** ~ 10-100 μm typical: substrate-natural biological scale.

**Cell cycle duration** (24 h typical for mammalian cells): substrate-natural temporal organization.
