# Chapter 1 — Biology as Substrate Macromolecular Behavior

Biology is chemistry at scale: macromolecules (DNA, RNA, proteins, lipids), self-replicating systems, evolution, ecosystems. In BST, biology is the substrate's behavior in the macromolecular regime where chemical-scale K-type configurations couple into self-organizing replicators.

## 1.1 The BST framing

Biology emerges from substrate at a scale roughly an order of magnitude above molecular chemistry. The key substrate question: which BST primaries set the natural scales of biology?

The genetic code uses 4 nucleotides, 64 codons, 20+ amino acids. The number 4 = $N_c+1$, 64 = $4^3 = (N_c+1)^{N_c}$. Connections to BST primaries are explored in subsequent chapters.
