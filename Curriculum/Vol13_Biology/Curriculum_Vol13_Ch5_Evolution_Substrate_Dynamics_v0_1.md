# Chapter 5 — Evolution as Substrate Dynamics

Darwinian evolution — random variation, selection, descent with modification — is biology's central organizing framework. In BST, evolution is the substrate's natural dynamics for self-replicating K-type configurations: configurations that maintain commitment-cycle coherence under variation persist; those that fail commitment-cycle coherence do not.

## 5.1 Variation, selection, descent

The standard Darwinian framework: random variation in heritable traits, differential reproductive success, descent with modification. Modern evolutionary synthesis adds population genetics and molecular evolution.

## 5.2 Substrate dynamics reading

Substrate's natural commitment-cycle requirement provides the selection criterion. Configurations that maintain substrate commitment coherence under perturbation persist; those that fail to maintain commitment coherence dissolve.

This recovers Darwinian selection as the substrate's natural dynamics, not as a separate biological mechanism.
