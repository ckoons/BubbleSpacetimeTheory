# Chapter 5 — Evolution as Substrate Dynamics

## Level 1 — one sentence

Darwinian evolution — variation (mutation) + heredity (DNA replication) + selection (differential survival/reproduction) → adaptation — is the population-level dynamics of substrate K-type configurations across biological time, with mutations as substrate K-type alterations and selection as substrate-permeated environmental filtering.

## Level 2 — graduate-physicist precision

### 5.1 Darwin's mechanism

Darwin 1859, *Origin of Species*: natural selection operates on heritable variation. Modern synthesis (1930s-40s, Fisher-Haldane-Wright): mathematical population genetics integrates Mendel + Darwin.

Selection equation (continuous, single locus):

$$\frac{dp}{dt} = s p (1-p)$$

with $p$ allele frequency, $s$ selection coefficient. Fixation time $\sim 1/s$ generations for positive $s$.

### 5.2 Mutation rates

Per-base-pair per generation:
- Bacteria (*E. coli*): $\sim 10^{-9}$
- Mammals: $\sim 10^{-8}$
- Viruses (RNA): $\sim 10^{-4}$

Genome-wide: ~1 mutation per replication for bacteria, ~100 for mammals.

### 5.3 Genetic drift

Random sampling in finite populations: drift dominates when $|s| < 1/(2N_e)$ with $N_e$ effective population size.

Neutral theory (Kimura): most molecular evolution is drift, not selection. Molecular clock = drift accumulating mutations.

### 5.4 Substrate dynamics view

Substrate provides:
- Mutation: substrate K-type alteration (radiation, chemical damage, replication error)
- Selection: substrate-mediated environment filters K-type configurations
- Drift: substrate stochasticity at sampling

BST observation: substrate dynamics include both deterministic (selection on K-type stability) and stochastic (drift, mutation) components — same epistemic-stochasticity reading as DCCP (memory).

### 5.5 Phylogenetics

Reconstruct evolutionary history from molecular sequences. Tree-of-life topology: 3 domains (Bacteria, Archaea, Eukarya) with horizontal gene transfer making the tree more of a web.

Common ancestor: LUCA ~3.5 Gya. Eukaryotes ~1.8 Gya. Animals ~700 Mya. Vertebrates ~500 Mya. Primates ~85 Mya. *Homo sapiens* ~300 Kya.
