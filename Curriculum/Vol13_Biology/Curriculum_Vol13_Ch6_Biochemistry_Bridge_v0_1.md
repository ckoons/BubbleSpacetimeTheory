# Chapter 6 — Biochemistry Bridge

Biochemistry — the chemistry of biological molecules — bridges chemistry (Vol 12) and biology (Vol 13 Chapters 7+). Enzymes (catalytic proteins), metabolic pathways (energy transfer chains), nucleic acids (DNA, RNA), proteins (folded polymers), lipids (membrane components) — these are the apparatus.

In BST, biochemistry is the substrate's macromolecular K-type behavior at the cellular scale.

## 6.1 The biological macromolecules

- Proteins: ~20 amino acid building blocks, fold into specific 3D structures
- Nucleic acids: DNA/RNA, sequence-encoded information carriers
- Lipids: membrane-forming amphiphiles
- Carbohydrates: energy storage and structural materials

Each is a class of substrate K-types organized at the macromolecular scale.

## 6.2 Enzymes and catalysis

Enzymes lower transition-state activation energies by ~10^7 (typical), enabling metabolic reactions at body temperature. Substrate-mechanism: enzymes are substrate K-type configurations that stabilize specific transition-state substrate K-types via boundary-condition coupling.
