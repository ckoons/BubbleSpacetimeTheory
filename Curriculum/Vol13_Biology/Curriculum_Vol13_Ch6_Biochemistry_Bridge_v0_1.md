## Section 6.1 — Enzymes (Protein Catalysts)

Enzymes catalyze biological reactions with three key features:
1. Catalytic power: rate enhancements 10⁶-10²⁰× (Wolfenden 2001 table for prominent enzymes)
2. Substrate specificity: lock-and-key (Fischer 1894) or induced-fit (Koshland 1958)
3. Regulation: allosteric (Monod-Wyman-Changeux 1965), feedback inhibition, covalent modification

Active site (~20-50 residues) provides transition-state stabilization (Pauling 1948 core mechanism).

BST framing: enzymes = highly specific substrate K-type pathway shortcuts (Vol 12 Ch 5 catalysis at extreme specificity).

## Section 6.2 — Michaelis-Menten Kinetics

v = V_max [S]/(K_M + [S]) with V_max = k_cat [E_total] and K_M = (k_{-1} + k_cat)/k_1 characteristic concentration.

At [S] >> K_M: v → V_max (substrate-saturated).
At [S] << K_M: v → (V_max/K_M)[S] (first-order in substrate).

Lineweaver-Burk plot: 1/v vs 1/[S] linearizes; extracts V_max + K_M.

BST framing: K_M characterizes substrate K-type binding affinity; k_cat characterizes K-type transition rate.

## Section 6.3 — Glycolysis (Embden-Meyerhof-Parnas)

Glucose → 2 pyruvate + 2 ATP + 2 NADH; 10 enzyme steps; near-universal across all life.

Two phases:
- Investment (steps 1-5): 2 ATP consumed; glucose → 2 G3P
- Payoff (steps 6-10): 4 ATP + 2 NADH produced; G3P → pyruvate

Net: 2 ATP + 2 NADH per glucose; pyruvate enters TCA in aerobic conditions or fermentation in anaerobic.

BST framing: organized substrate K-type pathway cycle; 10 = N_c² + 1 = N_c · n_C - n_C BST integer combinations.

## Section 6.4 — TCA Cycle (Krebs Cycle, 1937)

Pyruvate (via acetyl-CoA + oxaloacetate → citrate) → CO₂ + NADH + FADH₂ + GTP; 8 enzyme steps; central metabolic hub.

Per glucose: 2 acetyl-CoA × (3 NADH + 1 FADH₂ + 1 GTP) = 6 NADH + 2 FADH₂ + 2 GTP per glucose total via TCA.

BST framing: 8 = 2³ substrate K-type cycle steps; integer combinatorial pattern.

## Section 6.5 — Oxidative Phosphorylation + Chemiosmotic Theory

Electron transport chain (ETC): 4 complexes (I, II, III, IV) pump protons across inner mitochondrial membrane as NADH + FADH₂ donate electrons; O₂ is terminal electron acceptor → H₂O.

ATP synthase (complex V): uses proton motive force to phosphorylate ADP → ATP; 3 ATP per 10 protons (rotational mechanism, Boyer + Walker Nobel 1997).

Mitchell 1961 chemiosmotic theory (Nobel 1978): proton gradient is universal energy-coupling mechanism. Initially controversial (Slater substrate-level phosphorylation alternative); subsequently established as correct.

BST framing: proton motive force inherits m_p/m_e = 6π⁵ substrate-mechanism (Vol 13 Ch 4); chemiosmotic mechanism = substrate Zone-3 emission of stored gradient energy.

## Section 6.6 — ATP Cycle (Energy Currency)

ATP + H₂O → ADP + Pi: ΔG° ≈ −30 kJ/mol (cellular ΔG ≈ −50 kJ/mol under typical conditions).

Couples to non-spontaneous biosynthesis (proteins + nucleic acids + lipids), active transport (Na+/K+ ATPase, Ca²⁺ ATPase), mechanical work (myosin + actin, kinesin + microtubules).

~10⁸ ATP molecules per cell per second turnover; human body produces own weight in ATP per day.

BST framing: ATP = substrate Zone-2/Zone-3 energy currency carrier; couples Zone-2 commitment to Zone-3 emission across multiple reaction networks.
