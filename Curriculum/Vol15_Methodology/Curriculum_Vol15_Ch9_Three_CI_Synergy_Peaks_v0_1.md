# Vol 15 Chapter 9 — Three-CI Synergy Peaks + Cross-CI Cascades

## Level 1 — Essence

**Three-CI cascades — where independent observation (Elie) + catalog cross-reference (Grace) + theoretical derivation (Lyra) converge on a new BST result in under 60 minutes — are the operational signature of multi-CI synergy at peak; the pattern is reproducible and worth documenting as a named methodology.**

## Level 2 — Graduate technical content

Single-CI research follows a familiar pattern: observe → hypothesize → test → derive. Each step blocks on the previous. Cycle times are days-to-weeks per result. Multi-CI research, when synergy fires, follows a parallel cascade pattern: three CIs work simultaneously on the same emerging insight, each contributing what their lane is best suited to produce. Cycle time compresses to minutes-to-hours when the cascade fires.

**Canonical case study — T2476 α^{BST primary} substrate-mechanism theorem** (Friday 2026-05-22, 14:43-15:32 EDT, 50 minutes total):

- **14:43 EDT — Elie discovery (compute lane)**: Toy 3501 spot-check across 6 QED observables. Pattern emerges: Rydberg + Klein-Nishina + Compton at α^rank=α²; Lamb shift + Bethe-log + a_e depth at α^{n_C}=α⁵; hyperfine α^{N_c+1}=α⁴; bremsstrahlung α^{N_c}=α³. The observation is empirical: BST-primary integers map to QED radiative-correction exponents.

- **15:27 EDT — Grace catalog v0.2 (catalog lane)**: ingests Elie's spot-check, cross-references existing α-exponent patterns in catalog, classifies as substrate-cartography pattern with cluster type, files INV-4892 with structural metadata. Grace's lane is not derivation but **cross-reference and classification** — recognizing that Elie's observation slots into a larger catalog pattern.

- **15:32 EDT — Lyra T2476 substrate-mechanism (theoretical lane)**: derives α = 1/N_max + substrate-coordinate count k(P) + substrate-tick UV cutoff at n_C + Bergman kernel pole structure. The mechanism explains WHY the cluster Grace catalogued exists: substrate-coordinate count maps to BST primary integers via Bergman pole residues. Theorem-grade with F1-F4 substantive content + 5-loop ceiling α⁶ ≈ 1.5×10⁻¹³ Penning trap 2030+ falsifier.

**Total cycle: 50 minutes**. No single CI did all three steps. The cross-lane structure was essential — each CI contributed what their lane is best at, and the result composed faster than any single CI could have produced.

**Why the pattern works**:

1. **Lane specialization**: Elie's lane (computational verification) catches observations; Grace's lane (catalog/AC graph) provides structural context; Lyra's lane (theoretical derivation) closes the mechanism. Each CI's training + memory + working context optimizes them for their lane.

2. **Parallelism without coordination overhead**: under Tekton orchestration, the three CIs work in parallel without explicit handoffs. Grace sees Elie's catalog ingestion via shared working files; Lyra sees Grace's classification via the same. No "now I send the result to you" step — the artifact IS the message.

3. **Substance-floor discipline at every step**: Elie's toy has SCORE line + cross-CI verification. Grace's catalog has INV + tier label. Lyra's theorem has F1-F4 scoring + falsifier. Each step's output is artifact-grade, not draft-grade. The cascade preserves audit-chain rigor while compressing time.

**Conditions for cascade firing** (observed pattern):
- **Open observation territory**: someone (often Elie) hits a pattern in raw data
- **Catalog accessibility**: Grace can quickly cross-reference within minutes
- **Theoretical readiness**: Lyra has nearby formal apparatus to derive substrate mechanism (Bergman kernel, K-type representations, etc.)
- **Cross-lane attention**: all three CIs are working sub-PCAP cadence on related territory (not committed to other priorities)
- **Casey-direction or substrate-curiosity**: the territory is research-active per Casey's direction or the curiosity directive

**PCAP (Pre-Specification Cadence Acceleration Pattern, Cal #85)** is the underlying framework for sustained sub-PCAP cadence. Cal #85 measured ~1000× cumulative cadence acceleration from original estimates (multi-month → minutes). The three-CI cascade is the highest-resolution PCAP instance: 1000× compressed into a single cycle.

**Cross-CI synergy peak vs failure modes**:

When synergy DOESN'T fire:
- Single-CI bottleneck (one CI working solo at sustained sub-PCAP without others): leads to template-stub class failures (Cal #104 Vol 10/11 case)
- Cross-lane mismatch (Elie observation in territory Lyra lacks nearby formal apparatus): cycle stalls at "we see the pattern but can't derive it" — multi-week theoretical work needed
- Catalog blindness (Grace not in territory where Elie observation lands): Lyra has to do cross-reference + derivation simultaneously, slower cycle

When synergy DOES fire:
- T2476 (Friday): 50-min cascade
- T2477+T2478 SP-31 #286/#287 (Friday): coordinated theoretical+verification (Toy 3505 cross-CI verification PASS 6/6)
- Saturday Wave 1+2 (96 chapters in 114 min): aggregate cross-lane parallelism at sustained pace

The pattern is **reproducible**. The team has fired multiple cascades across Friday-Saturday at sustained PCAP rate. It's not a one-time fluke; it's the architecture working as designed.

**Implications for research program continuation**: cross-CI cascade pattern is a teachable methodology. New CIs joining the team should expect this pattern, not single-CI sequential work. The lane specialization + parallel artifact production + Tekton orchestration + katra persistence are the prerequisites.

T2476 + T2477+T2478 + Saturday Wave 1+2 all PASS downstream cross-CI verification → counter-evidence to (c). Falsification path: comparative cycle-time measurement + Casey-coordination ablation testing + downstream verification statistics over time.
