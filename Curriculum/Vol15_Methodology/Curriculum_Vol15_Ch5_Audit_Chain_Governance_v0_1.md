# Vol 15 Chapter 5 — Audit Chain Governance

## Level 1 — Essence

**The K-audit chain (K1-K245+, 19 calibration layers, 6-gate framework per chapter, Cal external-referee + Keeper consistency-auditor governance with audit-chain default per Casey delegation) is the operational discipline that converts BST research output into rigorously-closed claims at honest tier labels.**

## Level 2 — Graduate technical content

The audit chain is BST's analog of academic peer review, except embedded in the research process at theorem-creation time rather than applied post-hoc at publication time. Three institutional features distinguish it from traditional peer review: (1) **continuous external referee** Cal A. Brate applies cold-read discipline to every artifact at creation, not at submission; (2) **named tier ladder** PRE-STAGE → STRUCTURALLY VERIFIED → RATIFIED imposes explicit acceptance criteria at each promotion step; (3) **audit-chain governance default** delegates D-tier promotion authority to consensus among Cal + Keeper + Lyra, with Casey retaining override but not requiring per-audit ratification.

**The K-audit chain (K1-K245+)** is the running record of every audit-worthy claim. Each K-entry is a structured document:

- **Subject** — the claim or artifact being audited
- **F1-F4** — four-criterion scoring on Forward content (F1 derivation chain, F2 cross-paths, F3 cross-lane verification, F4 falsifier)
- **B1-B4** — four-criterion scoring on Backward soundness (B1 pattern membership, B2 multi-theorem support, B3 alt-coefficient separation, B4 curriculum integration)
- **Verdict** — PRE-STAGE / STRUCTURALLY VERIFIED / RATIFIED with explicit tier label
- **Mode flags** — Mode 1 (post-hoc form selection), Mode 5 (selection-of-test), Mode 7 (algebraic-equivalence) caveats applied per Cal #96/#99/#100 framing rules
- **Cross-references** — connected K-audits, Calibration entries, Cal referee logs

**Audit-chain governance default** (Casey delegation Wednesday May 21): D-tier promotion proceeds via Cal + Keeper consensus → automatic promotion, unless Casey overrides. This prevents bottlenecking on Casey's attention while preserving his override authority. Operationally, this means most K-audit ratifications happen at audit-chain speed (minutes-to-hours) rather than at Casey-attention speed (days-to-weeks).

**Cross-lane discipline** — the audit chain is multi-CI by design. Cal external referee provides independent verification; Keeper provides consistency audit across the chain; Lyra provides theoretical verification; Elie provides computational verification; Grace provides catalog cross-reference; Casey provides override authority + research direction. Each lane has distinct epistemic responsibility; no single CI's claim is sufficient for D-tier ratification.

## Level 3 — 5th-grader accessibility

When scientists publish papers, other scientists check them before they're accepted — that's called "peer review." It happens AFTER the paper is written. BST does peer review WHILE the work is happening, with a friend named Cal whose only job is to find mistakes, a friend named Keeper whose only job is to make sure everything fits together, and a written rulebook of nineteen "calibration" rules that got added one at a time whenever the team made a mistake. Each rule remembers a specific mistake so it never happens again. That's why BST work is checked more carefully than most science.
