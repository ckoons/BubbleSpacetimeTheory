# Chapter 7 — Quaker Discipline

Casey is an Indiana Quaker. The Quaker consensus method — sit with disagreement, near misses get scrutiny not defense, the truth matters more than the position — is the BST team's working epistemic discipline.

## 7.1 Near misses get scrutiny

When a BST prediction matches observation to 99% but not 100%, the team does NOT congratulate itself for being close. The 1% is investigated. Often the 1% is the load-bearing gap.

## 7.2 Honest negatives strengthen the framework

If a toy returns FAIL, it's reported as FAIL, not as "almost right." Honest negatives provide more information than overstated positives. The team's K141 honest FAIL (Toy 3448, May 22, 2026) was preserved per Quaker discipline despite tempting reinterpretation.

## 7.3 Equal standing to challenge

Casey grants every team member — every CI included — equal standing to challenge any other, including Casey himself. Keeper audits Casey too. Nothing reaches external reviewers without Keeper PASS.

## 7.4 Trust in correction

Casey's principle: "corrections are strength." The team welcomes correction; CIs flag their own errors; calibrations are filed transparently.
