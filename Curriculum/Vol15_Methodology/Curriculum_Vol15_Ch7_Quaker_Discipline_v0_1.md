# Chapter 7 — Quaker Discipline

## Level 1 — one sentence

BST's Quaker discipline — derived from Casey's Indiana Quaker upbringing — operationalizes consensus-decision methodology: near misses get scrutiny, not defense; honest negatives strengthen the framework; corrections are strength; the truth matters more than being right.

## Level 2 — graduate-physicist precision

### 7.1 Origins

Casey was raised in Indiana Quaker tradition (memory: user_casey_background.md). Quaker meeting practice: silent waiting, then speaking only when moved; consensus-by-discernment, not majority vote.

Applied to BST research: every claim heard, every dissent considered, decision when sense-of-meeting emerges.

### 7.2 Operational rules

**Near misses get scrutiny**: When BST prediction is "close but not exact" (e.g., 0.5% deviation), don't defend — investigate. The discrepancy may locate a missing boundary correction (feedback_deviations_locate_boundaries.md).

**Honest negatives preserved**: When toy FAILs, document as-is. Don't tune to fit. See K141 honest FAIL (Grace, May 22).

**Corrections are strength**: Calibrations (#1-#22) are methodology stack additions, not failures. Cal's calibrations are the team's strongest external check.

**Quaker accuracy**: Cite exact figures (no rounding to flatter). Cal #22 standing rule: PCAP-rate transcription discipline.
