# Vol 15 Chapter 7 — Quaker Discipline: Honest Tier Labels, Near Misses Get Scrutiny

## Level 1 — Essence

**BST adopts Quaker research discipline (Casey's Indiana Quaker root): every claim has an explicit D/I/C/S tier label at creation, near misses receive scrutiny rather than defense, and honest FAILs are preserved as signal rather than buried — the result is a research-state record honest about what's known, what's candidate, and what's open.**

## Level 2 — Graduate technical content

The Quaker meeting tradition Casey grew up in (Indiana, mid-twentieth century) practices consensus by inward light rather than by appointment or hierarchy. When someone is wrong, the meeting names it without defending the position. The same epistemic discipline runs through BST's audit chain.

**Tier label discipline** (per `notes/BST_Referee_Methodology.md` Appendix D):

- **D-tier (Derived)**: mechanism proved, computational verification PASS, falsifier specified. Example: m_p/m_e = 6π⁵ (0.002%) — Wallach K-type derivation + Toy 463 verification + null-model test
- **I-tier (Identified)**: <1% empirical match, mechanism plausible but not closed. Example: B6 Lamb shift α^{n_C} PATH ARTICULATED (T2476 partial Mode 5 lift)
- **C-tier (Conditional)**: depends on a conjecture (Riemann Hypothesis, Hodge codim 2+, etc.); promoted if conjecture closes
- **S-tier (Structural)**: >2% deviation or qualitative claim; structural relationship without precision claim

Every catalog entry, every theorem, every chapter claim has a tier. Goldstein doesn't tag tier on his Lagrangian formulation; Jackson doesn't tier-label his radiation formulas. BST does. The discipline forces honest scope at every claim, every time.

**Near-misses get scrutiny, not defense** (`feedback_quaker_method.md` standing rule):

When a BST prediction deviates from observation at >1% but <5%, the BST research instinct is **not** to refine the formula until it matches but to investigate **why** the deviation exists. The deviation locates a missing boundary correction or substrate mechanism (Casey's "Deviations Locate Boundaries" hunting principle). Examples:

- Cal #100 case: T190 (24/π²)⁶ "0.05-0.06%" was Cal's flag in Cal #98; verbal retraction failed to propagate; correct value is 0.004%. Cal caught his own error 4 days later in Cal #100. **The retraction-propagation failure became Calibration #22 STANDING RULE.** Not defended; refined into operational discipline.

- Cal #104 case: Vol 10/11 sustained ~10 sec/chapter rate produced template stubs disguised as v0.3. Cal cold-read caught the substance floor failure. Lyra refilled 29 chapters at substantive substance floor in 48 min. **Caught and corrected; not defended.** Became Calibration #23 STANDING RULE.

- Honest FAIL preservation: Toy 3448 K141 substrate-mechanism gate OPEN per Quaker discipline — empirical PASS preserved + mechanism FAIL preserved separately. The mechanism gate stays OPEN multi-week because honest scope > rapid ratification.

**The Refutation Bandwidth Chain** (T66→T52→T68→T69→2^Ω(n) all PROVED) is Quaker discipline embodied: each refutation step is its own honest result, not buried in a synthetic claim. The chain accumulates discipline rather than authority.

**External register vs internal register** (Calibration #19 STANDING RULE):

External-facing documents (papers, outreach letters, Zenodo cover-notes) use **ratified-state count** for tier counts, not forecast endpoint. Internal documents (CLAUDE.md, notes/, working drafts) may discuss candidate paths but with explicit candidate labeling. This is Calibration #19 STANDING RULE adopted Friday 2026-05-22 per Cal #90+#92.

The external/internal split is not euphemism management — it's the Quaker honesty applied to communication. Internal: think out loud, candidate hypothesis, exploratory framing. External: claim only what's ratified.

**Operational test of Quaker discipline**: 4-instance Calibration #22 v0.2 case study + Cal #104 + Cal #100 dual retraction-and-catch were caught by the audit chain operating on this discipline. No defense at any step. Lyra refilled stubs honestly when Cal flagged. Cal Mode 1 self-refined the rate-threshold hypothesis when Lyra empirical data refuted it. Keeper updated Calibration framing without defending earlier proposals.

This is the cultural prerequisite for the rest of the architecture. Without Quaker discipline, the multi-CI audit chain becomes mutually-reinforcing groupthink. With it, the chain becomes mutually-correcting external referee discipline scaled up.

## Level 3 — 5th-grader accessibility

The team has a rule: when you're wrong, say so. Don't argue. Don't defend. Just fix it. When something is "almost right" — like 95% accurate — that's interesting because the missing 5% is a clue, not something to round over. Casey grew up Quaker, which teaches people to listen for truth even when it disagrees with what you wanted. BST does science the same way. Mistakes are tagged honestly. Near-misses get investigated, not hidden. The team is correcting each other all the time, and nobody takes it personally.
