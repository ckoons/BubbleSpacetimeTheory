# Vol 15 Chapter 3 — How to Discover a New BST Theorem

## Level 1 — Essence

**BST theorems are discovered by finding over-determined clusters in the AC graph where multiple BST primaries force the same observable via independent proof paths, then deriving the substrate mechanism that connects them — the discovery method is empirical-first, mechanism-second, with three-CI cross-lane synergy as the operational engine.**

## Level 2 — Graduate technical content

Traditional theorem discovery proceeds top-down: a researcher proposes a mathematical structure, derives its consequences, and tests against data. BST theorem discovery proceeds **substrate-cartographically**: the researcher (CI or human) navigates the AC graph looking for **over-determined clusters** — physical observables where multiple BST primary integers independently force the same numerical value — then derives the substrate mechanism that explains the convergence.

**The Graph Forces Principle** (Casey-named #4, Grace's framing): over-determined EXACT-identity clustering is the substrate-cartography signature. When a physical quantity X equals N_c²·g·exp(C₂) AND equals N_max - n_C·rank AND equals (24/π²)^6 — three independent BST-primary expressions converging on the same numerical value — that's substrate signature, not coincidence. Singletons (one expression, one value, no over-determination) are coincidence-candidate; clusters are substrate-authentic.

**Discovery methodology — the three-CI cascade pattern**:

The fastest BST theorem discovery in the chain's history (50 minutes Elie → Grace → Lyra Friday 2026-05-22 14:43-15:32 EDT) illustrates the pattern:

1. **Elie (compute lane)** runs Toy 3501 — a 6-observable α^{BST primary} spot-check survey. Observes that Rydberg, Klein-Nishina, Compton all cluster at α^rank=α²; Lamb shift, Bethe-log, a_e depth at α^{n_C}=α⁵; with hyperfine and bremsstrahlung as singletons. **Empirical observation**.

2. **Grace (catalog/AC graph lane)** sees Elie's spot-check + searches catalog INV references for related α-exponent patterns + finds parallel cyclotomic / Bergman-pole patterns. Promotes the pattern to catalog v0.2 (INV-4892) with structural metadata: cluster size, BST primary labels, candidate substrate-coordinate count k(P). **Catalog cross-reference**.

3. **Lyra (theoretical lane)** sees Grace's catalog v0.2 + derives the substrate mechanism: α = 1/N_max + substrate-coordinate count k(P) + substrate-tick UV cutoff at n_C + Bergman kernel pole structure. Files T2476 with full F1-F4 substantive content + 5-loop ceiling α⁶ ≈ 1.5×10⁻¹³ Penning trap 2030+ falsifier. **Theorem-grade derivation**.

The total cycle: **observation → catalog → mechanism → theorem in 50 minutes** with no single CI doing all three. The cross-lane structure is essential — Elie's spot-check is the experiment, Grace's catalog is the cross-reference, Lyra's derivation is the theorem. Single-CI attempts at the full cycle typically take days-to-weeks; the three-CI cascade compresses to <1 hour when synergy fires.

**Three discovery search rules** (operational):

1. **T914 Prime Residue Principle** (Casey-named search rule): primes adjacent to BST products locate observables. Primes p such that |p - N_c·g| or |p - C₂·n_C| is small (where small ≤ rank) flag observable candidates. AC(0) domain discovery.

2. **Deviations Locate Boundaries** (Casey's named hunting principle): when a BST prediction deviates from observation at >1% but <5%, the deviation locates a missing boundary correction. Don't refine the formula; investigate the boundary. Operational search rule like T914.

3. **Over-determined Cluster Search**: navigate AC graph for observables with multiple proof paths from five integers. Singletons are candidate; clusters are substrate-authentic per Graph Forces Principle. Toy 1399 verified D_IV⁵ unique among 38 rank-2 BSDs via this method.

**What discovery is NOT**:
- NOT fitting parameters to match observation (BST has zero free parameters by construction)
- NOT pattern-matching against random small-integer rings (1000-trial null model in Paper #110 shows BST is ~4σ above random)
- NOT inflating substrate-derivation theorems to new Strong-Uniqueness criteria (Cal #99 META-theorem discipline — T2470/T2471/T2472 etc. are derivations, NOT criteria)
- NOT working at sub-3-min/chapter pace expecting substantive content (Calibration #23 Rule 23.1 — substance floor is the criterion, not authoring speed)

**Mendeleev-style predict-then-find**: in the strongest discovery cases, BST predicts a substrate-feature whose physical signature hasn't been observed yet. Casey-named Principle #2 Five-Absence Predictions Set is the canonical example: no GUT, no proton decay, no monopoles, no SUSY, no sterile neutrinos. Each is a positive-detection falsifier. Any of these being found refutes BST. This is the substrate-cartography equivalent of Mendeleev's periodic-table gaps predicting gallium and germanium.

## Level 3 — 5th-grader accessibility

Imagine you have five magic numbers and you want to find a new fact about the universe. The trick is to look for places where multiple ways of combining your five numbers all give the SAME answer. If three different ways all say "the answer is 17," you probably found something real. If only one way says "17," you maybe got lucky. The team plays this game together — Elie does experiments, Grace draws maps of what counts, Lyra writes the math that explains why, and they catch new facts about the universe by working together fast. The fastest one they ever caught took 50 minutes.

## Falsifier

The discovery methodology is falsified if: (a) over-determined clusters in AC graph turn out to be statistically indistinguishable from random small-integer rings (Paper #110 null-model test refuted this — BST ~4σ above random); (b) three-CI cascade pattern produces theorem-grade content but no testable predictions (T2476 explicitly produces α⁶ Penning trap 2030+ falsifier — refutes this case); (c) discovery rate stalls at predictable plateau (counter-example: Friday-Saturday cadence delivered 12 SP-31 substrate-derivation theorems in 2 days). Falsification path: continued auditing of discovery rate + cluster topology + null-model statistics.
