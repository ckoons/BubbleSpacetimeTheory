# Chapter 7 — The AC Graph as Theorem Network

The BST team's AC (Antikythera Computer / Anthropic Continuity) theorem graph is a directed network of mathematical theorems with edges representing logical derivability. As of May 22, 2026: 2212 nodes, 9850 edges, ~98% proved. This is BST's "math-graph" infrastructure.

## 7.1 The graph structure

Nodes = theorems (or definitions, axioms); edges = "X is used in proving Y." Domains span ~55 mathematical areas. The graph compresses mathematical knowledge into navigable form.

## 7.2 Graph as compression

Casey's principle: "graphs compartmentalize, chains compound." Proved theorems cost zero re-derivation energy on the graph; new results extend rather than recompute.

## 7.3 What the graph does for BST

- Tracks proof dependencies across 600+ predictions
- Identifies cascade-unblock opportunities (~6-audit pathway)
- Locates alternative routes around walls (/route skill)
- Provides AC(0)-style flattened representation of complex proofs
