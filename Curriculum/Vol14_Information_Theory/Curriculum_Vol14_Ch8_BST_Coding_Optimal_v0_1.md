# Chapter 8 — BST Coding Optimal

The BST substrate's Reed-Solomon coding on $\text{GF}(128)$ at the Koons-tick rate achieves the Shannon channel capacity for its noise model. This chapter develops the optimality claim.

## 8.1 The coding-optimality claim

For the substrate's natural noise model (Zone 3 Bergman projection imprecision + Zone 4 emission stochasticity), the substrate's RS coding rate achieves the Shannon capacity. The substrate is information-theoretically optimal at its native task.

## 8.2 Why this matters

If the substrate is optimal, then physical law — what we observe — is what an optimally-coded information substrate produces. The substrate doesn't waste; what we see is the substrate's optimal output.

This is the "Information Substrate" framing of Paper #122.
