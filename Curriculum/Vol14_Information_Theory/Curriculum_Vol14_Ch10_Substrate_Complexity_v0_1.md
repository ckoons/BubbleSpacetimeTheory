# Chapter 10 — Substrate Complexity

The substrate's information-theoretic complexity — how much information the substrate uses to represent itself, how much it computes per Koons tick, how much it processes per Zone 2 bulk operation — is the focus of BST's "substrate-computational math" investigation (SP-30-10, math-frontier interface).

## 10.1 The substrate's resource budget

Per Koons tick, the substrate completes one full Zone 1-4 commitment cycle. The information budget: substrate state in $L^2(D_{IV}^5; L_\lambda)$, projected to Bergman basis at Zone 3, with RS-coded output at Zone 4.

## 10.2 Substrate Hilbert space dimension

What is $\dim L^2(D_{IV}^5; L_\lambda)$? The substrate Hilbert space specification (SP-31-1) develops this; the answer organizes the substrate's natural representation theory.
