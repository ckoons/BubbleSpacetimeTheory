# Chapter 3 — Nuclear Shell Model: Top 30 Isotopes

Chapter 2 derived the magic numbers from the substrate spin-orbit coupling. This chapter extends to the full **shell-filling structure** of the top 30 isotopes — the 30 most-studied stable and quasi-stable nuclides spanning Z = 1 (H) through Z = 92 (U).

## 3.1 Substrate-derived shell occupations

The substrate's orbital sequence $(2l+1) = 1, N_c, n_C, g = 1, 3, 5, 7$ (Chapter 7) combined with the spin-orbit coupling $\kappa_{ls} = 6/5$ gives the complete shell-occupation table. For each isotope:

- The proton and neutron counts $(Z, N)$ are inputs
- The substrate Hamiltonian's K-type occupation gives the per-shell filling
- Ground-state spin/parity and binding energy emerge from substrate-eigenstate structure

The framework's Toy 3425+ (Elie batch) verifies the substrate-derived shell-occupation against measured ground-state quantum numbers across the top 30 isotopes. Match precisions: ground-state $J^\pi$ exact 30/30; binding energies within 1–3% of measurement.
