# Chapter 1 — The Periodic Table from Substrate

The periodic table is the most-recognized organizing structure in all of science. Mendeleev's 1869 ordering by atomic mass, refined to atomic number after Moseley, captures the chemical periodicity of the elements. The shell structure 2, 8, 18, 32 — Madelung filling — and N_max = 137 stability limit are framework-level facts.

In BST, the periodic table is the substrate's natural organization of multi-electron atomic K-types under nuclear Coulomb boundary conditions. The substrate's N_max = 137 sets the natural limit; the shell structure follows from the substrate's K-type grouping.

## 1.1 The shell structure

Madelung filling — 1s 2s 2p 3s 3p 4s 3d 4p ... — gives the standard periodic table layout. Shell capacities 2, 8, 18, 32 follow from $2(2\ell+1)$ summed over $\ell$ values.

## 1.2 N_max = 137

The substrate's natural integer $N_\max = N_c^3 \cdot n_C + \text{rank} = 27 \cdot 5 + 2 = 137$ is the BST-derived limit on stable elements. Elements at Z > 137 are predicted unstable (BST Task #111 — superheavy island stability).
