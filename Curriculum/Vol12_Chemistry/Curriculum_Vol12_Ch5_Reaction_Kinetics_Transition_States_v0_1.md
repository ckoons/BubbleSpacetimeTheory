# Chapter 5 — Reaction Kinetics and Transition States

Reaction kinetics describes how fast chemical reactions proceed. Arrhenius rate law $k = A e^{-E_a/RT}$, transition state theory (Eyring 1935), and steady-state approximation are the standard framework.

## 5.1 Rate laws and reaction orders

Empirical rate laws $r = k[A]^a[B]^b$ characterize how reaction rate depends on concentrations. Reaction order $a + b$ may differ from stoichiometric coefficients.

## 5.2 Transition state theory

The reaction passes through an activated complex (transition state) at the saddle point of the potential energy surface. Eyring's equation $k = (k_BT/h)\exp(-\Delta G^\ddagger/RT)$ gives the rate constant from transition-state thermodynamics.

## 5.3 Substrate reading

The transition state is the substrate's local saddle-point K-type configuration. The activation energy is the substrate's energy gap between reactant and transition state.
