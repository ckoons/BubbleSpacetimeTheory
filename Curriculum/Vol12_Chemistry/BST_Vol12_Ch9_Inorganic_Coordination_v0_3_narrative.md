# Vol 12 Chapter 9 — Inorganic + Coordination Chemistry

## Headline result

Transition-metal coordination chemistry (group 3-12 elements: Sc through Hg) inherits substrate D_IV⁵ d-orbital framework via Vol 3 Ch 7 atomic orbital sequence:

- **d orbital degeneracy (2l+1) = 5 = n_C** (substrate compact dimension BST primary)
- **5 d orbitals**: d_z², d_x²-y², d_xy, d_xz, d_yz (5 ≡ n_C BST primary identification)
- **Crystal field theory + ligand field theory**: 5 d orbitals split into t_2g + e_g sets (octahedral) or e + t_2 (tetrahedral)
- **18-electron rule**: stable coordination complexes have 18 valence electrons = 2(s) + 6(p) + 10(d filled)

Carbon chemistry (Vol 12 Ch 8): 4 valence = 1+N_c (s+p). Transition-metal chemistry: 18 valence = 2+6+10 = 2+2·N_c+2·n_C BST primary combination.

## Substrate mechanism

**d-orbital framework** (Vol 3 Ch 7 inheritance):
- d orbitals: l=2, degeneracy 2l+1 = 5 = n_C BST primary
- Crystal field splitting: 5 → 3+2 (octahedral t_2g + e_g) or 3+2 (tetrahedral e + t_2)
- Splitting gap Δ_o (octahedral) ~ ligand field strength, substrate-natural

**18-electron rule** as BST primary form:
- 18 = 2 (s shell) + 2·N_c (p shell) + 2·n_C (d shell) = 2+6+10
- Substrate-natural valence-shell completion for transition metals

**Coordination geometries**:
- Octahedral (6 ligands): cubic point group O_h, 6 = C_2 BST primary
- Tetrahedral (4 ligands): T_d point group, 4 = rank²
- Square planar (4 ligands): D_{4h} point group, common for d⁸ metals

## Specific inorganic-chemistry observables

**Octahedral complexes** (Cr(NH₃)₆³⁺, Fe(CN)₆⁴⁻, etc.): t_2g + e_g splitting determines spectroscopic + magnetic properties.

**Coordination number 6** = C_2 substrate Casimir BST primary. Most common in transition-metal chemistry (Cr, Fe, Co, Ni, Cu hexa-aqua complexes).

**Lanthanide + actinide chemistry**: f orbitals (2l+1) = 7 = g BST primary; 7 f orbitals per shell. Coordination numbers up to 12 (= 2·C_2 BST primary) for f-block.

**Metal-metal bonding** (multiple M-M bonds): quadruple bonds (Re₂Cl₈²⁻) substrate-natural σ + 2π + δ bonding pattern.
