# Chapter 4 — Chemical Reactions and Thermodynamics

Chemical reactions involve breaking and reforming bonds with associated enthalpy, entropy, and Gibbs free-energy changes. Equilibrium constants, reaction quotients, and Le Chatelier's principle organize the thermodynamics.

## 4.1 Enthalpy, entropy, free energy

$\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$. Negative $\Delta G$ → spontaneous; positive → non-spontaneous; zero → equilibrium.

## 4.2 Equilibrium and kinetics

Equilibrium constant $K_{eq} = \exp(-\Delta G/RT)$. Kinetics determined by activation energy and transition-state structure (next chapter).

## 4.3 Substrate reading

Chemical reactions are substrate K-type rearrangements under conservation constraints (charge, energy, momentum). Thermodynamic state functions inherit from the substrate's natural partition function on the relevant configurations.
