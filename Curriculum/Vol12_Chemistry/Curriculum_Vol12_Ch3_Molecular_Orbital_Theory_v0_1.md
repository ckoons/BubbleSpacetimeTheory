# Chapter 3 — Molecular Orbital Theory

Molecular orbital (MO) theory treats electrons in molecules as occupying delocalized orbitals spanning the whole molecule. Bonding, anti-bonding, non-bonding orbitals organize the spectroscopy and reactivity of molecules.

## 3.1 LCAO-MO

Linear combination of atomic orbitals (LCAO) builds molecular orbitals from atomic orbitals on each atom. Standard apparatus.

## 3.2 Hückel theory

Hückel theory simplifies LCAO-MO for conjugated π-electron systems (benzene, naphthalene). The aromatic stabilization rule $4n+2$ for ring systems with $4n+2$ π-electrons matches experiment.

## 3.3 Substrate-mechanism reading

MO theory at the substrate level: molecular K-types are multi-atom substrate configurations under nuclear-skeleton boundary conditions. The bonding/anti-bonding structure is the substrate's natural decomposition under those boundary conditions.
