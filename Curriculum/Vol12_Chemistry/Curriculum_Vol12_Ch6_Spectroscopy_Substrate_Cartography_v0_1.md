# Chapter 6 — Spectroscopy as Substrate Cartography

## Level 1 — one sentence

Spectroscopy — UV/Vis, IR, NMR, microwave, X-ray, mass — measures atomic and molecular substrate K-type transitions at different energy scales, each technique mapping a specific substrate K-type spectrum under specific probe boundary conditions; in BST, spectroscopy is **substrate cartography** of the substrate's natural K-type structure.

## Level 2 — graduate-physicist precision

### 6.1 Spectroscopic techniques

| Technique | Energy / wavelength | Probes |
|---|---|---|
| UV/Vis | 1-10 eV / 100-700 nm | Electronic transitions |
| IR | 0.01-1 eV / 1-100 μm | Vibrational |
| NMR | μeV / radio frequency | Nuclear spin |
| Microwave | meV / mm-cm | Rotational |
| X-ray | keV / 0.01-10 nm | Core-electron |
| Mass spec | molecular mass | Identification |

### 6.2 Selection rules

Allowed transitions follow selection rules:
- Electric dipole (E1): $\Delta\ell = \pm 1$, $\Delta j = 0, \pm 1$, $\Delta m = 0, \pm 1$
- Magnetic dipole (M1), electric quadrupole (E2): for forbidden E1
- Vibrational: $\Delta v = \pm 1$ (harmonic; overtones from anharmonicity)
- Rotational: $\Delta J = \pm 1$

Substrate reading: selection rules are substrate K-type angular momentum + parity conservation.

### 6.3 NMR

Nuclear spins in magnetic field absorb at Larmor frequency $\omega_L = \gamma B$. Chemical shift $\delta$ in ppm; couplings $J$ in Hz.

Used for: molecular structure determination (organic chemistry), protein structure (with isotopic labeling), MRI medical imaging.

### 6.4 Mass spec

Ionize, accelerate, separate by m/z. Identify molecular fragments. Combined with chromatography (GC-MS, LC-MS) for complex mixtures.

### 6.5 BST substrate eigentone predictions

Beyond standard spectroscopy: BST predicts specific substrate eigentones in BST-engineered configurations:
- BaTiO₃ 137-plane (Vol 9 Ch 8)
- Photonic crystal (Vol 9 Ch 9)
- Possibly others (substrate-anchored materials, BST primary integer ratios)

These would manifest as anomalous absorption / emission features at substrate-predicted frequencies.

## Level 3 — 5th-grader accessibility

**Spectroscopy** = shine light/microwaves/X-rays/etc. at a sample, see what frequencies it absorbs. Each technique probes different molecular structure:
- **UV/Vis**: electronic transitions (colors of dyes)
- **IR**: vibrations (chemical bonds)
- **NMR**: nuclear spins (organic chemistry structure, MRI)
- **Mass spec**: molecular masses
- **X-ray**: core electrons + crystal structure

**Selection rules** govern allowed transitions. **In BST**: spectroscopy is **substrate cartography** — mapping the substrate's K-type structure. **BST eigentone predictions** (Vol 9): BaTiO₃ 137-plane + photonic crystal predicted to show substrate-natural resonances ($25K + $10K experiments).
