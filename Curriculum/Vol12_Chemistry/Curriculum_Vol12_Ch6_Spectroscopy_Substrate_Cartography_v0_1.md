# Chapter 6 — Spectroscopy as Substrate Cartography

Spectroscopy — UV/visible, IR, NMR, microwave, X-ray, mass — measures atomic and molecular substrate K-type transitions. Each spectroscopic technique maps a specific energy/momentum scale of the substrate.

In BST, spectroscopy is substrate cartography: each measurement maps the substrate's natural K-type structure under specific probe conditions.

## 6.1 The spectroscopic techniques

- UV/visible (electronic transitions, 1-10 eV)
- IR (vibrational transitions, 0.01-1 eV)
- NMR (nuclear spin transitions, $\mu$eV)
- Microwave (rotational transitions, meV)
- X-ray (core-electron transitions, keV)
- Mass spec (molecular mass identification)

Each maps a different substrate scale.

## 6.2 BST substrate eigentone predictions

The BST substrate predicts specific natural eigentones — substrate-K-type-coupling frequencies — observable in spectroscopic experiments. The BaTiO3 137-plane test (Volume 9 Chapter 8) is one signature; substrate-natural microwave/optical eigentones in BST-anchored materials provide others.
