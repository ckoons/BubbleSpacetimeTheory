# Chapter 5 — Electromagnetic Waves and the Photon

Maxwell's equations in vacuum predict transverse electromagnetic waves propagating at the speed of light $c$. Heinrich Hertz's 1887 experiment confirmed Maxwell's prediction; Einstein's 1905 photoelectric-effect paper established that EM waves are quantized into photons. Modern quantum electrodynamics treats the photon as the massless gauge boson of $U(1)_{em}$.

In BST, EM waves are the substrate's $U(1)_{em}$ bundle excitations propagating on the substrate's Bergman geometry. Photons are the quantized excitations — massless boson K-types with $SO(2)$ phase carrying spin 1.

## 5.1 The wave equation

In vacuum, Maxwell's equations imply $\nabla^2 \vec{E} - (1/c^2) \partial^2 \vec{E}/\partial t^2 = 0$ and similarly for $\vec{B}$. Solutions are plane waves $\vec{E} = \vec{E}_0 e^{i(\vec{k}\cdot\vec{r} - \omega t)}$ with $\omega = c|\vec{k}|$.

Substrate-mechanism: EM waves are the substrate $U(1)_{em}$ bundle's massless propagating modes; $c$ is the substrate's natural information-propagation speed set by the Bergman-bundle metric.

## 5.2 Polarization

EM waves carry polarization — linear, circular, elliptical. The substrate's $SO(2)$ phase structure on photon K-types produces the polarization states directly.

## 5.3 The photon

Photons are massless spin-1 bosons in the $U(1)_{em}$ gauge sector. Substrate-mechanism: massless bosonic K-type with $SO(2)$ phase weight $\pm 1$ (the two transverse polarizations). Photon energy $E = \hbar\omega$; momentum $\vec{p} = \hbar\vec{k}$.

## 5.4 Photoelectric effect and Compton scattering

The photoelectric effect (Einstein 1905) and Compton scattering (Compton 1923) are early experimental confirmations of photon particle structure. Substrate-mechanism: photons interact with matter via the substrate $U(1)_{em}$ vertex, with energy and momentum conserved per the standard relativistic kinematics.
