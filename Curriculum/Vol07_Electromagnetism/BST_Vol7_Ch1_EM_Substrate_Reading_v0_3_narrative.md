# Chapter 1 — EM as Substrate-Bundle Gauge Sector

Electromagnetism, in its conventional Maxwellian form, has been one of physics' two most successful theories — the other being quantum mechanics. Maxwell's four equations unify electric and magnetic fields into a single gauge-invariant framework; quantum electrodynamics, the quantum-field-theoretic completion, is the most precise scientific theory ever tested.

In BST, electromagnetism is not an independent theory. It is the substrate's $U(1)$ gauge sector — the substrate's $SO(2)$ isotropy factor (Volume 0 Chapter 4 §4.3) acting as the Bergman-bundle connection on $H^2(D_{IV}^5)$. This volume develops the operational machinery for working physicists: Maxwell's equations from the substrate, electrostatics, magnetostatics, electromagnetic waves, radiation, the Lagrangian formulation, multipole expansions, EM in matter, and plasma physics — all with the substrate-mechanism explicit.

## 1.1 The substrate $U(1)$

The $SO(2)$ factor of the substrate's isotropy decomposition (Volume 0 Chapter 4) is one-dimensional and Abelian — equivalent to $U(1)$. Acting on substrate scalar states, it gives **electric charge** $\hat{Q}$ as the $SO(2)$ weight (Lyra T2470). Acting on substrate spinor states, it gives **chirality** $\hat{\gamma}^5$ as the $SO(2)$ half-weight (Lyra T2471). The same factor produces both.

After electroweak symmetry breaking — which Volume 2 Chapter 9 treats — the unbroken combination $U(1)_{em}$ is what we call electromagnetism. Its coupling constant is $\alpha = 1/N_{\max} = 1/137$, structurally derived.

Lyra T2477 (Volume 1 Chapter 8 §8.6) identifies the gauge fields as **connections on the line bundle** $L_\lambda \to D_{IV}^5$. The electromagnetic four-potential $A^\mu$ is the $U(1)_{em}$ connection; the electromagnetic field tensor $F^{\mu\nu}$ is its curvature.
