# Chapter 4 — Magnetostatics

Magnetostatics treats steady currents and the static magnetic fields they produce. Ampère's law, the Biot–Savart law, magnetic vector potential, magnetic materials — the apparatus parallel to electrostatics for the magnetic sector.

In BST, magnetostatics is the substrate's steady-current $U(1)_{em}$ configuration with magnetic-field component of the substrate bundle curvature.

## 4.1 Ampère's law

The integral form $\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enc}$ enables magnetic-field calculations for symmetric current distributions. Standard.

## 4.2 The Biot–Savart law

The differential form for magnetic field from a current element: $d\vec{B} = (\mu_0/4\pi) I d\vec{l} \times \hat{r}/r^2$. Substrate-mechanism: the magnetic field is the substrate $U(1)$ bundle curvature's spatial component, with $1/r^2$ falloff inherited from the substrate's Bergman-kernel asymptotic.

## 4.3 The magnetic vector potential

$\vec{B} = \nabla \times \vec{A}$ — the magnetic field is the curl of the vector potential. This is the substrate's bundle-connection picture made explicit.

## 4.4 Magnetic materials

Ferromagnets, paramagnets, and diamagnets correspond to different substrate K-type spin alignments. The substrate's natural spin structure (Volume 1 Chapter 6) sets the magnetic susceptibility classes; specific materials' properties follow from their atomic K-type structures (Volume 9 condensed matter).
