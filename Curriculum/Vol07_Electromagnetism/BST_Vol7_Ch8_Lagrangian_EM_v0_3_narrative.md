# Chapter 8 — Lagrangian and Hamiltonian Electromagnetism

The Lagrangian formulation of electromagnetism — $\mathcal{L}_{EM} = -\frac{1}{4} F_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu}$ for the free field, plus interaction terms — packages Maxwell's equations into a single covariant action principle. The Hamiltonian formulation then sets up the canonical structure for quantization.

In BST, the Lagrangian formulation emerges as the substrate's action functional in the $U(1)_{em}$ gauge sector.

## 8.1 The free-field Lagrangian

$$\mathcal{L}_{EM} \;=\; -\frac{1}{4 \mu_0} F_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu}.$$

The Euler-Lagrange equations for $A^\mu$ give Maxwell's equations in vacuum. Substrate-mechanism: the Lagrangian is the substrate's natural action functional for the $U(1)_{em}$ bundle connection, with gauge invariance built into the bundle structure.

## 8.2 The interaction Lagrangian

$$\mathcal{L}_{int} \;=\; -J^\mu A_\mu \;=\; -\rho \phi + \vec{J} \cdot \vec{A}.$$

Couples the EM field to a current source. The Euler-Lagrange equations now give the inhomogeneous Maxwell equations.

## 8.3 The Hamiltonian formulation

The canonical Hamiltonian formulation of EM has well-known subtleties — the gauge constraint makes the canonical momentum partially constrained. The Dirac quantization procedure handles this. Standard graduate-text material.

Substrate-mechanism: the Hamiltonian formulation is the substrate's natural time-evolution apparatus in the $U(1)_{em}$ sector; the gauge constraints are the substrate's bundle-invariance conditions.

## 8.4 The action principle and Noether's theorem

The action $S = \int \mathcal{L} \, d^4x$ is stationary on physical field configurations. Noether's theorem applied to the EM Lagrangian's symmetries gives the conservation of energy, momentum, angular momentum, and electric charge — the standard EM conservation laws.

Substrate-side: these conservation laws are the substrate $SO_0(5,2)$ symmetries' Noether currents in the EM sector (Volume 0 Chapter 8).
