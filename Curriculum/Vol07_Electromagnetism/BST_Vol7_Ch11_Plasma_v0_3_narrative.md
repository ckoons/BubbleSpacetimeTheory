# Chapter 11 — Plasma and Magnetohydrodynamics

Plasma — ionized gas with collective electromagnetic behavior — is the fourth state of matter and the most common in the universe. Stars, interstellar space, fusion devices, lightning — all are plasma. Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) treats plasma as a conducting fluid in magnetic fields.

In BST, plasma is the substrate's collective $U(1)_{em}$ response of many ionized atomic K-types; MHD is the substrate's hydrodynamic limit.

## 11.1 Plasma frequency and Debye length

The **plasma frequency** $\omega_p = \sqrt{n e^2/(\epsilon_0 m_e)}$ is the natural oscillation frequency of plasma electrons. The **Debye length** $\lambda_D = \sqrt{\epsilon_0 k_B T/(n e^2)}$ is the screening length over which electric fields are damped.

Substrate-mechanism: $\omega_p$ is the substrate's natural $U(1)_{em}$ oscillation rate at the relevant electron density; $\lambda_D$ is the substrate's screening length set by thermal-substrate competition.

## 11.2 Magnetohydrodynamics

MHD treats plasma as a single-fluid system coupled to electromagnetic fields. The ideal MHD equations:

$$\rho \frac{D\vec{v}}{Dt} = -\nabla P + \vec{J} \times \vec{B}, \qquad \frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t} = \nabla \times (\vec{v} \times \vec{B}).$$

Substrate-mechanism: MHD is the substrate's hydrodynamic Scale-2 limit (Volume 0 Chapter 3) applied to plasma states.

## 11.3 Alfvén waves and reconnection

Alfvén waves propagate along magnetic field lines at the Alfvén speed $v_A = B/\sqrt{\mu_0 \rho}$. Magnetic reconnection — the topological rearrangement of magnetic field lines — drives solar flares, magnetospheric substorms, and tokamak instabilities.

Substrate-mechanism: Alfvén waves are substrate $U(1)_{em}$ bundle excitations along field lines; reconnection is substrate bundle topology change.

## 11.4 Fusion plasmas

Tokamak and stellarator confinement fusion devices use magnetic fields to confine plasma at fusion-relevant temperatures. The substrate framework provides the underlying QED machinery for fusion plasma calculations.
