# Chapter 6 — Radiation and Antennas

Accelerating charges radiate electromagnetic energy. The Larmor formula gives the radiated power for non-relativistic acceleration; the Liénard-Wiechert potentials describe the full relativistic case. Antennas, dipole radiation, synchrotron radiation, cyclotron radiation — these are the working physicist's radiation toolbox.

In BST, radiation is the substrate's $U(1)_{em}$ bundle response to accelerating charged K-types. The standard apparatus applies directly.

## 6.1 The Larmor formula

The power radiated by a non-relativistic charge $q$ with acceleration $a$:

$$P = \frac{q^2 a^2}{6\pi \epsilon_0 c^3}.$$

Substrate-mechanism: the substrate $U(1)_{em}$ bundle emits massless photon K-types in response to charge acceleration, with the rate given by the standard QED vertex evaluated at the appropriate K-type weights.

## 6.2 Dipole radiation

An oscillating dipole at frequency $\omega$ radiates power $P \propto p_0^2 \omega^4/c^3$ with characteristic angular pattern $\sin^2\theta$. The substrate framework reproduces the standard dipole-radiation formulas via the substrate's $SO(2)$ phase action on dipole-coupled states.

## 6.3 Synchrotron and cyclotron radiation

Accelerated charges in magnetic fields emit synchrotron (relativistic) or cyclotron (non-relativistic) radiation with characteristic spectra. Substrate-mechanism: the substrate's $U(1)_{em}$ bundle response to circular motion in a magnetic field produces these spectra via standard QED vertex evaluations.

## 6.4 Antennas

Antennas are radiators with engineered geometry. Standard antenna theory — dipole, loop, array — applies directly in the substrate framework with the substrate $U(1)_{em}$ bundle providing the radiation mechanism.
