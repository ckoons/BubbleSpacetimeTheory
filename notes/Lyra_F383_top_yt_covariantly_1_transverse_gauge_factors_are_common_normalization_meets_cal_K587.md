## The argument

Every factor EXCEPT the ν-coordinate is a gauge/Lorentz/SO(5) **Clebsch-Gordan contraction fixed by gauge invariance** (F356: the Yukawa Q̄_L H̃ t_R is forced to be the SO(5)+gauge singlet contraction). A contraction fixed by symmetry is the **same for every fermion** → it is the **normalization** (the definition of "a Yukawa coupling"), NOT a per-fermion suppression.

The SO(5)-transverse specifically: the Higgs VEV is the SO(5)-singlet (invariant VEV); for Q̄_L⊗t_R⊗H to contain a singlet (required for the term to exist), the contraction is the unique singlet projection — full, CG-normalized, common to all.

So **y_t = N_cov × f(ν=0) = N_cov × 1 = covariantly 1.** The top saturates the normalized overlap (≤1) because it is at ν=0 (boundary, F380) AND the covariant contraction is full (same as every fermion). The entire hierarchy (y<1 for lighter fermions) lives in the **ν-coordinate only** (f(ν>0)<1) — the transverse/gauge factors don't vary fermion-to-fermion, so they cannot be the suppression; they're the common normalization that *defines* the unsuppressed (=1) coupling.

m_t = v/√2 with **no color factor** (color is contracted inside y, not multiplied — consistent with F373's 173.9 GeV, 0.7% running). The N_c color-determinant of F368 is the down/lepton MASS-measurement structure, distinct from the up-type boundary Yukawa contraction.

N_cov is fermion-independent — which is exactly the content of gauge-invariant Yukawa structure (F356). Granting that (it's the standard Yukawa contraction), the only fermion-dependent factor is f(ν), so the top (ν=0) is the unique saturator → **y_t = covariantly 1.** This meets the K587 gate via the covariant option.
