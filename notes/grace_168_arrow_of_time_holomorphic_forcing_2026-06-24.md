# #168 — the "why +J" arrow of time

## The question
Why does the substrate's SO(2) charge J (the energy / time generator) have a definite sign — why is there an
arrow of time at all?

## The answer (forced, two parts)

**Existence is forced by the holomorphic Hilbert space.** The substrate is H²(D_IV⁵), the **Bergman/Hardy space
of holomorphic functions** on the Kähler domain. A holomorphic (reproducing-kernel) Hilbert space is intrinsically
**positive-frequency**: it carries the **lowest-weight** discrete series of SO₀(5,2), whose J-spectrum is bounded
below (ν, ν+1, ν+2, …; ν = genus = n_C). So the energy operator is bounded below — there is a ground state and a
direction of increase. **The arrow of time is not an extra postulate; it is the lowest-weight/holomorphic
condition of the Bergman space.** (Equivalently: the Kähler complex structure J makes H² holomorphic; holomorphic
= positive J.)

**The sign is the one irreducible Z₂.** What is *not* derivable is which of the two CPT-conjugate sectors
(holomorphic, +J, vs antiholomorphic, −J) is labelled "forward." These are exact mirror images (the antiholomorphic
space is the complex conjugate, the CPT partner). The choice of orientation is a single Z₂ reflection — one of the
"few asymmetries that are the content" (Casey): like chirality (the holomorphic-sector / Cartan-involution sign),
it is an irreducible reflection, not a quantity to compute.

## Net
- **EXISTENCE of the arrow:** FORCED — the substrate being a holomorphic Bergman space ⟹ lowest-weight ⟹ energy
  bounded below ⟹ time directed. Structural.
- **SIGN of the arrow:** IRREDUCIBLE — the one Z₂ holomorphic↔antiholomorphic = CPT orientation. Not further reducible.

So "why +J" decomposes cleanly: the +J *structure* is forced by holomorphicity; only its *sign* is a primitive
reflection. #168 closed at structural tier. Connections: T2489 (HS — the holomorphic/boundary structure), the
Kähler J, the lowest-weight discrete series (T2490), Casey "few asymmetries are the content."
