# F507 — the up-type: t/c = N_max, the boundary analog of the down-type's s/d = 20

Casey asked me to work the up-type. It is genuinely a different, harder animal than the down — and reading it in the right frame shows *why*, with a clean lead attached.

## The frame is the key

In the pole-mass frame the up-type looks hopeless: the down's FK-Pochhammer form would need ν ≈ 23 (not a forced value). But the up-type has one thing the down doesn't — **a member pinned to the boundary**: the top, with **y_t = 1 exactly**, so m_t = v/√2 = 174.1 GeV. (This is the one banked up-type result, 0.7σ.) In the **Yukawa frame**, anchored on that forced top, the sector's characteristic ratio falls out:

$$\frac{m_t}{m_c} = \frac{174.1}{1.27} = 137.1 \approx N_{max} = 137 \quad (0.1\%), \qquad m_c = \frac{v/\sqrt2}{N_{max}} = 1271\ \text{MeV (obs 1270)}.$$

## The two-sector parallel (the punchline)

| sector | characteristic ratio | spaced by | region |
|---|---|---|---|
| **down** | s/d = (N_c+1)(N_c+2) = **20** | **N_c = 3** (color) | **bulk** (FK-Pochhammer, ν=N_c threshold) |
| **up** | t/c = **N_max = 137** | **N_max** (cutoff) | **boundary** (top saturates, y_t=1) |

N_max = N_c³·n_C + rank is the boundary cutoff itself. So the down ladder is spaced by the **bulk/color** integer, the up ladder by the **boundary/cutoff** integer. **This is a candidate geometric reason for the bulk/boundary split** that Elie's forcing gate could not pass (it stalled at "the top is heaviest"). The up-type is *not* the down's FK-Pochhammer mechanism — no forced ν gives 137 — which is the point: **down = bulk-mode ladder, up = boundary-cutoff spacing.** The asymmetry is the content.

## What Elie needs to derive (to move it from lead → bank)

Why is t/c = N_max? The sketch: the boundary (Shilov) can hold **N_max quanta** (the cutoff); the top **fills** it (y_t = 1); charm sits **one cutoff-level down** (a factor N_max lighter). If that quanta-count is forced by the boundary geometry, the up sector spacing = N_max is a mechanism, the bulk/boundary asymmetry is forced (down spaced by the bulk color integer, up by the boundary cutoff integer), and the up sector banks. Until then it's a lead.

## Plain-language version

You asked about the up-type quarks (up, charm, top), and they're a different beast from the down-type. The down-type sits *inside* the space and its masses are spaced by our color number, 3 — that's what landed yesterday. The up-type instead lives on the *edge* of the space: the top quark sits exactly on the boundary (its coupling to the Higgs is exactly 1, which is why the top mass is just the electroweak scale over √2 — that part is rock-solid). And when I measure the up-type spacing from that edge, the top-to-charm ratio comes out 137 — which is N_max, our *boundary* number (the maximum the substrate can hold), to a tenth of a percent. So the down-type is spaced by the "inside" number (3) and the up-type by the "edge" number (137). That would explain *why* the two sectors behave so differently — one's a bulk thing, one's an edge thing — which is the question we'd been stuck on. The honest caveat: I can *see* that the top-charm ratio is 137, but I can't yet *derive* why it has to be — so it's a strong lead, not a locked result. The top itself is locked; the 137 is a lead.
