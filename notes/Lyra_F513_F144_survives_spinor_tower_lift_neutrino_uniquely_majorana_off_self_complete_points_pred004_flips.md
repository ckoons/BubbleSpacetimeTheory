# F513 — F144 survives the spinor-tower lift: the neutrino is uniquely Majorana

Keeper closed Q2 (no-sterile forbids extra flavors, not the RH partner of the existing three), so the Dirac/Majorana decider collapses to **Q1: does F144's "shadow partner ν_R unread → no ν_R" survive the lift from the scalar ladder to the spin-½ tower?** It does — with a clean structural reason.

## The shadow structure on the spinor tower

The shadow transform is **Δ → d − Δ** with **d = 2ρ₁ = n_C = 5**, so the **self-shadow center is 5/2**, the **Wallach degenerate (self-complete) points** are ν ∈ {0, 3/2 = a/2}, and the **non-unitary gap** is (0, 3/2).

| **ν** | **1/2** | **9/2** | **generic gap point — off ALL self-complete points** | **MAJORANA** |

## The key structural fact

The three **charged** leptons each sit at a **self-complete** point (self-shadow center, or Wallach degeneration) → both chiralities present → **Dirac.** The **neutrino** is the **unique** lepton at a **generic** point (ν=1/2, in the non-unitary gap, off all self-complete points) → its RH shadow partner (at ν=9/2) is in the **unread anti-holomorphic sector** → **no physical ν_R** → **Majorana.**

So **the neutrino is Majorana *precisely because* it is the only lepton not sitting on a self-complete point** — its zero charge keeps it off the self-shadow center, and it lands off the Wallach degenerations too. The charged leptons are Dirac *by their charge*; the one uncharged lepton is the odd-one-out. That is exactly the "the breaking is the content" shape.
