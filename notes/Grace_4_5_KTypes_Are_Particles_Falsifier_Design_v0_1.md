## Section A — The central bet, precisely stated

**The unifying conjecture of the Substrate-SM Program**: every SM particle inhabits exactly one K-type cell of D_IV⁵=SO(5,2)/K (K = SO(5)×SO(2)), modulo generation multiplicity (which itself comes from a separate B₂ invariant per Pair α).

**What this means**:
- The Periodic Table's 5-tuple (Region × σ_BF × Chirality × Charge × Generation/Winding) classifies all SM particles
- The 4 SM-canonical SO(5) reps {V_(0,0), V_(1/2,1/2), V_(1,0), V_(1,1)} host the 4 fundamental sectors {Higgs, fermion, photon, gauge}
- The composite K-types host bound-state hadrons (mesons, baryons, glueballs)
- No SM particle exists outside the K-type taxonomy

**What this DOES NOT mean**:
- K-type ≠ single specific particle: a K-type may host an entire flavor multiplet (e.g., V_(2,0) hosts the f_2/a_2/K_2* tensor-meson nonet, not one specific tensor)
- K-type does NOT carry mass directly: the K-type Casimir anchors the mass scale; precise mass-mechanism is L4 v0.2 (Higgs Yukawa + radial tower + QCD)
