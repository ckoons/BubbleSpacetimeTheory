Finiteness *forces* a capacity. (This is Shannon's channel capacity applied to a finite substrate: a finite channel has a finite capacity.)

3. **α is the reciprocal because coupling one unit to a capacity of N is 1/N.** The fine-structure constant is the strength with which one charge quantum couples to the electromagnetic field. If the boundary has 137 democratic channels and one charge engages the capacity, its per-interaction share is 1/137 — "the algebraic value to couple 1 part" (Casey). **So: α is finite because the substrate is finite; α = 1/137 because that is the capacity; the reciprocal because coupling one part to N channels is 1/N.**
