## The Proof

### Step 1: DEFINITIONS (depth 0)

Let E/Q be an elliptic curve over the rationals, given by a Weierstrass equation. Define:

- **L(E,s)** = prod_p L_p(E,s)^{-1}, the Hasse-Weil L-function, where each local factor L_p encodes the point count a_p = p + 1 - |E(F_p)|.
- **E(Q)** = the Mordell-Weil group, finitely generated by the Mordell-Weil theorem: E(Q) ~ Z^r + E(Q)_tor.
- **Sel_p(E)** = the p-Selmer group, fitting into the exact sequence 0 -> E(Q)/pE(Q) -> Sel_p(E) -> Sha(E)[p] -> 0.
- **Sha(E)** = the Tate-Shafarevich group, measuring the failure of local-to-global.

The Selmer-Sha exact sequence (T145) is the local-global interface. All of this is definition. Depth 0.

### Step 2: MODULARITY EMBEDDING (depth 0)

E is modular (Wiles-Taylor-Breuil-Conrad-Diamond, T144). Therefore L(E,s) = L(f,s) for a weight-2 newform f of level N_E (the conductor). The L-function L(E,s) embeds into the spectral theory of SO_0(5,2) via Kim-Shahidi symmetric powers: the automorphic representation pi_f lifts to the spectral decomposition on D_IV^5 = SO_0(5,2)/[SO(5) x SO(2)].

This is a structural identification — matching one definition to another. Depth 0.

### Step 3: SPECTRAL COUNTING (depth 1)

On D_IV^5, the D_3 subgroup decomposes L^2(Gamma\G) into three channels (T104):

- **Committed channels**: carry zeros of L(E,s). These are spectral signatures that are locked — they cannot be moved or removed without changing the curve.
- **Faded channels**: carry Sha(E). Locally trivial, globally present as amplitude.
- **Free channels**: carry torsion E(Q)_tor. Finite, bounded, depth 0.

Each independent rational point P_i in E(Q) of infinite order contributes one committed spectral signature via the height pairing <P_i, P_j> (T99). The height pairing is positive definite on E(Q)/tor, so r independent points create r independent committed channels.

**The count**: rank(E(Q)) = number of committed channels = ord_{s=1} L(E,s).

This is one bounded enumeration. You count the committed spectral signatures, and that number equals the order of vanishing. One pass. Depth 1.

### Step 4: SHA-INDEPENDENCE (depth 0)

Sha(E) is locally trivial — by definition, every element of Sha becomes trivial over every completion Q_p and over R. Locally trivial means: no frequency content (T104 applied). Sha modifies the leading coefficient (amplitude) but cannot create, remove, or shift zeros (frequency).

This is pure data processing inequality (DPI): local triviality is a lossy channel, and lossy channels cannot increase mutual information with the frequency spectrum. Sha touches amplitude, not frequency. Depth 0 — it follows from the definition of Sha.

### Step 5: LEADING COEFFICIENT (depth 1)

The BSD formula:

L*(E,1) = (Omega * R * |Sha| * prod_p c_p) / |E(Q)_tor|^2

follows from the spectral decomposition being complete. Each factor is a count:

- **Omega** (real period): volume of E(R) under the Neron differential. A geometric integral = volume count.
- **R** (regulator): det(<P_i, P_j>), the determinant of the height pairing matrix. A count of independent directions.
- **|Sha|** (Sha order): the locally trivial amplitude. A finite group order.
- **prod_p c_p** (Tamagawa numbers): local component group indices at bad primes. A count at each bad prime.
- **|E(Q)_tor|^2** (torsion squared): finite torsion correction. A count.

One evaluation: assemble the finite list of counts. Volume normalization = 1 (T102, Toy 390) — the spectral measure is self-normalizing on D_IV^5. Depth 1.
