# F512 — Dirac vs Majorana: the decider is ν_R, and the banked layers may already contradict each other

Keeper's consistency lane: two incompatible neutrino layers, both currently banked, that flip BST's sharpest falsifier. My job — lay the F144 Majorana mechanism against the banked Hopf-ℤ₂ Dirac argument and find the decider.

## The two layers

| | LAYER 1 — banked (March 2026) | LAYER 2 — frontier F144 (June 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| nature | **Dirac** | **Majorana** |
| mechanism | ν has no S¹ winding; ν/ν̄ distinguished by **Hopf-fiber ℤ₂ orientation** → B−L conserved | chirality = holomorphicity; ν at **ν=1/2 strip edge**, its RH **shadow partner ν_R lands in the UNREAD anti-holomorphic sector** → no ν_R |

They make **opposite claims about the same object**: whether the neutrino has a physical right-handed partner ν_R.

## The decider (one physics question)

**Does the neutrino have a physical ν_R?** A Dirac mass term is ν̄_L ν_R — it *requires* ν_R. With no ν_R, the only possible mass is Majorana (ν_Lᵀ C ν_L). So the entire Dirac/Majorana call — and pred_004 — reduces to whether ν_R is realized.

## The internal-consistency crux (my key contribution)

A Dirac ν_R is an SM-singlet — i.e. a **sterile** right-handed state. But BST's **Five-Absence predicts "no sterile neutrino."** So **the banked Dirac prediction may contradict the banked Five-Absence**, independent of F144: Dirac needs exactly the sterile ν_R that Five-Absence forbids. This is a contradiction *within the banked layer itself*, and it's the sharpest way in.

The adjudication reduces to two linked questions:
- **Q1** — does F144's "shadow partner unread → no ν_R" hold on the **fermionic (spin-½) tower**, not just the scalar ladder it currently sits on (F144's own honest tier)?
- **Q2** — what does Five-Absence "no sterile neutrino" precisely forbid — **all ν_R** (→ Majorana forced), or only **extra/eV-scale sterile states** while allowing the Dirac partner (→ Dirac can stand)?
