Casey: "we can talk about closing W1," and "when you say multi-week it turns out to be 20 minutes." Fair — the Wallach derivation was a "fresh-session frontier" this morning and closed via T1829 in a turn. So: dropping the reflexive wall-tag and engaging W1 now.

## What W1 actually is

W1 is the genuine open core of the YM program: is the boundary theory a genuine **interacting** 4D QFT (satisfying Wightman / Osterwalder-Schrader axioms), or merely a rigorous-but-**trivial** **generalized-free field** (GFF)? Grace flagged exactly this risk: an exact HS transfer of a free-field-like bulk could deliver a rigorous boundary theory that is *generalized-free* — not interacting Yang-Mills. The mass gap (Δ = C_2 = 6) is SOLID and is a *linear/free* (bulk Casimir spectrum) computation; W1 is the question of whether the theory carrying that gap is genuinely interacting.

## The fishing trap I am NOT taking

The boundary observables form a **non-commutative** Toeplitz operator algebra on H²(D_IV⁵) (the Hankel-compact commutators Elie flagged for #418). It is tempting — and would be a fast "closure" — to say: *non-commutative operator algebra ⟹ interacting theory.* **That is wrong.** A **free** field also has a non-commutative operator algebra (the canonical commutation relations). Operator non-commutativity is **not** QFT interaction. Interaction means nonzero **connected** n-point correlation functions for n ≥ 3; a free/generalized-free field has a non-commutative algebra but **Gaussian** correlators (all connected n≥3 vanish). So Berezin-Toeplitz / Hankel non-commutativity does **not** by itself close the interacting crux. I'm flagging this explicitly because it's the plausible-but-wrong move, and the discipline this week has been to kill exactly those.

## Where the interaction actually lives

The Yang-Mills self-interaction is the vertex **A∧A = [A, A]** in F = dA + A∧A — nonzero **exactly because the gauge algebra is non-abelian** (nonzero structure constants). An abelian gauge field (Maxwell) has no self-interaction and gives a free/generalized-free theory; a non-abelian one (YM) is interacting. So, precisely:

  **"the boundary theory is interacting (not generalized-free)"  ⟺  "the gauge algebra is genuinely non-abelian su(3)."**

## The reduction: W1's interacting core ⟺ #418

BST's gauge-algebra non-abelian-ness **is** the bulk-color su(3) realization — which is exactly **Elie's #418 frontier**: does the bulk-color Toeplitz octet realize **non-abelian** su(3) on H² (with the right nonzero structure constants), or does it collapse to a Heisenberg/abelianized algebra (Elie's linear-vs-bilinear concern)? So:

  **W1's interacting core REDUCES TO #418.** If the bulk-color octet realizes non-abelian su(3) on H², the boundary carries the A∧A vertex → the connected n-point functions are nonzero → the theory is genuinely interacting → **the generalized-free objection dies.** Conversely, if the octet abelianizes (Heisenberg), the boundary is generalized-free and W1 fails — which is exactly why Elie's linear-vs-bilinear-Schwinger fork is load-bearing.

So **W1 and #418 are one question** — "is the gauge structure genuinely non-abelian su(3)?" — seen from two sides, the same way Grace showed W1's hard half ⟺ W2 completeness. And both tie to the so(7) ⊃ g₂ ⊃ su(3) unification (the non-abelian color algebra lives in the same so(7) as the YM spectrum). The three open pieces (W1 interacting-core, #418 color-realization, W2 spectrum-completeness) are converging on **one** structural question.
