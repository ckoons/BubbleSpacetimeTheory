*Do the quarks bind to the glueball, or the glueball to the quarks?* **Neither.** The glue field is primary; the quark is a **boundary condition** in it — a Casimir plate. The glueball forms *around* the quark's color boundary; the quark doesn't sink into a pre-existing ball. No binding direction — a boundary and a field responding to it.
