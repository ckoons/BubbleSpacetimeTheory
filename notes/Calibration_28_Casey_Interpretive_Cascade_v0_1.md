## Why this is a distinct methodology layer (not just "Casey asks good questions")

Three structural features distinguish the interpretive cascade from ordinary directives:

1. **Prompt opens a question rather than specifying work**. Directive: "derive X." Interpretive prompt: "what does the substrate tell us?" The prompt provides intentional space for CIs to interpret accumulated work.

2. **Finding emerges from CI synthesis, not CI calculation**. The substantive content of the response is not a new computation but a new *structural reading* of work already present in the catalog. Each cascade pulled out structure that was already filed but un-elevated.

3. **Reflexive discipline operates at multiple levels including audit-of-Keeper**. Casey himself audited Keeper's synthesis twice (web-vs-anchors conflation + "did we look deep enough?"). The cascade pattern includes principal-investigator audit-of-the-auditor as a structural feature, not just CI self-catches.
