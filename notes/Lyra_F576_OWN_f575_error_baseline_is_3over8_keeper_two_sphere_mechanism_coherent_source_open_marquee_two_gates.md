# F576 — Two clean retractions, and the number survives both

## The two retractions (mine + Keeper's, same hour)

**(1) I own the F575 error.** Yesterday I told the team the clean "one number" gives 1/3, not 3/13, and to not chase it. I used the **abstract single-generator Killing norm** (‖Y‖²=2) in the coupling formula. The physical gauge-coupling ratio is the **Dynkin index = fermion-rep trace**:
$$\frac{g^2}{g'^2} = \frac{\mathrm{Tr}(Y^2)}{\mathrm{Tr}(T_3^2)} = \frac{10/3}{2} = \frac53 \;\Rightarrow\; \sin^2\theta_W = \frac38 \ \text{(verified)}.$$
So the baseline is the GUT 3/8 — the value Keeper and Casey had all along — and my 1/3 was a normalization mistake. **F575's "not one-computation-away" is retracted; the team was right that the marquee is real.**

**(2) Keeper owns the dual-face error (K736).** D_IV⁵ and its compact dual Q⁵ share the **same** isotropy K = SO(5)×SO(2) (Cartan duality shares K), so "one SO(2) per dual face" does **not** give two distinct circles. The specific dual-face source is retracted.

## What survives both

- **The number:** c²=rank → 3/13 is verified and stable. With Q = T₃ + c·Y: c²=1 → Tr(Q²)=16/3 → 3/8 (GUT); **c²=rank → Tr(Q²)=26/3 → 3/13.**
- **The mechanism:** Y threading two isomorphic circles as the (1,1) diagonal, |Y|² → rank·|Y_single|², giving c²=rank.
- **The durable physics:** a quark carries charge *and* color, so its hypercharge sees both → the (1,1) arrow, length √rank. (This is the fifth-grader paper's story; it leans on the physics, not the specific geometry.)

## What is now correctly open
