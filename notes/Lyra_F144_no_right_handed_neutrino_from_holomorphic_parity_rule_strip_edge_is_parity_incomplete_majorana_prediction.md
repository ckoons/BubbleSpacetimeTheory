## The standing pieces

- **Chirality = holomorphicity** (BST's parity steer; "SU(2)_R unread"). The substrate is the **holomorphic** Hardy space `H²(D_IV⁵)`. Holomorphic = one chirality (left-handed). The anti-holomorphic conjugate sector (the right-isospin `SU(2)_R`) is **not read**.
- **Shadow = parity.** On the conformal boundary, the shadow transform `Δ ↔ d − Δ` is the parity/reflection. Each rep has a shadow partner; the two are the two parity (chirality) members.
- **Electron at the self-shadow center** `ν = 5/2 = d/2` (F142, forced) — the unique shadow-fixed point.

So the holomorphic/LH rule **reads only one member** of each shadow pair `{Δ, d−Δ}` — the holomorphic one. A particle has a readable right-handed partner only if its locus *supplies* the second member as a readable (holomorphic-or-boundary) state.

## The four lepton loci split by parity-completeness

| lepton | locus | shadow status | both chiralities readable? | mass |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **electron** | strip **center** `ν=5/2=d/2` | **self-shadow** (its own partner) | **yes** (self-complete) | massive **Dirac** |
| **muon** | Shilov **S⁴** (compact) | boundary: Szegő reflection pairs holo↔anti-holo boundary values | **yes** | massive **Dirac** |

| **neutrino** | strip **EDGE** `ν=1/2` | off-center, **non-compact** | **NO** — shadow partner unread | **LH-only** |

The three charged leptons are **parity-complete**: the electron because the self-shadow center *is* its own parity partner; the muon and tau because a deposit **on the compact boundary** is where holomorphic and anti-holomorphic boundary values meet (the Szegő/Schwarz reflection pairs `L ↔ R`). Both chiralities are readable → a Dirac mass exists → they are massive.

The **neutrino is the unique parity-INCOMPLETE one**. Its locus, the strip *edge* `ν = 1/2`, is **neither** the self-shadow center **nor** a compact boundary. Its shadow partner — the would-be right-handed neutrino `ν_R` — is a **distinct** state, and the holomorphic/LH rule **does not read it**. So there is **no readable right-handed neutrino**.
