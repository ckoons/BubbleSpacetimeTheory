## Part 1 — the MECHANISM clears the literal flag (group-theoretically GJ-free)

The color-parity route (K581, verified by my F372 and Elie 4450) derives the down-row exponents from **D_IV⁵ color-root crossings**, not GUT structure:
- the down-quark weight is the U(1)_color charge → responds only to the color roots e₁−e₂ (c=1), e₁−e₃ (c=2) — the su(3) ⊂ g₂ ⊂ so(7) color structure of D_IV⁵ (F449),
- gen-1 (ν=5/2): 2 color roots crossed → exponent **+1** → N_c^{+1} = **3**,
- gen-2 (ν=3/2): 1 crossed → **−1** → N_c^{−1} = **1/3**,
- gen-3 (ν=0): 0 crossed → **0** → N_c^0 = **1**.

**No SU(5), no 45-of-SU(5) Higgs, no Clebsch-Gordan input, no gauge-coupling unification, no proton-decay operator.** The {N_c, 1/N_c, 1} pattern comes from color-root parity on the substrate domain. So on the *literal* question K639 posed — "does the mechanism use GUT machinery?" — the answer is **no**. The ledger's framing ("GJ texture is confirmation not input") is correct at the group-theory level. Part 1 PASSES.

## Part 2 — but the VALUES are GUT-SCALE, and m_q/m_l is not RG-invariant (the F444 flag)

Here is what K639's flag did not reach, and it's the F444 lesson: **{3, 1/3, 1} are not scale-free numbers — they are the values the down/lepton ratios take at the GUT scale (~10^16 GeV), and they run.**

**m_quark/m_lepton is NOT RG-invariant.** The quark mass runs with QCD (decreasing with scale); the lepton mass is essentially fixed (QED only). So m_d/m_e runs — from ~3 at M_GUT *up* to ~9 at 2 GeV. Concretely (PDG-ish MS-bar quarks at 2 GeV; re-pin at ratification):

| ratio | BST/GJ (claimed) | PHYSICAL (low-energy) |
|---|---|---|
| m_d/m_e | 3 | **9.1** |
| m_s/m_μ | 1/3 = 0.33 | **0.88** |
| m_b/m_τ | 1 | **2.35–2.76** |
| product (GJ det = 1) | 3·(1/3)·1 = **1** | **22** |

**The banked values {3, 1/3, 1} do not match the measured low-energy ratios {9.1, 0.88, 2.35}.** They match the GUT-scale values. So to make the down-row banks agree with observation, one must impose {3,1/3,1} at ~M_GUT and run down with QCD — which means either:
- **(a)** BST imposes the color-parity texture at ~10^16 GeV (the GUT scale) — but Five-Absence's no-GUT means BST has no principled reason to select that scale; picking the GUT scale without a GUT is exactly the adjacency a referee will attack; or
- **(b)** BST claims {3,1/3,1} as scale-free geometric predictions — in which case they are **~3× off the measured ratios** and the banks fail at the value level.

Neither branch is clean. The color-parity forces the exponent *texture* {+1,−1,0}, but the *scale* at which that texture equals the observed ratios is the GUT scale, and that selection is unaddressed.
