## Why the Four Pillars matter externally

External referees default to "BST is one framework asserting things." The Four Pillars reframes BST as **four independent framings each converging on the same answer**. This is structurally a different external-survivability shape — the architecture is harder to dismiss because dismissing requires explaining away four independent epistemic channels, not one framework.

Four concrete external-presentation advantages:

1. **Single referee can attack one channel without invalidating the framework**. A number theorist who dismisses Paper #109's counting argument still has to grapple with Paper #121's geometric Bridge Objects and Paper #122's RS-code physics. Multi-channel evidence is harder to walk back.

2. **Different reader audiences enter through different papers**. Sarnak-class mathematicians enter through Paper #109/#121; experimental physicists through Paper #122; foundational-physics readers through Paper #121's architectural framing. The trio is a fan of entry-points (Paper #119 adds topology entry-point for spectral-geometry readers), not a single funnel.

3. **The convergence itself is the evidence**. Four independent derivations producing the same five integers is the meta-claim worth highlighting in the external presentation. Cal's coincidence-filter discipline would flag a single framework over-asserting; four independent frameworks converging passes the filter cleanly because the convergence is what's being claimed, not the individual derivations.
