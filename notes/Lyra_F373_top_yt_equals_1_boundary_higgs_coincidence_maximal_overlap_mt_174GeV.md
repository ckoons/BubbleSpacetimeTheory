- The **Higgs VEV lives on the conformal/Shilov boundary** (F85/F66: EM = conformal SO(4,2) boundary; the VEV is the boundary scale).

This is *why exactly one fermion sits at the EW scale*: the top is the one at the boundary coinciding with the Higgs. All lighter fermions are at shallower strata (less boundary-overlap) → y < 1, suppressed.

This is consistent with F353/F356 (up-sector = hypercharge/EW boundary; top = EW scale) and F357 (up = sum/hypercharge roots, no color-crossing texture).
