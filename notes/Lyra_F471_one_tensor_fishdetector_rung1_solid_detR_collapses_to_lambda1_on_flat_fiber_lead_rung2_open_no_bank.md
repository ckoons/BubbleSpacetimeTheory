# F471 — one tensor, fish-detector: rung-1 solid, a real unification lead, rung-2 open — no bank

Keeper's fish-detector is the right knife: **one D_IV⁵ curvature tensor, restricted to the three strata, yielding whatever it yields — not rank·C_2 chosen for one step and N_c³ for the other.** I ran the full overlap with the boundary term restored against it, forward. Honest result: rung-1 holds, the lepton/quark unification gets a genuine candidate mechanism, but rung-2 does not emerge — so nothing banks.

## The test — one functional for the muon AND the down rungs?

| object | functional | value |
|---|---|---|
| **muon** (colorless, compact-sphere stratum) | det′(R) = **product** of C_2 eigenvalues, each 24/π² | (24/π²)^{C_2} = 206.76 (0.003%) |
| **down rung-1** (colored, s-stratum) | λ₁ = **leading** eigenvalue = C_2·rank | 12 (Bergman gap, T1238, cross-confirmed) |

On its face these are **two functionals** (a product vs a single eigenvalue) with **two different eigenvalues** (24/π² vs 12) — which is exactly what reverse-reading looks like, and what the fish-detector is built to catch.

## The candidate rescue — det′ collapses to λ₁ on the flat color fiber (a real lead)

There is, however, a *principled* reason they could be **one functional**, and it is grounded in the sector split (F445), not chosen to fit:

- The quark's **color fiber is FLAT** (zero curvature). Flat directions contribute a factor **1** to the regularized determinant. So for a colored object, det′(R) **collapses** — all the flat color directions drop out — leaving only the single surviving *curved* mode, which is the leading eigenvalue **λ₁**.
- The lepton is a **color singlet on the curved locus** — no flat directions to trivialize — so it keeps the **full determinant** over all C_2 modes → (24/π²)^{C_2}.

**If that holds, det′(lepton) and λ₁(quark) are the SAME functional — the regularized curvature determinant — with the quark's flat color directions trivialized.** One tensor, one functional, the sector split doing the selecting. That would answer the fish-detector's "why a different form for each" with a mechanism instead of a choice. **This is a genuine forward LEAD for the F468 unification — but it is NOT closed:** it needs Grace to exhibit the flat-fiber determinant-collapse explicitly (does det′ over the flat color fiber literally reduce to λ₁ = C_2·rank?).

## Rung-2 — does NOT emerge from the same object

The same object must also give rung-2, and here it fails forward:
- F460's independently-computed 2→3 deposit is **64 = rank⁶**, which **overshoots** the observed m_b/m_s = 45.
- So the required b-factor is 45/64 = **0.703 — a fractional SUPPRESSION (<1).** But a curvature determinant / spectral gap **enhances (≥1).** No forward source produces 0.703. (F467 already withdrew the 27; 64 overshoots.)

So **rung-2 does not emerge forward from the same object.** The down-ladder stays **rung-1-forward / rung-2-open.**
