## Thesis (one line)
**Can one hear the shape of a drum? For one particular drum — D_IV⁵ — the answer is the Standard Model.** Physics is the spectrum of one rigid shape; because the shape is rigid, the spectrum is forced, and there are no free parameters.

## The two moves that make it honest (not just a metaphor)
1. **Geometry ↔ spectrum is not a coincidence — it's that the Casimir is central.** On a symmetric space the Casimir (Laplacian) acts as a single scalar on each irreducible mode, so every geometric mode has exactly one energy (its Casimir eigenvalue). That is Peter–Weyl, and it is what Fourier saw: any state is a sum of pure frequencies. Here the frequencies are the K-types, and the K-types are the particles. *This is why every geometric question in BST turns into an energy/gap.*
2. **The one object is the Bergman kernel, and Born = Bergman is proven.** So the measure whose spectrum we read is compelled, not chosen. Quantum mechanics *is* the spectral structure of the geometry. Every physical quantity is a spectral coefficient of the one kernel (the containment theorem): masses = radial moments, mixings = angular overlaps, couplings = counts, forces = support, the gaps = eigenvalue spacings.

## Why no free parameters — the rigidity
Kac's question ("can one hear the shape of a drum?") has answer *no* in general — isospectral non-isometric drums exist. But a **rigid** drum (D_IV⁵ is rank-2, maximally symmetric — the Rigidity Principle) has exactly **one** spectrum: geometry and spectrum determine each other with zero slack. That rigidity is the *reason* there are no free parameters. The five integers (2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 137) are the drum's fundamental frequencies — its lowest Casimir eigenvalues and dimensions.

## The condensate = the tension (answering Casey's question)
The condensate is **not the skin** (that's the Hardy space, the medium); it is the **tension in the skin.** A drum's pitch ω = √(T/σ)·k splits into shape (the pattern k) and tension (the pitch T). BST's mass = y·v splits the same way: geometry gives the pattern (the overlaps y), the condensate gives the pitch (the scale v). Turn the tension off (v → 0) and the skin goes slack — massless, conformal, pure light (the nucleation story: light pouring in before the drumhead tightens). The condensate reaches the Shilov rim (F603) exactly as tension is applied at a drum's clamped edge; and heavy = near the tensioned rim (the top most boundary-localized → feels the most tension → heaviest).
