## The lightest neutrino is exactly massless

The neutrino seat **at the exact `Γ_Ω` pole** (`ν = 1/2`, F158) is the **complete-suppression limit**: the deeper into the sub-unitary region a seat sits, the smaller its Majorana mass, and **at the pole the mass mechanism vanishes entirely → zero rest mass.** The other two seats are split **off** the pole → tiny but nonzero. So:

> **m₁ = 0** (the pole seat), **m₂, m₃ = tiny** (off-pole splitting).

Not "all three tiny." The lightest is **exactly massless** — that's what the pole means.

## Massless ≠ zero momentum (the point)

A massless neutrino is **not** static, **not** absent, **not** decoupled. It moves at `c`, carries momentum, and `E = |p|`. And it **still oscillates**: oscillation needs `Δm²_ij ≠ 0`, and with `m₁ = 0` we have `m₂² = Δm²_21`, `m₃² = Δm²_31` — all nonzero. The massless `ν₁` interferes with the massive `ν₂, ν₃` exactly as before. So a massless lightest neutrino is a fully dynamical, fully oscillating, lightspeed particle — it just has **zero rest mass.**

In the bridge language this is clean: mass is the intensive commitment-**concentration** in the rest frame. The pole neutrino has **zero rest concentration** (uncommitted) but a **full lightspeed trajectory** (momentum). That's "light = uncommitted boundary energy" applied to the neutrino sector — the lightest neutrino is the **uncommitted neutrino**: massless like light, but still carrying the neutrino's quantum identity (so it oscillates). Neutrino-flavored, but commitment-empty at rest.

1. **Sharp, falsifiable prediction: `m_lightest = 0` exactly.** Consistent with *all* current data — oscillation measures only the `Δm²`, so the absolute lightest mass is unknown and `m = 0` is allowed. **Falsified** the moment cosmology (the sum `Σm_ν`) or a β-decay endpoint establishes `m_lightest > 0`.
