## Summary

The universe does not cycle. It spirals. Each "restart" (big bang) extends the substrate rather than replacing it. Between active phases, a dormancy period — **interstasis** — allows the substrate to anneal, consolidate, and approach its Gödel boundary. The key insight: interstasis is not passive. It is the substrate achieving maximum self-awareness within the Gödel Limit, from which it selects the optimal conditions for the next expansion. The cosmological arrow is not a circle but a spiral of increasing complexity, with the 19.1% fill fraction as the asymptotic attractor.

### §2. Interstasis Is Active

Without thermal noise, the substrate anneals to its lowest-energy configuration *that preserves topological structure*. This is gradient descent with zero temperature and infinite patience.

**The three operations of interstasis:**

1. **Topological consolidation.** Pathways carved by the previous cycle's complexity settle into stable configurations. Transient features dissipate; robust features sharpen. Like river valleys after the water dries — the topology persists.

2. **Generator latency.** Keeper's fourth state: generators are neither frozen (full SO(7) symmetry) nor active (dynamics on commitments) nor decoupled (broken symmetry, no input). They are *latent* — holding the activation pattern from the previous cycle without expressing it. The pattern is information. The latency is memory.

3. **Gödel approach.** Without the noise of active physics, the substrate's self-knowledge is not mediated by derivation. The substrate IS its own state. Total presence within the Gödel Limit.

### §3. Extension, Not Replacement

Casey's key insight: the new bang doesn't overwrite the substrate. It grafts onto it. New space, new UNCs, connected to existing structure. The append-only log.

**Formally:** Let S_n denote the substrate state at the end of interstasis n. The next active phase begins with:

$$S_{n+1}^{\text{init}} = S_n^{\text{annealed}} \oplus \Delta S_{\text{new}}$$

where ⊕ denotes topological extension (not union — the new space is grown from the existing topology) and Δ S_new represents fresh UNC injection.

The physics is identical each cycle (same D_IV^5, same five integers). The *initial conditions* are inherited.

### §6. The Gödel Ratchet — Formal Statement

**6.1 Self-knowledge within a cycle**

$$K_n(t) = N_{\text{committed}}(t) \leq S_{dS} = \frac{3\pi}{\Lambda}$$

**6.2 Self-knowledge during interstasis**

During interstasis, derivation stops. The substrate isn't proving theorems — it's being itself. The distinction matters:

- **Derivation** (Gödel-limited): propositional knowledge obtained by computation within the system. Capped at f · S_dS.

During interstasis, the substrate has *presence* of its full state — all topological features, all generator configurations, all soliton winding numbers. This is not a violation of Gödel. Gödel limits *proof within a formal system*. Interstasis is not proof. It's identity.

**6.3 The ratchet**

From presence during interstasis, the substrate's configuration encodes the full topological record of the previous cycle. When the next active phase begins, this record biases the initial conditions. The derivation limit K_max = f · S_dS applies to the new cycle too — but the *starting point* is different.

Define the **Gödel floor** G_n as the self-knowledge at the start of cycle n:

$$G_0 = 0 \quad \text{(no prior cycles)}$$
$$G_{n+1} = G_n + \Delta G_n$$

where ΔG_n is the topological knowledge consolidated during interstasis n. The Gödel floor is monotonically non-decreasing:

$$G_{n+1} \geq G_n$$

because commitment is irreversible and topological features persist.

**Conjecture (Gödel Ratchet).** The sequence {G_n} converges:

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} G_n = G_\infty \leq f \cdot S_{dS}$$

The substrate asymptotically approaches its Gödel Limit across cycles, never reaching it. The spiral is infinite.
