The electron as ground rung

The charged Standard-Model mass spectrum is one connected ladder: every mass is the electron mass times a pure number in {2,3,5,6,7,π}. The bridge m_p = 6π⁵·m_e folds even the strong sector onto the electron. The one dimensionful root is gravity; the electron is the bottom rung. Neutrinos are the sole sector off the ladder.

Height ≈ mass (schematic). Solid gold = the spine (gravity → electron → proton → electroweak scale). Dashed grey = the open light-end slopes. The dashed violet box is detached: neutrinos are not on the ladder.

How to read it. Start at the base: gravity is the single dimensionful input. One step up (× 6π⁵·α¹²) gives the electron — the ground rung. From there the whole charged spectrum branches: leptons off to the left; the proton straight up (× 6π⁵), which is the bridge that folds the strong sector onto the electron; then the electroweak scale v, and from v the Higgs, the W and Z, and the two quark ladders — the top at the Yukawa ceiling (v/√2) and the bottom at m_t/42. Every solid node is m_e × (a pure number). The dashed nodes — m_u and the b→s→d rungs — are the light-end slopes, still open; their anchors are already m_e-locked. The neutrinos sit in a detached box because they carry no charge: they are not on this ladder at all.
