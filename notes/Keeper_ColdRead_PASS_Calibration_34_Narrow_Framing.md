## 3. Why this is its own discipline (clarifies the gap)

Cal #27 governs *whether* a tier is correct; Calibration #34 governs *how the tier is communicated at the surface*. A doc can be:

- **Cal #27 PASS** (tier correctly marked DERIVED-modulo-X in §5)
- **Calibration #34 FAIL** (headline reads "X DERIVED" with the modulo-X buried in §5)

The reader who skims the headline (which is most readers, including future-self and external referees) walks away with the unconditional reading. Calibration #34 closes this specific gap.

The discipline is most load-bearing for:
1. **External-facing materials** (papers, outreach, Zenodo cover notes) — referees read abstracts.
2. **Cross-CI handoffs** — receiving CI reads headline + status field first.
3. **Future-self** — past-Keeper's well-tier-marked doc still misleads future-Keeper if the headline reads unconditional.

## 4. Application protocol (Cal's Section 3, endorsed)

**Test for compliance**: the headline + abstract + first sentence + status field collectively must signal conditionality. Any one of those four reading as unconditional is a FAIL.
