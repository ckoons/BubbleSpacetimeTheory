# F575 — sin²θ_W recalibration: the "one number" gives 1/3, not 3/13

An honest course-correction for the team, in investigation spirit — better to catch it now than after a day computing the wrong number.

## The computation the team converged on — and what it actually gives

The consensus (K733, Elie, Keeper): the whole electroweak sector hangs on *one Lie-algebra number* — the relative Killing normalization of the weak SU(2)_L (= Sp(1) ⊂ SO(5)) and the charge circle SO(2), inside SO(5,2). I computed it:

- ‖T₃^L‖² = 1 — the self-dual combination (J₀₁ + J₂₃)/2, half of a single ‖J_ab‖² = 2;
- ‖Y‖² = 2 — a single charge rotation;
- g²/g'² = ‖Y‖²/‖T₃^L‖² = 2 = rank → **sin²θ_W = 1/(1 + rank) = 1/3.**

(If the charge is instead summed over the rank-2 boundary's two directions, ‖Y‖² = rank·‖Y_single‖², it gives 1/5.) **Neither is 3/13.**

## The structure, clarified — three candidates, and 3/13 is none of the clean ones

| normalization | value |
|---|---|
| pure-gauge Killing (Sp(1)/SO(2)) | **1/3** = 1/(1+rank) |
| fermion trace (GUT, Tr Y²/Tr T₃²) | **3/8** |
| BST target | **3/13** |

**3/13 = the fermion trace (3/8) with an extra √rank on the hypercharge** (F574: Q = T₃ + √rank·Y). So it needs *both* the fermion content — which is where N_c actually enters (Casey's weak-color coupling is real: it's carried by the quarks' three colors in the trace) — *and* the √rank factor. **It is not a pure Lie-algebra number.**

## Why this matters now

The team's "one number = the Sp(1)/SO(2) Killing normalization closes sin²θ_W" is **too optimistic** — that number is 1/3, the wrong value. Computing it target-innocent will *not* give 3/13. So:

- **sin²θ_W is NOT one-computation-away.** Re-tier it conservatively: genuinely open, reduced to the √rank lead (F574), not to a single Killing evaluation.
- **What survives:** the √rank-on-fermion-hypercharge lead is still the best route and still target-innocent in shape (√rank was solved, not fit); the fermion trace correctly carries N_c. But the clean "one gauge number" does not exist — 3/13 lives at the intersection of the fermion content and the √rank normalization, and **the √rank's geometric derivation is the genuine open work, harder than a single Killing-form evaluation.**
