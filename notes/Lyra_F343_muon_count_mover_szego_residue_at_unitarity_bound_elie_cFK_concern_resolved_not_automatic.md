## "Szegő = 1" is NOT automatic — the electron precedent

F118 says the muon derives modulo the FK Szegő absolute constant = 1. But honesty requires this: the **electron** (F114/F115) carries a *rational O(1)* = **9/16 = N_c²/rank^{n_C−1}** at its BF zero (ν=5/2). So a rational O(1) **does** appear — for the electron. Therefore "Szegő = 1" for the muon is a **claim to verify, not a default.**

The reason it *could* still be 1: the muon sits at the **unitarity bound (ν=3/2)**, a *different* degeneracy-point type than the electron's **BF zero (ν=5/2)**. So the muon residue could genuinely be unity while the electron's is 9/16 — *because* of the different degeneracy type. But that must be **shown**, not assumed (the 0.003% agreement is strong evidence, not proof — the electron-9/16 precedent is exactly why).
