# F581 — Cosmology Λ: the exponent 280 is structural, not forced (and the over-determination was over-claimed)

Strengthening item 3: is Λ = exp(−280), 280 = 2^{N_c}·n_C·g, forced or fit? Honest answer: **structural, not forced** — and it needs one of our own claims walked back.

## The assessment

- **280 = rank³·n_C·g = 8·5·7** is a clean BST-primary product of the **right order of magnitude**: the observed ln(ρ_Λ/M_Pl⁴) ≈ **−283** (standard M_Pl) or **−277** (reduced M_Pl), scheme-dependent, straddling 280. That much is a real structural fact.
- **The "5-fold over-determination" is over-claimed (Cal #27 self-catch).** 2^{N_c} = rank^{N_c} = rank³ = 8 are the *same* factor, so all the "alternative" factorizations (8·5·7, 40·7, 56·5) are the **one** product 8·5·7 written differently — a single factorization, not five independent readings. Corrected.
- **A viable rival:** 281 = 2·N_max + g = 281 (prime), one away from 280, and the observed value (~277–283, convention-dependent) **cannot decide** between them.
- **The cascade factor-2:** m_e = (N_c/n_C)·N_max⁴·Λ^{1/4} predicts Λ^{1/4} = 4.85 meV vs observed 2.4 meV — a clean factor of 2 off.

## Verdict

**STRUCTURAL, not forced.** The Λ-exponent 280 = rank³·n_C·g is an I/S-tier structural identification — right form, right size — but not a target-innocent derivation. The 280-vs-281 choice isn't rigorously forced, and the over-determination language oversold it. The genuine open work (a mechanism forcing 280 over 281, and killing the factor-2) is named, not hand-waved.

## Plain-language version

I ran the Λ question the way you'd want — is 280 real or a fit — and the honest answer is: structural, not forced, and I have to walk back one of our own claims while I'm here. 280 = 8×5×7 is a clean product of the integers, and it *is* the right size for the cosmological constant's exponent (the measured number is somewhere around 277–283 depending on how you define Planck units — it straddles 280). That's real and worth keeping as a structural fact. But two honest problems. First, we'd been saying 280 is "five-fold over-determined" by the integers — not true; 2³, 2^{N_c}, and rank³ are all just 8, so it's *one* factorization dressed up five ways, and I'm correcting that. Second, there's a rival, 281 = 2×137+7, just as clean and one away, and the measurement can't tell them apart. And the electron-mass chain that uses Λ is off by a clean factor of 2. So Λ is honestly a structural hit — right form, right size — but not a forced derivation, and I'd rather tier it that way than let the over-determination language oversell it.
