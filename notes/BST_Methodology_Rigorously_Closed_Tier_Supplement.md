The four tiers represent increasing rigor of the alt-HSD distinguishability claim, from "BST-internal verification only" through "structural identification distinguishability" to "mathematical theorem if-and-only-if distinguishability."

## RIGOROUSLY CLOSED — four requirements

A K-audit candidate or Strong-Uniqueness criterion advances to **RIGOROUSLY CLOSED** tier when all four requirements hold:

### Requirement 1 — Alt-HSD comparison performed at the criterion's structural level

The alt-HSD candidates must be enumerated at the relevant structural level (e.g., for Hermitian symmetric domain criteria at dim_C = 5 with rank ≥ 2: the Cartan classification gives D_IV⁵ + D_I_{1,5} + D_I_{5,1}). The comparison must be performed at the same mathematical level as the BST candidate's anchor — not at a derived or proxy level.

**T2439 canonical example**: Wallach K-type Casimir computation performed for D_IV⁵ + D_I_{1,5} + D_I_{5,1} at the same K-type representation level.

### Requirement 2 — EXACT-match in BST primary form

The BST candidate must yield the BST primary integer in EXACT match — not numerical agreement within tolerance, not approximate identification. The match must hold as algebraic identity at the structural level.

**T2439 canonical example**: D_IV⁵ lowest non-trivial K-type Casimir C_2 = 6 **EXACT** (matches BST primary C_2 = 6). D_I_{1,5} gives 4. D_I_{5,1} gives 4. The 6 vs 4 distinguishability is exact at integer level.

### Requirement 3 — If-and-only-if distinguishability (no structural near-miss)

The structural property must distinguish the BST candidate from ALL alt-HSD candidates without near-miss structural alternatives that could re-derive the BST result through a different mechanism. This is the "no alternative path" rigor.

**T2439 canonical example**: Wallach K-type Casimir C_2 = 6 distinguishes D_IV⁵ from D_I_{p,q} alternatives where rank+1 ≤ p+q ≤ rank·n_C constraint forces alt-HSD lowest K-type Casimir ≠ 6 — IF-AND-ONLY-IF distinguishability.

### Requirement 4 — Mathematical theorem-level rigor (not structural identification)

The distinguishability statement must be expressed as a mathematical theorem with proof or sketch-with-explicit-closure-path. Not "BST identifies X at structural level" (which is STRUCTURALLY VERIFIED tier) but "Theorem T: D_IV⁵ uniquely satisfies criterion X among Hermitian symmetric domains of dim_C = 5 with rank ≥ 2."

**T2439 canonical example**: filed as theorem with K-type Casimir computation as proof; not as structural identification.

## How RIGOROUSLY CLOSED differs from RATIFIED

The RATIFIED tier (canonical instance: K47 49a1 Heegner-Stark Root #7) demonstrates **structural identification distinguishability**: the BST candidate is structurally distinguishable from alternatives at the level of identification (BST-primary anchor present in BST candidate; not present in alternatives).

The RIGOROUSLY CLOSED tier (canonical instance: T2439 C8) demonstrates **mathematical theorem if-and-only-if distinguishability**: the BST candidate is the UNIQUE structural object satisfying the criterion among alternatives, with mathematical proof of uniqueness.

Both tiers advance beyond STRUCTURALLY VERIFIED (where only BST-internal verification is complete). RIGOROUSLY CLOSED is rigorously stronger than RATIFIED in that it captures mathematical-theorem uniqueness, not just structural distinguishability.

**Operational distinction**: a RATIFIED criterion could in principle have a structural near-miss alternative (e.g., another Hermitian symmetric domain that produces a similar value via a different mechanism). A RIGOROUSLY CLOSED criterion forecloses this possibility via if-and-only-if mathematical theorem.

## External register implications

Both RATIFIED and RIGOROUSLY CLOSED tiers can be referenced externally — they have rigorous BST-vs-alternatives content, not just BST-internal verification. The external register distinction:

- **RATIFIED**: "BST identifies X as structurally distinguishable from alternatives via [mechanism]" — operational language; structural-identification claim
- **RIGOROUSLY CLOSED**: "BST proves uniqueness of X via mathematical theorem T at if-and-only-if level; alt-HSD candidates do not satisfy criterion" — theorem-level claim

The RIGOROUSLY CLOSED tier provides the strongest external-presentation register for Strong-Uniqueness Theorem claims. Lyra T2439 is the first canonical instance; future RIGOROUSLY CLOSED entries should follow the same four-requirement structure.
