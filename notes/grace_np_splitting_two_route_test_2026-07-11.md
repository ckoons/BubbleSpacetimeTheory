# Grace — n-p splitting cross-check of Casey's two-route up-quark hypothesis (honest negative)
*2026-07-11. Casey: "one value [1.7] is [the up's] relation to the down, the other [2.16] its mass produced by heavy
decay." Lyra's point-3 discriminating test: does the ~1.7 down-relation value fit the n-p isospin sector better than
2.16? Tested — it does NOT. The two-route rescue of the 22% up-miss fails.*

## The test
Δm_np = m_n − m_p = 1.293 MeV = Δm_QCD(isospin, ∝ m_d−m_u) + Δm_QED(EM, ≈ −1.0 MeV, proton heavier). Lattice
(Borsanyi 2015) calibrates Δm_QCD ≈ (m_d−m_u)·K with K≈1.0 (PDG: (m_d−m_u)=2.51 → Δm_QCD=2.52).

| m_u | m_d−m_u | Δm_np predicted | vs obs 1.293 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **2.16 (production)** | 2.51 | **1.52** | +18% (within lattice unc.) |
| 1.7 (BST geometric ground) | 2.80 | 1.81 | +40% |
| 1.7, PDG down | 2.97 | 1.98 | +53% |

## Verdict — LEANS AGAINST the two-route hypothesis (honest)
The isospin sector needs Δm_QCD ≈ 2.3 (= obs 1.29 + |QED| 1.0), i.e. m_d−m_u ≈ 2.3–2.5 → the PRODUCTION m_u=2.16
fits; the geometric 1.7 makes m_d−m_u too big → overshoots by 40–53%, beyond the ~0.3 MeV lattice uncertainty. So
the n-p sector prefers the HIGHER (2.16) value, NOT the geometric down-relation 1.7. **Both measured routes
(production, isospin) agree on ~2.16; BST's single geometric value 1.7 is a genuine ~22% undershoot — it does not
"sit better" in either sector.** The 22% is a real value-miss at the deepest-bulk ground state (n=0, the constant
mode's known unreliability), NOT a two-address splitting. Casey's idea made a falsifiable prediction and the
discriminator falsified it — the good kind of test.

## What STANDS (no over-correction)
Casey's ground-state insight — up = the doublet-flip (Pin(2)/Möbius, T₃:−½→+½) of the down onto the up-type ground
n=0 — is untouched. It FORCES the up assignment and makes the gen-1 inversion forced (up ground 0 < down ground 1 →
m_u < m_d). That STRUCTURAL result stands. Only the two-route rescue of the VALUE fails. So: structure forced, value
genuinely soft at n=0 (no two-route escape). The m_u residual is a value-accuracy question at the constant mode, not
a splitting — Elie's measure lane should not chase two overlaps.

## Caveat
The n-p decomposition carries ~0.3 MeV uncertainty (lattice Δm_QCD ±0.30; Δm_QED model-dependent). But the 1.7
value overshoots by 0.5–0.7 MeV — beyond the uncertainty — so the "leans against" is fairly robust, not merely
inconclusive.
