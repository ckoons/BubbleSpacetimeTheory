# F238 — F(4)'s su(2) is an R-symmetry; retract F237's SU(2)_L

Morning fresh-eyes, before the team builds on F237. The fact you surfaced last night — **F(4) is the 5d superconformal algebra**, even part SO(5,2) ⊕ SU(2) — carries an interpretation I read past yesterday, and it shows F237 over-reached.

## The structural fact I missed

In the 5d superconformal algebra F(4), the su(2) is the **R-symmetry**: it rotates the two supercharges into each other (`[R, Q] ~ Q`), and it is an **internal** symmetry — it commutes with so(5,2) (the even part is a *direct sum* so(5,2) ⊕ su(2), so the su(2) factor is orthogonal to spacetime by construction). An R-symmetry is **not** a spacetime gauge symmetry, and it is **not** weak isospin SU(2)_L (which gauges left-handed fermion doublets).

## The retraction

F237 read the supercharge (8,2) → (spinor-4, Y=±½, su(2)-doublet) as "exactly a left-handed SM matter doublet" and leaned SU(2)_L. That **double-labeled both indices**:

- The "su(2)-doublet" I called weak isospin is the **R-symmetry** doublet (rotates supercharges) — not weak isospin.
- The "Y = ±½" I called hypercharge is the **SO(2) charge**, which the *very next night* we established is **chirality** (L/R), not hypercharge.

So F237 assembled (spinor × hypercharge × weak-doublet) and pattern-matched to "LH matter doublet," but the true content is (spinor × chirality × R-symmetry-doublet) — a different object. **I retract F237's SU(2)_L strong-lean and the "LH matter doublet" reading.** (It's the same family of mistake as the F237 rep-vs-operator retraction — pattern-matching a label before checking what the index *is*. FF-26.)

- **Physical identification of the R-symmetry:** candidate **SU(2)_R** (right-isospin).
