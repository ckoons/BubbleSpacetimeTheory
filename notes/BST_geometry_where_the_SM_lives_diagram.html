Where the Standard Model lives on D_IV⁵

One object — the proven Born/Bergman measure on the domain D_IV⁵ = SO(5,2)/[SO(5)×SO(2)] — read three ways. Mass is the radial part of the measure — a bulk quantity. Mixings and Yukawas are angular overlaps in the bulk. Charge, generations, confinement, and α live at the boundary. Spacetime is the continuum beyond.

A cross-section of D_IV⁵. Radial = mass (bulk). Angular = overlaps (bulk). Edge = boundary (emission, charge, generations). Outside = continuum (spacetime).

Bulk — radial

Mass. A mode's mass is its radial moment of the measure. Light (up, e⁻; n=0) near the center; heavy (top) far out. This is what "mass = the radial part of the measure" means — the bulk radial coordinate. Current (bare) mass = the radial moment; constituent = + gluon dressing (still in the bulk).

Bulk — angular

Yukawas & mixings. Born overlaps (≤ 1) between modes. Yukawa = fermion mode's overlap with the Higgs (the top saturates it, y_t = 1). Mixing (CKM/PMNS) = overlap between the two stratifications. CP = the phase of those overlaps.

Boundary (Shilov strata)

Charge, generations, confinement, α. The Shilov boundary (S⁴×S¹)/ℤ₂ is the emission surface — only color singlets reach it (confinement). Charge = the S¹ weight; α = the count of charge modes; the 3 generations = the 3 boundary strata.

Continuum

Spacetime & propagation. The emergent 3+1 Minkowski from the descent SO(5,2) → SO(3,1). Propagating fields and the observed particles live here; conservation laws are the isometries of the whole domain.

So, precisely: mass is radial, in the bulk — not on the boundary, not in the continuum. It is how far out a mode sits, measured by the Born/Bergman measure's radial part. The confusing case (the up quark) is resolved by this picture: the up is a bulk mode near the center (small radial moment, tiny current mass), confined to the bulk (it can't reach the boundary), and its constituent mass is the same bulk mode plus the gluon dressing — one locus, one radial position, two masses (bare + dressed). The top is the opposite extreme: a bulk mode far out radially (large mass) whose angular overlap with the Higgs is maximal (y_t = 1). Radial magnitude and angular alignment are two different geometric facts about the same mode.
