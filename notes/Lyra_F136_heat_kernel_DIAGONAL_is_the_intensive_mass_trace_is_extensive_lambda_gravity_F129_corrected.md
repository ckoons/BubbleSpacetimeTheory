## The tension

F129 argued "the heat **trace** uses the Riemannian `dvol`, so the geometric volume is forced." But `Tr(e^-tH) = INTEGRAL K(x,x,t) dvol` is **extensive** (a total over the manifold), while Grace's correction makes the single-particle mass **intensive** (concentration = content / stratum-volume). So a trace coefficient is the **wrong object** for a single-particle mass.

## The resolution: diagonal vs trace

- The heat-kernel **diagonal** `K(x,x,t) ~ (4 pi t)^(-d/2)[1 + a_1(x) t + ...]` -- the **local** coefficients `a_k(x)` are **densities** (per unit volume) = **intensive**. This is the single-particle **mass**.
- The heat **trace** `INT K(x,x,t) dvol = (4 pi t)^(-d/2)[Vol + (INT a_1) t + ...]` -- the **integrated** coefficients are **extensive**: `Lambda` (`INT a_0 ~ Vol`), gravity (`INT a_1 ~ INT R`).

So **one operator `H_B`, two kinds of coefficient**: the local/diagonal density `a_k(x)` at a stratum = the particle **mass** (intensive, Grace); the integrated/trace `INT a_k` = `Lambda` + gravity (extensive, Casey's mole / the global action). The muon's `÷vol(S^4)` is the diagonal being a density **per unit `S^4` volume** = Casey's "per object."
