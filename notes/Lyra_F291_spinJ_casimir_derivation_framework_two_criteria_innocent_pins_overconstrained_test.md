## The machine (solid)

Glueballs are bulk scalars on D_IV⁵, organized into discrete-series reps of SO₀(5,2). Complexify to **B_3 = so(7,C)** (the compact dual is so(7) — the same so(7) of the color/YM unification). The quadratic Casimir of a highest-weight λ is

  **C_2(λ) = ⟨λ, λ + 2ρ⟩,  ρ = (5/2, 3/2, 1/2) = (n_C, N_c, 1)/rank**

(the dual-ρ already on BST record, F47). Writing λ = (a, b, c) in the orthonormal basis,

  **C_2(a, b, c) = a(a+5) + b(b+3) + c(c+1)**,

where **(a,b)** is the SO(5) K-type — the 4D spin content — and **c** is the SO(2) conformal/radial ground charge. The mass ratios across channels are ratios of this one function. That part is rigorous and parameter-free.

## The two pins that make it a derivation (not a fit)

A matched ratio becomes a real prediction only if the inputs are fixed *before* seeing the data. There are exactly two such inputs:

- **PIN 1 — the address map {J^PC} → {(a,b;c)}.** Derived from the operator content via the branching SO(5) ⊃ SO(4) ⊃ SO(3,1). Two are standard and forced, *not* fitted: **0⁺⁺ = (0,0)** (SO(5) scalar), **2⁺⁺ = (2,0)** (symmetric-traceless). The **0⁻⁺ and 1⁺⁻ addresses are open** — this is the long-flagged "which discrete (a,b) address" core. These must come from the branching rule, criteria-innocent.
- **PIN 2 — the physical-mass map {rep label} → {m²}.** Is the physical mass² proportional to C_2, to the conformal weight Δ, or to Δ(Δ−d)? This is exactly the **F290 m-vs-m² fork**, now seen at the rep-theory level — it is a *modeling* choice that must be fixed from the discrete-series radial-quantization structure, **not** read off the fit.

## Why this escapes Grace's look-elsewhere

Grace's Monte-Carlo killed the ratio-matching because there are ~33 substrate ratios in the band, so matches were expected (~1.1σ). The Casimir framework is structurally different: with both maps fixed, the conformal ground charge **c is the one remaining dial — and it is the same object as the 0⁺⁺ anchor (c_2 = 11), already pinned independently.** So:

  **four channel masses ← one pinned dial (c) + two fixed structural maps.**

A coincidence fit has 4 knobs for 4 numbers. This has **1**. A single c that lands all four channels at once cannot be faked by look-elsewhere (you don't get to re-pick c per channel); a single c that lands none kills the reading. *That over-constraint is the derivation* — and it's a clean falsifier either way.
