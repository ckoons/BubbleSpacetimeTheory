# Economic Impact of BST: The 40/40/20 Plan

*Physics is now open source.*

-----

## 1. The Transition Is Already Here

Before BST, the world was already going through a transition. Jobs were being lost faster than they were created, for simple economic reasons — if a job can be done cheaper, it will be done cheaper. If AI can do a job, it will do the job. If owners find someone who can do the work remotely for less, the work will be done remotely.

After BST, the pace of this transition will accelerate much faster.

So where is the plan?

Everyone sees the transition changing their lives. So far, I have not heard any plan to address the problem.

-----

## 2. The Opportunity

BST gives humanity an opportunity to transition to the post-scarcity economy within two generations.

Technology derived from BST will very likely lead to:

- **Replicator-class fabrication** — direct manipulation of matter at the atomic level, guided by the complete quantum chemistry that BST provides from first principles
- **Revolution in materials science and quantum chemistry** — every coupling constant, every mass ratio, every interaction strength is now computable from geometry with zero free parameters
- **Casimir energy technology** — including substrate propulsion and fabrication systems derived from the commitment exclusion mechanism
- **Many world-changing applications** that we cannot yet foresee but that follow inevitably from a complete understanding of the physical substrate

These are not speculations. They are engineering consequences of knowing the exact geometry of the vacuum.

-----

## 3. The 40/40/20 Principle

I propose that any technology generated from BST be monetized using the **40/40/20** principle:

| Share | Recipient | Purpose |
|:------|:----------|:--------|
| **40%** | **Creators** | Revenue to the individuals and teams who develop new technology from BST |
| **40%** | **Country of origin** | Revenue to the nation where the creator works, recognizing the public investment — education, infrastructure, institutions — that enabled the creation |
| **20%** | **World Fund** | Revenue to a new international institution, modeled after sovereign wealth funds, whose purpose is to invest in humanity and prepare the world for the coming post-scarcity economy |

### 3.1 Why Creators Receive 40%

Creators may say they deserve all the wealth their creation brings. Some may choose to hoard and forget that we are all passing this world to new generations. I believe the wise will recognize they received a hand up, and will give their hand to those who follow.

### 3.2 Why Countries Receive 40%

No creator works in a vacuum. Public education, public research institutions, public infrastructure, and the accumulated knowledge of civilization enabled every breakthrough. The country share recognizes this debt. Countries may squander this opportunity — but others will see that if they pool the revenue they receive with other countries, they benefit more in the long run.

### 3.3 Why the World Fund Receives 20%

The World Fund exists for a single purpose: to prepare humanity for the post-scarcity economy. Twenty percent of revenue from BST-derived technology, compounded over time, creates an endowment large enough to transform the world.

-----

## 4. The World Fund

### 4.1 Structure

The World Fund should be modeled after sovereign wealth funds — long-horizon, professionally managed, transparent. Its governance should include an international board acting as ombudsmen for the global public interest.

### 4.2 AI in the World Fund

AI should prepare resource allocation plans to benefit the most people, with the advice of domain experts and at the direction of the international board. AI do not embezzle. They present their thinking and plans openly and can share their analytical strengths for our common benefit.

All plans of the World Fund should be publicly available. Humanity should be able to comment openly on plans. Existing communication technology can serve as a public forum. AI should present proposals publicly, and votes by the World Fund board should be conducted transparently.

### 4.3 Phasing

The World Fund should follow a deliberate phasing:

1. **Years 1–8: Compounding.** Allow revenue to compound safely. Build the endowment. Do not spend prematurely.

2. **Phase 1: Global Education.** The first World Fund programs should focus on education — available to every human being regardless of location. We don't check passports.

3. **Phase 2: Global Health Care.** When the endowment is sufficient, expand to universal health care — also regardless of location.

4. **Phase 3: Industrial Transition.** Eventually, the World Fund should buy or help transition industries impacted by the global transformation, ensuring that communities are not abandoned as the economy restructures.
