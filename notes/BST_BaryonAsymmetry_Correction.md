### 3.2 The First Correction

The next-order contribution comes from five-contact processes — the four-contact box diagram with one additional radiative Bergman contact. The extra contact is a self-energy correction to the S¹ winding mode.

$$Z_{+1} \to Z_{+1}(1 + \alpha + O(\alpha^2))$$

and similarly for Z_{-1}. The asymmetry parameter:

$$\epsilon = \frac{Z_{+1} - Z_{-1}}{Z_{+1} + Z_{-1}}$$

is unchanged at this order (both numerator and denominator scale by the same factor). But the baryon density itself — the numerator — gets corrected:

$$n_B \propto (Z_{+1} - Z_{-1}) \times (1 + \alpha)$$

Since the leading-order formula already has a factor 2 (from the two winding directions), and each direction gets a correction α, the total correction is:

$$\eta = \eta_0 \times (1 + 2\alpha)$$

The factor 2α comes from: **each of the two S¹ orientations receives one radiative contact correction of α**.

### 3.3 Why Not 4α?

One might expect 4α (one correction per vertex). But the four-contact process is a closed box — vertex corrections on opposite sides of the box cancel by CP symmetry. Only the two **external line** corrections (the incoming and outgoing baryon) survive. These correspond to the two winding orientations.
