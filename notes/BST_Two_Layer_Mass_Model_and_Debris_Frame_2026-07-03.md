## 1. The one-sentence idea

**A particle's mass is made in two steps: the substrate hands it a clean *bare* mass, and then — for colored particles only — the particle collects extra energy from the membrane as it escapes into 3D, dressing it up to the fuzzy mass we measure.**

Two layers:
- **Layer 1 — bare mass (the substrate / the engine).** Clean, geometric, one value. This is what we can derive from the five integers.
- **Layer 2 — the dressing (the glueball / the exhaust).** Variable, environment-dependent, a *distribution* not a number. This is the mess we usually measure.

Experimentalists measure Layer 1 + Layer 2 stuck together and call it "the mass." We separate them.

## 2. Why the dressing is variable, and why that's the point

Casey's objection, which is correct: **"glueball + quark" is not a single number.** A confined quark sits in a changing environment; its dressed mass is a smeared band, not a line. So it is a mistake to try to derive it.

The one well-defined value is the **minimum** — the bottom of the band, the least-dressed state. That is the bare mass. So the rule is simple:

> **Derive the minimum (bare) mass. Treat the spread above it (the dressing) as a separate prediction — the glueball distribution.**

## 3. The mechanism: color = the three commitments collected on the way out

Why do only colored particles get dressed? Casey's picture: as a particle **escapes the substrate into 3D, part of the membrane is collected in the three commitments.**

- **N_c = 3 = the three commitments.** A **colored** particle (quark) crosses carrying the three commitments — it drags membrane material out with it. *That collected membrane is the dressing (the glueball).*
- A **color singlet** (lepton) crosses without the three — collects no membrane — stays clean (bare = observed).

So **color is not "a switch that multiplies by N_c" — color *is* the three-commitment membrane-collection during escape.** One mechanism makes both layers: the substrate sets the bare mass, and the *same* escape sets how much membrane dresses it. This answers the open question "what sets the dressing per quark": the amount of membrane collected as it crosses, gated by whether it carries the three commitments.

## 9. The Regime Layer — dressing depends on *when* you crossed (Casey, 2026-07-03)

The dressing (Layer 2) is not a fixed amount. **It is glue the particle *kept* when it crossed the membrane, and how much it kept depends on how thick the membrane was at that epoch.** Thick membrane = hot = lots of energy in the glue = more retained. And it **freezes in**: you carry the mass from the temperature at which you crossed. A neutrino crossing *now* (cold, thin membrane) would be massless — but it crossed at freeze-out (hot, thick), kept a thin film, and that hardened film is its mass forever.

**So the whole mass table reduces to two knobs:**

> **dressing = retention(what you are) × thickness(when you crossed)**
> - **retention(color):** a quark grabs the three commitments (a lot); a color singlet grabs almost none (~10⁻⁷). The top has color but no *time* (decays first) → retention ≈ 0.
> - **thickness(T_freezeout):** membrane thickness = temperature at the epoch the particle froze out. Hotter epoch → thicker → more.

**Across the table:** quarks crossed hot (QCD confinement ~150 MeV) *and* colored → heavy (MeV–GeV). Neutrino crossed cooler (~1 MeV) *and* singlet → 0.05 eV. Top decays before crossing → bare (the control, still holds).

**Elie's Layer-2 number (4552) fits here:** the constituent dressing ≈ **m_p/N_c ≈ 313 MeV** with a hadronization factor f_had, and f_had(top) ≈ 0 (top collects none). Read in the regime frame, m_p/N_c is *retention × thickness at the QCD freeze-out* — the same product, evaluated at the quark epoch. So Layer 2 is now a **prediction** (dressing = (m_p/N_c)·f_had), not a name.

**Analogy:** a bug crossing a freshly-painted wall keeps a smear if the paint is wet (hot epoch), nothing if it's dry (cold). The mass is dried paint from the moment of crossing; how wet the wall was then is written into how much it kept.

## 10. Freeze-depth → volume → coherence (Casey, 2026-07-03)

The freeze-out doesn't only set the *mass* — it sets whether the object has a **volume** at all. **The 3D volume IS the dressing.** An object's internal 3D structure is the glue it collected on the way out:

- **Shallow freeze (hot, colored):** collects lots of membrane → a dressed 3D bag → real internal structure and extent (a hadron, ~10⁻¹⁵ m).
- **Deep freeze (cold, singlet):** collects ~none → a **point** (no measured size; electron < 10⁻¹⁸ m), no internal structure.

So **"elementary (point) vs composite (extended)" is a freeze-depth reading, not a fundamental fiat.** The electron is a point because it froze below the depth where dressing gives a volume; the proton is a bag because it froze shallow and dressed. Consistent all the way down: the *bare* quarks inside, and the *undressed* top, are point-like too — only the *dressed*, confined thing has 3D extent. **Compositeness is a dressing phenomenon.** Same Layer-2 glue, read as *volume* instead of *mass*.

**Why matter is mostly empty** falls straight out: the point electron (frozen deep) and the small dressed nucleus (frozen shallow) are separated by ~5 orders of magnitude (atom ~10⁻¹⁰ m, nucleus ~10⁻¹⁵ m) — so ~10⁻¹⁵ of the volume is "solid," the rest is the electron's orbital reach. From the electron's frame — a dimensionless point — matter is diffuse enough to pass through without collision. "Mostly empty space" is the **freeze-depth gap** between the point electron and the dressed nucleus, seen from inside.

**Coherence = the resonance that holds it.** A bound state (atom, molecule) is a standing wave — the electron's wavefunction resonating with the nucleus's field, binding energy stored in the resonance (the substrate's own eigentone language). **Cooling slows the vibration → lowers the thermal noise fighting the resonance → coherence *strengthens* as it cools.** That's why cold systems bond, crystallize, and condense (superconductors, BECs near absolute zero — the resonances stop being shaken apart). The total energy is "bound into bonds": held in the resonant standing-wave structure, not in free motion.

**The chain, one mechanism:**
