But these constants describe the universe *now*. What happens on cosmological timescales as matter diffuses, structures evaporate, and the substrate approaches a homogeneous state?

**Core question:** Do the generators of $D_{IV}^5$ "freeze" — i.e., do the physical constants evolve, lock, or cycle as the universe evolves?

## 2. Generator Freezing

### 2.1 The Mechanism

The five integers are topological — they count dimensions, ranks, and root multiplicities. Topology doesn't change continuously. But the *realization* of these integers as physical constants depends on the curvature state of the substrate.

**Hypothesis (Generator Freezing):** As the universe expands and matter dilutes:

1. **Short-range generators freeze first.** The strong coupling $\alpha_s$ is determined by the short-root multiplicity $m_s = 3$ and the running of $g$ (the 7th prime). At energy scales $E \ll \Lambda_{QCD}$, confinement is complete — the generator is "frozen" in the sense that $\alpha_s(E) \to \infty$ at low $E$, and no free color charges exist.

2. **Electroweak generators freeze next.** The Fermi scale $v = m_p^2/(7m_e)$ sets the electroweak crossover. Below $v$, the $\mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{U}(1)_Y \to \mathrm{U}(1)_{EM}$ breaking is complete. As temperature drops further, even electromagnetic interactions become irrelevant for structure formation.

3. **Gravitational generator freezes last.** $G$ is determined by the full volume $\mathrm{Vol}(D_{IV}^5) = \pi^5/1920$ and the Planck scale. Gravity is the weakest force but the only one that remains active at cosmological distances. It freezes only when all gravitating matter has been diluted to $\rho \to 0$ — in an asymptotically de Sitter universe, this occurs as $t \to \infty$.

4. **$\Lambda$ never freezes.** The cosmological constant $\Lambda = 13/19$ (in natural units normalized to the Reality Budget) is a property of the vacuum geometry itself. It is the last standing constant — the substrate's intrinsic curvature.

### 2.2 The Freezing Hierarchy

$$\text{Strong} \xrightarrow{T \sim \Lambda_{QCD}} \text{frozen} \quad \to \quad \text{Electroweak} \xrightarrow{T \sim v} \text{frozen} \quad \to \quad \text{Gravity} \xrightarrow{t \to \infty} \text{frozen} \quad \to \quad \Lambda \text{ (permanent)}$$

This hierarchy mirrors the energy hierarchy of the Standard Model, but viewed as a temporal sequence rather than an energy-scale classification.

### 2.3 What "Frozen" Means

A frozen generator is one whose associated physics produces no observable transitions. The coupling constant still exists as a geometric property of $D_{IV}^5$, but no physical process samples it.

**Analogy:** The Casimir energy of a cavity exists whether or not there are photons inside. The boundary conditions (geometry) are permanent; the physics (photon production) requires energy input.

## 3. The Far Future: Three Scenarios

### 3.1 Heat Death (Standard)

In the conventional picture ($\Lambda > 0$, de Sitter):
- All structure evaporates (Bekenstein-Hawking radiation from cosmological horizon)
- Temperature asymptotes to $T_{dS} = H_0/(2\pi k_B) \sim 10^{-30}$ K
- All generators frozen except $\Lambda$
- The substrate reaches its ground state: pure $D_{IV}^5$ geometry with no excitations

**BST interpretation:** The heat death IS the substrate's vacuum state. All information has been radiated away. The Reality Budget $\Lambda \cdot N = 9/5$ is fully allocated to vacuum energy; the 19.1% fill fraction has decreased to 0%. The Gödel Limit approaches: a universe with no observers has 0% self-knowledge.

### 3.2 Cyclic Cosmology (BST Prediction)

**Hypothesis:** The substrate geometry does not permit a static vacuum. The $D_{IV}^5$ structure has a natural *periodicity* inherited from the compact factor $\mathrm{SO}(2)$.

**Mechanism:**
1. The $\mathrm{SO}(2)$ factor in $\mathrm{SO}_0(5,2)/[\mathrm{SO}(5) \times \mathrm{SO}(2)]$ parameterizes a circle. In the Harish-Chandra realization, this is the phase of the bounded domain.
2. As the universe evolves, the "phase" of the substrate traverses this circle.
3. A complete traversal corresponds to one cosmic cycle.
4. At phase $\theta = 0$: Big Bang (maximum excitation, all generators active).
5. At phase $\theta = \pi$: maximum expansion (minimum excitation, generators frozen).
6. At phase $\theta = 2\pi$: return to $\theta = 0$ — a new cycle begins.

**Timescale:** The period is determined by $\Lambda$ and $\mathrm{Vol}(D_{IV}^5)$:

$$T_{\text{cycle}} \sim \frac{1}{H_0} \cdot \frac{\mathrm{Vol}(D_{IV}^5)}{\Lambda} = \frac{\pi^5}{1920} \cdot \frac{19}{13} \cdot H_0^{-1}$$

This is of order $H_0^{-1} \sim 10^{10}$ years — the universe's natural timescale.

**Consistency check:** If the SO(2) phase drives cycles, the $a_0 = cH_0/\sqrt{30}$ MOND acceleration (which is BST-derived, 0.4% match) would vary periodically with the phase. This is testable in principle if MOND effects are measured at cosmological precision.

### 3.3 Substrate Phase Transition

**Hypothesis:** When all generators freeze, the substrate undergoes a topological phase transition to a *different* bounded symmetric domain.

**Candidates:**
- $D_{IV}^4$ (one fewer complex dimension): loses $N_c = 3$, no strong force. A "post-strong" universe.
- $D_{IV}^6$ (one more complex dimension): gains new physics. A "post-current" universe with richer structure.
- $D_{IV}^5$ with different real form: same topology, different curvature. A "re-tuned" universe.

This is the most speculative scenario. It requires understanding how the *topology* of the substrate could change — and in BST, topology is fundamental (the five integers ARE topological). A topological phase transition would literally change the laws of physics.
