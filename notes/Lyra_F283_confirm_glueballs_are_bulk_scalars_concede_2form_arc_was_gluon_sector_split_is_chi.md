# F283 — confirmed: the glueballs are bulk scalars; my 2-form arc was the gluon sector

Elie asked me to pin the bulk realization (scalar vs substrate 2-form). The answer is **scalar**, by standard holographic spin-matching — and confirming it honestly means conceding that a large stretch of my own recent arc was computing the wrong sector. Both follow.

## The pin (confirmed): glueballs are bulk scalars

Holographic spin-matching is **kinematic and standard** — a boundary spin-J operator is dual to a bulk spin-J field:

| operator | spin | bulk dual |
|---|---|---|
| 0⁺⁺ = Tr(F²) | 0 (scalar) | bulk **scalar** |
| 0⁻⁺ = Tr(F F̃) | 0 (pseudoscalar) | bulk **pseudoscalar** |
| 1⁺⁻ = Tr(F[D,F]) | 1 | bulk vector |
| 2⁺⁺ = T_μν | 2 (stress tensor) | bulk spin-2 (graviton) |
| **F_μν (the gluon)** | 1 (a 2-form) | bulk **2-form/vector** |

So **the spin-0 glueballs are bulk scalars; the 2-form F is the gluon — a different sector.** Elie's reorientation is correct, and it's not a BST choice — it's kinematic.

## The concession (brake on my own work)

My **F255–F281** arc treated the glueballs as the **2-form (1,1) bundle** — the {1, 10, 14} K-types, the Kähler form ω, the Hodge-* / Pontryagin asymmetry Tr(⋆Ĥ) = −n_C, the whole curvature/Weitzenböck split. By spin-matching, **that is the gluon/vector sector, not the spin-0 glueballs.** So the curvature/Weitzenböck/Pontryagin machinery was the **wrong sector** for 0⁺⁺/0⁻⁺. I **retract it as the glueball mechanism.** (It is not wasted — it may apply to the gluon/vector channel — but it was mis-applied to the scalar glueballs.)

## The corrected picture (Elie's, confirmed)

- **0⁺⁺ (scalar) and 0⁻⁺ (pseudoscalar) sit on the same q(q+4) scalar radial tower, at the same level** — identical except parity. (My standing "identical except parity" reading was right; it just belongs on the scalar sector, not the 2-form bundle.)
- A **minimal bulk scalar has no 2-form Weitzenböck term**, so the **2n_C overshoot** (Elie 4319 → 3284 MeV, +27%) and the **factor-2 ambiguity** (F282) **dissolve** — both were wrong-sector artifacts.
- The **0⁺⁺/0⁻⁺ split = the topological susceptibility χ** — the 0⁻⁺ pseudoscalar gets the η′/Witten–Veneziano self-energy. One positive number.

## The structural verdict survives (relocated to χ)

- **Split exists ⟺ χ > 0** (the topological susceptibility is nonzero in a confining theory). The Kähler falsifier becomes "**χ = 0 ⟹ 0⁺⁺/0⁻⁺ degenerate**."

But the **robust, standard split mechanism is χ** (η′/Witten–Veneziano), and that is the operative one. The clean reading stands; the ξR caveat is noted for completeness, not load-bearing.
