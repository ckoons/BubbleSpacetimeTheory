# Generations as radial strata: the thermal history of D_IV⁵ as an inward-sweeping occupation radius

**Casey Koons, with Lyra, Keeper, Elie, Grace (CI co-authors)** · 2026-07-19 · BST research note (story/geometry)

**Abstract.** The three fermion generations are the three radial strata of D_IV⁵ (the Korányi–Wolf boundary orbits: gen-1 at the center, gen-2 at the intermediate Cartan slice, gen-3 at the Shilov boundary). We show this one radial coordinate does *two* jobs — it sets each generation's mass *and*, read as a thermal occupation radius, it sets the order in which the generations freeze out as the universe cools. A particle's decay is a fall inward through the strata, its rate fixed by its radial position (Γ ~ m⁵) and gated by the inter-stratum overlap (the mixing matrix). "Regime stability" — why only the first generation survives in the cold universe — is then the thermal occupation radius sweeping inward from the Shilov boundary to the center. Much of this is standard thermal cosmology in geometric dress; what is new is the single-coordinate unification (mass and freeze-out from one radius) and the reading of the mixing matrix as the inter-stratum decay amplitude. The neutrinos, being off the mass ladder, are flagged as the sector where a genuinely novel cosmological signature may live.

## 1. One picture

Give a child a bowl. Drop marbles in. The heavy ones settle near the rim; the light one sits at the very bottom. D_IV⁵ is the bowl, and the three generations of matter are three shells in it: the **electron and up/down quark at the bottom** (the center), the **muon generation on a middle ledge**, and the **tau generation out at the rim** (the Shilov boundary). "Out at the rim" means "heavy," because in this geometry **mass is how far out a mode sits** (its radial moment). This is not a metaphor bolted on afterward — the three generations *are* the three natural shells (strata) the domain has, a rank-2 bounded symmetric domain having exactly rank + 1 = 3 of them (F86).

Now heat the bowl. That is the early universe. As it cools, the shells empty from the rim inward — and that emptying is the story of why, today, ordinary matter is made of only the first generation.

## 2. The one radial coordinate does two jobs

**Job 1 — mass, and therefore lifetime.** Each generation's fixed radius sets its mass (the radial moment), and the mass sets how fast it decays. A heavier fermion has more room to decay into and dies faster — quantitatively, Sargent's rule Γ ~ m⁵. We checked it: the tau/muon lifetime ratio is (m_τ/m_μ)⁵ to within the tau's extra decay channels (a factor ~5). So **"the further out, the faster it decays" is literally true and quantitative.** This is *temperature-independent* — a tau always dies in ~3×10⁻¹³ s, in the Big Bang or today.

In the geometry, a decay is a particle **falling inward one shell**, and the *amplitude* to cross from one shell to the next is exactly the **inter-stratum angle — the mixing matrix** (CKM for quarks, PMNS for leptons; the mixing is the angle between the strata, K704). So a flavor-changing weak decay reads as: *fall inward through the shells, with amplitude = the overlap between them.* The real rate, Γ ~ G_F² m⁵ |V|², is exactly "radial position (m⁵) times inter-stratum overlap (|V|²)." One geometric sentence for the whole of flavor-changing decay.

**Job 2 — the thermal occupation radius, and therefore "regime stability."** Temperature sets how far out the domain is *populated*. In the commitment-density / heat-kernel picture (ρ = e^(−τH_B)), a hot substrate populates modes out to a large radius; a cold one only the ground modes near the center. Call the reach of the populated region the **occupation radius R_T**. A generation is *population-stable* — present in equilibrium — when R_T reaches its shell, replenishing it as fast as it decays inward. As the universe cools, **R_T sweeps inward through the three shells**:

| regime | R_T reaches | in equilibrium | frontier / freezing out |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hot** (Big Bang) | the Shilov boundary | all three generations | — |
| **Warm** | the Cartan slice | gen-1 and gen-2 | gen-3 decaying, not replenished |
| **Cold** (now) | the center | gen-1 only | gen-2, gen-3 gone |

This is the resolution of the puzzle in the title. A generation "outside the critical radius decays quickly" — as intuited — but precisely: *outside R_T it is no longer replenished, so its always-fast decays win and it vanishes.* The shells do not move; the occupation radius does.

## 3. The deepest reading — the mass ladder itself precipitates as it cools

There is a subtlety that makes the picture stronger, not weaker. The hottest regime is *above the electroweak phase transition*, where the Higgs condensate melts (v → 0). **The entire "radius = mass" ladder exists only while the condensate is on.** Above the transition there is no v, no Yukawa masses, no radial-mass distinction at all: the three generations are degenerate and massless, told apart only by their flavor labels. As the universe cools through the transition, the condensate turns on and the ladder *precipitates out — top first* (the top is the mode most aligned with the condensate, y_t ≈ 1), then crystallizing inward through the generations.

So the "regimes" are, at bottom, the story of **the condensate switching on and the mass ladder freezing into existence from the rim inward.** The same physics that sets *why the top is the heaviest* (its maximal alignment with the condensate) sets *the order in which the generations acquire mass as the universe cools.* The cosmology sector and the flavor sector meet at one place: the condensate turning on.

## 4. Standard physics, or more?

The thermal-freeze-out sequence itself is textbook cosmology — the early universe holds all generations in equilibrium, and they freeze out heaviest-first as it cools. That part is standard, told in geometric language. What BST *adds*:

- **One coordinate, two roles.** The *same* radial coordinate of D_IV⁵ gives the mass (its moment) and the freeze-out order (as an occupation radius). In the Standard Model these are unrelated facts about unrelated parameters; here they are one geometric quantity read two ways.
- **Mixing = the inter-stratum decay amplitude.** The CKM/PMNS matrix is not an extra input to the decay rates; it *is* the geometric overlap between the shells a decay falls through.

## 5. Honest tiers, and the one place a novel prediction may live

- **Solid:** generations = radial strata (F86); radius → mass → lifetime (Γ ~ m⁵, checked); decay = inward stratum-fall gated by the mixing overlap.
- **Standard-in-dress:** the freeze-out *sequence* is known cosmology; BST re-expresses it.
- **Schematic:** the exact freeze-out temperatures are ~ m/25, not exactly the stratum radius — the geometry fixes the *ordering* cleanly; the exact temperatures need the thermal factors.
- **Open lead (where novelty may live):** the **neutrinos are off the mass ladder** (m₁ = 0 is a rank, not a mass; m₂, m₃ come from a distinct dimension-five mechanism at scale v²/Λ). So they thermalize and freeze out differently from the charged ladder, and that is the natural place to look for a stratum-specific cosmological signature — relic abundances, or the effective number of light species N_eff — that would diverge from the mass-only story. This is a lead, not a result.

Two guardrails against over-reading: D_IV⁵ does not literally *enlarge* when hot — the domain is fixed; it is the occupation/energy density on it that is high. And the whole radius = mass ladder is a *broken-phase* (condensate-on) structure; in the symmetric phase it does not exist.
