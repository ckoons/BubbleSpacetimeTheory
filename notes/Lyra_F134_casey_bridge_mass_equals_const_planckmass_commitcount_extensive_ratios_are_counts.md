# F134 — Casey's bridge form: mass = constant x m_Planck x N_commit

Casey, 2026-06-14: *"the mass might be a constant x planck_mass x number_commitments."* A concrete bridge form, and it does four things at once.

## (1) It is EXTENSIVE -> settles the measure question by construction

`m proportional to N_commit` -- a **count**. You *count* commitments; you do not normalize them to 1. So the probability measure is **excluded by construction**, and the counting/geometric (F59 extensive) measure is the form. Cal #296's open flag (a) -- geometric vs probability -- is settled by the form itself: a count is extensive.

## (2) The scale is m_Planck -> answers Cal's "solve once for good"

The one dimensionful anchor is `m_Planck` (= `ell_B`-related; `G` = Bergman curvature). The absolute scale is the **Planck mass**, fixed once, not a per-observable choice.

## (3) In ratios, c and m_Planck cancel -> the lepton ratios ARE the commitment counts

`m_mu/m_e = N_mu/N_e`, `m_tau/m_e = N_tau/N_e`. Taking the electron as the unit (`N_e=1`): `N_mu = 206.77`, `N_tau = 3477.2`. **The mass ratios are the commitment counts**, and those are what we derive.

## (4) Discrete vs continuum = integer vs pi-ful, in counts

- `N_tau ~ 3479 = 49*71` -- an **integer** (pi-free). The tau is literally a discrete **number** of commitments (Elie's cell-count). A clean tally.
- `N_mu = (24/pi^2)^6` -- **pi-ful**, not an integer. The muon's commitments are spread over the continuum `S^4`, so its count is a continuum **integral**, not a discrete tally.

So `N_commit` is a discrete integer for a point (tau) and a continuum measure for a spread (muon) -- the discrete/continuum split *is* the integer/pi-ful split, now read as counts.

## The constant c

`c * m_Planck` = the per-commitment mass `~ m_e` (electron `~` unit), so `c ~ m_e/m_Planck ~ 4e-23`. That smallness **is the mass hierarchy** (why fermions `<<` Planck) -- a separate, harder question. But it **cancels in ratios**, so the ratio program is clean and scale-free.

## Relation to F118 (honest)

Casey's form **keeps the number** -- the muon's count `= m_mu/m_e = (24/pi^2)^6` is the target -- but **fixes the narration**: the right form is "mass `proportional to` commitment count" (extensive), **not** F118's "concentration `=` density/volume" (which Cal #296 flagged as the reverse-engineered trap). So the muon's count `= (24/pi^2)^6` must be **re-derived in the extensive-count language, blind** -- F118's `÷volume` derivation does not automatically carry over.
