## The map (standard CKM construction)

The CKM matrix is **V = U_u† U_d**, where U_u, U_d diagonalize the up- and down-type mass matrices. The mixing arises because the mass matrices have **overlap-sourced off-diagonal elements** (~ s_ij·√(m_i m_j), the geometric-mean texture standard for hierarchical fermions). At leading order in small mixing, the off-diagonal V_ij is monotonic in the inter-generation overlap s_ij.

## What I ruled out (self-caught wrong model)

My first guess — "V = the unitary polar factor of the single Gram/overlap matrix" — is **wrong**. The unitary polar factor of a symmetric positive-definite Gram matrix is the **identity** (S = U·P with U=I), giving V=I and no mixing. The toy confirmed it (returned 10¹⁵ garbage from dividing by a near-zero V_13). The CKM is **not** the unitarization of one Gram matrix; it requires the **up–down configuration difference**. Caught and discarded before banking.

## What I verified (correct model)

Hierarchical up/down mass matrices with **multiplicative overlap-sourced off-diagonals** (Grace's s_13 = s_12·s_23 × apex-correction), diagonalized, give

  V_ub/(V_us·V_cb) = O(1), tracking the apex correction (0.66, 0.88, 1.16 for apex_corr = 1.0, 0.6, 0.5).

The ratio is **O(1) and monotonic** in the overlaps — never additive, never divergent. So Grace's "the multiplicative structure survives any monotonic map" is confirmed **with the specific map identified**: V_ij is monotonic in s_ij, hence multiplicative overlaps → multiplicative V → the **Wolfenstein hierarchy**.
