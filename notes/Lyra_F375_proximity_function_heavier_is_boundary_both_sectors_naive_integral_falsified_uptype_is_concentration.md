# F375 — the proximity function: heavier = boundary (both sectors); the naive integral is falsified; up-type = boundary concentration

Continuing the boundary-coupling proximity-function build (the shared object for the up-quark suppressions and the neutrino scale). It produced a clean structural unification + a falsification of a naive expectation.

## The unification: heavier = boundary (both sectors)

| sector | gen-1 (origin, N=1) | gen-3 (boundary, N→0) |
|---|---|---|
| leptons/down-type (d(ν) deposit) | electron — **lightest** | tau — **heaviest** |
| up-type (F373) | up — lightest | top — **heaviest** (y_t=1) |

In **both** sectors, **heavier = closer to the Shilov boundary (smaller domain-norm N).** This unifies the mass-hierarchy *direction* across all four fermion sectors — one geometric statement.

## The falsification (corrects a naive expectation)

The naive "Higgs = constant boundary mode → Yukawa = ⟨1_boundary|w⟩ = N(w)^{p/2}" **increases** with N → maximal at the **origin** (N=1) → would make the origin fermion (electron) the **heaviest.** But the electron (origin) is the **lightest** lepton. So the naive constant-Higgs **boundary-integral is falsified** as the mass law. The Yukawa is *not* the constant-Higgs integral.

## The correct structure (two sectors, both heavier=boundary)

- **Down-type** (leptons + down-quarks): mass = the **bulk deposit density d(ν)**, which grows toward the boundary (|d(0)|=60 at gen-3 boundary vs |d(5/2)|=0 at gen-1 origin) → heavier=boundary. This is the established deposit-locus (F345–F347).
- **Up-type:** mass = the **boundary concentration** — the Poisson/Szegő amplitude of a depth-N state *at* the boundary — maximal for the boundary fermion (top, y_t=1), suppressed in the bulk. This f(N) **decreases** with N (f(0)=1 boundary, f(1)→0 origin), opposite of the falsified integral.

## Why up is steeper (F357 / Grace's frame)

The up-type boundary **concentration** (Poisson-like, sharply peaked at the boundary) is **steeper** than the down-type bulk **deposit** d(ν) — consistent with the up-type being ~90× steeper than the down-type (F373). So the up/down asymmetry = boundary-concentration (up) vs bulk-deposit (down), both heavier=boundary, different sharpness. This is the geometric content of Grace's up=boundary / down=bulk frame, with the *direction* (heavier=boundary) unified and the *sharpness* difference (up steeper) explained.
