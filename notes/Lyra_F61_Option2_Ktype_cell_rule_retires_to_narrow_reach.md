# F61 — Option 2: the K-type cell-rule retires to narrow reach

## 0. Result

**Option 2 retires — and that retirement is Option 4 (narrow reach), a valid substantive outcome.** Tested whether a consistent *derived* K-type → cell-count rule holds across spins (J=1, ½, 3/2). It does not. What holds cleanly is the **bulk n_C + Z₂ bit** rule for the 4 light u/d *ground* states; extending it to the spin-3/2 Δ requires a 2-point fermion ladder at 1.5% — the same over-reach pattern Grace/Elie just retracted. So the substrate cleanly reaches the ground-state mass *scale*, not the excited spectrum. That is substrate-architectural information, not failure.

## 1. The test

|---|---|---|---|---|
| ρ, ω | 1 (boson) | V_(1,0) | 4.98 → **5 = n_C** (0.4%) | ✓ clean |

The clean two — ρ/ω and p/n — are exactly the **bulk n_C + Z₂ bit** rule: boson → n_C, fermion → n_C+1 (the Z₂ twist, T185, derived four ways). That rule is solid and *ground-state only*: it says nothing about a spectrum.

The Δ at ~8 *can* be fit by a fermion λ₁-ladder, cells = n_C + 2λ₁ (V_(½,½)→6, V_(3/2,½)→8). But:
- it is a **2-point fit** (two fermion states → two integers, fits by construction);
- it sits at **1.5%** (loose — base-rate territory for an integer this size);
- it is the **same ladder over-reach** Grace/Elie retracted this morning (the λ₂-ladder), just moved from λ₂ to λ₁. The discipline that killed the λ₂-ladder (Z₂ is a *bit*, not a Z; base-rate; derive-don't-fit) kills this one too.

So **there is no consistent *derived* K-type → cell rule across spins.** Per Option 2's own falsifier ("if no consistent K-type-to-cell rule across spin 1/½/3/2, retire"), Option 2 retires.

## 2. What this confirms: Option 4 (narrow reach), with a sharp framing

Option 2 retiring *is* Option 4: **the substrate cleanly reaches the 4 light u/d ground states** (ρ, ω at n_C; p, n at n_C+1 via the Z₂ bit) — the bulk volume plus the statistics bit — **and does not reach the excited hadron spectrum** with a derived rule. The Δ and the excited states are not substrate cell-counts; they are QCD dynamics.

And here is the framing that makes this a *result*, not a shrug — it's the same boundary the weekend already found for lifetimes:

> **The substrate reaches SCALES, not SPECTRA or RATES.**
> - It sets the ground-state mass *scale*: bulk volume (π^{n_C}) + statistics bit (Z₂). Kinematics. (F52, F57.)
> - It does *not* set the excited spectrum (Δ, resonances): that's QCD dynamics. (This note.)
> - It does *not* set decay rates (lifetimes): that's phase space, Sargent m⁵. (F53, Lead-1 refuted.)

Twice now the substrate picture has hit the *same* boundary: it grounds the kinematic scales (mass = volume + bit) and stops at the dynamics (spectra, rates). That boundary is consistent and informative — it tells us exactly what D_IV⁵ is responsible for (the existence and scale of the ground states) and what the Standard Model dynamics handle (the spectrum above them, the rates between them). The substrate is the *floor*; QCD builds the *building*.
