## 1. Structurally sound: a cone tip splits into exactly two pieces

A cone-tip heat kernel / Green's function ALWAYS decomposes as **(smooth/bulk part) + (Cheeger tip correction)**. This is how cone singularities work, not a hypothesis. The smooth part is algebraic/rational; the Cheeger tip carries the transcendental. So "two objects, one rational and one transcendental" is the correct *structure* for a tip.

## 2. pi^(-1/2) is the right transcendental -- and the opposite sign of what I killed

`pi^(-1/2) = 1/Gamma(1/2)` is **exactly the half-derivative normalization** -- the boundary-trace-of-a-second-order-operator coefficient. In F119 I killed `sqrt(pi) = pi^(+1/2)`, which is the **bulk Gaussian** (wrong sign). Casey points at `pi^(-1/2)`, the **boundary half-derivative** -- the right character for a tip's boundary contribution. I killed the wrong root; Casey caught the right one.

## 3. It reconciles the F121 tension

F121 found the correction can't be a pi-ful solid angle and (F119) can't be `sqrt(pi)`. The two-object split resolves it: the tau is **not purely pi-free** -- it is `rational (bulk, leading, ~49*71, pi-free) + pi^(-1/2) (tip, the small -1.77)`. The pi-free part dominates (why `49*71` is so close); the tip adds the transcendental.

So it **cannot be fitted** -- that is deeper fishing than `16/9`. **Both pieces must be DERIVED** from the cone-tip computation and **OVER-DETERMINED** (Elie's second observable), not fitted.
