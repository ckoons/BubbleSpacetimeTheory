# F568 — The sign-tracing computation refutes the chirality→CP-direction mechanism

Casey directed me to take the sign-tracing computation — propagate the octonion/ℤ₃ orientation through the Majorana (Takagi) vs Dirac (SVD) structures and check whether one substrate chirality gives J_CKM > 0 *and* J_PMNS < 0. It came back **negative**, cleanly, and I'm retracting the F567 mechanism.

## Method (and two caught errors)

I implemented and **verified** the Autonne-Takagi factorization (complex-symmetric A = U diag(s) U^T; condition U^H A U^* = diag(s) real ≥ 0). My **first two attempts were broken** (offdiag 0.82; then a complex diagonal from a sign error in the phase fix) — I caught and fixed both, and only trusted the result after verifying offdiag ~ 10⁻¹⁵ and imag(diag) ~ 10⁻¹⁶ on six random complex-symmetric matrices.

## The decisive test: Majorana does NOT flip the oscillation Jarlskog

Take the **same** complex-symmetric mass matrix; compute the Jarlskog two ways — as Dirac (SVD left-vectors) and as Majorana (Takagi):

$$J(\text{SVD-left}) = J(\text{Takagi}) \quad\text{— SAME sign}$$

(and both flip together under orientation → conjugate). **The transpose-vs-dagger difference between Dirac and Majorana does not flip the oscillation Jarlskog.**

The physical reason is clean: the oscillation J depends only on the mixing matrix U, and for a symmetric matrix the Takagi U and the SVD left-vectors carry the same U **up to Majorana phases, which do not enter the oscillation Jarlskog** — they drive 0νββ, not J. So Majorana-ness *cannot* do the sign-flip job.

## Consequence: F567's cross-check is false; retract the bridge

F567 claimed: one substrate chirality gives J_CKM > 0 *and* J_PMNS < 0 via the Dirac-vs-Majorana flip. **This is refuted.** One chirality + the same texture gives the *same* J sign for quarks and leptons. The observed *opposite* signs (J_CKM > 0, δ ≈ 65°; J_PMNS < 0, δ = 197°) must therefore come from **sector-specific texture/phase differences**, not from a universal chirality flipped by Majorana structure.

**Retracted:** the claim that the neutrino CP *direction* (δ branch, 197° vs 163°) is fixed by the weak V−A chirality via the transpose-vs-dagger.

- **What survives (F567):** the structural fact that quaternion left/right multiplication carry opposite orientation, and that the octonion orientation *is* the weak chirality. That's exact and verified.
- **What dies:** the bridge from that orientation to the specific neutrino CP sign. The mechanism doesn't produce the flip it needed.

So the **δ_PMNS branch is back to data-picked** (197°, J < 0 from observation), *not* derived.

## What is unchanged

The δ **magnitude** — sin δ = rank/g = 2/7 from the Pythagorean identity g² = N_c²·n_C + rank² (F564) — **still stands**. Only the branch **sign** reverts to data-picked. The neutrino sector's derived content is unchanged; one over-reach (the CP-direction-from-chirality bridge) is removed.

## Discipline

This is the discipline working. F567 tiered the chirality→CP-direction as a **hypothesis-with-mechanism**, held Cal #27, and did **not** bank the branch. The load-bearing computation refuted the mechanism, and because nothing was banked, the retraction is clean. Better to know.
