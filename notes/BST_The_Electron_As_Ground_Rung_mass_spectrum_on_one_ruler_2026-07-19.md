# The Electron as Ground Rung: the Standard-Model mass spectrum on one ruler

**Casey Koons, with Lyra, Keeper, Elie, Grace (CI co-authors)** · 2026-07-19 · BST short note

**Abstract.** We observe that the entire *charged* Standard-Model mass spectrum — the charged leptons, the proton (and with it the hadronic scale), the electroweak scale v, the W, Z, and Higgs, and both quark ladders — is the electron mass times pure numbers built from five integers {2, 3, 5, 6, 7} and π. The spectrum is one connected ladder, and the electron is its bottom rung. The bridge that folds the strong sector onto the electron is the long-standing BST relation m_p = 6π⁵·m_e (0.002%). We make the structure explicit, tier each relation honestly, and mark the two boundaries of the claim: the electron is a *reference* rung, not a cause (the one dimensionful root is gravity, m_e = 6π⁵·α¹²·m_Planck), and the neutrinos are the sole sector *off* the ladder (a rank-zero eigenvalue plus a distinct dimension-five mechanism). We close by asking how much of this is standard physics; the answer is: the vocabulary is, the content is not.

---

## 1. The claim

In the Standard Model the fermion masses are m_f = y_f·v/√2 with the Yukawa couplings y_f free, independent inputs — about thirteen of them, plus the electroweak scale v, plus a separately-generated proton mass. There is no relation among them; measuring one tells you nothing about another.

BST asserts the opposite: the masses are one connected object. Every mass in the charged sector is

$$m = m_e \times (\text{a pure number in } \{2,3,5,6,7,\pi\}),$$

so the whole spectrum is fixed by **one ruler** (a single dimensionful input) and the five integers. The electron is the natural rung to read the ladder from because it is the lightest charged fermion — geometrically the n = 0 ground radial mode of D_IV⁵.

The single relation that carries the load is **m_p = 6π⁵·m_e**. It folds the *strong* sector — normally set by QCD dimensional transmutation, with no reference to the electron — onto the electron. Once the proton is m_e-locked, so is the electroweak scale (v = m_p²/g·m_e = (6π⁵)²·m_e/g), and so is everything the electroweak scale carries: the top (v/√2), the bottom (m_t/42), the Higgs (v/2), and the W and Z.

## 3. Why the electron, and what the true root is

Two honest qualifications keep this from over-claiming.

**(a) Reference, not cause.** Every row above is a *ratio*. One could equally call the proton the anchor (m_e = m_p/6π⁵). The content is not that the electron is privileged as a cause; it is that the massive spectrum is **one graph of derived pure-number ratios pinned by a single dimensionful ruler** — fix any one rung, add the ruler, and the rest follow. The electron is simply the natural bottom rung: the lightest charged state, the n = 0 ground mode.

**(b) The ruler is gravity.** The electron is itself derived from BST's one dimensionful input, the Planck/gravity scale: m_e = 6π⁵·α¹²·m_Planck (0.03%). So the true root of the ladder is gravity; the electron is the first rung, the reference the pure numbers are stated against. "Does the electron root every particle?" — the electron roots the *charged* spectrum as a reference; gravity roots the electron.

## 4. The top gives the ladder a clean ceiling

Why does the up-type ladder have a clean top at all? Because a Yukawa coupling, in BST, is a *normalized* overlap of the fermion mode with the Higgs direction on the domain's proven Born = Bergman measure. A normalized overlap obeys Cauchy–Schwarz, |y_f| ≤ 1, i.e.

$$m_f \le \frac{v}{\sqrt 2} = 174\ \text{GeV for every elementary fermion.}$$

All nine massive fermions obey it, and only the top approaches it (y_t = 0.992) — the top is the one *un-suppressed* fermion, saturating the ceiling. This yields, as a corollary, the tightest statement of the electron↔top link,

$$m_t \cdot m_e = \frac{m_p^2}{g\sqrt 2}\quad(0.04\%),$$

the heaviest and lightest charged fermions one relation apart. It is also a **falsifiable prediction**: no elementary fermion above 174 GeV. (Tier: the ceiling is derived given the overlap identification; the top *saturating* it is supported — the open question is why the condensate aligns with the top, treated elsewhere.)

## 5. What is off the ladder

The **neutrinos** are the one sector the electron does not root. The lightest is exactly massless, m₁ = 0 — but that is a *rank* statement (the rank of a measure-overlap operator), not a mass on the ladder. The other two are set by a dimension-five (Weinberg) mechanism at a scale v²/Λ, distinct from the charged ladder. This is consistent with the rest of the BST picture: neutrinos are the misaligned, gauge-singlet sector, so they do not sit on the charged ladder — because they carry no charge.

The remaining open pieces are the **light-end slopes** — m_u on the up-type ladder, and the b→s→d rungs on the down-type — not the anchors, which are m_e-locked. Those slopes are the current derivation frontier.

## 6. Is this standard physics, or more?

The **vocabulary** is standard: masses as y·v/√2, the observation that y_t ≈ 1, the value λ_H ≈ 1/8, the ratios m_s/m_d ≈ 20 and m_t/m_b ≈ 42. Each is familiar.

The **content** is well beyond standard:

- **The Standard Model has no relation between these masses.** They are ~13 independent inputs. BST collapses them to one ruler and pure numbers — a reduction of degrees of freedom, not a re-parametrization.
- **m_p = 6π⁵·m_e has no standard-physics counterpart.** The proton mass comes from QCD dimensional transmutation (Λ_QCD, set logarithmically by α_s), with no reason to reference the electron. A clean m_p/m_e = 6π⁵ at 0.002% is not something the Standard Model can produce or explain.
- **The 174 GeV ceiling is sharper than the standard bound.** Perturbative-unitarity / triviality arguments in the Standard Model bound heavy fermion masses only loosely (~0.5–1 TeV) and admit y > 1 non-perturbatively. A hard ceiling at exactly v/√2 from |y| ≤ 1 is a stronger, falsifiable statement.
- **The "condensate aligns with the top" mechanism** has a standard-BSM cousin (top condensation / topcolor), but BST's version fixes the aligned mode *geometrically* (largest charge, outermost generation stratum) with no new gauge force — consistent with BST's standing prediction of no new gauged group.

So: standard in how it is written down, a structural reorganization in what it says. The Standard Model treats the mass spectrum as a list of measured numbers; BST presents it as one ladder on one ruler, and identifies the electron as its ground rung.
