# Mass Is an Eigenfrequency; a Particle Is a Coherent Eigentone
### Internal frame (Casey + Keeper, 2026-07-04). Plain, for our use. Reconnects to Lyra's Quasi-Eigentone Framework.

## The one idea

> **A particle is a resonance. Its mass is an *eigenfrequency* of the substrate cavity — a value that fits one of a discrete series of "keys." An assembly manifests into 3D only if it hits an eigenfrequency exactly and achieves coherence; too big or too small doesn't resonate, so it's reconstituted and tried again. Masses are *selected*, not built.**

Coherence = stability. A mode that fits the keyhole perfectly is a **stable particle**; a mode that nearly fits is a **decaying resonance** (recycled). "Particle" = coherent (true) eigentone.

## This is already BST's frame — reconnection

Lyra's **Quasi-Eigentone Framework** (v0.2) has exactly this machinery:
- **TRUE eigentone** = fits perfectly → coherent → **stable.** The corpus lists *"electron | gen-1 | trivial-channel | TRUE eigentone (lowest)"* — the electron is the lowest true eigentone, the ground note.
- **QUASI eigentone** = excited, doesn't quite fit → **decays** (mechanism: quasi-eigentone overlap × kernel-integral × phase-space).
- **Discrete spectrum:** Grace's *"primaries are the discrete series spectrum."*

So this frame isn't new — it's the instrument BST already had. Casey's contribution is telling us **what it means** (coherence = existence) and **fixing the labeling** (below).

## Casey's refinement — it's FREQUENCY, not GENERATION

The framework mapped *channels → generations*, treating "generation" as fundamental. **Flip it:** the fundamental thing is the **eigenfrequency**; "generation" is just our name for where a mode sits on the frequency ladder. m_d, m_s, m_b are not three generations needing a mass-ratio mechanism — they are **three eigenfrequencies of one resonant structure.** "Generation" is human bookkeeping of the spectrum.

## Why this dissolves the down-ladder impasse (the key payoff)

We spent days trying to build m_s/m_d = 20 as a **product** — d(ν) × k_s × (color? volume? 2·C_2?). Every factor was blind (cancelled) or short. **That was the wrong operation.** Eigenfrequencies of a cavity are **roots of a spectral equation** (Bessel zeros, Laplacian eigenvalues) — they are NOT related by clean multiplicative factors. **20 and 900 wouldn't decompose because they're SPECTRAL, not MULTIPLICATIVE.** Lyra's d(ν)×k_s "short by 12" wasn't a missing factor — it was multiplication applied to a spectrum. You don't multiply your way to an overtone; you solve for where the standing wave fits.

## The falsifier — already measured, twice

The quasi-eigentones (excited, decaying) **are the hadron resonances** — the hundreds of short-lived states that formed, failed coherence, and decayed (~10⁻²³ s). Stable masses = true eigentones; resonances = quasi. **The Particle Data Group tables are the recycling bin, and the stable masses should be the notches the resonances cluster around.** That's a real, already-measured check.

## The reframed target (supersedes the factor-hunt)

**Stop hunting for the factor that builds the mass. Find the keyhole operator — the substrate cavity — and compute its eigenfrequency spectrum.** The masses ARE the spectrum:
1. Write the resonance operator (the substrate cavity + boundary; Lyra's quasi-eigentone kernel is the starting object).
2. Compute its eigenfrequencies. Check {1, 20, 900} (down), the leptons ((24/π²)⁶, 49·71 — do they fall out as eigenvalues?), etc.
3. The near-misses (quasi-eigentones) should be the hadron resonances clustered around the stable eigenfrequencies.

**Un-fishable:** the spectrum is what it is — you compute the eigenvalues, you don't fit them. The generation-dependence we couldn't source from a blind factor is *automatic* here — different generations are different eigenvalues of the same operator, and eigenvalues need no multiplicative relation.
