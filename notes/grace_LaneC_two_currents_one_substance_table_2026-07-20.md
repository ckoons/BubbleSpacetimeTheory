## The frame — one substance, two currents (two Poincaré Casimirs)
A relativistic particle is labelled by the two Casimirs of the Poincaré group: **P² = m²** (mass) and **W² = −m²s(s+1)** (spin, via Pauli–Lubanski). Casey's reading: the substrate condensate is ONE substance, and these are its two **currents** — least action on each:
- **P² — the linear current → mass/inertia.** Spin-blind (a scalar). This is the **Higgs channel**: O = SO(5) vector (1,0), spherical, sets the pitch v (F603, drum-skin tension). Mass = the radial/dilation moment.
- **W² — the angular current → spin.** Spin-entangled. Casey's candidate: the **QCD/strong channel** — the chiral condensate ⟨q̄q⟩, color-carrying, coupling to the spin sector.

**Reading:** the Higgs (mass) current lives on the **SO(2)/conformal-weight** direction — the spin-blind dilation that sets the scale; the QCD (spin) current lives on the **SO(5) compact** direction — the internal rotations that carry color and couple to spin. ## ★ The guard (Cal) — held prominent
**"QCD = W² angular" is a REACH, not a derivation:**
1. The QCD chiral condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ is a **Lorentz scalar (spin-0)** — it does NOT carry angular momentum. So "QCD current = W² (the spin Casimir)" cannot be literal at the condensate level.
2. Spin-dependence in QCD (hyperfine splittings, the Δ–N gap, spin-orbit) is **dynamical** — it comes from gluon exchange, not from a Casimir label on the condensate.
3. What IS defensible: the QCD condensate is a **different SO(5) rep** than the Higgs vector (it carries color N_c, it's non-spherical) — that part is structural and checkable. The identification of that rep with "the W² current" is the LEAD/reach.

So the honest statement: **two condensates = two different SO(5) reps of one K (defensible, checkable)**; the **P²/W² current labelling is Casey's organizing frame (LEAD)**; the literal "QCD carries the spin Casimir" is a reach flagged for Cal.
