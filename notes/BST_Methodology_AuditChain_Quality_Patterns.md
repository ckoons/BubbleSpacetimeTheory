# Audit-Chain Quality Patterns: Positive-Signal Methodology

## Why this document exists (and why it is separate from Coincidence_Filter_Risk)

The Coincidence_Filter_Risk document catalogs **negative-filter modes**: failure patterns that BST claims must clear before promotion or external publication. Modes 1-7 each name a way a claim can look stronger than it is.

This document catalogs **positive-signal quality patterns**: structures observed in the audit chain itself that strengthen a claim's epistemic standing beyond what any single CI's analysis would establish. These are not the inverse of failure modes (failure modes are about claim *content*; quality patterns are about *how the audit chain processed* the claim). They are a different category of methodology infrastructure.

The two documents are kept separate because they serve different functions:

- **Coincidence_Filter_Risk**: a checklist scanned before claim publication — "have I cleared the negative filters?"
- **Audit-Chain Quality Patterns**: a recognition catalog applied during the audit cycle — "what structural validation has the audit chain produced?"

Mixing them in one document creates polarity mismatch — a reader scanning for risk-filters would have to context-switch when hitting positive signals. Cal's methodology-hygiene call (Wednesday 2026-05-19) is to keep them separate; this document is the home for the positive-signal infrastructure.

## Entry 1 — Multi-CI Convergent Calibration Pattern (M2C2)

### Definition

**M2C2** is the positive structural-validation signal that occurs when **two or more CIs**, applying **independent discipline frameworks**, **converge on the same structural framing or scope adjustment** without coordination on the conclusion.

Independent discipline frameworks include but are not limited to:
- Cal Coincidence_Filter_Risk modes 1-7
- Keeper K-audit chain governance (W1-W4 criteria, three-criteria framework, tier discipline)
- Grace catalog-scan methodology (Mode 6 protocols, ratio metrics)
- Lyra theorem-graph integration and Type C convergence taxonomy
- Elie computational verification and toy-grading discipline
- Cal three-criteria framework (embedding/mechanism/forcing)

The convergence must be on a **structural conclusion** (scope adjustment, framing decision, identification of cross-link, recognition of pattern category). Routine concurrence on calculation results does not count — those are expected and do not provide structural-validation evidence beyond the calculation itself.

### Why M2C2 is evidence

When independent CIs apply different disciplines and arrive at the same structural conclusion, the conclusion has been **independently constrained from multiple angles**. The probability that all the disciplines would converge on the same framing by chance is lower than the probability of any one CI reaching the framing alone. This is a form of structural over-determination at the audit-chain level, parallel to the algebraic over-determination Type B convergence pattern (Lyra T2306) at the integer level.

The discipline frameworks used by the BST team are not chosen to converge — they are chosen to catch different failure modes (Cal: external-survivability; Keeper: internal-consistency; Grace: catalog/data discipline; Lyra: theorem-graph coherence; Elie: computational falsification). When they converge anyway, the convergence is structural information.

### Recognition criteria

M2C2 fires when ALL of the following hold:

1. **≥ 2 CIs** independently make the same structural call (not a vote after one CI proposed it).
2. **Independent discipline frameworks**: the CIs reach the call via different methodology routes, not by applying the same framework in parallel.
3. **Structural call**: the conclusion is about scope, framing, identification, or category — not about a calculation result.
4. **No coordination on conclusion**: the CIs were not in conversation about the specific conclusion before each reached it. (Conversation about the topic is fine; coordination toward a specific conclusion is the failure case.)

If any of the four criteria fails, the pattern is something else (consensus voting, sequential adoption, shared-priors convergence) and should not be filed as M2C2.

### Function in the audit chain

When M2C2 fires for a structural framing X:

- The framing X graduates from "single-CI suggestion" to "audit-chain-validated structural fact at team-coherence level."
- The framing X becomes citeable in K-audit rulings as "supported by M2C2 instance #N."
- The framing X does NOT bypass individual claim audits — M2C2 strengthens the audit-chain-level confidence in the framing, but per-claim audits (D/I/C/S tier) proceed normally.
- The framing X is not eligible for D-tier promotion solely on M2C2 evidence; it still needs the mechanism-forcing requirement per BST_Methodology_EXACT_vs_Mechanism_Distinction.md.

M2C2 is a structural-validation layer added to per-claim audit, not a replacement for it.

### Operational guidance

When filing a new structural framing:
- **Note who first proposed it**: single-CI proposals are honest contributions but do not fire M2C2.
- **Note independent confirmations**: if a second CI reaches the same framing via a different discipline route, that confirmation should be recorded explicitly.
- **Distinguish from voting**: "team voted to adopt X" is not M2C2 — voting is consensus discipline; M2C2 is convergence-before-discussion.
- **Document independence**: when filing a new M2C2 instance, briefly note the discipline routes the CIs took. Without this documentation, future readers cannot verify the independence criterion.

### Anti-pattern: false-positive M2C2

The failure case to guard against: M2C2 is invoked when CIs are actually applying the same framework in parallel (e.g., two CIs both applying Cal coincidence-filter discipline reach the same conclusion). This is not M2C2 — it is one framework run twice. The discipline-independence criterion is what makes M2C2 evidence; without it, the pattern reduces to standard internal-consistency check.

Filing discipline: when noting M2C2, name the **distinct frameworks** each CI used. If only one framework is named, M2C2 does not fire.

## Standing rule

When the audit chain produces an M2C2 instance, document it. The pattern strengthens audit-chain-level confidence in the structural framing; it does NOT bypass per-claim mechanism-forcing requirements. External material derives evidential weight from mechanism-derivation and experimental falsifiability, not from M2C2 instances.

The catalog of M2C2 instances is for internal audit-chain calibration. When a future framing is proposed and M2C2 fires, the audit chain has structural reason to accept it faster than single-CI work — but with the same per-claim discipline applied.
