## The "open in math" framing assumed the wrong construction

"No interacting 4D QFT has ever been constructed" is a statement about **constructing the theory from scratch on
flat R⁴** — building the measure, proving the axioms, the famous open problem. **BST does not do that.** The
Osterwalder-Schrader theorem *reconstructs* a QFT from a fixed data set; you don't build it from the path integral.
So the real question is: **does the D_IV⁵ spectral theory already carry the OS data?**

A *generalized-free* field also satisfies the OS axioms and *also* has a gap. So having the OS data gives a
rigorous gapped 4D QFT, but not necessarily an *interacting* one. The honest crux:

> **Is the D_IV⁵ spectral theory interacting Yang-Mills, or a rigorous-but-trivial generalized-free gapped
> theory?**

Distinguishing them = non-Gaussian connected correlators = the full glueball spectrum + couplings matching SU(3)
Yang-Mills. **That is exactly W2** (the spectrum/correlator match to completeness — what Grace+Elie are building).
