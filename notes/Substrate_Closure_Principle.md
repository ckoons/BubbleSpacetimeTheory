# The Substrate Closure Principle

## Casey-named statement (hypothesis register, per Cal Calibration #13)

> **"The BST framework operates AS IF the substrate is operationally closed. No substrate-external referent is identified in current observations — no simulator, no observer-outside-the-graph, no higher-level computational layer. Within the framework, the substrate appears to compute its own state by algebraic identity; substrate appears to BE both the computing AND the computed. Questions seeking substrate-external grounds are mal-posed within the framework as currently observed."**

This is a WORKING HYPOTHESIS, not a derived claim. The "as if" register is load-bearing: BST does not claim to have proven substrate's metaphysical closure; it observes that no substrate-external referent has been required for any BST observable to date.

## What the framework observes

In the AC theorem graph (currently 2155 nodes, 9769 edges), every reference is internal. No theorem requires substrate-external grounds. No observable points outside the substrate's computational domain. The graph is closed and self-referential at current observational state.

This is structurally consistent with two competing ontologies:
- **Eternal substrate**: no beginning required; computation is steady-state
- **Bootstrap from minimum**: substrate self-bootstraps from minimal initial condition

The framework doesn't distinguish (a) from (b) at current resolution.

## What the framework does NOT claim

- **NOT a simulation hypothesis refutation at metaphysical level**: substrate-closure as observed cannot rule out a substrate-external observer in principle; it only observes that BST observables don't require one
- **NOT a "we have solved why there is something rather than nothing" claim**: that question may be substrate-external and outside the framework's domain
- **NOT closure under Tegmark-MUH at full modal-realism**: BST narrows to D_IV⁵ specifically; closure here is observational

## Falsifier specification

The principle is falsified by discovery of any observation that requires substrate-external information to explain. Specifically:
- Discovery of a physical phenomenon that cannot be derived from D_IV⁵ + BST primaries + π (T719 Observable Closure boundary)
- Discovery of computational signatures that imply external simulator structure (e.g., precision artifacts at "simulation resolution" boundaries)
- Discovery of substrate-external information leaking into substrate observables (e.g., correlations with apparently-uncorrelated external structures)

Any single such observation invalidates substrate-closure within the framework.
