# F156 — Casey principle candidate #12: interior is discrete, the boundary residue is a curvature at a specific root of π

Casey: *"the interior of D_IV⁵ is discrete and we can linearize everything up to the boundary/edge, and when we look closely at the residue it's nearly always a curvature we can characterize by the specific root of π."*

This is the **geometric mechanism** under everything we've been finding — the trichotomy, the two-tier floor, F143's locus rule, and the tau's √π residue. Formalizing it as a named-principle candidate.

## Statement

> Every BST observable decomposes as
> **(a) a finite DISCRETE LINEARIZATION** — the **interior** content of `D_IV⁵`: π-**free**, exact / Tier-1-eligible, the integer/rational count — **plus**
> **(b) a BOUNDARY CURVATURE RESIDUE** — characterized by a **specific root of π** set by the boundary stratum the observable's trajectory touches.

The interior linearizes (it's discrete — the cell lattice, the K-types, the integer counts). The curvature is exactly what you *can't* linearize (Casey's Curvature Principle), and it surfaces as the boundary residue, carrying a power `π^{k/2}` fixed by the geometry.

## The gradient (interior → boundary)

| observable class | locus | content | root of π |
|---|---|---|---|
| **pure interior** (mixing angles, θ_QCD) | interior / discrete | fully discrete, **exact** | π⁰ (π-free) |
| **vertex / near-interior** (tau) | 0-dim cone tip | discrete-dominated `49·71` + small residue | **√π = π^{1/2}** (odd-Peirce / minimal curvature) |
| **non-compact boundary** (electron) | spectral strip | π-free (no closed curvature, χ-trivial) | π⁰ |
| **compact boundary** (muon) | Shilov S⁴ | curvature-**dominated** `(24/π²)⁶` | **π²** (vol S⁴ = 8π²/3) |

So an observable's position on the interior→boundary gradient — which stratum its trajectory touches — fixes both its discrete-vs-curvature content *and* its specific root of π.

## The root of π is characterizable by the stratum dimension

`vol(S^n) = 2π^{(n+1)/2}/Γ((n+1)/2)`, so the net π-power is set by `n`:
- **S⁴** (muon) → `π²` (`vol = 8π²/3`);
- **0-dim vertex** (tau) → the bare `Γ(1/2) = √π`;
- **non-compact** (electron strip) → `π⁰`.

That's Casey's "specific root of π we can characterize" — it's the π-content of the boundary stratum's volume/measure.

## Falsifiable

1. A **pure-interior** observable claimed forced must be **exactly π-free** — any π in it falsifies the interior classification.
2. A **boundary residue** must carry the **root of π matching its stratum dimension** — a mismatched root (e.g., a √π where the stratum demands π², or vice versa) falsifies.

## Why this matters now — the count strategy

This is the **mechanism** under the F149/F150 mixing-angle pivot, not just an observation:
- The **count moves on the INTERIOR-discrete part** — forced, π-free, exact/structural.
- **Mixing angles are interior** (counting) → **π-free → exact-eligible → the cleanest count-movers.** That's *why* they're the right target.
- **Masses are boundary-touching** → forced discrete linearization **+** a characterized (root-of-π) residue, **noted open** (the tau pattern, F155). That's *why* masses are structural-tier with residues.

So the principle tells us, for any of the 26: is it interior (forceable, exact) or boundary (linearization + characterized residue)? That's a map of which parameters are clean count-movers and which carry curvature.

## Rep-theory backing

- **Interior** = the discrete series / K-type lattice → linearizable, π-free (the discrete spectrum).
- **Boundary** = the Plancherel measure / c-function / boundary volumes → π-ful (the continuous measure carries π).
- **Residue** = the touched boundary stratum's volume π-power.
