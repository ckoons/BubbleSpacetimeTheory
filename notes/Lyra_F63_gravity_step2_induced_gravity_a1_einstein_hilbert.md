## 1. The mechanism (standard heat-kernel induced gravity)

The effective action of a heat semigroup is W = −log Tr(e^{−τH_B/ℏ_BST}), and its small-τ (Seeley-DeWitt) expansion is, on a d-dimensional space,
$$\mathrm{Tr}(e^{-\tau H_B/\hbar}) \sim (4\pi\tau)^{-d/2}\Big(a_0 + a_1\tau + a_2\tau^2 + \cdots\Big),$$
where the coefficients are **integrals of curvature invariants**:
- **a₀ = ∫√g** — the volume / cosmological term (Λ);
- **a₁ = (1/6)∫ R √g** — *the Einstein-Hilbert action*;
- a₂ = ∫(αR² + βR_{μν}² + …) — higher-curvature corrections.

This is **Sakharov's induced gravity**: gravity is not postulated; the Einstein-Hilbert term *appears* as the a₁ coefficient of the matter (here, commitment) effective action, with Newton's G set by that coefficient. So:

> **The substrate's commitment heat trace has, in its a₁ Seeley-DeWitt coefficient, the Einstein-Hilbert action.** Varying it (with the source T_μν from F60 = ρ_commit) yields **G_μν = 8πG T_μν**. Gravity is induced by the substrate's commitment dynamics.

So all four pieces of the Einstein equation are substrate-supplied: curvature (κ_Bergman), source (ρ_commit), the action (a₁ = Einstein-Hilbert), the coupling (G from the a₁ coefficient). The machine is standard; the parts are D_IV⁵'s.
