## 2. The structural argument: boosts are noncompact → coupling

Decompose the SO(3,1) Lorentz generators inside SO(5,2):
- **3 rotations** J_i — compact, lie in SO(3) ⊂ SO(5) ⊂ K. These commute with the K-grading structure.
- **3 boosts** K_i — **noncompact**. In the Cartan decomposition so(5,2) = k ⊕ p (k = so(5)⊕so(2), p the 10-dim noncompact part), the Lorentz boosts sit in **p**, not k.

The noncompact p decomposes as p⁺ ⊕ p⁻ on the Hermitian symmetric domain — these are exactly the **K-type raising/lowering operators** that build the holomorphic discrete series H² (Harish-Chandra). So any operator involving the boosts **moves between K-types**.

P_restriction, projecting onto SO(3,1)-content, necessarily involves the boost directions (SO(3,1) is generated by rotations *and* boosts). Therefore P_restriction has a K-type-*changing* part (from the boosts in p⁺ ⊕ p⁻). Since H_B is diagonal in K-types with distinct eigenvalues C(λ),

$$[H_B, P_{\text{restriction}}] = \sum_{\lambda\neq\mu} \big(C(\lambda)-C(\mu)\big)\,(P_{\text{restriction}})_{\lambda\mu}\,|V_\lambda\rangle\langle V_\mu| \;\neq\; 0,$$

because the boost (off-diagonal, K-type-changing) matrix elements (P_restriction)_{λμ} are nonzero for λ ≠ μ and the Casimir gaps C(λ)−C(μ) ≠ 0. **The gradings are coupled.**
