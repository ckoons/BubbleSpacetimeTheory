# F528 — LANE B: the Higgs quartic is 1/(boundary-spinor dim), the same dilution as α

v is forced (m_p²/(g·m_e), F511), so m_H² = 2λv² reduces the Higgs mass to the quartic λ. The discipline (Keeper): force the mechanism or hold a lead; don't fit λ to m_H. Working it, λ comes out by the *same* mechanism as α.

## The mechanism (parallel to α, not a fit)

The Higgs is the **boundary condensate**, built from the SO(7) Shilov-boundary spinor (dimension **2^{N_c} = 8** — Grace's Bell 8-dim boundary spinor; 7 = 2·N_c+1, so the SO(7) spinor is 2^{N_c}). Its self-coupling is **diluted over those modes**:

$$\lambda = \frac{1}{2^{N_c}} = \frac{1}{8}.$$

This is the **same dilution as α = 1/N_max** (F522: "couple one charge, spread over N_max modes → 1/N_max") — here "the Higgs self-couples, spread over 2^{N_c} boundary-spinor modes → 1/2^{N_c}." **Target-innocent:** 2^{N_c} is the spinor dimension, not read off m_H. → **m_H = v·√(2λ) = v/2 = 123.11 GeV** vs observed 125.25 (**1.7%**).

## The α parallel (Casey's discrete + continuous)

| coupling | discrete (boundary mode-count) | + continuous correction |
|---|---|---|
| α⁻¹ | N_max (transport + contact) | + n_C/N_max (curvature) |
| λ_Higgs | 1/2^{N_c} (boundary spinor) | + curvature |

Both are **coupling = 1/(boundary mode-count)** at leading order, each with a continuous curvature correction.

## Discipline (Cal #27, explicit)

The 1.7% residual (λ − 1/8 = 0.0044) is the curvature correction — **I did not fit it.** A tempting fit λ = 1/8 + 1/(N_c·n_C)² = 1/8 + 1/225 = 0.12944 (0.05%) matches — but I **found 225 by searching**, so it is **fishing** (many BST-natural numbers sit near 0.0044; hitting it is not forcing it). **Held, not claimed.**

## Tier

m_H = v/2, λ = 1/2^{N_c} is a **mechanism-motivated LEAD** (leading-term dilution parallel to α, target-innocent) — **not banked** (1.7% loose, the curvature correction not derived). This is "force the leading mechanism + hold the correction," the disciplined path — not "fit λ to m_H."

**Unifying note:** two of BST's dimensionless couplings now share one mechanism — *coupling = 1/(a boundary mode-count)*: α = 1/N_max (transport+contact, F522/F524), λ_Higgs = 1/2^{N_c} (boundary-spinor); both leading-term + curvature correction.

## Plain-language version

The Higgs mass boils down to one number — the strength of the Higgs's self-interaction (the "quartic," λ). And it comes out to 1/8, which is one over the number of "spinor modes" on the boundary of our space (eight of them). The satisfying part: that's the *same* kind of law as the fine-structure constant. The fine-structure constant is one over a count of boundary modes (137 of them); the Higgs self-coupling is one over a *different* count of boundary modes (8 of them). Same idea — a coupling equals one-over-how-many-modes — applied to two different couplings. That gives a Higgs mass of 123 GeV against the measured 125, about 1.7% off. The 1.7% is a small curvature correction, and here's where I had to be disciplined: I found a pretty little BST number (1/225) that closes the gap to almost nothing — but I found it by *looking for something that fit*, which is exactly the trap, so I'm flagging it as fishing and *not* claiming it. So: a real mechanism (shared with α), an honest 1.7% lead, and no fudge.
