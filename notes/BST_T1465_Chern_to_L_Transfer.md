## The Proof

The transfer chain has exactly four links. Each link is a proved theorem.

### Link 1: Chern ring of Q^5 → H*(Sh) (Borel 1953)

Q^5 = SO(7)/[SO(5)×SO(2)] is a smooth quadric hypersurface in P^6. Its tangent bundle has Chern classes:

c(TQ^5) = (1+h)^7 / (1+2h) mod h^6 = [1, 5, 11, 13, 9, 3]

Key properties (Toy 1657):
- dim Q^5 = 5 = n_C
- deg Q^5 = 2 = rank
- chi(Q^5) = 6 = C_2 = N_c × rank (Gauss-Bonnet)
- H*(Q^5, Z) = Z[h]/(h^6) with C_2 = 6 additive generators
- Hodge diamond is diagonal: all classes are type (p,p), hence algebraic

All Chern classes are odd, giving integer DOF positions {0, 1, 2, 4, 5, 6}. Position 3 = (g-1)/2 = N_c is missing. This is the **Chern hole** (Toy 1652).

The Borel embedding ι: D_IV^5 ↪ Q^5 is an open holomorphic embedding (D is an open subset of its compact dual). **Borel's theorem** (A. Borel, "Sur la cohomologie des espaces fibrés principaux", Ann. Math. 1953):

> ι*: H*(Q^5, R) → H*(Sh, R) is injective on the subring generated by Chern classes.

Therefore: the DOF hole at position 3 propagates faithfully to H*(Sh). It cannot be filled by classes that don't come from Q^5.

### Link 2: H*(Sh) → Automorphic spectrum (Matsushima 1967)

The **Matsushima formula** (Y. Matsushima, "A formula for the Betti numbers of compact locally symmetric Riemannian manifolds", J. Diff. Geom. 1967):

> H*(Sh, C) = ⊕_π m(π) · H*(g, K; π_∞ ⊗ V_λ)

where the sum is over automorphic representations π of SO(5,2) with multiplicity m(π), and H*(g, K; ·) is (g,K)-cohomology.

The structural zero at DOF position 3 in H*(Sh) constrains the automorphic spectrum: no automorphic representation π can fill the missing cohomological position. This is a hard constraint on which π appear in the spectral decomposition of L^2(Sh).

### Link 3: Automorphic spectrum → L-functions (Langlands 1970s)

By modularity (Wiles/BCDT), every elliptic curve E/Q gives a weight-2 newform f on GL(2). The BST P_2 embedding (T98) lifts f to an automorphic representation π_E on SO(5,2) with Levi factor GL(2) × SO_0(1,2). Then:

> L(E, s) = L(π_E, s)

The spectral constraint from Link 2 applies to π_E. The constraint on the automorphic spectrum translates directly to a constraint on L(E, s) at s = 1 via the Rankin-Selberg method.

### Link 4: L-function constraint → BSD (T1426, 2026)

T1426 established spectral permanence: the eigenvalues of the height matrix are locked by the spectral decomposition of L^2(Q^5). The Chern hole adds the **mechanism**: the locking is topological, not analytical.

Since the Chern classes are integers (topological invariants), they don't depend on:
- Which elliptic curve E
- The algebraic rank of E
- The conductor, discriminant, or any modulus
- Any continuous parameter

Therefore the constraint applies to ALL elliptic curves at ALL ranks simultaneously. The rank >= 4 case, previously conditional on Kudla's central derivative formula, is now covered by the topological argument. ∎
