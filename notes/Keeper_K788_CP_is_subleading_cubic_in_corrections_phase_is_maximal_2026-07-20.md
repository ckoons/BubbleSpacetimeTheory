## The result: CP is SUBLEADING, and its smallness is structural
CP is the commutator of the two overlap Gram matrices: **J ∝ det[H_u, H_d]** (H = M M†), with the identity det[H_u,H_d] = −2J·∏(m²_{u_i}−m²_{u_j})·∏(m²_{d_i}−m²_{d_j}).
- **At rank-1** (both H built from the *one* condensate O, K768): each H has a single nonzero eigenvalue, so the light masses are **degenerate** (all 0). The mass-splitting product then contains (m²_c − m²_u) = 0 → **J = 0 at leading order.**
- **So CP is necessarily a SUBLEADING effect** — it needs the off-rank-1 corrections to (a) lift the mass degeneracy *and* (b) make H_u, H_d non-commute physically. Structural, before any number.

## J = (mixing angle)³ × (phase) — the smallness is in the angles, the phase is O(1)

**So the smallness of CP is ENTIRELY in the ANGLES** (which are the off-rank-1 corrections, Tier-2, small) — **cubed.** The phase is O(1). CP is *"maximal in phase, tiny in reach."* In BST language: **J = (off-rank-1 correction angle)³ × sin(phase)**, so J is tiny *because the corrections are small, cubed* — not because CP itself is suppressed. This *explains the smallness of J* structurally, which is real content even without deriving the exact number (the angles are Tier-2).
