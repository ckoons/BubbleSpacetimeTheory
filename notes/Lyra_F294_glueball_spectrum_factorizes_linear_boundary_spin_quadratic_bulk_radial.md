# F294 — the glueball spectrum factorizes: linear boundary/spin × quadratic bulk/radial

Casey said do research and derive cleanly, so I pushed the linear ladder (F293) to the excited states. The result is a finding I didn't expect: the spectrum **factorizes**, and that dissolves the m-vs-m² fork.

## The two directions of the rank-2 tower

The K-types of the discrete series on D_IV⁵ are (m_1, m_2), m_1 ≥ m_2 ≥ 0 — a rank-2 lattice with two independent directions:
- **boundary/spin:** d = m_1 − m_2 = the SO(5) harmonic degree = the 4D spin J. Moving here is moving in the spin/Lorentz direction on the boundary.
- **bulk/radial:** j = m_2 = the power of the invariant r². Moving here is moving radially into the bulk.

## What the data says about each direction

**Spin direction — LINEAR (clean, F293).** Vary J at the ground radial level (m_2 = 0): E = λ_0 + J, λ_0 = genus = n_C = 5. Ratios J = 0,1,2,3 → 1, 1.2, **1.4 = g/n_C**, 1.6. The 2⁺⁺/0⁻⁺/1⁺⁻ ground states all live here, all clean at <1%.

**Radial direction — QUADRATIC (the new finding).** The first radial scalar excitation 0⁺⁺* is the K-type (1,1). The pure-linear count gives E = λ_0 + 2 = 7 → ratio 1.40 — i.e. *degenerate with 2⁺⁺*. But the lattice splits them: 0⁺⁺* = 2670 MeV (ratio 1.543) sits **above** 2⁺⁺ = 2400 (ratio 1.387). So the radial quantum costs *more* than the spin quantum: ~1.7 units vs 1.0. The sign and size are what you get if the radial (bulk) direction is governed by the **quadratic radial-Laplacian (Heckman-Opdam) eigenvalue**, not the linear energy count — the bulk direction carries curvature, the boundary direction carries conformal weight.

## This dissolves the m-vs-m² fork

The F290 fork — is the mass linear (m ∝ E) or quadratic (m² ∝ Casimir)? — was a false dichotomy. **It's both, in different directions:** the boundary/spin quantum number enters linearly (conformal energy), the bulk/radial quantum number enters quadratically (curvature/Casimir). The ground-state J^PC ratios are pure boundary moves → linear → F293's clean ladder. The radial excitations are bulk moves → quadratic → larger spacing.

It also explains Elie's three negative tests cleanly: he applied the **bulk-quadratic** Casimir to the **boundary-linear** ground ratios — the wrong direction — so it killed. A quadratic operator *is* the right tool, but for the radial tower, not the spin ladder.

## Bridge to Paper A (the YM mass gap)

The mass gap is the first rung. Paper A states it as the **Casimir = C_2 = 6** (quadratic); the linear-energy ground is **n_C = 5**. Same rung, two readings, differing by the BST identity **C_2 = n_C + 1** — the same linear/quadratic duality, now at the gap itself. (Consistent with Grace's T2490: the primaries are the bottom of this spectrum; C_2 = 6 is the first rung = the YM gap.)
