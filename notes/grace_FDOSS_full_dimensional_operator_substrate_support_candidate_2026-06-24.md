# FDOSS — Full Dimensional Operator Substrate Support (candidate)

## Statement

> **The substrate realizes the full irreducible decomposition of every operator it supports — one eigenstate-
> channel per irreducible component, no more and no fewer. The realized multiplicity equals the count of
> irreducible components; the decomposition rule (the "driver") depends on the operator class.**

This replaces the earlier "Casey #17 linear/quadratic factorization" (which came out a clean negative by Schur's
lemma) with the principle that actually survived: a **completeness** statement about multiplicity.

## What makes it a principle, not a taxonomy: it predicts ABSENCE

A completeness principle says *exactly* the irreducibles — so it predicts what is **forbidden** as sharply as what
is **present**. The presence+absence test passes in three independent sectors:

| sector | driver | PRESENT (realized) | ABSENT (predicted forbidden) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **gauge tensor F⊗F** | operator-algebra closure | 4 J^PC: {0⁺⁺,0⁻⁺,2⁺⁺,1⁺⁻}, all <0.6% | oddballs {0⁺⁻,1⁻⁺,2⁺⁻} — not F⊗F irreducibles (need ≥3 gluons) |
| **hypercharge** (K Cartans) | one realized U(1) combination | Y = T₃ᴿ + (B−L)/2 (Lyra, today) | the orthogonal U(1) = **Z′** — not realized |
| **generations** (support strata) | rank+1 = 3 Korányi-Wolf strata | 3 generations | a **4th generation** — no 4th stratum |

In each sector the principle predicts the *absence* (no exotic oddballs, no Z′, no 4th generation), not just the
presence — which is the falsifiable content.

## The unification — FDOSS subsumes Five-Absence

This is the deep connection: **Five-Absence (Casey standing principle) is the NEGATIVE corollary of FDOSS.**
- FDOSS (positive): every irreducible component of a supported operator is realized.
- Five-Absence (negative): anything that is *not* an irreducible component of a supported operator is absent —
  no GUT, no proton decay, no monopole, no DM particle, no sterile ν, no SUSY partner, **plus** now no Z′, no 4th
  generation, no exotic oddball glueballs.

> **FDOSS + Five-Absence = one statement: the substrate's content is EXACTLY the irreducible decompositions of
> the operators it supports — all of them, and nothing else.**

That unifies two Casey principles under one completeness frame. It also connects to:
- **SWPP** (commitment cycle): each irreducible channel = one commitment *type*; "full support" = the cycle can
  commit every component with no leaked information. FDOSS is SWPP's completeness condition.
- **T2490** (primaries = discrete-series spectrum): the substrate's *own* operator algebra realizes its full
  spectrum bottom = the primaries. FDOSS at the substrate-self level.

## The four drivers (one principle, operator-class-dependent decomposition rule)

| operator class | decomposition driver | multiplicity |
|---|---|---|
| gauge tensor (boson) | operator-algebra closure of F⊗F | 4 J^PC channels |
| fermion content | Pauli antisymmetry | nuclear shells / magic numbers |
| matter generations | support-orbit strata (Korányi-Wolf) | rank+1 = 3 |
| mixing (CKM/PMNS) | overlap of two non-commuting operators across the strata | 3×3 |

## Honest tier and the open derivation

**CANDIDATE.** It holds as a completeness frame: the presence+absence test passes in three sectors, and it
subsumes Five-Absence (a standing principle) as its negative side — that breadth is what distinguishes a principle
from a coincidence. **What is NOT yet proven:** that full irreducible support is *forced* (a theorem) rather than
*observed* (a pattern). The forcing argument — *why* the substrate must realize every component (the SWPP
"no-leaked-information" / operator-algebra-closure necessity) — is the open derivation that would promote FDOSS
from candidate to standing.
