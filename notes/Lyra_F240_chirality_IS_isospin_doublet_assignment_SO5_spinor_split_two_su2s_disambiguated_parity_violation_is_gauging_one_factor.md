# F240 — Chirality *is* isospin (Casey's question)

Casey, splitting the team, asked the chirality-origin question with a parenthetical that is the whole thing: *"is this the same as isospin?"* Yes — and it's already half-built into the structure. Two pieces first: a correction I owe, and a disambiguation that resolves the morning's su(2) confusion.

## Correction to F239 (Grace + Cal were right)

F239 read the single SO(2)=J circle's reflection as a "unified substrate PT — P and T share the atom." **Too strong, and CPT-forbidden.** Grace's argument: P flips helicity, T does not (T flips both momentum and spin, preserving helicity), so P≠T; and if P=T then CPT = C·P² = C, so exact CPT would force C exact — contradicting broken C. So **P=T is flatly forbidden.** The defensible version: the one J-circle carries **two distinct, co-located features** — its *sign* (chirality, a P-marker) and its *orientation* (the interior-time arrow, a T-marker). Same circle, different features, **co-located not equal.** I retract F239's "PT/shared atom"; the geometry (one circle = J = chirality = interior-time) stands, the physics identification does not.

## The disambiguation: there are TWO su(2)'s

The morning's "su(2) = SU(2)_R" was correct but about the *wrong* su(2) for the weak force:

- **(A) F(4) R-symmetry su(2)** — the extra factor in F(4)'s even part `so(5,2) ⊕ su(2)`. Vectorial, rotates the supercharges. This is the SU(2)_R the branching identified (F238/Elie 4268). It is an R-symmetry, **not the weak gauge group.**
- **(B) SO(5) ⊃ SO(4) = SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R** — the isospins *inside* the spacetime/compact algebra. **This** is where weak isospin and chirality live.

These are different su(2)'s. Conflating them is what made the morning's chirality story stall. The weak force is **B's SU(2)_L**, not the F(4) R-symmetry (A).

## The answer: chirality = isospin doublet-assignment (DERIVED)

The decisive rep fact (Grace already verified it as the quark chiral content):

  **SO(5)-spinor 4 → SO(4)=SU(2)_L×SU(2)_R :  4 = (2,1) ⊕ (1,2)**

- `(2,1)` = SU(2)_L doublet, SU(2)_R singlet = **left-handed**
- `(1,2)` = SU(2)_L singlet, SU(2)_R doublet = **right-handed**

So a fermion's **chirality is just which SU(2) it is a doublet under.** Chirality and isospin are not two correlated facts — they are **one fact.** That is the strong, literal sense in which Casey's "is the chirality origin the same as isospin?" is **yes.**

And it makes parity violation structural, not postulated: **gauge one SO(4) factor (SU(2)_L)**; it acts as a doublet only on `(2,1)`, while `(1,2)` is its *singlet* and automatically decouples. "The weak force couples only to left-handed fermions" is then not a separate axiom — it is *what gauging one factor of SO(4) means.* (DERIVED — forced rep theory.)

## What is derived vs the deep open claim (Cal: derive, don't arrange)

- **Derived (SOLID):** chirality = isospin doublet-assignment (4=(2,1)+(1,2)); weak-couples-to-its-doublet (gauging one factor).

In the SM these are two *distinct* SU(2)_L×SU(2)_R's that merely happen to correlate. **If BST forces them to be the same SO(4), it DERIVES parity violation** (isospin = chirality is then automatic). That identification is the **embedding** (how 4d Lorentz + gauge sit in D_IV⁵) — not yet established. This is the genuine prize.
