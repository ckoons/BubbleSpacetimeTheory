## Q3 (the linchpin): virtual particles = the un-frozen flicker of the cooling bleed

Read a virtual pair as one SWPP turn: **absorb** (a quantum lifts from the reservoir — the pair appears) → **commit-attempt** → **emit** (commitment fails for lack of energy — the pair annihilates). A *real* particle is the same cycle where commitment *succeeds*. So:

- **virtual particles = the un-frozen (failed-commitment) flicker** of the bled heat;
- **real particles = the frozen-out (committed) part**;
- **Λ = the energy of the still-flickering sea** (the un-committed residue).

This slots onto F216: the **bare zero-point** (heat-trace `a_0 = 225`, Planck-scale — the cosmological-constant *problem*) **is** the un-cooled virtual-particle sea; the **bleed cools/suppresses it** to the observed Λ; "freezing out" is the commitment that condenses some into real matter and suppresses the rest. So virtual particles *are* evidence of the cooling — the bare vacuum heat caught mid-condensation, with the suppression to the tiny observed Λ being the bleed. (The cosmological freeze-out sequence — ν decoupling, BBN, recombination — is the literal cooling-condensation; the virtual sea is what never froze.) The virtual lifetime `~ ℏ/E` is the commitment window: commit within it (real) or fail (virtual annihilation) — plausibly the Koons-tick window.

**Observable hooks (real physics):** the **Casimir effect** measures the virtual/zero-point sea directly (BST SP-29); **Hawking radiation** is a *literal* freezing-out — a virtual pair split by the horizon, one member made real.

## Q1: will the bleed end?

The boundary is an effectively-**infinite** reservoir (infinite Bergman distance, diverging kernel), so the bleed **asymptotes** — never strictly ends — toward equilibrium over the commitment time `τ_commit` (gated; plausibly near-infinite on observable scales = Casey's near-infinite time). Observable handle: ongoing bleed → Λ slowly **evolving** (`w > −1`, `w(z)`); equilibrium → constant Λ (`w = −1`). Observed near-constancy → equilibrium OR enormous `τ_commit`. A measured tiny `w(z)` evolution would be the ongoing-bleed signature.

## Q2: measuring a quiet black hole losing energy

From **outside**: the interior bleed is **screened by the horizon** — heat goes *in*; only Hawking escapes, and that's unmeasurably tiny (`~6×10⁻⁸ K` for 1 M_sun, *colder* than the CMB, so a quiet hole grows today, not shrinks). **Not** measurable from outside. From **inside** (where we sit, relative to our host): the bleed **is** measured — as the **cosmological constant**. So we already measure a black hole's energy bleed — our host's — as our dark energy; our BHs lose energy to their interiors, screened from us. Measurable only from inside, never outside.
