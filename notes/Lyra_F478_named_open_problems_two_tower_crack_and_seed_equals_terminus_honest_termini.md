# F478 — the two honest termini, as named open problems

Closing-push lane (mine): write the two open cracks so they read as *well-posed open problems* — precise claim, evidence, close condition, honest terminus — rather than as gaps a referee has to discover. Both are real, both are named, neither is dressed as a result.

## OP-1 — The two-tower crack (Λ is not on the mass tower)

**Claim:** BST mass observables ride a **base-α tower** with clean integer exponents, but Λ does **not** — Λ rides a **base-exp(−n_C)** tower. The "one shell-tower, exponent = shell count, Λ at 56 shells" picture is **false as a one-tower claim.**

**Evidence (Elie 4564):**
- Masses: m_e/m_Planck ~ α^{12} = α^{2·C_2}; Koons tick ~ α^{36} = α^{C_2²} — clean integer powers of α.
- Λ = exp(−280): as a power of α this is **α^{56.9} — non-integer**; read as α^{56} it misses observed Λ by **~87×**.
- Forcing Λ onto the α tower would require N_max = e^{n_C} = 148.4, but **N_max = 137** (8.3% off; ln 137 = 4.92 ≈ n_C only approximately — and *that* 1.6% gap is the 87× when compounded over 56).
- And 280 = 2^{N_c}·n_C·g is a substrate **product**, not a power of one base.

**Verdict:** two genuinely distinct structures — masses on a **coupling (base-α) tower**, Λ on a **heat-bleed exp(−substrate-product) tower**. Not one shell tower.

**Close condition:** *either* derive a structural reason the two bases coexist (why masses use α and Λ uses exp(−n_C)), *or* prove they are irreducibly distinct mechanisms. Until then, the shell-cosmology "α-tower = shell count" reading holds for masses only, and is **retracted for Λ.**

**Tier:** the mass-base-α vs Λ-base-exp fact is **operational** (a real structural constraint, forward-checkable). The shell-cosmology interpretation around it is Level-2 / internal.

## OP-2 — seed = terminus (the absolute scale)

**Claim:** the absolute mass scale — the substrate length ℓ_B — is BST's **one remaining dimensionful input.** If the cascade fixes it, BST is **fully parameter-free** (the anti-landscape statement, now on a *number* rather than a law). The whole question reduces to **one** claim.

**The one claim (Elie 4571):** the collapse→seed map **f** (parent black-hole collapse energy → baby-universe seed scale) has a **unique attracting fixed point**, S = f(S) — "seed = terminus," the energy that seeds *us* equals the equilibrium *our* collapses hand forward.

**Where it stands:**
- **Climb side — countable:** equilibrium at τ_commit = 56; ℓ_B preserved across branches by Rigidity (#7). *But* it **inherits OP-1's crack** — the "56-shell climb" rides the Λ-side (base-exp), not the clean mass-side (base-α), so the climb itself is not clean.
- **Collapse side — vision boundary:** the existence + uniqueness of the fixed point needs the substrate **collapse dynamics** (Shilov-saturation) — not yet derivable. This is the honest terminus.

**Close condition:** derive the collapse→seed map f from the substrate dynamics and show it has a unique attracting fixed point.

**Terminus:** fixed-point **existence is unreached.** ℓ_B stays an **input**, not derived — seed=terminus is the one derivable-number target of the whole cosmology, and it is **not** reached this arc. Level-2 / internal register.

## Plain-language version

Two loose ends from the weekend's work, written up honestly so nobody mistakes them for either solved or swept-away. First: we'd hoped one elegant "tower" explained both the particle masses *and* the dark-energy number (Λ) — masses as α-to-a-power, Λ as α-to-the-56th. It doesn't. The masses really do sit on clean powers of α, but Λ lands on a *non*-whole power (56.9), and forcing it to 56 misses the real value by a factor of 87. So they're two different machines, not one — and I'm retracting the "one tower" story for Λ, cleanly. Second: the one number BST still has to *assume* rather than derive is the overall size/mass scale. There's a beautiful way it could become derived — if each universe's starting scale equals the equilibrium its own black holes hand to the next universe (a self-consistent loop). But proving that loop closes needs the physics of how black holes collapse into the substrate, which we don't have yet. So that number stays an input for now — and I've written *why*, and *exactly what would have to be shown* to change it. Both are honest open problems with the doors clearly marked, not holes in the floor.
