# F44 (F38 v0.2) — Reading Decision

## 0. The decision asked for

Cal #259 + Keeper K230-ITERATE require one consistent meaning of "(1−P)/boundary," applied to both the vacuum and the muon. I commit here, and I commit on grounds, not convenience.

**Decision: Reading (a). Everything physical lives in H²(D_IV⁵). "Bulk" and "boundary" are two isometric realizations of that one space; there is no separate physical (1−P) sector.**

## 1. Why (a), on Tier-0 grounds

The Tier 0 framework (Sunday 2026-05-31) puts the substrate commitment dynamics on the **Hardy space H²(D_IV⁵)**: ρ_commit(τ) = exp(−τH_B/ℏ_BST) acts on H², and the arrow of time is the positivity of spec(H_B) **on H²**. The physical (committed) states are holomorphic. The orthogonal complement (1−P) of L²(bulk) is the non-/anti-holomorphic part, and the framework gives it **no physical role** — it is not a commitment sector.

So the only physical Hilbert space is H². Within H², the two things I had been calling "bulk" and "boundary" are the **Bergman** and **Szegő** realizations of the *same* H², related by the Hardy isometry. That is Reading (a). Reading (b) (the complement is a physical second sector with its own vacuum energy) would require promoting the anti-holomorphic modes to physical status, which Tier 0 does not support. Reading (a) is the grounded choice.

## 2. Consequence for the vacuum: the factor-2 needs the cycle, not the isometry

Under (a), the substrate vacuum is one H² state. The observer (bulk-localized) sees its **Bergman** realization; the substrate's full description also carries its **Szegő/Shilov-boundary** realization. The Hardy isometry says these are the **same** (E[P]_bulk = E[P]_boundary) — it gives *sameness*, and Cal #259 is exactly right that sameness does **not** license the *twoness* (the factor 2).

So the honest open question is no longer "compute ρ = E[(1−P)]/E[P]" (there is no physical (1−P)). It is: **why does the substrate count the one H² vacuum twice?** The candidate physical justification is the **SWPP commitment cycle** (Casey #5: absorption → commitment → emission). If a cycle touches the vacuum once on the Shilov boundary (absorption) and once in the bulk (emission), the vacuum energy enters the substrate's bookkeeping twice — factor 2 — while the bulk-localized observer measures it once. *That* is a physical mechanism for the twoness; the isometry only guarantees the two touches have equal weight. Open question re-scoped, and on firmer (physical) ground: **justify the double-count via the absorption/emission cycle traversing both realizations.** The residual ε (whatever its size, and recall Elie's catch that its target is rounding-limited) is then the cycle's asymmetry between absorption and emission weights — not a complement ratio.

## 3. Consequence for the muon: additivity must be K-type orthogonality, not bulk-vs-boundary

This is the harder consequence, and I state it plainly because it puts my own Composite v0.5 under question.

Under (a), Composite v0.5's m_μ/m_e = Term 1 (bulk) **+** Term 2 (edge, 81/8) **cannot** be read as "bulk realization + boundary realization" — those are the *same* H² data, and adding them double-counts. A legitimate "+" within H² requires Term 1 and Term 2 to be **distinct, orthogonal K-types** (Peter–Weyl orthogonality within H²). Then the addition is genuine.

So the muon edge-term 81/8 must be re-derived as the matrix element of a **distinct H² K-type**, orthogonal to the V_(3/2,1/2) of Term 1 — **not** as the Szegő boundary realization of Term 1's K-type (which is what Elie's Toy 4006 computed; that was, correctly per its own terms, Meaning B). Elie's 81/8 boundary computation is clean Hardy theory but computes the wrong object for the *additive* role.

**Two honest outcomes, and I do not get to choose which is true:**
- **(i)** there is a distinct H² K-type whose matrix element is N_c⁴/2^{N_c} = 81/8, orthogonal to V_(3/2,1/2) — then Composite v0.5's "+" is legitimate and 81/8 is re-derived correctly. **K229d A1's derivation path is: find that K-type.**
- **(ii)** there is no such distinct K-type and 81/8 is genuinely only the boundary realization of the bulk term — then Composite v0.5's additive form is **wrong**, and m_μ/m_e = 207 needs a non-additive derivation.

Which holds is now a concrete, falsifiable question (does a distinct H² K-type give 81/8?), and it is the real K229d A1 step — not the boundary computation already done.
