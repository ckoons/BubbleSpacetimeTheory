## What I did
Took the K733 "rank-2 boundary has two rulers → charge measured over both = √rank" mechanism and asked the obvious next question a referee would: **are the two rulers equal?** If they are, a norm over 2 equal directions gives √rank cleanly. If not, the factor is whatever the *weighted* norm gives — and it might not be √rank.

## The finding
- The two rank-2 Cartan directions are **NOT equal.** The domain's ρ-vector is **ρ(D_IV⁵) = (n_C/rank, N_c/rank) = (5/2, 3/2)** — two unequal components (that's the whole content of "rank 2, two inequivalent boundary components").
- A charge sitting equally on both rulers, measured in the ρ-weighted metric, gets norm² factor **ρ₁+ρ₂ = (n_C+N_c)/rank = 8/2 = 4**, not the naive equal-ruler **rank = 2**. **These differ.** So "√rank" is the *equal-ruler approximation*, not automatically the exact answer.
- Clean identities the weighted sum lands on (leads, not results): ρ₁+ρ₂ = (n_C+N_c)/rank = 4; the numerator n_C+N_c = 8 is exactly the GUT denominator N_c+n_C; ρ₁·ρ₂ = N_c·n_C/rank² = 15/4.

## Why this SHARPENS (does not kill) the marquee
- The direction is unchanged and still right: the hypercharge norm is **enhanced** across the rank-2 boundary (that's why g'² drops and sin²θ_W moves 3/8→3/13). What's now sharp is that the exact factor is set by **how the hypercharge distributes over the two UNEQUAL rulers**, weighted by ρ.
- The marquee computation is therefore precisely stated: **compute the ρ-weighted SO(2) charge norm across the rank-2 Shilov boundary.** Two outcomes, both bankable:
  - **(a)** the hypercharge sits on the rulers such that the ρ-weighting collapses to exactly **√rank** → sin²θ_W = 3/13 **DERIVED**, clean.
  - **(b)** it gives a different factor → then **3/13 is the equal-ruler approximation** and the exact BST value is whatever the weighting yields — still a *prediction*, just not 3/13, and we'd revise the form honestly.
- Either way we learn something real. That is the "investigate, see where it goes" payoff: we now know the crux isn't "is there a √rank" but "**how does the hypercharge weight against the two unequal rulers**."
