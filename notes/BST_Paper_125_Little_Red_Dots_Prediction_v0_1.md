## 1. The JWST Little Red Dots phenomenon

### 1.1. Observational summary

JWST observations (2022-2026) have revealed a class of compact red objects at high redshift (z > 4) that have proven difficult to fit with standard astrophysical models. The observational signature consists of:

- **Compactness**: typical angular size implies physical radius ~20% of host galaxy radius (kpc scale)
- **V-shaped SED**: prominent UV emission feature + far-IR emission feature, with relatively quiet mid-spectrum (rest-frame optical region between the two bumps)
- **No dust signatures**: spectra lack silicate absorption features and the warm-dust thermal gradient characteristic of dust-reprocessing torus models
- **Heavy spectral features in UV and long-wavelength IR**: both bumps are luminous; spectral asymmetry suggests two distinct emission mechanisms

| Overmassive seed BH (DCBH) | Existence at z>5 | SED shape; compactness mechanism |

The honest puzzle: a V-shaped SED with UV bump + IR bump + no mid-spectrum + no dust signatures is hard to assemble from standard astrophysical ingredients. Each explanation requires hand-waving one or more of the four signatures.
