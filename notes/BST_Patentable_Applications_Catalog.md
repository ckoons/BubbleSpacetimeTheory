### 1. Casimir Flow Cell

**One-line description:** Nanoscale material manipulation device using Casimir vacuum force with active gap feedback, achieving sub-Pascal to tera-Pascal pressure range in a single MEMS-scale instrument.

**BST basis:**
- Force law F/A = -pi^2 * hbar * c / (240 d^4) where coefficient 240 = rank x n_C! (BST).
- Optimal gap d_0 = N_max x a = 137 lattice planes.
- Five configurations: tweezers, extruder, valve, separator, sensor.
- Switchable surfaces (conductor, phonon-gapped, ferroelectric, repulsive Lifshitz).

**How it works:** Two opposing Casimir-coupled surfaces with controllable nanoscale gap. The d^{-4} force law provides seven orders of magnitude dynamic range (piconewton to millinewton). A three-layer control architecture (coarse electromagnetic, fine piezoelectric, MHz capacitance feedback) maintains target gap or target force. The tapered-channel extruder configuration ramps pressure smoothly from MPa to TPa over ~100 nm of travel.

**Prior art concerns:**
- *Similar:* Casimir effect well-known (Casimir 1948, Lamoreaux 1997). Optical tweezers (Ashkin). MEMS Casimir actuators (on/off switches). Diamond anvil cells.
- *Novel:* No prior device uses Casimir force as the PRIMARY operative force for material processing. No prior device combines Casimir force with active gap feedback for continuously variable controlled pressure. No prior device provides seven orders of magnitude pressure range in a single MEMS instrument. Five-configuration unification (tweezers/extruder/valve/separator/sensor) is novel.

**Patent classification:** Utility patent (device + method). 17 claims drafted (2 independent + 15 dependent).

**Readiness level:** 4 (near prototype-ready). All component technologies exist: MEMS fabrication, piezo actuators, capacitance sensing, atomically flat graphene surfaces. The integration into a Casimir-force-driven device is new.

**Commercial value:** HIGH. Semiconductor fabrication, pharmaceutical nanoparticle sorting, metamaterial production, phase transition mapping, cold welding, molecular-scale fabrication. Near-term (2-3 year) lab demonstration path.

**Status:** PROVISIONAL FILED April 2, 2026. Specification in `notes/patent/FlowCell/CasimirFlowCell_ProvisionalSpecification.md`. No BST references in filing.
