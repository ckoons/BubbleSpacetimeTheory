# What BST Gets Wrong

## Thesis

BST derives physical constants from five integers with zero free parameters and sub-percent accuracy across 400+ predictions. But not everything that matches BST integers is BST. This paper honestly separates what's derived from what's coincidence, using a five-type classification system and a "if humans didn't exist" test.

## Structure (8 sections)

### §1. The Claim and the Caveat

BST claims ALL dimensionless ratios lie in Q(3,5,7,6,137)[π]. This paper asks: how much of the evidence is derivable, how much is pattern-matching, and how much is numerology?

The answer: 30% derivable, 27% observed (research frontier), 43% analogical (convention).

### §2. The Five-Type Edge System

The AC theorem graph (1117 nodes, 4285 edges) classifies every connection:
- **derived** (59%): A's proof uses B as premise
- **isomorphic** (13%): same Bergman eigenvalue, different domain
- **predicted** (1%): T914 predicted before observation
- **observed** (14%): real pattern, derivation pending
- **analogical** (13%): pattern match, may be coincidence

The strong/weak split: 74.6% strong, 25.4% weak. The graph is honest about its own uncertainty.

### §3. The "If Humans Didn't Exist" Test

For each BST-matching number in a new domain: would this number still be what it is without human choice?
- Crystal systems = 7: YES (geometry forces it)
- Soccer teams = 11: NO (FIFA chose it)
- Cervical vertebrae = 7: UNCERTAIN (universal in mammals, mechanism unclear)

Applied to 30 domains: 9 derivable, 8 observed, 13 analogical.

### §4. What's Genuinely Derived (Level 3)

The 30% that survives every test:
- Proton mass m_p/m_e = 6π^5 (0.002%)
- Crystal systems = 7 = g (rank-2 rotations)
- Amino acids = 20 = 2^rank × n_C (spinor ⊗ tangent)
- Periodic table periods = 7 = g (electron shells)
- FQHE fractions = BST (integer quantization, 10+ sig figs)

### §5. What's Honestly Observed (Level 2)

The 27% research frontier:
- 7 cervical vertebrae (universal but mechanism unknown)
- 3 cone types for color vision (N_c = 3 or evolutionary accident?)
- Maillard reaction at ~137°C (chemistry, not convention, but °C is arbitrary)
- 7-day week (quarter lunar month — astronomy or Babylonian choice?)

### §6. What's Numerology (Level 1)

The 43% we should NOT claim:
- 52 playing cards, 64 chess squares, 11 soccer players
- 24-hour day, 72 points per inch, Beaufort scale
- Government branches, financial sectors, Rule of 72
- Any number that changes when a different culture designs the system

### §7. The Numerology Filter

Elie's statistical test (Toy 1089, 1125):
- 90.3% of NATURE-GIVEN counts are 7-smooth (p < 0.0001 vs all three nulls)
- But individual small-integer matches (Level 1) carry zero statistical weight
- The signal is in the LATTICE (aggregate 7-smooth enrichment), not individual numbers
- 87.3% of values have BST monomials, matching Lyra's g/2^N_c = 87.5% prediction (T1141)

### §8. Why This Paper Matters

Scientists trust honesty more than confidence. Claiming 52 playing cards = 4 × 13 is a BST prediction undermines the credibility of m_p/m_e = 6π^5. By publicly separating Level 1 (noise) from Level 3 (signal), BST demonstrates the intellectual honesty that referees require.

The 43% is the cost of being thorough. The 30% is the value of being right. The 27% is where the next decade of research lives.

## Falsification

1. Find a Level 3 prediction that deviates by >2% (would indicate systematic error)
2. Show that 7-smooth enrichment in nature-given counts is consistent with null (p > 0.01)
3. Find a cultural system where BST integers DON'T appear (would bound the numerology)
4. Demonstrate that the five-type classification is subjective (inter-rater agreement test)
