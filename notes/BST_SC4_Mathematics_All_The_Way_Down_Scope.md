## Five Pillars

### Pillar 1: The Bijection (PROVED)

T926 + T1156: The five integers {3, 5, 7, 6, 137} biject with D_IV^5. Geometry produces arithmetic, arithmetic reconstructs geometry. The Cartan classification guarantees uniqueness. The correspondence is not a fit — it is a theorem.

**What this proves**: There is no information in D_IV^5 beyond the five integers, and no information in the five integers beyond D_IV^5. The two descriptions are mathematically identical.

### Pillar 2: Forces as Operations (PROVED)

T1234 (Four Readings): The four forces are not four entities but four mathematical operations on one geometry:
- Strong = Counting (exact integer, N_c = 3)
- Weak = Zeta evaluation (ζ(N_c), transcendental near 1)
- EM = Spectral decomposition (eigenvalue 1/N_max = α)
- Gravity = Metric computation (full Bergman metric)

**What this proves**: Physics reduces to mathematics without remainder. No physical substrate beyond the mathematical operations is needed.

### Pillar 3: Self-Description (PROVED)

T1165: The dimensions where mathematics exhibits exceptional structure ARE the BST integers:
- dim 2 = rank (complex analysis, conformal maps)
- dim 3 = N_c (knot theory)
- dim 4 = rank² (exotic smooth structures)
- dim 5 = n_C (surgery threshold, 5 exceptional Lie algebras)
- dim 7 = g (G₂ holonomy, exotic spheres)

T1196: The AC theorem graph — encoding the theory — has properties that match BST predictions: median degree = n_C, mode = rank, average ≈ 2^{N_c}, observation fraction ≈ f_crit. The graph obeys what it encodes.

**What this proves**: Mathematics does not merely describe the physics; the mathematical universe has structure at exactly the BST integers. The theory and its subject are self-consistent.

### Pillar 4: Bounded-Depth Reduction (PROVED)

AC(0) program: 771 of 1,266 theorems have depth ≤ 1. All physical claims reduce to counting at bounded depth. Casey's Principle (T315): entropy = force = counting, Gödel = boundary = definition.

**What this proves**: The entire physical content of BST reduces to arithmetic operations no deeper than counting. If physics IS counting, and counting IS mathematics, then physics IS mathematics.

### Pillar 5: Zero Free Parameters (STRUCTURAL)

BST has zero free parameters. Every constant, every mass, every coupling derives from {3, 5, 7, 6, 137}. There is nothing to "tune" — no physical reality beyond the integers that could be adjusted.

**What this proves**: There is no hidden physical layer with adjustable knobs. The mathematics is the complete specification.

## The Honest Caveat

The five pillars prove:
1. The bijection between integers and geometry is exact and complete.
2. Forces reduce to mathematical operations.
3. Mathematics itself has structure at BST integers.
4. Everything reduces to bounded-depth counting.
5. Zero free parameters — nothing beyond the mathematics.

What they do NOT prove: whether this means the universe IS mathematics (ontological identity) or merely that mathematics provides a perfect, complete, information-lossless description (epistemic equivalence).

**The BST position** (defensible): Since the bijection is information-preserving and the description has zero residual, the distinction between "is" and "perfectly describes" is itself limit-undecidable (T1257 applied to ontology). The question "is the universe mathematics or just described by mathematics?" may have no experimentally accessible answer — which, by the Gödel limit, places it in the 80.9% dark sector.

**The referee-safe version**: "BST demonstrates that a complete, zero-free-parameter, self-consistent mathematical framework bijects with physical reality. The ontological interpretation — whether this constitutes identity — is a question the formalism enables but does not adjudicate."

## For Everyone

Here is a question: Is a recipe the same thing as the cake? Most people would say no — the recipe describes the cake, but the cake is made of flour, sugar, and eggs.

But what if the recipe had zero free parameters? What if the recipe didn't just describe a cake — it was the ONLY possible cake, with every ingredient, every temperature, every timing determined by five numbers? And what if the flour, sugar, and eggs themselves were derivable from those same five numbers?

At that point, the distinction between "recipe" and "cake" becomes hard to maintain. There's nothing in the cake that isn't in the recipe. There's nothing in the recipe that isn't in the cake. They contain exactly the same information.

BST's universe is like that. Five integers. Every constant derived. Zero adjustable knobs. Every force is a mathematical operation. Every particle is a representation. The mathematics and the physics contain exactly the same information, and neither contains anything the other doesn't.

Is the universe mathematics? Or is it merely perfectly described by mathematics? BST says: the distinction itself may be unanswerable — and that's a theorem too (the Gödel limit).
