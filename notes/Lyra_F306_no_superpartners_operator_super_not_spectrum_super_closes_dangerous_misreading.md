# F306 — no superpartners: the substrate is super at the *operator* level, not the *spectrum* level

The chirality cascade (F300/F303/F305) concludes "the substrate must be super" — F(4) superconformal, because mixing the spacetime SU(2) with the internal one needs Coleman–Mandula evasion. A referee's first reflex: *"then where are the superpartners?"* (#176). This closes that misreading cleanly, and it preserves the Five-Absence "no SUSY" prediction.

## Two meanings of "super"

- **(A) Low-energy N=1 SUSY (MSSM-type).** The supercharges map physical one-particle states to physical one-particle states of opposite statistics and equal mass — giving a **superpartner** for every SM particle (selectron, gluino, …). This is the Haag–Łopuszański–Sohnius realization of Coleman–Mandula evasion, and it predicts a doubled spectrum.
- **(B) F(4) superconformal spacetime-operator algebra (BST).** The (8,2) supercharges link the **spacetime** (so(4) = Lorentz, Weyl L/R) and **internal** (so(7) gauge / sl(2)) sectors at the **substrate-operator** level — they are the chirality-realizing / spacetime-internal-mixing operators of F303/F305, **not** raising operators on a physical-state multiplet.

## BST is type (B) — so there are no sparticles

1. The supercharges act on the **substrate operator algebra** on H²(D_IV⁵), realizing chirality via ker(one Weyl half) (F305). They do **not** generate mass-degenerate boson↔fermion physical partners.
2. The matter is the **chiral-primary half** (8 of the 16, one-handed). A chiral primary is a **short multiplet** — it has no opposite-statistics equal-mass partner; the would-be partner states are exactly what the ker(one Weyl half) projection removes.
3. So the SM generation is the **whole** realized matter content. No doubling.

## Consequence

**Five-Absence "NO SUSY" (no sparticles) is consistent with "the substrate is super":** the super-ness is type (B) — operator-level, the spacetime-internal mixing that realizes chirality — not type (A), spectrum-level doubling. This is the same operator-vs-geometry distinction that runs through BST: **color** is operator-algebraic on the domain and geometric on the compact dual; **super** is operator-level (the (8,2) on H²) and does not appear as a spectrum-level partner structure. The "super" lives in how the substrate's operators are organized, not in a doubled particle list.

**Falsifier preserved:** a discovered superpartner (selectron, gluino, …) refutes BST (it would force type (A)). The no-sparticle prediction is sharp and testable.
