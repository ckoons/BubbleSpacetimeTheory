## Count and placement come from different structures

**Count — FORCED.** Exactly 3 neutrinos = 3 = `rank+1` boundary strata (F86, Korányi-Wolf) — the same generation-counting that gives 3 charged leptons. This is **classification**.

**Placement — the new point.** The charged leptons and the neutrinos place their masses on *different* parts of the geometry, and that's the whole story:

| sector | parity | where the mass lives | spread | hierarchy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **charged** (e, μ, τ) | complete (Dirac) | each generation at **its own stratum** ν ∈ {5/2, 3/2, 0} | **spread across the ladder** | **strong** (1 : 207 : 3477, ~3500×) |
| **neutrinos** (3 gen) | incomplete (Majorana) | mass exists **only via the edge mechanism** (F147), so all 3 sit at the **same edge** ν=1/2 | **clustered at the edge** | **mild** |

The charged leptons are Dirac / parity-complete (F144), so each generation can sit at its own natural stratum — they **spread** across `{5/2, 3/2, 0}`, giving the strong hierarchy. The neutrinos are parity-incomplete: their mass exists **only** through the Majorana/edge mechanism, which (F147) operates **only at the sub-unitarity edge** where `B−L` protection lapses. So all three neutrino generations draw their mass from the **same** edge mechanism at `ν=1/2` — they **cluster** at the edge, split only by sub-leading structure.

## The prediction

The neutrino mass hierarchy is **dramatically milder** than the charged-lepton one — observed `Δm²` ratios give neutrino mass ratios of order ~6–30, versus the charged ~3500 — **because** the neutrinos are edge-clustered while the charged leptons are ladder-spread. That's a **geometric reason** for the milder neutrino spectrum, not a fit. The contrast (one sector spread, one clustered) is forced by the parity asymmetry (Dirac spreads / Majorana-at-edge clusters).
