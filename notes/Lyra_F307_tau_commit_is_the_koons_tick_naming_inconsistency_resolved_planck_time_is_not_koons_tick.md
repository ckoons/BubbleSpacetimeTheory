# F307 — τ_commit is the Koons tick; and the Planck time is *not* the Koons tick

Forward pull for the right-column (#134): pin τ_commit, the substrate commitment time. Doing so surfaced a corpus naming inconsistency that must be resolved first (pin-to-primary-source, per the standing rule).

## τ_commit (the pin)

The Tier-0 commitment operator is ρ_commit(τ) = exp(−τ · H_B / ℏ_BST) on H²(D_IV⁵), with τ the commitment-flow parameter and the dynamics realized as **repeated application of the infinitesimal generator — one commitment per tick.** That tick is the substrate's fundamental time unit:

  **τ_commit = the Koons tick = t_Planck · α^(C_2²) ≈ 6.5×10⁻¹²¹ s ≈ 10⁻¹²⁰ s** (T2405).

This is the substrate clock *below* emergent spacetime: the substrate writes one commitment per Koons tick and produces 4D spacetime (resolved at the Planck scale) as its coarse-grained output.

## The naming inconsistency (found, resolved)

The corpus uses "Koons tick" for **two different quantities**:

- **BST_Koons_Substrate_Constants.md:** τ_K = ℓ_K/c = **5.391×10⁻⁴⁴ s** labelled "Koons tick";
- **T2405 / substrate-time framing:** "Koons tick" = t_P · α^(C_2²) ≈ **10⁻¹²⁰ s**.

These differ by ~76 orders of magnitude — they cannot both be "the Koons tick." Resolution (state by value + role, cite, stop relabeling):

| quantity | value | role | name |
|---|---|---|---|
| t_P = ℓ_P/c | 5.39×10⁻⁴⁴ s | emergent-spacetime time resolution | **Planck time** (NOT the Koons tick) |
| τ_commit | ≈ 10⁻¹²⁰ s | substrate commitment-cycle period | **the Koons tick** |

So BST_Koons_Substrate_Constants.md's table entry mislabels the Planck time as the Koons tick; the Koons tick is the sub-Planck commitment tick τ_commit. (The substrate operating *below* spacetime is the whole point — its tick must be sub-Planck, not the Planck time.) This should be corrected in that constants file.

## Honest flag (derived-vs-fit)

The exponent **C_2² = 36** in α^(C_2²) is from T2405. Whether C_2² (rather than C_2, or 2·C_2, or another combination) is *mechanism-forced* or *matched* must be checked before the numerical value 10⁻¹²⁰ is banked as derivation-grade. The parts that are clean and SOLID here are (a) the **identification** τ_commit = the Koons tick = the commitment-cycle period of the Tier-0 ρ_commit, and (b) the **naming resolution** (Planck time ≠ Koons tick). The value's derivation-tier rides on T2405's exponent justification.
