# SP-19b P-1 Bridge Paper — Outline

## Working title

"Curved Arenas and the Common Insight: AdS/CFT and Bubble Spacetime Theory as Two Readings of the Same Geometry"

## One-sentence framing (Casey, May 12)

> "AdS/CFT and BST share an arena and a structural insight, differ in choice of starting axioms and what is held fixed; together they map a common landscape that neither sees from one side alone."

## Scope and audience

- Audience: theoretical physicists familiar with AdS/CFT, weakly familiar with BST.
- Goal: present BST not as a competitor but as a parallel reading that respects, absorbs, and extends AdS/CFT's structural insights.
- Length target: 40-80 pages (Physics Reports format).
- Tone: review + perspective, not original derivation. The original derivations are cited to Papers #76-#103.

## Outline (7 sections, ~10 pages each)

### Section 1: Two Programs, One Geometry (~8 pages)

The historical setup: how AdS/CFT (Maldacena 1997) and BST (Koons 2026) independently arrived at curved arena physics with related geometries.

Subsections:
- 1.1 The 1997 conjecture: AdS_5 × S^5 as bulk dual to N=4 SYM on the boundary
- 1.2 The 2026 framework: D_IV^5 as the unique APG with five Cartan integers
- 1.3 The shared insight: physics lives on a Lie geometry with a 4-dim spacetime boundary
- 1.4 What this paper does: identify the bridge, not the differences

**Output**: clear statement of the parallel-readings thesis, citation map to Maldacena 1997 and Koons 2026 papers.

### Section 2: The Common Arena (~12 pages)

The geometric containment SO(4,2) ⊂ SO(5,2) and what it means at the symmetry level.

Subsections:
- 2.1 SO(4,2) as the conformal group of 4D Minkowski (Klein 1872, Bondi 1962)
- 2.2 SO(5,2) as the symmetry of D_IV^5 (Cartan classification)
- 2.3 The embedding SO(4,2) ⊂ SO(5,2): the missing dimension and its meaning
- 2.4 What BST adds: one extra spectral dimension forces the five integers (Wallach uniqueness, T1830)

**Output**: Lie-algebraic table of subgroup chain SO(4,2) ⊂ SO(5,2) and the spectral consequences.

### Section 3: SO(4,2) ⊂ SO(5,2) — Technical Embedding (~12 pages, AB-2)

Explicit construction of the embedding at the level of root systems, parabolic subalgebras, and unitary representations.

Subsections:
- 3.1 Root systems: D_3 (so(4,2)) inside B_3 (so(5,2))
- 3.2 Parabolic subalgebras: P_1 of so(4,2) inside P_2 of so(5,2)
- 3.3 Unitary representations: Wallach modules of so(5,2) and their restrictions to so(4,2)
- 3.4 The lowest K-type story: pi_rank's behavior under SO(5,2) → SO(4,2) restriction
- 3.5 Holographic principle in BST: the boundary is the K3 spectral slice (Toys 2265+2267)

**Output**: explicit restriction matrices for the first few K-types; identification of the AdS/CFT central charge with a Wallach-module parameter on BST side.

### Section 4: Absorption — BST Analogs of AdS/CFT Results (~12 pages)

For each major AdS/CFT result, the corresponding BST statement.

Subsections:
- 4.1 Anomaly matching: a_AdS vs a_BST (Wallach Casimir computation)
- 4.2 Wilson loops: minimal surfaces on D_IV^5 vs AdS
- 4.3 Two-point functions: Bergman kernel vs AdS bulk-to-boundary propagator
- 4.4 Entanglement entropy: Ryu-Takayanagi vs BST Hodge-area duality
- 4.5 Thermal states: Bekenstein-Hawking on AdS vs BST horizon spectroscopy
- 4.6 Holographic c-theorem: AdS scale vs Bergman scale in BST

**Output**: side-by-side comparison table of 6 standard AdS/CFT results and their BST translations. Each entry cites the BST paper/toy.

### Section 5: Divergences (~8 pages)

Where AdS/CFT and BST disagree, and what each reading gets that the other doesn't.

Subsections:
- 5.1 SUSY: AdS/CFT requires N=4 SYM; BST does not require SUSY
- 5.2 Free gauge group: AdS/CFT lets SU(N) vary; BST forces SU(3) uniquely
- 5.3 Predictivity for our universe: AdS/CFT few; BST 600+
- 5.4 Uniqueness: AdS/CFT none (any near-horizon brane); BST unique (Wallach Universality)
- 5.5 What AdS/CFT has that BST lacks: explicit dynamics on the bulk (BST gives spectrum, not dynamics)
- 5.6 What BST has that AdS/CFT lacks: predictive numerics for SM observables

**Output**: balanced critique. NOT a "BST wins" section. Both programs contribute.

### Section 6: Common Implications (~12 pages)

What both programs say about the relationship between geometry and physics.

Subsections:
- 6.1 Holography is forced, not chosen
- 6.2 The boundary is where physics is computable
- 6.3 Anomalies are topological invariants of the bulk geometry
- 6.4 Information theory and Bergman kernels (BST) / Ryu-Takayanagi formula (AdS)
- 6.5 The 4D boundary and SO(4,2) conformal structure
- 6.6 Spectral dimension and bulk curvature

**Output**: synthesis of the two programs' shared implications for quantum gravity and the holographic principle.

### Section 7: Future Directions (~6 pages)

Concrete research questions that emerge from absorbing AdS/CFT into BST.

Subsections:
- 7.1 BST bulk dynamics from AdS/CFT bulk equations of motion
- 7.2 SUSY embedding: can BST recover N=4 SYM at a specific Wallach level?
- 7.3 Higher-dimensional generalizations: D_IV^n for n != 5 and their boundary CFTs
- 7.4 Information-theoretic dictionary
- 7.5 Experimental tests: where do AdS/CFT and BST predict different physics?

**Output**: 5-10 explicit research questions, each citable, each with proposed methodology.
