# F170 — Option 2: the pole residue gives the neutrino SCALE, not the RATIO — and the ratio is the circulation

Casey said try option 2 (compute the Γ_Ω pole residue directly, no K-types) and see where it goes. It goes somewhere useful: it **separates** the neutrino problem cleanly.

## Computed (forward, no neutrino data in)

`Γ_Ω(s₁,s₂) = (2π)^{3/2} Γ(s₁) Γ(s₂−3/2)` for D_IV⁵ (rank 2, a=3). The neutrino pole is at `s₂ = 1/2` (where `Γ(s₂−3/2)` hits its pole at −1), and it's a **line** (`s₂=1/2`, `s₁` free) — confirming F166's "the pole is a 1-D locus, not a point."

## Positive — the SCALE (ties to Λ)

- residue at the neutrino pole = exactly **−1** (O(1), clean);
- residues at the deeper poles (`s = −1/2, −3/2, …`) = the **1/n! sequence** {1, 1/2, 1/6, 1/24}.

The residue magnitude being **O(1)** means `m_ν ~ κ·|res| ~ κ ~ Λ^{1/4} ~ meV` — this **confirms F167's scale-coincidence from the residue itself** (the dimensionless magnitude is 1, so the neutrino scale *is* the residue/Λ scale — nothing huge, nothing tiny). The sign is **−1** (vs the muon-edge residue `+1` at `s=3/2`) — a sign flip at the sub-unitary edge, possibly a parity/ghost signature (flagged speculative).

## Negative — the RATIO is not in the residue

The bare residue ratios are small **integers** — consecutive {2, 3, 4}, two-step {6, 12} — **not** the observed `m₃/m₂ = 5.77 = √33.3`. So the mass **ratio is not the discrete residue structure.**

## The separation (the real result — and it validates F166)

The pole residue cleanly **splits** the neutrino problem:
- **SCALE** → the residue (O(1) magnitude, ties to Λ — F167, mostly understood);
- **RATIO** → the **circulation-radius structure on the pole line** (the δ's of F160, continuous, the `s₁` mode-positions) — a **dynamical** input, not the static residue.

This **validates the F166 circulation picture**: the mass *ratio* is **dynamical** (the circulation/ping-pong on the line), not a static seat or residue — which is precisely *why* no static structure (K-types F165, bare residue here) ever gave it. The ratio was never going to come from a static address; it's the dynamics.

`5.77 ≈ 6 = C₂` appears **again** (the residue two-step ratio is exactly 6; yesterday's δ-ratio was ~5.75; F160). Third time. `5.77` vs `6` is ~4% (the structural floor). Either a real `C₂`-structure with a sub-leading correction, or a persistent coincidence — **not crowned** (refused each time, per Cal #27 / Grace #37). Worth noting the *persistence*, not banking it.

The **circulation dynamics on the pole line** — the `s₁` mode-positions / the δ-ratio — is what gives `5.77`, and it's the F166 **ping-pong**, not a residue or a K-type. That's the remaining computation: the circulation, not the static structure.
