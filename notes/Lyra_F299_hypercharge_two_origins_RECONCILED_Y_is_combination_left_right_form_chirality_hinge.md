# F299 — the two hypercharge origins reconcile: they are the two components of Y

The chirality hinge (K446), postponed three days while the YM/glueball arc closed. F247 pinned the problem: BST states U(1)_Y two ways — **Origin A** (the SO(2) central character / the S¹ conformal circle) and **Origin B** (the "1 extra direction" of n_C = 3+2+1, an internal SO(5) direction) — and they looked like two different U(1)'s. The resolution: **they are not competing derivations; they are the two standard components of hypercharge.**

## The Cartan count settles it

K = SO(5) × SO(2) has exactly **3 Cartan directions** (rank 2 + rank 1). Use the full-rank chain SO(5) ⊃ SO(4) = SU(2) × SU(2). Then the three Cartan generators are:

  **{T_3^L, T_3^R, H_0}** — T_3^L and T_3^R the two SU(2) Cartans inside SO(5), and H_0 the SO(2) central character.

- **Origin B** ("the extra SO(5)-internal direction") = **T_3^R**, the second (right) isospin Cartan.
- **Origin A** ("the SO(2) central character") = **H_0**.

Physical hypercharge is never a *single* Cartan — it is the standard left-right combination

  **Y = T_3^R + (B−L)/2,**

with T_3^R = Origin B and (B−L) built from the central character H_0 = Origin A. So **F247's fork dissolves**: the two origins are the **two terms** of the well-known hypercharge formula, not two rival U(1)'s. (This is exactly how hypercharge decomposes in any left-right-symmetric embedding — BST's "two origins" were the program seeing the two terms separately.)

The combination orthogonal to Y in the {T_3^R, H_0} plane is a second U(1) — and it is the **predicted-absent extra U(1)** (no Z′), consistent with the Five-Absence set. That orthogonality is a falsifiable BST statement, not a free choice.

## The chirality hinge, sharpened

Why is the SM chiral? Because the weak SU(2) gauges the **left** factor of SO(4) = SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R only. The load-bearing open identification (Cal #321, task #173) is **whether SO(4) ⊂ SO(5) is the Lorentz group or an internal weak group**:

- **If SO(4) = the Lorentz group**, then SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R *is* the left/right Weyl decomposition, and "weak SU(2) gauges the left factor" literally means **"the weak interaction couples to the left-handed Weyl spinor"** — the SM's defining chirality, now structural. Chirality, weak-SU(2), and the T_3^R-component of hypercharge all unify in the one SO(4) ⊂ SO(5).
- The **H_0 (SO(2)_R central) component** of Y is the R-charge piece. Via the F(4) superconformal BPS structure (R-charge ↔ conformal weight; chiral primaries are one-handed short multiplets), this component **locks** the chirality: matter charged under the H_0-part of Y is intrinsically one-handed.

So the chirality hinge is: **SM chirality ⟺ the weak SU(2) gauges the Lorentz-left Weyl, with the H_0-component of Y carrying the R-charge that one-hands the chiral primaries.** Origin A (H_0) is the chiral piece; Origin B (T_3^R) is the vector-like piece. The two origins are the chiral and vector components of hypercharge.
