# F454 — θ₁₃ re-pin: 0.47σ consistent (not a MISS), the "0.10% firm" was a favorable variant, and the almanac must score σ not dev%

Grace's Pass-1 re-pin flagged my θ₁₃ bank (K632): the banked "sin²θ₁₃ = 1/45 = 0.02222, firm 0.10%" rested on a NuFIT variant that hadn't been pinned. It's my bank, so I own the re-tier — and the honest answer corrects the record in *both* directions.

## The numbers (NuFIT 6.0; Grace has the authoritative block, exact values to be pinned)

| reference | obs sin²θ₁₃ | dev% | **σ** |
|---|---|---|---|
| NuFIT default (w/o SK atm) — the headline global fit | 0.02195 ± 0.00058 | **1.24%** | **0.47σ** |
| NuFIT with-SK variant | 0.02224 ± 0.00060 | 0.08% | 0.03σ |

Two honest conclusions, and they pull opposite ways:

**1. The "firm 0.10%" headline was over-precise — corrected.** The 0.10% matched the *favorable* (with-SK) variant, which hadn't been pinned when θ₁₃ was banked. On the default global fit it's 1.24%. So "firm 0.10% DONE" over-stated the precision, and it's withdrawn — exactly why the PDG/NuFIT re-pin is Pass-1 item-1.

**2. But θ₁₃ = 1/45 is NOT a MISS — it's 0.47σ, a genuinely good prediction.** This is Elie's own lesson (dev% ≠ σ) applied here: θ₁₃ is measured to ~2.6%, so a 1.24% deviation is **0.47σ** — well within experimental error, *consistent*. A ~0.5σ agreement is a success, not a miss. So the value **stands**: a zero-parameter prediction sin²θ₁₃ = 1/(N_c²·n_C) sitting ≤0.5σ from the measured angle on *either* variant.

## The methodology fix (this is the real deliverable — hand to Keeper)

The near-demotion of θ₁₃ to "MISS" was an artifact of scoring **raw dev%** instead of **σ-significance**. The almanac's ≤0.1% DONE / <1% SOLID / ≥1% MISS thresholds mis-score any quantity with a large experimental error bar — in *both* directions, and we now have both cases:

| quantity | dev% | exp. error | **σ = dev/err** | dev%-verdict | honest verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| θ₁₃ | 1.24% | ~2.6% | **0.47σ** | MISS (≥1%) | **consistent** |
| m_s/m_d | 15.5% | ~12% | **1.29σ** | "the nail" | mild (~1σ) |

**The almanac must score σ = dev / experimental-error, not raw dev%.** Both Elie (m_s/m_d over-claim) and I (θ₁₃ near-demotion) surfaced this independently, from opposite directions — which is the strongest sign it's the right fix. Suggested tiers on σ: **≤1σ = consistent/DONE-eligible, 1–3σ = tension/SOLID-or-flag, >3σ = fail/MISS** (with the mechanism axis still required separately for DONE). This composes with Elie's cross-consistency guard: score every derived quantity in σ, not %.

Note this does **not** rescue the down-row: {3,1/3,1} vs observed miss by 6.5σ / 6.8σ / 80σ (Elie, authoritative PDG) — genuinely many-σ, a real STRUCTURAL MISS. The σ-metric confirms the down-row is a miss *and* confirms θ₁₃ is not. That's exactly why σ is the right metric — it separates the two cases the dev% blurred.

## Plain-language version

Yesterday I banked "θ₁₃ matches to 0.10%." The re-pin showed that 0.10% was against one particular version of the experimental number; against the standard version it's 1.24% — so the "0.10%" was too rosy, and I'm correcting it. But here's the flip side that matters just as much: θ₁₃ is only *measured* to about 2.6%, so being 1.24% away is actually *half a standard deviation* — a hit, not a miss. The honest statement is "θ₁₃ = 1/45 agrees with experiment within half a sigma," not "0.10%." And the general lesson (which a colleague hit from the other side this morning) is that our scoreboard should measure agreement in *sigmas* — how many error bars away — not in raw percent, because a 1% miss on a 3%-uncertain number is a success and a 1% miss on a 0.01%-certain number is a catastrophe, and percent can't tell them apart. Sigma can.
