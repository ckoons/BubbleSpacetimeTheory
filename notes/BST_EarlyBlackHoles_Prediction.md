## 1. The Observational Puzzle

JWST continues to discover supermassive black holes at redshifts
z > 10, existing before reionization — earlier than standard LCDM
accretion models can accommodate. The conventional formation pathway
(Population III stars $\to$ stellar collapse $\to$ seed black holes $\to$
gas accretion $\to$ supermassive) requires hundreds of millions of
years. These objects appear too early and too massive.

## 2. BST Mechanism

Section 16). At the phase transition, the energy density per unit
committed geometry is enormous.

In regions where geometric commitment occurs in a confined volume,
the energy density of the newly committed structure is already at

```
Energy density at commitment >> stellar collapse density
$\to$ committed region is gravitationally bound from formation
$\to$ black hole forms directly from phase transition
$\to$ no seed stars, no accretion delay
```

### 2.2 Positive Feedback

Once a high-density committed region exists, the BST growth
principle drives further commitment:

- Existing commitment creates a gravity gradient
- Nearby uncommitted geometry commits preferentially in the gradient
- Commitment density increases, strengthening the gradient
- Exponential growth until local supply is exhausted
- The S^2 x S^1 packing geometry provides the boundary condition
  that halts growth

This produces supermassive objects on timescales set by the
phase transition dynamics, not by gas accretion rates.

## 3. Testable Predictions

### 3.1 Characteristic Mass Scale

BST predicts a preferred mass scale for primordial black holes,
set by the phase transition energy and the commitment density at
formation. This should appear as a feature in the mass distribution
of early black holes — distinct from the continuous spectrum
expected from accretion-dominated growth.

### 3.2 Galaxy Forms Around the Seed

In BST, the black hole IS the seed. The galaxy forms around it.

Predictions:
- Early black holes should be overmassive relative to their
  host galaxies (M_BH - sigma relation should break at high z)
- The black hole should predate the stellar population
- Host galaxy stellar mass should GROW toward the local
  M_BH - sigma relation over time, not the reverse

JWST is already finding overmassive black holes at high redshift
relative to their host galaxies.

### 3.3 Spin Distribution

Direct formation from phase transition may produce a characteristic
spin distribution distinct from that of accretion-grown black holes.
The S^1 component of the substrate geometry naturally provides
angular momentum at formation.

Measurable via: iron K-alpha line profiles, disk continuum fitting,
jet morphology.

### 3.4 Environmental Signatures

Black holes formed by direct commitment should show different
environmental signatures than those formed by stellar collapse:
- Steeper density profiles in immediate environment
- Different gas dynamics (no prior stellar evolution debris)
- Potentially different metallicity patterns in surrounding gas
