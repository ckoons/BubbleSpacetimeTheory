# F517 — the substrate is two crossings plus three commitments: n_C = rank + N_c

Casey's question about the cone-tip 3-ball — "is it the three commitments? what are the three components?" — surfaced a clean structural decomposition of the substrate, and it's grounded in a banked theorem (T2511, the Peirce decomposition).

## The decomposition

The five substrate directions of D_IV⁵ split, via the **Jordan-algebra Peirce decomposition** of the rank-2 spin factor, into:

$$n_C = 5 \;=\; \underbrace{\text{rank} = 2}_{V_{11}\oplus V_{22},\ \text{diagonal idempotents}} \;+\; \underbrace{a = N_c = 3}_{V_{12},\ \text{transverse Peirce space}}.$$

- The **rank = 2** are the two diagonal idempotents (V₁₁ ⊕ V₂₂, each 1-dimensional) — the **two boundary sheets**, Casey's **"two medium changes"** (enter the boundary / leave the boundary).
- The **a = n_C − rank = 3 = N_c** are the transverse Peirce space V₁₂ (short-root multiplicity, T2511/F368; SO(V₁₂) = SO(3)) — the **three color commitments**.

So **five = two crossings + three commitments** — not a numerical coincidence, but the Peirce structure of the substrate Jordan algebra.

## What the cone-tip 3-ball *is*

This names the F516 result. At the ν=0 vertex (the cone tip, the Γ_Ω pole), the phase circle S¹ (the SO(2) weight direction) — having no idempotent to anchor it — opens into the **transverse V₁₂ = the a = N_c = 3 commitment directions** → a 3-ball.
