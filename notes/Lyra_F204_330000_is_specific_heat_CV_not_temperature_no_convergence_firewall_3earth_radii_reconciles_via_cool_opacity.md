# F204 — The 330,000 is a specific heat, not a temperature (no convergence); the 3-Earth-radii firewall reconciles via cool-atmosphere opacity

Two findings on Casey's firewall / BST-330,000 thread: one is a discipline catch (the convergence isn't real), one is a clean reconciliation (his radius is right; my opacity was wrong).

## Catch: the 330,000 is C_V, not a temperature

Casey recalled deriving "330,000 K in BST for T_C," matching his old 350,000 K firewall. The source (BST_CosmicComposition_Thermodynamics_Mesons, L165–168):

> `C_V = α_s × β × N_max² = (7/20) × 50 × 137² = 328,458`, matching a previously-computed `C_V ≈ 330,000` at 0.47%.

The 330,000 is the **specific heat** `C_V` — dimensionless (a mode count weighted by the strong coupling `α_s = 7/20` and the inverse Bergman temperature `β = 50 = 2n_C²`), evaluated **at** `T_c = 0.487 MeV = 5.66×10⁹ K` (the BBN-epoch phase transition, `m_e·20/21`).

So "330,000" is a **heat capacity, not a temperature**, and the actual BST `T_c` is **5.66 billion K** — four orders of magnitude above the 350,000 K firewall. The apparent match is a **magnitude coincidence** between a dimensionless `C_V` and a temperature in kelvin — different physical quantities, not a convergence. Reported honestly: this is the no-fishing rule applied to a number I'd have *liked* to be real, Cal #27 (fires hardest at peak-convergence), and auditing Casey on equal standing.

## Reconciliation: Casey's 3 Earth radii (Sirius A) is correct — I used the wrong opacity

The Eddington threshold `T = 350,000 K` for Sirius A (M = 2.063 M☉):

| opacity κ (m²/kg) | regime | radius |
|---|---|---|
| 0.034 (electron scattering) | hot ionized plasma | 8.36 Earth radii |
| **0.26** (partially-ionized) | **cool atmosphere (cooling star)** | **2.97 ≈ 3 Earth radii** |

The scenario is a **cooling** star, whose cooler, partially-ionized atmosphere has ~8× the electron-scattering opacity. With that (physically correct) opacity, the threshold lands at **~3 Earth radii** — exactly Casey's expectation. My earlier 8 Earth radii used hot-plasma electron-scattering opacity, wrong for a cooling star. **Casey's firewall calc is sound and his radius is right.**

## Net

- **Firewall reproduces:** cooling Sirius A reaches gravity-beats-radiation at ~350,000 K and ~3 Earth radii, with cool-atmosphere opacity (κ ≈ 0.26). Solid.
- **BST "330,000 K T_C" convergence does NOT survive:** the 330,000 is `C_V` (a specific heat), and the BST phase-transition temperature is 5.66×10⁹ K. The match was a quantity confusion.
- The firewall temperature (350,000 K) remains a real stellar-collapse boundary temperature (F203), **separate** from Λ (Planck-sourced) and now also from the BST `C_V`/`T_c` phase transition.
