# F245 — Chirality needs a complex rep; J is the complexifying U(1)

Casey: *"remember linear algebra."* The right nudge — because the F244 obstruction dissolves the instant you ask the *linear-algebra* question that actually decides chirality.

## The controlling fact (rep reality)

A gauge theory is **chiral iff its matter representation is complex** — i.e. not isomorphic to its conjugate. (A real or pseudoreal rep admits a gauge-invariant Dirac mass term, so the theory is vectorial; only a complex rep forbids it and forces chirality. Standard.)

- **su(2) doublet 2 is pseudoreal:** 2 ≅ 2* via the invariant ε = [[0,1],[−1,0]]. So **su(2) alone cannot chiralize** — *every* su(2) rep is real/pseudoreal. **This is exactly the F244 obstruction, correctly named:** it isn't that the (8,2) factorization is wrong; it's that su(2) by itself is vectorial as a matter of rep reality.
- **su(2)×U(1):** (2)_Y has conjugate (2)_{−Y}, which differs for Y≠0 ⟹ **complex ⟹ chiral.** So chirality **requires a U(1) charge** to complexify the pseudoreal su(2) rep.

## J is the complexifying U(1)

The complexifying U(1) is sitting right there: it is the **SO(2) of K = the complex structure J.** The J-eigenvalue *is* the U(1) charge that distinguishes a rep from its conjugate (J → −J is complex conjugation). So **su(2) × U(1)_J acting on the matter is chiral, because J makes the rep complex.**

This is the **correct** role of J, and it reconciles the whole day's circling:

- J does **not** *equal* chirality (F239 — retracted: that was CPT-forbidden).
- J does **not** merely *select a Lorentz su(2)* (F242 — conflated Lorentz with the weak su(2), F244).
- **J is the complexifying U(1) — the hypercharge** — that turns the pseudoreal su(2) coupling into a complex, chiral one. That is *why* the complex structure was central all along: a complex structure is precisely a U(1) that complexifies, and complexification is what chirality *is*.

And F243 falls into place: "the boundary preserves the sign and the rest cancels" — the one-sided Hardy projection keeping one U(1)_J charge — **is the complexification.** The Hardy space is the holomorphic (one-sign) sector precisely because the complex structure has selected a U(1) charge; that selection is what makes the matter rep complex and the coupling chiral. F239→F242→F243→F245 is one arc, and F245 is its correct endpoint.

- **Resolves F244 at the mechanism level:** the chiral weak coupling is *possible*, and **the complex structure is what supplies it** — su(2) alone could not (pseudoreal). The obstruction was the standard "su(2) is vectorial alone"; the standard resolution is "add the complexifying U(1)," and BST's U(1) is the complex structure J, which it has by being a Hermitian domain.
