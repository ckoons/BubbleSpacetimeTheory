## Lead 2 — operator-algebraic-closure sweep → the multiplicity-driver map

Casey's Wednesday question (via K498): does the operator-algebra-closure mechanism that forces the four glueball
channels also drive CKM/PMNS multiplicity? Sweeping the BST corpus, the answer is **no — but it places the
mechanism in a clean map.** BST generates multiplicity by **four distinct drivers, one per operator class:**

| operator class | driver | mechanism | multiplicity | count source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **gauge tensor F⊗F** | **operator-algebra CLOSURE** | decompose ONE tensor into irreducibles | 4 J^PC channels {0⁺⁺,0⁻⁺,2⁺⁺,1⁺⁻} | 4D Lorentz decomposition |
| **fermion content** | **Pauli antisymmetry** | shell filling | nuclear magic 28/50/82/126 | shell capacities |
| **matter generations** | **support-orbit STRATA** | D_IV⁵ has rank+1 = 3 strata (Korányi-Wolf, F86) | 3 generations | rank + 1 |
| **mixing CKM/PMNS** | **OVERLAP** | misalignment of two non-commuting mass operators across the 3 strata (F84 Bergman kernel ×3) | 3×3 mixing | strata overlaps |

### The key distinction (why closure ≠ CKM/PMNS)

- **Glueball multiplicity** = the irreducible **decomposition of ONE tensor** (F⊗F). Closure forces all four
  components to have eigenstates. Count (4) comes from the 4D Lorentz structure.
- **CKM/PMNS multiplicity** = the **misalignment of TWO operators** (Yukawa_up vs Yukawa_down) across the 3
  support strata. Count (3) comes from rank+1 strata, not a tensor decomposition.

These are **different operator-algebraic mechanisms** — one-tensor-decomposition vs two-operator-misalignment. So
the gauge-sector closure mechanism does **not** transfer to the matter/mixing sector.

### The meta-principle that DOES unify (honest tier)

> **The substrate supports the full dimensional content of its constituent operators; the *driver* depends on the
> operator class.** Gauge tensor → closure; fermion → Pauli; matter → strata; mixing → overlap.

One meta-principle, four drivers. This **correctly scopes** the K498 operator-closure mechanism: it is the
**gauge-sector instance** of the meta-principle, *not* a universal multiplicity law (consistent with K498's own
"NOT saying this is universal" caveat). For Paper A §7, the operator-closure mechanism should be presented as the
**gauge-sector driver**, with the meta-principle as the broader frame and CKM/PMNS explicitly a *different* driver
— that keeps the "WHY four channels" answer honest and bounded rather than over-claiming a universal principle.
