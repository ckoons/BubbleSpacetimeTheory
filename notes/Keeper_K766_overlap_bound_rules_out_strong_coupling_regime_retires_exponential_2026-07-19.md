## The resolution — the overlap bound rules out the overshoot regime entirely
A Yukawa is a *normalized* Born overlap, so |y| ≤ 1 (Cauchy–Schwarz) applies to the **physical, canonically-normalized, low-energy** Yukawa y_t = m_t/(v/√2). Two facts settle it:

1. **RG direction:** in the SM the top Yukawa β-function is negative at low scale ((9/2)y² = 4.5 < 8g₃² = 12), so y_t *decreases toward the UV*: y_t(Planck) ≈ 0.4 < y_t(EW) ≈ 0.94 < 1. A substrate-scale overlap ≤ 1 runs to ≈ 1 at the EW scale and **never reaches the 1.5 attractor.**
2. **Regime exclusion:** strong-coupling top-condensation (NJL/BHL) *requires* y_t **large** at the compositeness scale — that is what produces the 220 overshoot. But |y| ≤ 1 at the substrate scale **forbids** a large substrate coupling. So **the overlap bound rules out the strong-coupling condensation regime.**

**Therefore there is no 220 overshoot to reconcile.** BST sits in the weak/bounded regime (SM-like y_t ≈ 1), not the strong-coupling one. **K765's kinematic cap holds, and no tuned hybrid mix is needed** — Lyra's tension resolves in favor of the kinematic reading, because the overshoot she was reconciling never occurs in BST.
