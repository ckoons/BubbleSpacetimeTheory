# F303 — the chirality realizer is a BPS chiral primary on the *chirality-aligned* R-charge, not J

Afternoon primary (#294): the F(4) BPS chiral-primary computation that realizes chirality. Elie's #292 located the realizer at the supercharge sector and found [J, chirality] = 0; doing the explicit supercharge decomposition confirms that and sharpens *which* charge realizes the chirality.

## The supercharge decomposition (explicit)

F(4) is the exceptional Lie superalgebra, dim 40 = 24 + 16: bosonic so(7) ⊕ sl(2) (21 + 3), odd part the supercharges **(8, 2)** = spinor(so(7)) ⊗ fund(sl(2)). Branch the supercharge **8 = spinor(so(7))** down the physical chain so(7) ⊃ so(5) ⊕ so(2) ⊃ so(4) ⊕ so(2) (so(4) = the spacetime Lorentz of F300/F302, so(2) = the central J):

- **Step 1** (so(5) ⊕ so(2)): 8 = **4_{+½} ⊕ 4_{−½}** (4 = Dirac spinor of so(5); J-charge ±½).
- **Step 2** (so(4) = su(2)_L × su(2)_R): 4 = **(2,1) ⊕ (1,2)** (left Weyl ⊕ right Weyl).

So under so(4) × so(2):

  **8 = (2,1)_{+½} ⊕ (1,2)_{+½} ⊕ (2,1)_{−½} ⊕ (1,2)_{−½}.**

## What this confirms, and what it sharpens

**Confirms Elie [J, chirality] = 0.** At *each* J-charge (±½) *both* Weyl chiralities (2,1) and (1,2) appear. J (so(2)) and the Weyl label (so(4)) are different commuting K-factors, so they grade independently — there is no correlation between the SO(2) charge and the handedness.

**The sharpening (the consequence).** A BPS bound on J — Δ = J — annihilates the supercharges of one J-*sign*, not of one chirality. Because both chiralities sit at each J-sign, **Δ = J leaves both chiralities — it does not realize chirality.** So Elie's "BPS Δ = R" realizer cannot have R = J. (This corrects the provisional R = J reading.)

## What does realize it — the chiral-primary projection

The mechanism that selects one handedness is the **chiral-primary condition**: a chiral primary is annihilated by *one Weyl half* of the supercharges — the right-Weyl (1,2) ones (the superconformal analogue of D̄_α̇|chiral⟩ = 0) — so it is built on the **(2,1) left content only → one-handed**. The projection that distinguishes (2,1) from (1,2) is the **su(2)_L − su(2)_R (self-dual) Cartan**, *not* J. Its bosonic shadow is a BPS bound **Δ = R with R the chirality-aligned R-charge** (the su(2)_L − su(2)_R combination), and saturating *that* bound annihilates one Weyl half, giving a left-handed short multiplet. **Matter = left chiral primaries = one-handed**, which is SM chirality.
