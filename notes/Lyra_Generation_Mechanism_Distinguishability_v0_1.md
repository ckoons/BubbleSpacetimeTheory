## 2. The structure CAN distinguish them (the count can't) — two structural levers

### 2.1 Different DIRECTIONS of the affine algebra

In the affine B̂₂ picture (the engine's generation tower, v0.2 step 2):

- **Generations** live in the IMAGINARY-root (δ) direction — the REGULAR / tube modules, the periodic Coxeter orbit, h (Coxeter number = period of the Coxeter element / the regular-orbit count).

These are DIFFERENT directions of the same affine algebra (finite-adjoint vs δ-tower). Mechanism A (generations = regular/δ structure) and color (finite-adjoint) are then STRUCTURALLY SEPARATE — they coincide in count at B₂ but live in orthogonal parts of the algebra. Mechanism C (generations = N_c = color) would instead put generations and color in the SAME structure. The affine direction-separation leans toward A: generations are the δ-tower, not the color index.

### 2.2 CKM is color-blind

If generations = color (Mechanism C), the generation index and the color index are the same SU(3)/N_c structure — one would expect generation-mixing to entangle with color. Observationally, **CKM/PMNS mixing mixes generations but is color-blind** (quark mixing is identical across R/G/B; the strong interaction does not distinguish generations). This is a real empirical lean toward generations being INDEPENDENT of color — Mechanism A, not C.
