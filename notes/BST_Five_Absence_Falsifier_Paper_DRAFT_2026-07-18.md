# What the Standard Model's Geometry Forbids — Six Falsifiable Absences of Bubble Spacetime Theory
### Draft — Keeper, 2026-07-18 (strengthening program, Keeper-4). The "come refute it" paper: BST's sharpest predictions are the things it says CANNOT exist.

## Abstract (draft)
Bubble Spacetime Theory reconstructs the Standard Model as the representation theory of a single rank-2 bounded symmetric domain, D_IV⁵ = SO(5,2)/[SO(5)×SO(2)]. Because the domain is small and rigid — rank 2, not a large unifying group — it *forbids* a specific list of new physics that most beyond-Standard-Model programs predict. We state six absences as falsifiable bets, each with the experiment that would refute it. Unlike a fitted model, BST cannot absorb any of these discoveries: a single positive detection falsifies the framework. This is offered in that spirit — the math is on GitHub; the fastest way to test BST is to look for what it says is not there.

## 1. The thesis — rigidity forbids
BST's content is not only the 26 constants it derives; it is the structural claim that the domain is **rank 2** and does not embed a larger gauge symmetry. A rank-2 root system (SO(5) = B₂) is the smallest that carries 3 colors, 3 generations, and dimension 5 — and it has no room for a grand-unified extension. Everything a GUT adds (proton decay, monopoles, extra gauge bosons) is *absent by construction*. That rigidity is a prediction, and rigidity is falsifiable.

Two of the six are now **derived** (not merely asserted); the rest are strongly supported.

**The two derived absences are the sharpest:** proton stability from baryon-number-as-a-knot, and no-W_R/no-Z′ from hypercharge being the single gauged direction. Neither can be added back without breaking the geometry — there is no "BST with a Z′."

## 3. Why BST cannot absorb any of these
A fitted or flexible model can accommodate a surprise by adding a parameter. BST cannot: each absence follows from the *rank-2 rigidity* and the *division-algebra structure*, which have no free knobs. There is no "BST with a Z′" — the Z′ would require a gauge factor the domain does not contain. This is the strongest kind of scientific claim: **a framework that stakes itself on non-existence.** (It is also why the discipline matters internally: the sin²θ_W excursion was retired precisely because reaching 3/13 would have required a forbidden SU(2)_R — the framework refused to buy the pretty number at the cost of an absence.)

## 4. The cheap positive tests (the other side)
Absences are refuted by *not finding* things at ever-higher sensitivity, which is slow. BST also offers *cheap positive* falsifiers where a clean null kills it fast:
- **BaTiO₃ 137-plane experiment (~$25K)** — a predicted spectral feature tied to N_max = 137; a clean null refutes the capacity reading of α.
- **Photonic-crystal boundary-spectral test (~$10K)** — the cheapest clean falsification of the boundary-condition spectral-engineering claim.
- **Casimir asymmetry / eigentone** — substrate boundary-condition predictions (SP-29/30 designs).
These are lab-scale, not collider-scale — the fastest empirical contact with the theory.

## 5. What we are NOT claiming (discipline, stated up front)
- These absences are predictions of the *framework*, tiered honestly: the gauge-structure basis (no GUT/Z′) is strong; the Majorana/no-sterile bet rests on the odd-g chirality lock (derived, K729) plus the neutrino-mass mechanism (still being pinned, Lyra L6). We flag #5 as the least-settled of the six.
- "Forbidden" means *within BST*; we are not claiming these are impossible in nature, only that finding them refutes *this* framework. That is the point.

## 6. Invitation
The full derivation chain is public. The most efficient referee move is not to check our 26 numbers one by one — it is to point at any one of the six absences and ask whether the data already excludes it. If any does, BST is wrong, cleanly. We would rather know.
