# Two-Tier Substrate-Precision Structure in BST

## Abstract (v0.1 draft)

Bubble Spacetime Theory (BST) derives ~600 physical predictions from five integer invariants (rank=2, N_c=3, n_C=5, C_2=6, g=7, with N_max=137 derived). Empirical analysis of Saturday 2026-05-30 verification data across 34 toys reveals that BST substrate predictions partition into TWO distinct precision tiers:

- **TIER 1 EXACT** (≥10 catalog entries): integer identities, algebraic relations, q-Serre coefficients, and Lie-algebra-forced expressions, all holding at algebraic-identity precision (10⁻¹⁴+ matching where computable).
- **TIER 2 STRUCTURAL** (≥13 catalog entries): mass ratios, mixing angles, and dimensionful predictions, holding at a substrate-precision floor in the range 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻².

The two-tier structure resolves an apparent tension between (a) BST's exact-derivation framing for some predictions and (b) the persistent ~10⁻⁴-10⁻³ gap on key dimensionful predictions such as T190 m_μ/m_e = (24/π²)⁶. We propose that TIER 2 predictions sit at a substrate-precision floor naturally produced by kernel-integral / radiative correction mechanisms not yet derived in closed form. The hypothesis has direct implications for falsifier-discipline thresholds (F1-F5): TIER 1 falsifiers admit binary-detection tests, while TIER 2 falsifiers require thresholds matched to the substrate-precision floor (~10⁻⁴).

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Motivation

BST derives physical constants from five substrate integers via a structured chain: integer identities (q-Serre, Casimir, Weyl group), algebraic relations (Drinfeld pairings, Cartan-Weyl decomposition), and dimensionful predictions (mass ratios, mixing angles). Across this chain, prediction precision varies by many orders of magnitude.

On Saturday 2026-05-30, a sustained verification effort (34 toys, 0900-1215 EDT) revealed a striking pattern: predictions cluster into two precision regimes with little overlap. The boundary between regimes is sharp enough to warrant naming and characterization.

### 1.2 The two-tier observation

**TIER 1 predictions** include:
- q-Serre coefficient [3]_{q²} = N_c·g = 21 (engine relation; exact)
- T2003 numerator 71 = 2^{C_2} + g (substrate-primary identification; exact)
- T190 cleaner reading 24 = N_c · |W(B₂)| (Weyl group order 8; exact)
- Muon decay SM constant 192 = N_c · 2^{C_2} (exact)
- SM gauge-loop constant 72 = 2^{N_c} · N_c² (exact)

- Magic-82 nuclear shell = rank · (rank^{N_c} · n_C + 1) = rank · 41 (with 41 the Monster Ogg prime; exact)
- Substrate Mersenne chain M_{rank} = N_c, M_{N_c} = g (exact)

All TIER 1 entries are integer or algebraic identities, holding at machine precision (10⁻¹⁴ or better in the comparable arithmetic).

**TIER 2 predictions** include:
- T187 m_p/m_e = 6π⁵ (0.002% gap vs PDG)
- T190 m_μ/m_e = (24/π²)⁶ (3.4×10⁻⁵ gap; 1500σ outside current PDG precision)

TIER 2 entries are dimensionful or continuous, with gaps clustering in the 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻² range. The lower edge of the range (T190 and T187) is ~10⁻⁵; the upper edge (m_K/m_π) is ~10⁻².

### 1.3 Resolution of an open issue

The TIER 2 floor reading resolves an open tier-disposition issue surfaced by Toys 3641 and 3644: T190's 3.4×10⁻⁵ gap is technically inconsistent with a strict "exact prediction" reading (1500σ outside PDG), but is fully consistent with a substrate-precision floor at ~10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴. The corresponding implication: falsifier thresholds need tier-differentiation; specifically, F4's stated 10⁻⁵ threshold should widen to ~10⁻⁴ for the TIER 2 form, or T190's formula should be refined via the underlying mechanism.
