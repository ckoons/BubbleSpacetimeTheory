- The **Schwarz reflection** = the *unique* anti-holomorphic involution fixing the Shilov boundary — determined by the domain, **no free parameter.**

So the LH→RH connection **map** (if it *is* this map, F182) has **no free direction parameter** — it is **rigid.**

## The radial-scale point (the key)

Mixing matrices (CKM, PMNS) are **unitary** — they're the mass-eigenbasis **rotations**, depending only on the **directions** of the mass matrices, not the overall scale. A free overall **radial scale** (multiplying the whole mass matrix) **cancels** in the unitary diagonalization → it affects the **masses** (eigenvalues), **not the mixing** (eigenvectors). So even if the connection carries a free radial scale, it **cannot reach the mixing angles** — the angles are direction-only, and the directions are fixed by the rigid Szegő/Schwarz map + the forced seat directions (strata F86 + `T₃ᴿ = ±1/2`, Elie 4231/4232). **⟹ M_angle = 0** (the mixing angles carry no free parameter).
