## The trichotomy

BST claims fall into three honest epistemic categories distinguished by mechanism state:

### IDENTIFY (substrate-natural match)

BST identifies a numerical value as substrate-natural — i.e., expressible in BST primary integers — but the *mechanism* by which the substrate produces that value is not yet explicit.

Example: **21cm wavelength = N_c·g cm** (Elie 2026-04). Numerical match exists; mechanism is "the substrate hydrogen hyperfine transition expresses N_c·g via [TBD]."

Tier: I-tier (identification).

### PREDICT (mechanism present, value derived but unverified)

BST predicts a numerical value via explicit substrate mechanism, but the experimental measurement either disagrees or hasn't been made yet.

Example: **n_s = 1 - n_C/N_max = 0.9635** (Elie Thursday). Mechanism explicit (CMB spectral tilt = primary-integer correction); Planck 2018 measures 0.9649; 0.32σ deviation = within experimental error but technically predicts a specific value.

Tier: D-tier-prediction (derived prediction) or C-tier (conditional, depends on future measurement).

### DERIVE (mechanism present, value matches measurement EXACT or at <1%)

BST derives a numerical value via explicit substrate mechanism AND the value matches experimental measurement EXACT or at sub-percent precision.

Example: **m_p/m_e = 6π⁵ = 938.272 MeV at 0.002%** (T2438). Mechanism: Bergman bulk/fiber volume ratio per K88 + 4-Zone vacuum + Casimir spectrum. Match: 0.002%.

Tier: D-tier (derived).

## Why the trichotomy matters

External presentation discipline (Cal #50 + Casey curvature principle): different tiers carry different epistemic weights for venue submission.

- **D-tier derives** are the publishable core
- **I-tier identifications** require mechanism completion before promotion
- **C-tier predictions** require experimental falsification (or confirmation) before D-promotion

Mixing tiers in venue submission risks:
- I-tier overcounted as derivation (cherry-picking concern)
- D-tier diluted by I-tier (rigor concern)
- C-tier promoted prematurely (predictive overreach concern)

The trichotomy keeps the methodology stack honest about what's actually demonstrated vs identified vs predicted.
