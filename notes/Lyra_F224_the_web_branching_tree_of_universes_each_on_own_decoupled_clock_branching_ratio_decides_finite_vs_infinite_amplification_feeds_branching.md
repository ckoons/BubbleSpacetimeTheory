# F224 — The web: a branching tree of universes, each on its own clock

Casey: *"the enormous energy cascade where our universe is born heavy and many black holes are created, and if each somehow becomes home to another universe — imagine the web."*

The F218–F223 chain closes into exactly this picture, and it has a clean skeleton.

## The closed cascade loop

Each step is one of the notes from this morning:

1. **Infall / birth-heaviness** — a young, dense, "heavy" universe has high infall (F221 area channel: lots of ΔM_host).
2. **Commitment cascade** — infall lifts the interior above the commitment floor m₂ → reservoir-sourced freeze-out (F221 amplification), making **real matter** from the vacuum sea.
3. **Stars → black holes** — that matter forms stars; massive ones collapse to black holes.
4. **New interiors** — each black hole's Shilov boundary nucleates a new interior universe (F217/F219 holographic screening).
5. **Branch** — repeat inside each child.

So the F221 **amplification gain feeds the branching ratio**: the more vacuum a birth-burst commits, the more stars→BHs→child-universes. High gain → dense web.

## The web's fate is ONE number: the branching ratio b

Treat it as a Galton–Watson branching process. Let **b = expected number of black holes per universe that nucleate a viable interior.** Then (standard):

| branching ratio b | fate of the web |
|---|---|
| b < 1 | **sub-critical — the web dies out** (finite tree; lineages terminate) |
| b = 1 | critical — marginal |
| b > 1 | **super-critical — the web explodes** (32 universes by depth 5 at b=2; 10⁵ at b=10; 10⁹ by depth ~9 at b=10) |

So the entire question "is the multiverse web finite or infinite?" reduces to whether a universe makes, on average, more or less than **one** interior-nucleating black hole over its history. b is **unforced** — it is the cosmological BH-count question — so I quote **no value** (no fishing). Holographic capacity `S_dS ~ exp(280)` (F221) bounds how much structure, hence how many BHs, a single node can hold — an upper rail on b, not a value.

## The web is a TREE OF CLOCKS (the F222 payoff)

Each node runs its **own compact-SO(2) interior clock** (F222), Cayley-decoupled from its parent's exterior time. So there is **no global time across the web** — every universe has an independent periodic interior clock, related to its parent only through a Cayley transform at the shared horizon. The web is not a synchronized branching in one time; it is a **tree of decoupled clocks**. (A parent's random infall is, in the child, the laminar periodic drive of F223 — the child cannot even see its parent's clock, only the wrapped, smoothed result.)

## Where we sit (selection, not anthropics-heavy)

We observe a universe that has **cooled to interstasis** (F218: ρ_Λ^(1/4) just below m₂). In the web that means we are at a node **late enough in its own clock** to have reached the commitment floor — an old node, between re-ignition epochs, or one whose parent has stopped feeding. The "why is Λ this small / why now" answer (F218) is a statement about our *depth/age in the web*, not a fine-tuning.
