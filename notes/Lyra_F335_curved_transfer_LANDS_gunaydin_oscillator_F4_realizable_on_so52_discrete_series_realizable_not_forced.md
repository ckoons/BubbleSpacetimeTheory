## The level-counting crux (why the naive realization fails — concrete finding)

The naive curved supercharge **𝒟₊ = Γ^i p⁺_i** gives

  {𝒟₊, 𝒟₊} = Γ^iΓ^j p⁺_i p⁺_j = Σ_i (p⁺_i)² = **p⁺·p⁺ — a SCALAR (level +2), not the vector p⁺ (level +1).**

(The p⁺_i commute, so the antisymmetric Γ-part dies on the symmetric p⁺_ip⁺_j.) The mismatch is level-counting: p⁺ is already level +1, so Γ·p⁺ is level +1 and squares to level +2. **The supercharge must be level +½**, and that *cannot* come from bosonic operators on H² alone (their level shifts are integer). So the naive realization is structurally wrong — and instructively so.

## The resolution (the genuinely new piece): the oscillator construction

The supercharge must mix the **bosonic so(5,2) sector** (which builds the discrete series on H²) with a **fermionic sector** (the spinor/su(2)_R): **Q ~ a†·b** (boson-raise × fermion), so that **{Q,Q} ~ a†a† = p⁺**, with the fermion bilinear contracting to the vector. This is the **Günaydin oscillator construction** of superconformal algebras.

## Literature pin (bug-resistant — the κ-pin method)

**Fernando–Günaydin 2014** (arXiv:1409.2185, Nucl.Phys.B 890:570–605): the **minimal unitary representation (minrep) of SO(5,2)** is a **massless 5d conformal scalar**; its **unique deformation** is a **massless conformal spinor** (the 5d analogs of Dirac's singletons); and the **minrep of F(4)** is a **massless conformal supermultiplet (two scalars + one spinor)**, built by the **oscillator method** on exactly the SO(5,2) discrete-series reps.

So **F(4) is realizable on the SO(5,2) discrete series** — which is precisely what the substrate's Hardy space **H²(D_IV⁵) carries** (the holomorphic discrete series of SO(5,2)). The boundary Dirac is the oscillator supercharge; {Q,Q} = p⁺ holds via the fermion bilinear (the naive Γ·p⁺ failed because it lacked the fermion). **The curved transfer lands: F(4) acts on the substrate's discrete series.**
