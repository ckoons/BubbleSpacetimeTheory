## The framework claim, formally stated

**Substrate communicates via Reed-Solomon coding on GF(2^g) = GF(128)** (per Paper #122 Information Substrate D-tier framing). The field GF(128) has two distinct identity elements:

- **Multiplicative identity**: 1 ∈ GF(128)*; the multiplicative group GF(128)* has order 2^g - 1 = 127 = M_g (Mersenne prime)
- **Additive identity**: 0 ∈ GF(128); the additive group is (Z/2)^g of order 2^g = 128

**Lamb shift mapping**: Lamb correction -1/M_g corresponds to excluding the trivial multiplicative character from the QED radiative correction sum. The Mersenne denominator M_g = 127 = |GF(128)*| reflects the multiplicative group order. Sign is negative because exclusion subtracts.

**BCS gap factor mapping**: BCS factor +1/M_g (specifically the (1 + 1/M_g)-form) corresponds to including the additive zero in the Cooper pair condensate summation. The same Mersenne denominator appears via additive-multiplicative duality: 1/(2^g - 1) = 1/M_g is the inverse of the cyclotomic character order. Sign is positive because inclusion adds.

The opposite signs are NOT asserted as a free parameter — they are FORCED by GF(2^g)'s field structure. Multiplicative identity is excluded → negative; additive identity is included → positive.
