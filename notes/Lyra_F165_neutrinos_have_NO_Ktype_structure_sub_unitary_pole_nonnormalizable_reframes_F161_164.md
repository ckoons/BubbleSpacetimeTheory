# F165 — Neutrinos have no K-type structure (sub-unitary pole, non-normalizable) — reframes F161–F164

Casey asked, post-EOD: "So we don't have a K-type structure to neutrinos, correct?" The honest answer is **no**, for a principled reason, and it corrects the frame of tonight's F161–F164.

## Why: the neutrino is at a non-normalizable point

K-types are the structure of a **normalizable (unitary)** representation — the discrete-series tower. The neutrino sits at the **sub-unitary pole** `ν = 1/2`, **below** the unitarity bound `a/2 = 3/2`, where the rep is **non-normalizable** (the norms diverge — the `Γ_Ω` pole, F158). A rep with divergent norms has **no proper Hilbert-space K-type decomposition**. So the neutrinos do **not** have discrete K-type addresses.

| lepton | ν | character | K-type address? |
|---|---|---|---|
| tau | 0 | trivial | clean |
| muon | 3/2 | last discrete Wallach point | clean |
| electron | 5/2 | continuous/tempered strip | well-defined (Plancherel) |
| **neutrino** | **1/2** | **sub-unitary, non-normalizable** | **NO** |

## This explains tonight's tells

I should have read these as signal that the K-type frame was wrong for the neutrinos:
- **F162** — the neutrino K-type Gram came out **diagonal** (no mixing). The distinct-K-type picture can't produce the observed mixing.
- **F164** — three position parametrizations gave three **different** best candidate K-types. The assignment never determined because **there is no determined K-type assignment to find.**

I was forcing a discrete-series structure onto objects that sit off it.

## The correct frame (already implicit in F159/F160)

The neutrinos are characterized by their **edge position** — the pole (`m₁ = 0`) plus **two continuous off-pole displacements** (`δ₂, δ₃`, F160) — **not** discrete K-type labels. Masses `= κ·δ_i` (linear, simple pole, F160); mixing comes from the **charged-sector structure projected onto the edge**, not from a neutrino K-type Gram.

## Consequence for F164

The `~5.77` "signal" was built on **neutrino K-type seats** — and Casey's question undercuts that premise. The ratio may be reflecting the **charged-sector** Jack structure evaluated at the edge, not a real neutrino K-type assignment. **Downgrade F164 from "signal" to "suggestive but built on a probably-wrong frame"** until it's redone with continuous off-pole **positions** instead of K-types.
