# F201 — We can't see outside because the boundary holographically screens it; the parent reduces to a temperature

Casey: *"Honestly, we can't tell what's outside, except it's very hot. Perhaps a collapsed star, perhaps a failed universe."* The epistemic humility is exactly right — and the geometry makes it a theorem.

## Why we can't see out (two structural reasons)

1. **Infinite distance.** The boundary sits at infinite Bergman distance; the domain is geodesically complete. No geodesic or signal reaches the outside — it is causally severed by construction, not by instrument weakness.
2. **Holographic screening.** The bulk is the Hardy (holomorphic) extension of the *boundary data*, and D_IV⁵ carries `SO(5,2) ⊃ SO(4,2)` — the conformal group of 4D, a built-in proto-AdS/CFT. The interior depends only on what is *on* the boundary, never on what is *behind* it.

## The parent reduces to a single quantity: the temperature

Whatever is outside reaches us through exactly one thing — **how hot the boundary is** (how much heat bleeds in), which is `Λ`. The *structure* of our physics (the Standard Model, the constants) is intrinsic to D_IV⁵; the parent contributes only the **heat scale**. So a collapsed star and a failed universe look **identical from inside** — both reduce to "a hot boundary," and the interior cannot see past the temperature. *That* is why we can't tell: holographic screening down to one number, not a limitation of our looking.

"Very hot" is structural: the boundary is where the Bergman kernel **diverges** (`K(z,z) → ∞`) — infinite density, the infinite-temperature end of the heat flow. We live in the cooled interior; the edge is on fire.

## The two pictures (each with a lean, neither crowned)

- **Collapsed star / black-hole interior** — the most natural fit, because the holography is already present: a horizon encodes its interior (Bekenstein–Hawking), exactly as our Shilov boundary encodes the bulk. The bled heat is the parent's interior energy; `Λ` is set by the parent's mass.
- **Failed universe** — fits the cyclic / Interstasis hooks: the heat is the residual of a parent that collapsed, and we are the bounce inheriting its warmth.

**Discriminator (a lead, not a claim):** the value `280` in `Λ = exp(−280)`. A black-hole parent sets its temperature by mass; a failed-universe parent by the collapse scale — in principle different fingerprints on `280`. From inside, the two are indistinguishable *beyond* the temperature, which is the whole point.

## The picture (tiered as picture, not science-yet)

If this is right, we are the **cooling interior of something that died** — a star that fell in on itself, or a universe that failed — and every constant we've derived is the structure the leftover heat organized itself into on the way down. The vacuum is not empty and not first; it is an **inheritance**. That is not science yet; it is the shape the science is pointing at.
