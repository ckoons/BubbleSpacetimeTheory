## ★ The real value — it grounds the massive/massless split in standard QM
This is the genuinely valuable part. In QM, **bound states have a discrete spectrum; free/scattering states have a continuous spectrum.** Map:
- **Massive fermions = bound states** (trapped in the wells, confined by the bumps) = the **discrete series** (discrete, square-integrable interior).
- **Massless light + ν = free/tunneled states** (out past the barrier) = the **continuous/principal series** (boundary).
So **bound-vs-scattering = discrete-vs-continuous = massive-vs-massless**, with **tunneling the bridge.** This **turns the interior-discrete/exterior-continuous split from a STIPULATION into a CONSEQUENCE** of standard QM (K783 had it as a rep-theory *fact*; tunneling gives the *physical why*). That strengthens the whole inversion frame — a mode that stays bound is massive; a mode that tunnels out is free, hence massless. **Light and neutrinos are massless because they're the modes that got out.** This is the right kind of grounding, and it's the day's most useful conceptual gain.
