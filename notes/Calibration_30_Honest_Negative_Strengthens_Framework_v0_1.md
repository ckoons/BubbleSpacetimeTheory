# Calibration #30 candidate — Honest-Negative-Strengthens-Framework Pattern

## The pattern

When Cal-discipline catches a framing-flaw at peak-convergence moments (synthesis overstatement, back-fit reasoning, premature SVC/RATIFIED promotion, etc.), and the team REFRAMES to a sharper structural understanding that survives the catch, the **REFRAMED framework is genuinely sharper than the pre-catch version** — not just hedged or downgraded, but structurally clearer.

This is **DISTINCT from "Cal caught a flaw → team retracted":**
- Honest-negative-strengthens **produces NEW structural content** in the reframe
- Pure retraction produces NO new content (the claim is just withdrawn)

The pattern names the phenomenon where the discipline functions as a **generator**, not just a filter — each catch produces SHARPER framing, not just LESS framing.

## Formal pattern statement

For a candidate framing F_initial with flaw φ caught by Cal-discipline (Cal #27 / Cal #29 / Cal #133 / external-voice cold-read / etc.):

1. **Negative caught**: φ is explicitly identified + acknowledged
2. **Reframe produced**: F_reframed differs from F_initial in structural content (not just hedging)
3. **Strengthening verified**: F_reframed has stronger substrate-mechanism content OR sharper structural distinctions OR more honest tier disposition than F_initial would have had if uncaught

When all three conditions hold, the instance counts as honest-negative-strengthens-framework.

**Not honest-negative-strengthens** (similar-looking patterns that don't qualify):
- Cal flags risk → team retracts entirely (no reframe; pure retraction)
- Cal flags risk → team hedges with caveats but no new content (hedging, not strengthening)
- Cal asks open question → team provides multiple candidates (no flaw caught; pattern is multi-mechanism-convergence, distinct)
