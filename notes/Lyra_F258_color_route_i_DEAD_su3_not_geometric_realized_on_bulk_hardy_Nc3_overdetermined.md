## Three obstructions (all dimension-counting; nothing from memory)

**(1) SU(3) cannot act on 3 real dimensions.** The faithful real-dimensions of SU(3) irreps are {6 (the fundamental 3, complex → 6 real), 8 (adjoint), 12, 20, …}; the smallest faithful real dimension is **6**. There is no 3-real-dimensional faithful SU(3) representation. The short-root multiplicity space is **3 real** directions, and dim SO(3) = 3 fits it exactly (the SO(3) vector). So **M = SO(3) is forced; SU(3) is excluded on that space by rep-dimension.**

**(2) J cannot complexify an odd-dimensional space.** A complex structure satisfies J² = −1, which requires **even** real dimension. The short-root space is 3-dimensional (odd). So J cannot turn "3 real directions" into "C³ carrying SU(3)" — the parity obstruction I flagged last turn is real, not a hunch. (J is the *global* complex structure on the 10-real-dim tangent space; it mixes the short-root space with other pieces, it does not act within it.)

**(3) SU(3) is not even a subgroup of SO(5).** For SU(3) ⊂ SO(5) we'd need a faithful real rep of SU(3) of dimension ≤ 5. The faithful real dims start at 6. **No such rep exists** → SU(3) ⊄ SO(5). Since K = SO(5)×SO(2), **color is not an isometry subgroup of K**, and it does **not** act geometrically on the holomorphic tangent space p⁺ = C⁵ (the 5 of SO(5)) either. Color is *not* a tangent-space phenomenon at all.
