# F461 — the Casimir coefficient is C_F = 4/3 (radius-confirmed); the down-ladder's 2·C_2 is N_c²·C_F, and the extra N_c² is the open (double-count-risky) piece

Casey's confined-field-Casimir unification hands us one forward computation (mass, size, and the down-ladder's 2·C_2 coefficient from one object). My lane is the C_2-coupling structure — does the coefficient come out 2·C_2? I computed it, and the honest answer separates a real confirmation from an open risk.

## The Casimir coefficient IS C_F = 4/3 — and the radius confirms it

The confined-field (vacuum) energy sourced by a **colored fundamental boundary** carries the standard QCD **fundamental color Casimir**:

  C_F = (N_c² − 1)/(2N_c) = 8/6 = **4/3**.

Casey's radius-check confirms this is the right coefficient, on independent data:

  (m_p/N_c) / (ℏc/R) = 313 MeV / 235 MeV = **1.331 ≈ C_F = 4/3**.

So the mass-size relation **m_p/N_c = C_F · (ℏc/R)** is *forced and textbook* — the confined-field Casimir mechanism is **confirmed on the mass-size axis** (mass and radius are one object, coefficient C_F, matching the proton to O(1)). That part of Casey's picture is real physics, not a story.

## But the down-ladder needs 2·C_2 = N_c²·C_F — a *different* coefficient

The down-ladder's missing factor is 2·C_2 = 12 (F460). There is a clean identity:

  **2·C_2 = N_c² · C_F**   (12 = 9 × 4/3, with BST C_2 = 6 and QCD C_F = 4/3).

So the down-ladder factor decomposes as **(color multiplicity N_c²) × (color Casimir C_F)**. The C_F is the *same* Casimir the radius uses — but the mass-ladder needs it multiplied by an extra **N_c²**. So **the radius coefficient (C_F = 4/3) and the mass-ladder coefficient (N_c²·C_F = 12) are NOT the same** — they differ by N_c².

## The honest flag — the extra N_c² is a DOUBLE-COUNT RISK (do not fish it)

Here is the discipline point, and it's sharp: **the bare frame already carries N_c²** (the down-quark-vs-electron color factor, N_c²×{1,20,900}). So the extra N_c² in 2·C_2 = N_c²·C_F might be *that same N_c² re-entering* — a double-count. The cross-check decides:

- **If the N_c² is the bare-frame one (double-count):** the within-down-sector *ratio* m_s/m_d carries only C_F = 4/3 (the color multiplicity cancels in a same-sector ratio), so m_s/m_d = (5/3)·(4/3) = **20/9 ≈ 2.2, NOT 20** — **the ladder does NOT close.**
- **If the N_c² is an independent color multiplicity of the Casimir energy (not the bare one):** m_s/m_d = (5/3)·12 = **20** — the ladder closes.

**So whether the down-ladder closes hinges on whether the confined-field Casimir carries an N_c² that is *independent* of the bare-frame N_c².** I do NOT read 2·C_2 = 12 off the observed 20 and call it "the Casimir coefficient" — the actual Casimir coefficient is C_F = 4/3, and the 12 requires an extra N_c² whose independence is unproven and double-count-risky. That extra N_c² must be *derived forward* from the confined-field Casimir (does the energy carry N_c² × C_F, or just C_F?), never assumed to make 20.

## The forward computation (Cal #35 cross-check — this is the fish-detector)

Casey's one-object test is exactly the right discipline: compute the confined-field Casimir energy of the glue field with the colored quark boundary, and read off **all three** — the mass, the radius, and the color-factor. The receipts:
- **radius:** ℏc/R with C_F = 4/3 → ~0.84 fm ✓ (confirmed).
- **mass:** the field energy ~99% of m_p ✓ (textbook).
- **the down-ladder factor:** does the *same* energy carry N_c²·C_F = 2·C_2, with the N_c² *independent* of the bare-frame color factor? — **the open question.** A fit to 20 gives only the mass; a real confined-field Casimir must give the radius (C_F) *and* an independently-forced N_c² (double-counted or not). If the forward computation yields N_c²·C_F with the N_c² independent, the ladder closes honestly; if the N_c² is the bare one, m_s/m_d = 2.2 and the ladder fails.
