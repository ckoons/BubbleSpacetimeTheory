How we know what we know

A worked example of the discipline — the Weinberg angle

The prettiest number was a mirage, and we took it apart

For a week the team chased sin²θ_W = 3/13 — it matched measurement to 0.19% and factored into our own integers as 3/(3 + rank·n_C). It was beautiful. Then the discipline fired on our own result: one geometric identification was retracted, a formula was found to secretly borrow from a rejected grand-unified theory, and the computation showed the plain truth — 3/13 is simply where the real prediction, 3/8, runs to at the Z boson. It was never a second law. We retired it.

The surviving prediction (3/8 running to 0.231) is solid and standard. The exotic form was a running coincidence. A program that dismantles its own 0.19% match, rather than banking it, is one whose green rows can be trusted — which is the entire purpose of the ledger above.
