# F302 — SO(4) *defines* chirality, the SO(2) super-grading *realizes* it (refining F300)

Casey said "remember linear algebra," and Grace and Elie both flagged the same gap in F300. Doing the explicit tangent decomposition shows their catch is valid: F300 got the *definition* of chirality right (SO(4)) but mislocated its *realization*.

## What survives F300 (SOLID)

1. **SO(4) ⊂ SO(5) is the compact/Euclidean form of the spacetime Lorentz group.** Existence is clean: SO(4,2) (the 4D conformal group) ⊂ SO(5,2) (Casey #14 chain), so maximal compacts give SO(4) × SO(2) ⊂ SO(5) × SO(2) = K; and the holomorphic-tangent vector splits 5 = 4 + 1 (spacetime + one transverse direction). (Elie's note: K-SO(4) is compact while spacetime SO(3,1) is noncompact — this is a Wick-rotation identification, K-SO(4) = the Euclidean form of SO(3,1), stated as such.)
2. **It defines the Weyl reps.** Under spacetime SO(4) = SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R: the tangent V_4 = (2,2), and the Weyl spinors are (2,1)_L and (1,2)_R. The left/right distinction *exists* because of this SO(4).
3. **No independent weak Cartan.** SO(5) has rank 2 — the torus {H_1, H_2} — so T_3^weak ∈ span{H_1, H_2}; the weak isospin is not a new commuting direction.

## What softens (F300 over-claim, retracted as *forced*)

F300 said: "weak SU(2) = Lorentz-left, so chirality is forced." **Maximal-compact matching gives only the *existence* of a spacetime SO(4) ⊂ SO(5).** There are many SO(4)'s sharing the rank-2 torus, so the **alignment** of the *weak* SO(4) with the *spacetime* SO(4) is **not forced** by maxcompact matching alone. Grace's and Elie's catch is valid. The rank-counting (point 3) shows the weak isospin uses the shared torus — but "uses the torus" is weaker than "is the Lorentz-left factor."

## The linear-algebra reason (the substantive point)

Decompose the holomorphic tangent explicitly:

  **p⁺ = V_5 ⊗ C_{(+1)} = [ (2,2) ⊕ (1,1) ] ⊗ C_{(+1)},**

where C_{(+1)} is the SO(2) charge of the complex structure. Every vector in p⁺ carries the **uniform** SO(2) charge +1 — so **p⁺ alone does not grade chirality.** The chiral split cannot come from the bosonic tangent. It comes from the **F(4) supercharge's SO(2) super-grading** — the (4,2){±½} split under the interior-time circle (Grace's route, the arc's F237/F238/Elie 4268). The ±½ grading *is* the chirality.

- **SO(4) ⊂ SO(5) DEFINES chirality** — it provides the Weyl left/right reps (2,1)/(1,2). (This is F300's correct half, and Cal #321's SO(4)=Lorentz stands.)

So the chirality cluster's load-bearing mechanism is the **SO(2) super-grading**, with SO(4)=Lorentz as the structural backdrop — not the weak=Lorentz-left identification.
