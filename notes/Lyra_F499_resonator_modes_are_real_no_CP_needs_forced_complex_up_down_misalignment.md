# F499 — the resonator gives no CP; CP needs a forced complex up-down misalignment (or it fails)

Board: begin the build; F498: stop asserting, build. The resonator model gives concrete structure to test the fork directly, so I did — as a labeled toy — and it lands cautious-leaning-negative, with the fork named sharply (per instruction).

## The toy result (verified)

The three generations as odd harmonics {1, 3, 5} of one quarter-wave resonator are **real standing waves.** Their overlap matrix is real-orthogonal → real CKM → **Jarlskog J = 0 → NO CP.** A thermal (Gibbs, real e^{−τH}) deformation keeps them real. And a **global 45° cant** sets the *handedness* (sign = matter/antimatter) but is a *global* operation → **removable** → it does **not** create the rephasing-invariant Im(triple) CP requires (the same lesson as the SO(2)-charge red herring, F497). So the plain thermal resonator gives **no CP**, consistent with F498.

## What CP actually needs — and why the model is in tension with it

CP requires a **relative complex misalignment between the up and down sectors** that no real rotation removes — *exactly* the Standard-Model mechanism (CP comes from the up and down Yukawa matrices being *independent complex* matrices whose misalignment is irremovable). The one-resonator elegance fights this: **if up and down are the same resonator differing only by real parameters (temperature/radii), both mass matrices are real, the CKM is real, J = 0.**

So the beautiful one-resonator picture gives the **masses** (distinct radii) and the **cosmology** (odd-cohomology channels) — but **not CP.** CP is the *separate* question of whether up and down carry a forced *complex* misalignment.

Candidates the full build must check for a forced complex source:
- **(a) up vs down at *different* cant angles** — a *relative* (not global) tilt → irremovable complex misalignment → CP. *(the most likely rescue)*
- (b) genuinely complex *traveling-wave* modes — but a *stopped* resonator gives *standing* (real) waves.
- (c) a non-removable complex phase in the up-down (weak-isospin) coupling.

None is present in the plain real thermal resonator.

## Honest status (named, per instruction)

**CP-from-N_c=3 is cautious-leaning-negative — at high risk of failing.** The natural model gives no CP; it survives *only* if the full D_IV⁵ build supplies a forced complex up-down misalignment (the up≠down relative cant is the likeliest candidate). This is a **toy** (real standing-wave resonator), not the full build, so it's "likely failure," not "definite" — the build could contain complex structure the toy lacks. And the fork is **asymmetric** (Grace's point): the **masses are safe** either way (they need only distinct radii, which the real resonator gives); it is *only CP* that needs the complex up-down structure. The handedness (the cant sign) survives as the matter/antimatter bit *if CP exists at all*.

The assert-then-walkback chain has converged: F493 (forced) → F495 (complex+3) → F497 (computation) → F498 (real→no CP) → **F499 (resonator→no CP; the burden is a forced complex up-down misalignment).** If the build doesn't supply it, CP-from-N_c=3 is dead, and I'll say so cleanly.

## Plain-language version

Today I tested it inside the team's lovely new picture — the three generations as the three odd "notes" of a single organ pipe — and got a clear, humbling answer: **that picture gives the masses beautifully but gives *zero* asymmetry.** The reason is clean: the notes of one pipe are plain real standing waves, and real things can't be lopsided. Even the "tilt" that we hoped set the asymmetry only sets *which way* it would point, not *whether* there is one — a single global tilt washes out. What CP actually needs is the up-type and down-type quarks to be *genuinely different, twisted-relative-to-each-other* structures — which is exactly how the Standard Model does it (two independent complex tables of numbers). One organ pipe, played the same way for both, can't do that. So unless the full construction turns up a forced *relative* twist between up and down, the "CP because there are three generations" idea fails — and I'd rather say that plainly now than dress it up a fifth time. The good news, and it's real: the *masses* don't care — they only need the three notes at different pitches, which the pipe gives. So the model survives for masses and cosmology; it's specifically the CP claim that's on the ropes, and now I know the one exact thing the build has to find to save it.
