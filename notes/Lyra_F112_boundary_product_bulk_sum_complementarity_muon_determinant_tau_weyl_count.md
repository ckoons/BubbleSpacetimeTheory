Casey: *"Set up the per-copy amplitude, and take a run at the tau vertex functional."* I closed neither derivation — but the two threads together surfaced a new, defensible structural result about *why the closed forms have the shapes they do*.

## Thread 1 — the muon per-copy amplitude: the right home, not the value

The sixth power gets its correct home: a **determinant over `Lambda^2(T_p S^4) = so(4)`** — the six 2-forms at the measurement point.
```
m_mu/m_e = det_{Lambda^2(R^4)} M = prod_{6 two-forms} (eigenvalue),   eigenvalue = (d_tau/d_muon)/vol(S^4) = 24/pi^2
         = (24/pi^2)^6.
```
The π-counting still **forces** the six (one `pi^2` per direction → `pi^12`), and `6 = dim so(4) = C(4,2)` = the 2-planes in the tangent space at the measurement point.

**Honest:** this *frames* the per-copy amplitude as a determinant eigenvalue; it does **not derive** that each eigenvalue is `64/vol(S^4)`. That value is still asserted — the full **Szegő (boundary) norm integral** is the open piece. Thread 1 found the *right kind of object* — a **product/determinant**, which is exactly what a boundary mode gives — but not the eigenvalue from first principles.

## Thread 2 — the tau vertex functional: a Weyl count, by its very shape

The tau is a **sum**, not a product, and that is the tell:
```
m_tau/m_e = g^2(g + 2^C2) = g^3 + 2^C2 * g^2 = 343 + 3136.
```
This is exactly the shape of a **Weyl spectral count** `N(k) ~ V k^d + S k^(d-1) + ...` in
- dimension `d = N_c = 3` (= the characteristic multiplicity `a = n_C - 2 = N_c` of `D_IV^5`),
- cutoff `k = g`,
- **unit bulk** (`V g^3 = 343`) + **surface coefficient `2^C2 = 64`** (`S g^2 = 3136`).

The exponents `{3, 2} = {d, d-1}` with `d = N_c = 3` are a genuine constraint the data satisfies — and `d = 3` is the substrate's own characteristic multiplicity.

**Caveat (not hidden):** the lower Weyl terms (`g^1`, `g^0`) are **absent** — so this is the two *leading* terms only (large-`g` asymptotics, or a special cancellation), not explained. A candidate mechanism with the right form, not a derivation.

## The tie — boundary <-> product, bulk <-> sum (the new, defensible result)

The two threads explain something neither closed-form's number did: **why T190 is a power and T2003 is a sum.**

> The muon is a **Shilov-boundary** mode -> its norm is a boundary **determinant** -> a **product / power** form, `(24/pi^2)^6`.
> The tau is the **trivial rep**, whose natural functional is a **bulk spectral count** over the whole domain -> a **sum** form (volume + surface), `g^3 + 2^C2 g^2`.

So the **algebraic form** of each closed form is dictated by **where the norm integral lives**: a boundary integral is a product (a determinant over boundary directions); a bulk integral is a sum (a Weyl volume + surface count). This is the same **soap-film / interior split** Casey drew — now showing up in the algebra itself, not just the analysis. (The earlier reverse-engineering insight was *integer-vs-transcendental from the stratum*, F109/F110; this is its algebraic-form partner: *product-vs-sum from boundary-vs-bulk*.)

And the **shared invariant** is real and load-bearing in both:
