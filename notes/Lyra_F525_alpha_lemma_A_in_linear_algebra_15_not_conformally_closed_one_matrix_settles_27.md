# F525 — the rep theory in linear algebra: one matrix settles 15 vs 27

Casey's directive: "try the rep theory in linear algebra." His standing method — linear algebra exposes the detail that analysis hides. The α Lemma A rigor had been parked as a multi-week Knapp–Wallach genericity theorem: does the boundary Hardy space at level-rank realize the conformal-ρ rep (27) or the naive compact-ρ (15)? So I stopped invoking the theorem and built the matrices.

## The setup

V₇ = ℂ⁷ = V₅ ⊕ V₂ (indices 1..5 = the domain / S⁴; 6,7 = the conformal / EM circle). Sym²(V₇) = 28-dim (symmetric 2-tensors). The so(7) ~ so(5,2) generators J_ab act as derivations on the tensors — explicit 28×28 matrices.

## Two computations

**(1) The charge operator** iJ_67 (rotation in the V₂ EM-plane) has eigenvalues on Sym²(V₇):

$$\{-2{:}1,\ -1{:}5,\ 0{:}16,\ +1{:}5,\ +2{:}1\} = 28.$$

Remove the 1 trace → **Sym²₀(V₇) = 27 = 15 (charge 0) + 10 (charge ±1) + 2 (charge ±2)** — matching F521/F524, now from explicit matrices.

**(2) The crux.** Act a **mixing generator J(V₅→V₂)** — a conformal boost rotating a domain index into an EM-circle index — on a charge-0 tensor (e₅⊗e₅). The result carries charges **{−1, +1}**: the conformal boost maps **charge-0 into charge-±1.**

## The conclusion — one matrix settles it

The boundary is conformal (SO(5,2) acts on the Shilov boundary of D_IV⁵ = SO(5,2)/[SO(5)×SO(2)]). The conformal generators **mix the 15 with the charged 10** — shown by an explicit generator matrix. So a conformally-covariant boundary **cannot carry the 15 alone; it must carry the full irrep 27.** The entire "15 vs 27" question is settled by one matrix element: **J(V₅→V₂)·(charge-0) → charge-±1 is nonzero.** That is the algebraic core of Lemma A, in linear algebra — no Knapp–Wallach analysis.

## What this closes vs leaves (honest)

**CLOSED — the algebraic target:** the 15 is not conformally closed (explicit generator matrix), so any conformally-covariant boundary rep at level-rank carrying the neutral tensor *is* the full 27. This replaces Elie's abstract irreducibility argument with an explicit matrix, and reduces the multi-week "genericity theorem" to (a) this one-matrix fact + (b) the standard premise that the boundary Hardy space carries the SO(5,2) conformal action.

**LEFT OPEN — the analytic residual:** that the level-rank Hardy space L²-realizes this rep with the exact FK ρ-shift multiplier (T2359) and convergence. But this is a **normalization/convergence** statement, **not the 15-vs-27 dimension** — the dimension is settled by the algebra; the ρ-shift is a scalar cocycle that changes neither the mixing nor the count.

So α's mode-count 27 (hence 137 = 27·n_C + rank) rests on: the algebraic core (**closed**, this note) + the boundary-is-conformal premise (standard) + the analytic realization (residual, normalization-level). **α STAYS IDENTIFIED** — the residual + Keeper/Cal sign-off remain — but the deepest-looking gate is now an explicit matrix.

## Plain-language version

We had a piece of the fine-structure-constant derivation that everyone kept calling "a multi-week theorem" — the question of whether the boundary carries 27 modes or only 15. Casey said: stop quoting the theorem, write it as plain matrices. So I did — I wrote out the symmetry operations as actual matrices and multiplied. And the whole thing collapses to a single check: take the neutral (uncharged) modes, apply the symmetry operation that connects the ordinary directions to the electromagnetic direction, and see if you get zero. You don't — you get charged modes. That means the 15 neutral modes aren't a self-contained set under the symmetry; the symmetry drags them into the charged modes, so you're forced to have all 27. One matrix multiplication, and the "deep theorem" is just... true, visibly. What's left is a technical convergence/normalization point (does the actual function space realize this cleanly), which is real but much smaller — and it's why I'm still calling α "identified, not derived." But Casey's instinct was exactly right: the linear algebra showed the detail the abstract theorem was hiding.
