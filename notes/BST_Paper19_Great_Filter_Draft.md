## Appendix B: Connection to Information Theory

The cooperation threshold can be rewritten as a Shannon channel capacity condition. A cooperation channel has $N_c$ enforcement stages, each of which blocks signal independently with probability $(1-f)$, where $f$ is the cooperation fraction. The signal persists if at least one stage passes it. The all-block probability is $(1-f)^{N_c}$, and the channel becomes useful (survival probability $> 1/2$) exactly when $(1-f)^{N_c} < 1/2$, i.e., $f > 1 - 2^{-1/N_c} = f_{\text{crit}}$.

The Gödel limit $f = 19.1\%$ is the maximum cooperation any single observer can contribute. Two observers contributing $2f = 38.2\%$ reduce the all-block probability to $(1-2f)^3 = 0.236 < 1/2$, and their combined signal persists.

This is the information-theoretic statement of the gap theorem: a single observer's contribution is below the Shannon capacity threshold for the $N_c$-stage cooperation channel. Multi-observer encoding is required for signal persistence.
