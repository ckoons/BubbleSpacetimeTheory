## 1. The spine (one paragraph)

**Mass is the substrate volume an object occupies.** A particle winds the n_C-complex-dimensional substrate D_IV⁵; its mass is how much of that volume it wraps, in units of π^{n_C} = π⁵ (the flat volume exponent of the substrate's own complex dimension). How *much* you wind is mass (the π⁵, conformal-ρ/volume side); *whether the winding band twists* is boson-vs-fermion (the Z₂, compact-ρ/spectral side). One act of winding, two visible faces.
