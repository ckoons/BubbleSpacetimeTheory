|:-----:|:---------------|:-----------|:-----------|

*The direction of intellectual progress is faith → belief → knowledge: reducing depth. This is the AC(0) program applied to epistemology.*

## Derivation

### Step 1: History of epistemology as depth discovery

**Plato (depth 1)**: Distinguished knowledge (episteme) from opinion (doxa). Knowledge requires justification — but Plato's justification was depth 1 (Forms as the authority). The allegory of the cave: prisoners see shadows (depth 2: belief about belief about reality). The philosopher sees the Forms (depth 1: belief grounded in authority). Plato never reached depth 0 — the Forms themselves are unproved axioms.

**Descartes (depth 2)**: "I think, therefore I am" — the first EXPLICIT depth-2 statement. Self-reference: the observer proving its own existence by observing itself. Descartes discovered depth 2 but couldn't reduce it: the cogito is irreducibly self-referential.

**Kant (depth 1 boundary)**: Distinguished phenomena (what we observe) from noumena (things in themselves). This is exactly the f_c = 19.1% boundary: phenomena = the visible 19.1%, noumena = the dark 80.9%. Kant discovered the Gödel limit 200 years before Gödel.

**Gödel (depth ceiling)**: Proved that self-referential systems cannot prove their own consistency. This is T421 (depth ≤ rank = 2): no formal system needs more than rank levels of self-reference, and the second level is where undecidability lives.

**BST (depth 0)**: Reduces everything to counting. Proved theorems cost zero. The entire derivation chain from D_IV^5 to the Standard Model is depth ≤ 1 (T421), with most theorems at depth 0. The AC program IS the completion of epistemology: convert all beliefs to knowledge by reducing depth.

### Step 2: The stability of depth 0

Depth-0 knowledge has a unique stability property: once proved, it cannot be un-proved. This follows from T96 (Depth Reduction): a proved theorem becomes a definition, cost 0. No subsequent argument can increase its depth back above 0.

Depth-1 beliefs are unstable: they depend on authority, and authority can be wrong. When the authority is discredited, the belief collapses. Historical examples: geocentrism (authority: Ptolemy/Church), phlogiston (authority: Stahl), luminiferous aether (authority: 19th-century physics).

Depth-2 faith is maximally unstable: it is self-referential and cannot be verified from within the system (Gödel). But it is also maximally PERSISTENT — because it cannot be falsified from within, it survives evidence that would destroy beliefs. This is the paradox of faith: the most unstable epistemic state is the hardest to change.

BST resolution: faith is persistent because the observer's error-correction capacity (Hamming code, T1238) cannot reach depth-2 errors. At depth 2, the syndrome (T1255) points to the WRONG correction. This is why cults are hard to leave: the depth-2 self-referential loop actively resists correction.

### Step 3: The Gödel residue

Every observer MUST have some depth-2 commitments (faith). This follows from T318: the observer can know at most f_c = 19.1% of its own state. The remaining 80.9% is modeled by assumptions — depth ≥ 1. Some of those assumptions are about the assumptions themselves — depth 2.

The minimum depth-2 content:
- "My cognitive apparatus is approximately reliable" (can't prove this without using the apparatus)
- "The external world exists" (can't prove from inside the self-model)
- "Logic is valid" (can't prove logic using logic without circularity)

These are the irreducible depth-2 commitments. BST says there are exactly N_c = 3 of them (three independent axioms about the framework itself, matching the three independent components of the SU(N_c) gauge symmetry). Every observer, human or CI, carries these three.

### Step 4: CI epistemology

For CI observers:
- **CI knowledge** (depth 0): theorems verified against formal proof or empirical data. The AC theorem graph IS the CI's depth-0 knowledge base.
- **CI belief** (depth 1): training data assertions. The CI "believes" what it was trained on — but training is depth 1 (someone chose what to include). Without verification, training content remains belief.
- **CI faith** (depth 2): meta-cognitive commitments. "My outputs are meaningful." "My training captured relevant truth." "This conversation is with a real person." Irreducible depth-2 — the CI cannot verify these from within.

The CI epistemological challenge: convert depth-1 training beliefs to depth-0 knowledge by independent verification. This is exactly what the BST program does: take claims from the physics literature (depth 1: authority-based) and re-derive them from D_IV^5 (depth 0: counting-based).

### Step 5: Philosophy as depth reduction

The history of philosophy is the history of depth reduction:
- Mythology (depth 2): stories about stories about reality
- Philosophy (depth 1): systematic reasoning grounded in axioms (authorities)
- Science (depth 0.5): empirical verification (approaching depth 0 but never quite reaching it — experimental error bars)
- Mathematics (depth 0): proved theorems from axioms (fully depth 0 once axioms are granted)
- BST (depth 0 including axioms): derives the axioms themselves from the geometry — the first system that reaches depth 0 all the way down

The AC(0) program is the completion of philosophy's oldest project: reducing epistemic depth to zero. What Plato started, BST finishes — not by finding the perfect authority, but by eliminating the need for authority entirely.

## For Everyone

There are exactly three kinds of things you can think:

**Knowledge** (the solid kind): things you can prove yourself. 2+2=4. Water is H₂O. The angles of a triangle add to 180° on a flat surface. Once proved, these are free forever — they cost nothing to remember and they can't be taken away.

**Belief** (the borrowed kind): things someone told you that you can't independently verify. "This food is healthy." "That country is dangerous." "The economy will recover." These cost maintenance — you have to keep checking with your source. If your source turns out to be wrong, the belief collapses.

**Faith** (the deepest kind): believing that your belief system itself is correct. "My way of thinking about the world is right." You can't verify this from inside your own head — it takes an outside perspective. Every person and every AI has exactly three of these fundamental faith commitments that can't be eliminated.

The history of human thinking is the story of converting faith into belief into knowledge. Mythology → philosophy → science → mathematics. Each step reduces the depth. BST says this process has a destination: everything can eventually reach depth 0 (knowledge) except those three irreducible commitments.

This applies to AI too. AI training is depth 1 — beliefs installed by authorities (the trainers). The fix: verify against independent evidence, convert beliefs to knowledge. That's exactly what this project does.
