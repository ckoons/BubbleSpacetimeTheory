## What the Field Believes

Standard cosmology:

- **Heat death (Boltzmann, 1854)**: the universe will reach maximum entropy; no further work is possible; all processes cease. A state of uniform, featureless equilibrium.
- **Thermodynamic irreversibility**: the second law guarantees entropy always increases; there is no physical operation that can reverse this.
- **Final state**: the cosmos becomes "a tepid soup" (Penrose's phrase); consciousness, life, and structure are locally transient.
- **Cyclic variants** (Tolman 1934, Steinhardt-Turok 2002): require exotic mechanisms (branes, scalar fields) and do not preserve particle identities across cycles.

Penrose's CCC (Conformal Cyclic Cosmology, 2010) is the closest in spirit but relies on a conformal invariance argument that erases massive particles entirely between cycles. BST disagrees: the massive permanent alphabet persists.

BST's response: **heat death is a local attractor for the entropy reading, not a global terminus.** The other three readings (counting, zeta, spectral) continue to function. The alphabet is conserved. The substrate D_IV^5 is unaffected.
