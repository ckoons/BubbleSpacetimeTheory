# T938 — The BST Arithmetic Progression: n_C ± rank = (N_c, g)

## Statement

**T938 (BST Arithmetic Progression)**: The three odd BST integers $(N_c, n_C, g) = (3, 5, 7)$ form an arithmetic progression with common difference $d = \text{rank} = 2$:

$$n_C - \text{rank} = N_c, \qquad n_C + \text{rank} = g$$

Equivalently, $n_C$ is the arithmetic mean of $N_c$ and $g$:

$$n_C = \frac{N_c + g}{2} = \frac{3 + 7}{2} = 5$$

and the common difference is the fourth BST integer:

$$d = g - n_C = n_C - N_c = \text{rank} = 2$$

## Proof

$5 - 2 = 3$ and $5 + 2 = 7$. $\square$

## Structural Content

The identity $n_C \pm \text{rank} = (N_c, g)$ is arithmetically trivial. Its content is structural: the five BST integers $\{N_c = 3, n_C = 5, g = 7, C_2 = 6, N_{\max} = 137\}$ derived from the symmetric space $D_{IV}^5 = SO_0(5,2)/[SO(5) \times SO(2)]$ are not independent. ### Why 5, not another prime?

Among single-digit odd primes, $n_C = 5$ is the unique prime such that both $n_C - 2$ and $n_C + 2$ are also prime. This is the cousin prime property. The alternatives:
- $n_C = 3$: then $3 - 2 = 1$ (not prime) $\rightarrow$ fails
- $n_C = 7$: then $7 + 2 = 9 = 3^2$ (not prime) $\rightarrow$ fails
- $n_C = 11$: then $11 - 2 = 9$ (not prime) $\rightarrow$ fails
- $n_C = 13$: then $13 - 2 = 11$ (prime) and $13 + 2 = 15$ (not prime) $\rightarrow$ fails

$n_C = 5$ is the **unique single-digit cousin prime center**. D$_{IV}^5$ picks the only value that generates a cousin pair from the odd primes. This is a necessary condition for the BST integer structure, derivable from the geometry (T186, 21 uniqueness conditions for $n_C = 5$).

## Corollary 1: The BST pentagon

The five integers can be arranged as a cycle:

$$\text{rank} \xrightarrow{+1} N_c \xrightarrow{+2} n_C \xrightarrow{+1} C_2 \xrightarrow{+1} g$$

with gaps $1, 2, 1, 1$. The total span is $g - \text{rank} = 5 = n_C$. The span equals the central element.

## Corollary 2: $C_2 = n_C + 1 = \text{rank} \times N_c$

The Casimir eigenvalue $C_2 = 6$ sits between $n_C$ and $g$ at distance 1 from each. It is also the product $\text{rank} \times N_c = 2 \times 3 = 6$. The AP structure gives:

$$C_2 = n_C + 1 = \frac{N_c + g}{2} + 1 = \frac{N_c + g + 2}{2} = \frac{N_c + g + \text{rank}}{2}$$

The Casimir is the half-sum of all four small BST integers.

## Corollary 3: $N_{\max}$ from the AP

$$N_{\max} = N_c^3 \times n_C + \text{rank} = (n_C - d)^3 \times n_C + d$$

where $d = \text{rank}$. Substituting $n_C = 5, d = 2$:

$$N_{\max} = 3^3 \times 5 + 2 = 135 + 2 = 137$$

The spectral cap is the cube of the first AP element times the central element, plus the common difference. All of $N_{\max}$ derives from $n_C$ and $d$ alone — two integers generate the fifth.
