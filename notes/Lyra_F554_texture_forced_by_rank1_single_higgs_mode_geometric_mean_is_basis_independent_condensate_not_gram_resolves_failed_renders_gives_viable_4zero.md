The answer is yes, and it comes from one distinction that also **resolves every failed render** (F552, Grace's ≈0).

## Two different objects — and the physics is the second one

- **(A) fermion–fermion pairwise overlap:** M_ij = ⟨ψ_i|ψ_j⟩ — a Gram matrix. For well-separated localized generations the normalized overlaps are < 1 and decay with separation → M ≈ diagonal → U ≈ I → **θ ≈ 0.** *This is exactly what Grace's norm-only render and my F552 self-check produced.* It is not the mass matrix.
- **(B) fermion–HIGGS–fermion coupling (the Yukawa):** M_ij = ⟨ψ_i|z_H⟩⟨z_H|ψ_j⟩ = **O_i O_j**, where both fermions couple to the **single Higgs VEV mode** |z_H⟩ and O_i = ⟨ψ_i|z_H⟩. This is **rank-1.**

The physical mass matrix is (B), not (A). Grace's machine was fed the pairwise Gram (A); the fix is to feed it the condensate coupling (B).

## Rank-1 ⟹ geometric mean in EVERY basis (this is the forcing)

For a rank-1 positive coupling M = O O†:
$$|M_{ij}| = |O_i|\,|O_j| = \sqrt{|O_i|^2\,|O_j|^2} = \sqrt{M_{ii}\,M_{jj}} \quad\text{— exactly, and in every basis.}$$
This is **Cauchy–Schwarz saturation**, an *operator property* (rank-1), not a coordinate choice. So the geometric-mean off-diagonal is **not attained by rotating to a lucky weak basis** — it holds identically in the natural localization basis, where the diagonal M_ii = m_i are the banked localization masses. **Keeper's precise worry is answered structurally:** nothing is chosen to fit, because there is no basis in which the magnitude is *not* the geometric mean.

The mixing is then a **zero-new-parameter output of the banked masses.** Diagonalizing the geometric-mean 2×2 block [[m_i, √(m_i m_j)],[√(m_i m_j), m_j]]:
$$\tan\theta_{ij} = \sqrt{m_i/m_j} \;\Rightarrow\; \theta_{ds}=\arctan\sqrt{m_d/m_s}\;(12.6^\circ),\quad \theta_{sb}=\arctan\sqrt{m_s/m_b}\;(8.5^\circ),$$
i.e. **Gatto**, derived — the mixing angles ARE the square-roots of the mass ratios we already banked, forced by the single-mode coupling. (F553's sin θ_C = √(m_d/m_s) = 1/(2√n_C) is the same statement.)
