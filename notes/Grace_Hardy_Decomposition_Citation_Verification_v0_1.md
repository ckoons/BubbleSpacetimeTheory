## Specific theorem pinning (what's actually being cited)

The Hardy decomposition H²(D) ≅ H²(∂_S D) for a bounded symmetric domain D is a **classical result** in the theory of holomorphic function spaces on Hermitian symmetric spaces. The standard statement:

**Theorem (Hardy decomposition for bounded symmetric domains)**: Let D be a bounded symmetric domain with Shilov boundary ∂_S D. The Hardy space H²(D) of holomorphic L² functions on D is canonically isomorphic to H²(∂_S D), the space of L² boundary values on ∂_S D, via the Poisson-Szegő kernel:
- Forward (boundary → interior): f(z) = ∫_{∂_S D} P(z, ξ) F(ξ) dσ(ξ) where P is the Poisson-Szegő kernel
- Inverse (interior → boundary): F(ξ) = lim_{r → 1} f(rξ) almost-everywhere on ∂_S D
- Isomorphism preserves L² inner product

**Primary-source candidates** (chronological):
1. **Korányi & Stein 1962-1965**: original Hardy space theory for tube domains (Korányi-Vagi-Stein etc.)
2. **Wallach 1976** (verified BST citation): "The analytic continuation of the discrete series" — covers K-type classification + Hardy-space structure for general bounded symmetric domains
3. **Faraut-Korányi 1994** (verified BST citation): comprehensive treatment in Ch. XII-XIII

The Hardy decomposition pre-dates Wallach 1976 and Faraut-Korányi 1994 — both are presenting / extending earlier results. The most rigorous BST citation would be **Faraut-Korányi 1994 Ch. XII-XIII** (their explicit treatment for the type IV case used in BST) + **Wallach 1976** as the K-type / discrete series anchor.
