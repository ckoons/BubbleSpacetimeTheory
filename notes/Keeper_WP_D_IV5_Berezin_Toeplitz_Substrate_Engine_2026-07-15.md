## The arc, as four leaps (each with anchor + honest tier)

### 1. The transfer / projection operator (spectrum = masses, eigenvectors = mixing)
- The Bergman/Szegő projection on D_IV⁵ is a **genuine projection operator**, not a metaphor. Discretized on the K-type basis it is a matrix. The dynamical object is its **Toeplitz compression** P·(shift/dilation)·P (a pure projection is idempotent → carries no dynamics alone; the dynamics live in the compression). This is the operator class Lyra's bulk-color work already uses (su(3) Cartan–Weyl on the Hardy-space Toeplitz algebra).
- **Its eigenvalues are the masses (our Σ); its eigenvectors are the mixing (U, V).** This is the textbook lattice-QFT statement (transfer-matrix eigenvalues = the spectrum). So the static "physics matrix" M = UΣV† and the dynamical "step operator" are ONE object seen two ways.
- **Anchor:** transfer-matrix / Koopman methodology; lattice-QFT spectrum. **Precedented → not new physics as a method (solid ground).**
- **Tier: BANKED spectrum (masses done), mixing PENDING (tomorrow). The operator itself is NOT written down.**

### 2. The inverse projection = quantization (Berezin–Toeplitz)
- A projection is NOT invertible on the whole space (it has a kernel — the modes it annihilates). It IS a clean two-way isomorphism **on the physical (holomorphic) subspace**: Extension E (discrete boundary → 3D bulk continuum) and Restriction R (continuum → discrete), with R∘E = id on the subspace and E∘R = P on the bulk. **The inverse exists exactly where the physics lives; off it, the projection has thrown information away** (consistent with Casey's own "limits are lossy, integrals preserve" — the Bergman projection is an integral against the reproducing kernel, hence preserving).
- The name for "continuum classical data → discrete quantum operator via projection onto holomorphic sections of a Kähler domain" is **Berezin–Toeplitz geometric quantization on a bounded symmetric domain** (Berezin, Rawnsley, Bordemann–Meinrenken–Schlichenmaier). D_IV⁵ is Hermitian symmetric → exactly that setting.
- **Two payoffs from the identification:** (a) the "recursion" is literal — a reproducing-kernel space regenerates its own elements, f(z)=⟨f,K_z⟩, so the RKHS self-reference IS the continuum↔discrete tower Casey senses; (b) **our weight E₀ (=2, the FK weight that sets the masses) is the quantization parameter** (the role of 1/ℏ; classical/continuum limit = weight→∞). The mass-scale parameter and the classical↔quantum knob are the same number.
- **Novel wrinkle worth chasing:** ordinary geometric quantization leaves ℏ a free family (ℏ→0). Ours is **pinned by the geometry** (E₀=2 from spin, finite capacity 137). A quantization with NO free ℏ is genuinely unusual.
- **Tier: method PRECEDENTED (rigorous field — go stand on it); "this domain quantizes to the SM" + "ℏ pinned" is the OPEN BST content. Extension/restriction operators NOT written as matrices yet.**

### 3. Finite discrete interior → loops become finite sums (linear algebra, no renormalization)
- A loop integral is a **sum over the complete intermediate basis**; the continuum's `∫d⁴k` and its UV divergences come ENTIRELY from "infinitely many modes at every point." A **finite mode count** (the 137 capacity) makes that sum **finite** → a tensor contraction / trace over a discrete index. **UV divergences never appear; renormalization is simply not needed.** Every quantum correction becomes a finite, well-defined number.
- Berezin–Toeplitz makes it rigorous: the loop expansion reorganizes into the **1/N star-product expansion**, each term algebraic; if the operator is genuinely finite-rank in a sector, the exact answer is a finite contraction (no expansion).
- **Three honest edges:** (a) loops don't vanish — the quantum-correction *physics* stays, now as finite sums; (b) **finite ≠ small** — the multi-particle (Fock) state space grows combinatorially, so it is *conceptually* simpler (no renormalization) but only *computationally* cheap at low order/few-body (the lattice-QCD lesson: finite but still a supercomputer); (c) **undemonstrated** — not one substrate amplitude has been computed and matched.
- **Tier: conceptual claim (finite → linear algebra, no renormalization) CORRECT and important; computational-simplicity claim carries the finite≠small asterisk; whole thing ASPIRATIONAL until one finite loop is computed and matched.**

### 4. THE VALIDITY CONDITION (Casey — the Keeper-shaped one)
> "Linear math defeats analysis unless you have an identity which appears on the discrete side too."
- A discretization is exact **only** for structures with an exact discrete counterpart; anything that lives only in the limit is silently lost. This is the theorem behind every successful discretization: lattice gauge theory keeps **gauge invariance exact** (Wilson); Ginsparg–Wilson/overlap fermions supply a **lattice chiral symmetry** where naive lattices fail (Nielsen–Ninomiya doublers); symplectic integrators conserve an exact **shadow Hamiltonian**.
- **Why BST is well-placed (the precise reason):** BST's discreteness is NOT a lattice (which breaks Lorentz and loses identities); it is a **discrete spectrum** (K-type ladder, integer weights) carrying a genuine **unitary representation of SO(5,2)**. Representation theory carries a continuous group's identities onto a discrete basis *exactly* — Casimir eigenvalues, branching/recurrence relations, the reproducing kernel. So every symmetry-descended identity (Ward, conservation laws) is **guaranteed on both sides**. The identities aren't discretized; they're *represented*. NOTE: the space is infinite-dimensional (holomorphic functions); the discreteness is the discrete spectrum + finite capacity in specific sectors, not finite-dimensionality.
- **The at-risk identities + the first concrete test:** not every needed identity is symmetry-generated. Safe = representation-theoretic; at-risk = genuine-limit identities, and the sharpest is the **anomaly** (a classical symmetry that fails quantum-mechanically). The SM has the chiral anomaly (π⁰→γγ, measured). **First test of the whole arc: does the discrete substrate reproduce the chiral anomaly exactly?** If yes, the representation is faithful where it counts and the engine is trustworthy; if no, we've found a limit-only identity and located exactly where linear algebra is defeated.
