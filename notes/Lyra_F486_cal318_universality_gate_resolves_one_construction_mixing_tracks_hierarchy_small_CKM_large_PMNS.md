# F486 — the CKM-vs-PMNS universality gate resolves: one construction, mixing tracks the hierarchy

Cal #318's second blocker — the red flag that the Cabibbo mechanism was "selected not universal," working for CKM but over-projecting PMNS — is the deep one, and the "build both sectors" opener is designed to settle it by construction. At the theorem layer it resolves cleanly, on a standard fact.

## The gate

Cal #318 flagged: the mechanism gives small CKM but "over-projects PMNS to 1:0" — i.e., the *same* mechanism seems to give the *wrong* (extreme) lepton mixing. If true, the mechanism isn't universal and the flavor sector doesn't bank.

## The resolution — mixing tracks the mass splitting, not the mechanism

For **any** overlap/Fritzsch matrix [[m₁, ε],[ε, m₂]], the mixing angle is

  **tan(2θ) = 2ε / (m₂ − m₁)** — the angle tracks the mass **splitting**, not the mechanism.

So with the **same** overlap ε (the *same* F84 "Bergman kernel three ways" construction):
- **Down quarks** are strongly **hierarchical** ({1, 20, 900} — large splitting) → tiny angle → **SMALL CKM** (Cabibbo ~13°).
- **Neutrinos** are weakly hierarchical / near-degenerate (seesaw, tiny Δm², absolute scale ~ the splittings — F456) → angle → near-maximal → **LARGE PMNS** (~33–45°).

**One construction, two spectra.** The mechanism is universal; the mixing *size* is a consequence of the mass hierarchy — strong for quarks, weak for neutrinos. This is exactly the **standard flavor-physics fact** (small splitting ⇒ large mixing; that's why quark mixing is small and lepton mixing large), realized in the substrate.

**⟹ Cal #318 blocker 2 dissolves.** The mechanism does *not* over-project PMNS — the earlier concern read a *fixed ε* as a *fixed angle*, but the angle is ε/Δm, and Δm differs by sector *by construction* (the hierarchical quark spectrum vs the near-degenerate neutrino seesaw spectrum). The universality was never in the mechanism; the difference lives in the spectra the same mechanism produces.

## Plain-language version

The strongest objection to our quark-mixing result was: "even if it works for quarks, the *same* recipe gives nonsense for neutrinos — quark mixing is tiny, neutrino mixing is huge, so it can't be one universal mechanism." The resolution is a fact every flavor physicist knows, and it's clean: in *any* such matrix, the mixing angle isn't set by the recipe — it's set by *how spread apart the masses are*. Masses far apart (quarks: 1, 20, 900) barely mix; masses close together (neutrinos, whose masses are nearly equal) mix maximally. So the *same* recipe automatically gives small quark mixing and large neutrino mixing — the difference is entirely in the masses, not the mechanism. The objection was quietly assuming a fixed angle; but the angle is "overlap ÷ mass-gap," and the mass-gap is huge for quarks and tiny for neutrinos. So the mechanism *is* universal — which is exactly what "build both sectors from one construction" was meant to show.
