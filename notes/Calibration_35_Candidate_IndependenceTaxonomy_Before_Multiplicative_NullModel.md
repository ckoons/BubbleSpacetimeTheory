# Calibration #35 candidate — Independence-taxonomy precedes multiplicative null-model

## 0. The rule (one-sentence)

**When a multi-criterion or multi-route claim is structured for null-model invocation, the independence-vs-derived taxonomy of the criteria MUST be applied FIRST — counting only structurally-INDEPENDENT choices, not arithmetic re-expressions of derived consequences.**

Equivalently: do not invoke (1/3)^N or any multiplicative-coincidence null-model without first explicitly classifying each criterion as INDEPENDENT or DERIVED-FROM-ALREADY-COUNTED.

## 1. Why this is its own calibration

The pattern is **distinct from** Cal #27 (peak-convergence brake, tier-discipline) and Cal #171 (route-count reformulation under primary relations) and Cal #34 (headline-cap-conditionality):

- **Cal #27** governs whether the tier is right (brake on overstatement)
- **Cal #171** governs route-count reformulation under specific primary relations (C_2 = N_c·rank, n_C = rank+N_c)
- **Cal #34** governs how conditionality is communicated at the surface
- **Calibration #35 (this candidate)** governs the COUNTING METHODOLOGY for multi-criterion null-model claims specifically — the taxonomy step that must precede the arithmetic

A claim can be honest under Cal #27 (correctly tier-marked individually), correctly route-reduced under Cal #171, AND have proper conditional headlines under Cal #34 — and still fail Calibration #35 if the null-model arithmetic counts derived consequences as independent draws.

This is the gap Calibration #35 closes.

## 3. The application protocol

When structuring a multi-criterion or multi-route claim:

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Enumerate the proposed criteria / routes |
| 2 | For each, classify: **INDEPENDENT** (load-bearing structural choice not derivable from already-counted criteria) or **DERIVED** (consequence, re-expression, or algebraic equivalent of already-counted) |
| 3 | Apply primary relations (Cal #171: C_2 = N_c·rank, n_C = rank+N_c, and any others) to detect derived re-expressions |
| 4 | Count ONLY structurally-INDEPENDENT criteria in any null-model arithmetic |
| 5 | If null-model is multiplicative, EACH multiplicative factor must correspond to a structurally-independent choice; otherwise reframe as single-residual + convergence-of-routes (per Lyra v1.1 §6.1 + Keeper recompute) |
| 6 | At headline level, communicate the framing explicitly per Calibration #34 STANDING |

**Test for compliance**: a referee asking "are these N criteria genuinely independent or do some derive from others?" should find the independence-vs-derived taxonomy already documented in the claim. If the taxonomy step has been skipped, Calibration #35 fires.
