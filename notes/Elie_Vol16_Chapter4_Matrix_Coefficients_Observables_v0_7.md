## 1. Chapter principle (corrected): two metrics, two questions

An observable O = a substrate matrix coefficient. Comparing it to measurement asks
TWO distinct questions, with TWO metrics:

- **Structural closeness** — `rel% = |O_BST/O_obs − 1|`. "How close is the substrate
  matrix coefficient structurally."
- **Observational viability** — `sigma = |O_BST − O_obs|/σ_obs`. "Is the substrate
  matrix coefficient consistent with the measurement."

The relative-% threshold fails BOTH directions (Toy 4003):
- **false Tier 1**: tiny % but many σ (α⁻¹ 0.003% / ~202,500σ; m_μ/m_e 0.042% / ~19,100σ; θ_* 0.014% / ~5σ) — structural, not exact.
- **false rejection**: large % but few σ (sin²θ₁₃ 2.67% / 0.8σ; sin²θ₂₃ 3.05% / 0.8σ) — consistent, under-rated.

**Standing classification rule (v0.7):** report both metrics; reserve "Tier 1 EXACT"
for matrix coefficients that are BOTH structurally close AND within a few σ of
measurement. This subsumes the v0.6 relative-% bands and the 6-category 0.25/0.5
weights (Cal #256 σ-distance discipline).
