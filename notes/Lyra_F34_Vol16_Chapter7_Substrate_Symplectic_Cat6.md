## 7.1 The accidental isomorphism Spin(5) ≅ Sp(2)

$$\mathrm{Spin}(5) \;\cong\; \mathrm{Sp}(2)\quad(\text{compact form } \mathrm{USp}(4)),\qquad \dim = 10.$$

The check: dim SO(5) = 10 = dim Sp(2). ✓ The 4-dimensional spinor of SO(5) is the 4-dimensional fundamental of Sp(2). This is the same B₂ = C₂ coincidence that fixes the substrate root system (B₂, ρ = (5/2, 3/2)) — here read on the group rather than the root side.

## 7.2 The substrate stabilizer is symplectic

The substrate isotropy group is K = SO(5) × SO(2). Passing to the double cover of the first factor:

$$\widetilde{K} = \mathrm{Spin}(5)\times\mathrm{SO}(2) \;\cong\; \mathrm{Sp}(2)\times\mathrm{SO}(2).$$

So the K-types V_(λ₁, λ₂) — which Ch 2 organized as SO(5)×SO(2) representations — are equally Sp(2)×SO(2) representations. The half-integer-λ₁ (spinor) K-types are genuine Sp(2) representations that are only projective for SO(5); the double cover is doing real work, not bookkeeping.
