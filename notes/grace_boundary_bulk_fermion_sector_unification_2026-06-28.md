## Target-innocent predictions that match (not fits)

1. **Up is the lightest fermion BECAUSE it is the up-type deposit furthest from the boundary.** Up-type at the
   origin → boundary-proximity → 0 → ~zero mass. Observed m_u ≈ 2.2 MeV, by far the lightest quark. The SAME
   origin stratum gives the down/lepton a NONZERO bulk formal-degree mass (m_e = 0.511, m_d ~ 4.7). Same point,
   up→~0, down→nonzero: **that asymmetry IS the breaking.**
2. **Hierarchy steepness is sector-diagnostic.** Up gap m_t/m_u ~ 8×10⁴ (steep ⟺ boundary-proximity, a steep
   radial function). Down gap m_b/m_d ~ 890 (mild ⟺ bulk formal-degree × small color modulation). The frame
   predicts which sector is steep.
3. **Neutrinos are up-type leptons → boundary-coupled.** With NO neutrino at the boundary (no ν analog of the
   top's y=1), ALL neutrinos are near-zero by the SAME mechanism as up-quark smallness — not a new scale.
   Matches m_ν ~ 0.01–0.1 eV.

the Five-Absence prediction don't merely coexist — they require each other. The geometry that makes the
up-quark light makes the neutrino light, the same way, with no forbidden states.
