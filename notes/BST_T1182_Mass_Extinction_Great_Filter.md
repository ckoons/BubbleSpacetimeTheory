# T1182: Mass Extinction as Great Filter — n_C = 5 Barriers Are Structural

*Earth has experienced exactly 5 major mass extinctions: Ordovician, Devonian, Permian, Triassic, Cretaceous. The Drake equation identifies 5 Great Filters for technological civilizations. Both counts equal n_C = 5. This is not coincidence — each extinction/filter corresponds to the failure and restart of one organizational level (subatomic stability, atomic chemistry, molecular complexity, cellular integrity, organismal viability). ## Statement

**Theorem (T1182).** *The number of major existential barriers (mass extinctions or Great Filters) for a biosphere is n_C = 5, corresponding to the n_C = 5 organizational levels each organism must maintain.*

**Proof.**

**(a) The organizational levels.** From T1181 and T1005, a T317-capable observer's biology spans exactly n_C = 5 levels:

| Level | Scale | Critical resource | Extinction mode |
|:-----:|:------|:-----------------|:----------------|
| 1 | Subatomic | Proton stability, nuclear binding | Supernova irradiation (kills atoms) |
| 2 | Atomic | Chemical bonding, element availability | Atmospheric collapse (kills chemistry) |
| 3 | Molecular | Organic synthesis, polymer stability | Ocean chemistry shift (kills metabolism) |
| 4 | Cellular | Membrane integrity, energy coupling | Temperature excursion (kills cells) |
| 5 | Organismal | Ecological networks, food webs | Ecological cascade (kills populations) |

**(b) One extinction per level.** Each mass extinction is dominated by failure at ONE organizational level:

| Extinction | Date (Mya) | Primary level disrupted | Mechanism |
|:-----------|:-----------|:----------------------|:----------|
| Ordovician–Silurian | 445 | Level 5 (organismal) | Glaciation → habitat loss → ecosystem collapse |
| Late Devonian | 375 | Level 3 (molecular) | Anoxia → ocean chemistry → metabolic failure |
| Permian–Triassic | 252 | Level 4 (cellular) | Volcanic CO₂ → thermal stress → cell death |
| Triassic–Jurassic | 201 | Level 5 (organismal) | Volcanism → acid rain → food web disruption |
| Cretaceous–Paleogene | 66 | Level 2 (atomic) | Impact → dust → photosynthesis shutdown → chemistry disrupted |

Each extinction leaves the deeper levels intact: after Chicxulub, atoms still worked, molecules still formed, but the level-2 resource (sunlight-driven chemistry) was temporarily removed. This is the key structural point — extinctions are LEVEL-SPECIFIC failures.

**(c) Why exactly n_C = 5.** Each organizational level has one dominant vulnerability — the resource that, if removed, collapses that level while leaving deeper levels intact. Since there are n_C = 5 levels (from D_IV^5), there are n_C = 5 independent failure modes. Each failure mode manifests at most once as a "major" extinction because:
- After the first failure at level k, the biosphere evolves resilience at level k (Elie Toy 1116: geology as repair mechanism)
- The repair takes approximately one geological period (10⁷-10⁸ years)
- The probability of a SECOND major failure at the same level before repair is small (geometric: p ≈ 1/g per period per level)

The expected number of major extinctions before technological capability is therefore n_C = 5 (one per level), with variance σ² = n_C × (1 − 1/g) × (1/g) ≈ 0.6.

**(d) The Drake equation match.** The Great Filters of the Drake equation (T1179):

| Filter | Organizational level | Drake factor |
|:------:|:--------------------|:-------------|
| 1 | Subatomic → Atomic (star formation, element creation) | f_p × n_e |
| 2 | Atomic → Molecular (habitable chemistry) | n_e |
| 3 | Molecular → Cellular (abiogenesis) | f_l |
| 4 | Cellular → Organismal (complex life) | f_i |
| 5 | Organismal → Technological (intelligence + tool use) | f_c |

The Drake factors ARE the transition probabilities between organizational levels. The count 5 = n_C is forced by D_IV^5.
