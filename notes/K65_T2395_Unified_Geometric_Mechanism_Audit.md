## Why scope adjustment STRENGTHENS the theorem

External survivability discipline: a theorem claiming "5 absences unified via X" is harder to defend if one of the 5 doesn't actually follow from X. Critics will spot the weakest link and use it to challenge the whole theorem.

A theorem claiming "**4 absences unified via X** + 1 absence separate" is structurally honest and harder to attack:
- The 4-absence cluster has a clean unified mechanism (irreducibility)
- The 1-absence (sterile/SUSY) is explicitly acknowledged as separate; its own structural mechanism (PMNS closure + Bergman) is cited but not bundled

This is Mode 7 discipline (single-mechanism over-claim) applied preemptively rather than retrospectively. Elie's scope adjustment STRENGTHENS the theorem's external survivability rather than weakening it.
