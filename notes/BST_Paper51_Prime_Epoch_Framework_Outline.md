## Abstract (draft)

We demonstrate that the density of $B$-smooth integers crosses the Gödel self-knowledge limit $f_c = 3/(5\pi) \approx 19.1\%$ at arithmetically structured scales for $B \in \{7, 11\}$, the two epoch primes of BST. The crossing scales share the base $143 = 11 \times 13$, with multipliers rank² = 4 and $g = 7$ respectively. The smooth-number counts at these scales are T914 primes — integers adjacent to 7-smooth products — with opposite ±1 directions encoding the multiplicative arrow of time. Applied to physics: 75% of T914-predicted primes in the 11-smooth epoch correspond to known physical constants (spectral lines, lattice parameters, isotope masses), including the Ca II Fraunhofer line (396.85 nm, 0.04%) and the strontium optical clock transition (461.7 nm, 0.15%).
