## A. Casey-originated (the load-bearing ones this arc)

1. **"Engage, don't label."** When tempted to tag something "multi-week" / "open in math itself" / "the genuine frontier," stop — that's under-engagement wearing anti-fabrication clothes. Build the concrete model and compute; the anti-fabrication rule forbids *guessing the answer*, not *attempting the problem*. *Proof:* #418 was tagged multi-week, closed in 20 min (Elie 4301); W1 "open in math" folded onto W2 once we asked whether the OS data was already in hand. *Tier: STANDING-candidate (highest-value).*

2. **"Build the concrete model, don't label the abstraction"** (corollary of A1). The honest move at a hard step is to construct the explicit object (Fock model, matrix, eigenvalue problem) and compute it. *Tier: merge into A1 or STANDING-candidate.*

3. **"Check the gate before walking through it."** Test the *premise* of a planned task before executing it. *Proof:* Elie tested whether the factor-20 was a normalization issue (4306) before chasing the Lichnerowicz/Wu reference — found it scale-invariant (structural), so no reference could fix it; saved the team a wrong morning. *Tier: STANDING-candidate.*
