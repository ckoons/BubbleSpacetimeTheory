# F351 — the π-once principle: π-power = spatial-integration-locus dimension (answers U-1.5)

Today's lepton work made an April backlog item ripe: U-1.5, "why does π enter once — all from Shilov integrals?" Here is the consolidated answer.

## The principle

**π is never tuned and never appears by hand — it enters an observable exactly once per spatial integration, and its power equals the spatial dimension of the integration locus. The time circle is excluded for massive states.**

## Supporting cases

| Observable | π-power | locus / reason |
|---|---|---|
| muon m_μ/m_e = (24/π²)⁶ | π⁻¹² | **boundary** so(4) determinant over spatial-S⁴ 2-forms: 6 directions × π² = π¹² (F341/F350); time circle S¹ **excluded** (massive little group so(4)⊂so(5) is spatial, K552) → π¹² not π¹⁸ |
| tau m_τ/m_e = 49·71 | π⁰ (rational) | **Shilov 0-dim points** stratum: no spatial integral → π-free |
| proton m_p/m_e = 6π⁵ | π⁵ | **bulk** object (baryon): bulk volume π^{n_C} = π⁵ (D_IV⁵ complex dim n_C=5) |
| origin / electron | reference | bulk-point reference |

## The refinement of U-1.5

It is **not** literally "all from Shilov": **bulk** objects (the proton) take the **bulk volume π^{n_C}**; **boundary/massive** objects (leptons) take the **spatial S⁴ boundary** (π² per direction, count = stratum dimension). But in every case π enters from a **spatial volume measure**, never by hand, and the **power = the locus's spatial dimension**:

- **boundary determinants** → even powers (2 per S⁴ direction);
- **bulk objects** → π^{n_C};
- **0-dim strata** → π⁰ (rational);
- **the time circle S¹ is excluded for massive states** (the massive little group is the spatial so(4)) — this is the muon's π¹²-not-π¹⁸ forward check.

## Scope and a prediction

This is a substrate-architectural **principle** (a structural pattern across the cases), not a per-observable theorem. But it explains the lepton π-structure forward (today's work) and the bulk π^{n_C} (proton), and it **predicts**: any massive **boundary** observable has an **even** π-power equal to **2 × (its spatial stratum dimension)**. That is a falsifiable handle (an odd π-power for a massive boundary observable would break it).
