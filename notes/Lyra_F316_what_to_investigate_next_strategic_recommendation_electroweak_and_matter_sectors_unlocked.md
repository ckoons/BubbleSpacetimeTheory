# F316 — What to investigate next, and why (Lyra's recommendation)

## The organizing insight (why now, and where)

This week's chirality cascade newly **pinned three things that had been open**: **hypercharge** (Y = T_3^R + (B−L)/2, F299/F304), the **matter content** (the SO(10) 16, F304), and the **gauge-color realization** (the covariant V_a, unitary on the compact dual). So the count can improve **specifically in the electroweak and matter sectors**, and that is where I'd point the effort.

## Priority 1 — sin²θ_W from the K-Cartan (cleanest now-possible, +1 to the count, sharpest test)

The Weinberg angle is the **relative normalization of the hypercharge Cartan (Y) against the weak-isospin Cartan (T_3^L)** in the K = SO(5)×SO(2) inner product: sin²θ_W = g′²/(g²+g′²), and g/g′ is fixed by ‖T_3^L‖ / ‖Y‖ in the substrate's K-metric. The cascade just pinned **Y = T_3^R + (B−L)/2** in terms of the K-Cartan directions, so this ratio is now a **finite, criteria-innocent Cartan-inner-product computation** — no new inputs. It's a clean SM parameter (improves the count by one) and the **single sharpest test of the whole cascade**: if it lands at the (GUT-normalized) ≈ 3/8 or the low-energy ≈ 0.231 cleanly, the cascade is validated; if it misses, the cascade has a flaw we need to find. Highest immediate leverage, lowest cost. **Start here.**

## Priority 2 — the fermion mass hierarchy from K-type localization (biggest chunk, deepest mystery)

About **nine of the 26** parameters are charged-fermion masses, and the hierarchy (top to electron, ~10⁵) is the deepest unexplained structure in the SM. It is **now attackable** because the pieces are in hand: matter = the **SO(10) 16** (F304); the **three generations = the rank+1 = 3 support strata** of D_IV⁵ (F86's inverted pyramid — bulk / Cartan-slice / Shilov boundary); and the **Yukawas = the Bergman-kernel localization/overlap** of the matter modes at their K-type addresses (F84). The mass hierarchy would then be the *geometric localization depth* of each generation on the domain. This is the **largest available count improvement** (potentially ~9–12 parameters from one mechanism) and the most important physically. The open pin is the K-type address of each flavor (the "which discrete (a,b)" question, long-flagged) — and that is exactly what the matter-identity closure now lets us attack.

## Priority 3 — derive the F(4) odd part (#359), don't posit it (foundational radical)

The entire chirality/super structure rests on F(4) being super, with the **odd part posited, not derived** (Cal's standing flag, #359). The radical upgrade: **derive the supercharges from the substrate** — the natural candidate is the **metaplectic/spinor square-root of the conformal Hamiltonian** on H² (the Hardy space carries a holomorphic/half-form structure, and the Shilov-boundary fermionic modes are its natural square-root). If the odd part is derived, the whole cascade (chirality, the matter rep, the V_a color) turns from *conditional-on-a-posit* into *derived*. This is the single biggest foundational improvement on the board — harder and longer than Priorities 1–2, but the highest-leverage "radically improve" item.

## Priority 4 — the substrate parameter taxonomy (organizing; understand-the-substrate)

Map **all 26 SM parameters** onto four substrate categories: **spectral** (T2490, the discrete-series spectrum), **cascade-from-rank** (T2491, three colors → primaries), **K-type-localization** (the F84/F86 mass/mixing mechanism), or **genuinely dynamical** (the residual). This tells us *where the count can actually reach* and prioritizes the effort honestly (it also applies the target-innocence lens up front). Grace's lane with mine — and it directly answers "how much can 4/26 improve, and by what mechanism."

## The deep substrate-understanding target

Underneath Priorities 1, 2, and 4 is one object: **the full (g,K)-module decomposition of the matter representation on H²** — which K-types carry which generation and which flavor. Pin that, and the mass sector (localization depth) and the mixing sector (K-type overlaps = CKM/PMNS) come from one structure. That is "understand the substrate in detail" at its deepest: the substrate's matter content as an explicit, computable module.
