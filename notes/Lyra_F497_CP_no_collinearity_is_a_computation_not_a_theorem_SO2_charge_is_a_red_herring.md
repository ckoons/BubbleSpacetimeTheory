# F497 — CP no-collinearity is a computation, not a theorem (and the clock-charge is a red herring)

Keeper's ask: prove the 3 generations cannot be collinear given (complex substrate ∧ N_c=3). Detail-first (yesterday's lesson), I checked *what the non-collinearity actually needs* before trying to prove it — and it reframes the whole task, plus catches a red herring.

## The precise condition (verified, three cases)

CP is Im(⟨1|2⟩⟨2|3⟩⟨3|1⟩) ≠ 0. What sources it:
- **Different radii, same phase (collinear real):** Im = 0 → **NO CP.** Different radii alone do nothing.
- **Genuine 2-real-dim complex spread of the peak positions** (points at different places in ℂ): Im ≠ 0 → **CP.**
- **Different SO(2)/clock/U(1) charges** (a global rephasing |k⟩→e^{iθ_k}|k⟩): the phases **cancel** in the invariant (θ₁−θ₂+θ₂−θ₃+θ₃−θ₁ = 0) → removable → **NO CP.**

**⟹ CP requires the 3 states to PEAK at 3 non-collinear COMPLEX positions** — not just different radii, and *not* different clock charges.

## The red herring I caught

I'd been half-associating CP with the clock/winding through the F494 cant picture. **The clock CHARGE (SO(2) winding number) is a red herring for CP** — it's a rephasing, removable. What *does* survive from F494 is the **winding SIGN** = the *orientation* of the spread = the handedness = matter/antimatter. So: winding *sign* → handedness (real); winding *charge* → not CP (removable); CP source → the 2D spread of the peak positions. That distinction matters and I had it blurred.

## So it's not a theorem — it's a computation

"The 3 generations cannot be collinear" is **not abstractly provable**: collinearity (3 peaks on a common real geodesic) is a real codimension-1 condition, and whether the *actual* generation peaks avoid it depends on the explicit states — a computation, not a theorem.

- **Forced:** the substrate is complex (D_IV⁵ ⊂ ℂ⁵, holomorphic), so the peaks *can* be 2D-complex-spread (a real substrate couldn't) + there are 3 (KM). So CP is **generic** — the default — with CP=0 requiring a real-structure the complex substrate has no symmetry to impose. (This sharpens the F495 "generic modulo fine-tuning" caveat: the fine-tuning is *real-collinearity*.)
- **Genuine risk (honest):** if the 3 KW strata sit on a common real geodesic, Im(triple)=0 → **CP=0 → the candidate fails.** Not likely (it needs a real structure), but not excluded without the computation.

## The forward move (Casey's direction, not a claim)

Settle it by **constructing the 3 generation states explicitly** — the oscillator/Fock realization — reading off their peak positions, and computing Im(triple). That turns "generic modulo fine-tuning" into "computed for the actual states: non-collinear (CP forced) or real-collinear (CP=0)." I will **not** claim the theorem abstractly; the honest bank path is the explicit construction.

I'm saying: here's exactly what has to be true, here's the one way it could fail, and the only honest way to know is to construct the states (the linear-algebra build Casey pointed me at yesterday) and compute.
