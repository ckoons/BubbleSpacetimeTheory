# F91 — The roulette dynamics: shedding energy is the arrow of time; discrete slots quantize mass

## 0. Casey's refinement

The roulette image is exactly the F90 dynamics, and it asks two sharp, separable questions. Q1 is already answered by the existing Tier-0 framework. Q2 is the genuinely open one. And the image sharpens the central reduction question.

## 1. Q1 — it SHEDS energy, and the shedding IS the arrow of time

The substrate evolution is the heat-semigroup ρ_commit(τ) = exp(−τH_B/ℏ_BST), with H_B the Casimir of K = SO(5)×SO(2), and **H_B ≥ 0** (positive spectrum). For τ > 0 this is a **dissipative contraction semigroup**: amplitudes decay, the state **relaxes toward lower energy**. So along the substrate trajectory the particle **sheds energy** — exactly the roulette ball losing speed and settling, not a ball being driven faster.

And this isn't a new posit — it's the framework's arrow of time. The monotone energy-shedding *is* the positivity of spec(H_B) that gives the substrate its time-direction (vol17 / T2405: arrow of time = positivity of H_B). So **Q1 has a definite answer from structure already in hand: it sheds, monotonically, and the shedding direction is time itself.** The ball always settles (a dissipative semigroup has no perpetual orbits) — which is why there is no particle "stuck spinning" between slots, i.e., no stable intermediate generation (consistent with the F88 no-4th-generation falsifier).

## 2. The realization: relaxation to the canonical slot within a stratum

Relaxation toward lower energy would naively send everything to the global ground (the electron). The resolution — consistent with F90: the **initial impulse selects which stratum (sector)** the ball is in; *within* that sector the ball relaxes (sheds) to the **canonical minimal K-type** (the bottom of that stratum, F90). So:
- electron: bulk sector, relaxes to (0,0) — global bottom, lightest;
- muon: Cartan sector, relaxes to the canonical Cartan-stratum K-type;
- tau: Shilov sector, relaxes to the canonical Shilov-stratum K-type — most localized, heaviest.

The three masses are the **three canonical-bottom slot energies** — forced by the geometry, not by the impulse.

## 3. The structural payoff: discrete slots quantize the masses (the reduction, finally clear)

This is the part the roulette image makes vivid and answers the reduction-vs-relabel question at the root. **The slots are discrete** (K-types are discrete reps). So even though the initial impulse is a *continuous* quantity, the *realized* state can only be one of the discrete slots — **many continuous impulses funnel into the 3 discrete slots.** Therefore:

> The realized **masses are discrete = the forced slot energies** (the Casimir eigenvalues of the 3 canonical K-types). The continuous initial impulse only selects *which* of the 3 slots; it does **not** set the slot's energy.

That is exactly the reduction: **the mass *values* are forced (the discrete slot energies); the impulse is not a per-fermion free mass parameter — it is a selector among 3 forced slots.** This is why a continuous-looking process (impulse + relaxation) yields *discrete* masses, and it is the mechanism-level reason the lepton-mass lever can be a reduction rather than a relabel. (Whether the 3 slot energies *numerically* reproduce m_e, m_μ, m_τ is the open matrix-element computation — F89/F90 boundary — but the *quantization itself* is now mechanism-clear.)

Honest: Q1 (shedding) is answered from the existing framework; Q2 (the impulse) is open and is the right next *physical* question, distinct from the *computational* core (the stratum-attachment + matrix element).

The roulette ball isn't in a slot *until it settles* — and the time spent settling is the **off-endpoint superposition window** (F90). During it, the state is a superposition across slots = the generation mixing. So:
- **quarks** are confined / realize fast (short relaxation) → **small CKM mixing**;
- **neutrinos** travel far before settling (long relaxation) → **large PMNS mixing**, and **oscillation** is literally the ball still rolling between slots, observed mid-flight.

A qualitative prediction of the roulette dynamics, matching the observed CKM-small / PMNS-large pattern.
