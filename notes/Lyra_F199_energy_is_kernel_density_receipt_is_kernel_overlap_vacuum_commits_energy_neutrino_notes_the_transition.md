Casey's close on the neutrino thread: *"the neutrino is just a receipt; the vacuum transfers energy to the manifold in other ways."* That maps exactly onto the F193 one-kernel backbone — and it's the organizing line for the whole thread.

## The split, on the backbone

| F193 reading | object | kind | role |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — coincident `K(p,p)` | density at a point | density | **energy** — the Higgs VEV / scale |
| 2 — heat **diagonal** | local density `a_k(x)` | density | **energy** — rest mass |
| 3 — heat **trace** | integrated `∫a_k` | density | **energy** — Λ + gravity |
| 4 — **displaced** overlap | transition amplitude | overlap | **receipt** — the T₃ transition / mixing → the neutrino |

**Energy = the density readings (1–3):** how much commitment sits at a point, as a local density, or integrated over the whole manifold. That is *how the vacuum writes its energy into the manifold* — as mass, QCD confinement, and curvature (the massive channels, F198).

**Receipt = the overlap reading (4):** *what transitioned*, not how much sits there. The neutrino is the receipt of a **transition** (β decay = T₃, F196/F197) — it carries the lepton-number bookkeeping (F175), not a chunk of the density.
