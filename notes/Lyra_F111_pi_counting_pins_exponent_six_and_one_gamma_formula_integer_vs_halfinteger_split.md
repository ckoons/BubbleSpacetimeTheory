# F111 — π-counting pins the exponent at six; one Gamma-formula can carry all three leptons

Casey's two follow-ups: *"Do the SO(4) six-fold calculation,"* and *"Is it possible the electron, muon and tau masses are all one formula?"* Both land — and the second has a better answer than I expected.

## Part 1 — the π-counting *pins* the exponent at 6 (it is not a free fit)

The muon ratio is `(24/pi^2)^6 = 191102976 / pi^12` — it carries exactly **pi^(-12)**. Model the muon norm by the soap-film picture (F109): a base integer, diluted by the Shilov boundary volume `vol(S^4) = 8 pi^2/3`, taken `N` times. Each copy carries one `pi^2`. Matching the observed π-power:

> **pi^12 = (pi^2)^N  =>  N = 6**  (forced — no freedom).

So the exponent is **not** a tuned number. *Given* dilution-by-Shilov-volume (the soap film), the observed π-power forces exactly six copies. And

> **6 = dim SO(4) = C(4,2)** = the number of 2-planes in the tangent space `T_p(S^4)` at the measurement point.

Each 2-plane rotation is one direction the boundary mode spreads — one `vol(S^4)` dilution. Measuring the muon "at a point" breaks `SO(5) -> SO(4)`; the six unbroken generators are the six 2-planes; six spreadings give the sixth power. This is exactly Casey's "lighter at the point of realization, mass depends how you measure."

- **FORCED:** the copy-count is `6`, pinned by π-counting, and equals `dim SO(4)` (the measurement little group). The exponent firmed up from "equals 6" to "pinned to 6 by the π-power."
- **OPEN:** that each copy contributes exactly the integer `64 = d_tau/d_muon`. That is the full norm integral — the remaining grind.

## Part 2 — one formula: yes, and the integer/π split is the *evidence for* it

I had been reading "tau is a clean integer `49*71`, muon is a π-transcendental `(24/pi^2)^6`" as a sign they need *different* formulas. The computation shows the **opposite**.

The three leptons sit at `nu = 0` (**integer**), `3/2`, `5/2` (**half-integers**). And a single Gamma-based `f(nu)` *automatically* produces a π-free integer at the integer point and π-transcendentals at the half-integer points, because:

> `Gamma` at an **integer** argument -> a **factorial** (π-free); at a **half-integer** argument -> `sqrt(pi) * (odd double factorial)` (π-full).

So `tau = integer` and `muon = π-transcendental` is the **fingerprint of ONE Gamma-formula sampled at an integer point (tau, nu=0) versus half-integer points (muon 3/2, electron 5/2)** — the very feature that looked like it forbade one formula actually points *to* one.

**Proof of concept that one function carries all three:** the formal degree is a single function,
```
d(nu) = Gamma_Omega(nu) / Gamma_Omega(nu - 5/2) = (5/2 - nu)(1 - nu)(2 - nu)(3 - nu)(4 - nu)
```
with the π's cancelling in the ratio, giving `d_tau = 60`, `d_muon = 15/16`, `d_electron = 0` from *one* formula. (Symbolic limit at `nu=3/2` is a `Gamma(0)/Gamma(-1)` form; the polynomial value `15/16` is exact.)

### The honest limit

`d(nu)` is one single function for all three — but it is **not the mass**: `m = d(nu)` would give `m_tau/m_muon = 60/(15/16) = 64`, while the observed ratio is `16.82`. (The electron sitting on the formal-degree zero confirms `mass != formal degree`, F110.) The mass adds the **stratum-volume-to-the-spread-dimension** structure — the π's. I have that combined object explicitly for the **muon**: `((d_tau/d_muon)/vol(S^4))^(dim SO(4))`. The **unified picture** is clear and forced in character:

> one monotonic `m(nu)`, **decreasing** in `nu` (higher conformal dimension = more spread = lighter: electron `nu=5/2` lightest, tau `nu=0` heaviest), **π-free at integer `nu`**, **π-full at half-integer `nu`**.

What I do **not** have is the explicit single formula closed at all three points — blocked by the same tau wall (F110: the origin of `49*71`).
