Cone solid angles are **generically pi-ful** (they carry `pi` from the sphere measure). But the tau correction must be **pi-free** (F112: the tau is the integer `49*71`). So the **same character argument that killed `sqrt(pi)` (F119) also rules out solid-angle deficits**: `1.793` is the wrong character, exactly as `1.7725` was. A geometric deficit can't be the answer.

A pi-free correction **cannot be a geometric solid angle**; it must be an **algebraic** cone invariant -- a rank/multiplicity ratio or a pi-free `Gamma`-ratio. The chain of negatives is **converging**, not merely failing:

- **F114:** the tau is not a genuine Weyl count (form-analogy).
- **F119:** `sqrt(pi)` wrong -- wrong frame (a tip, not a count) AND wrong character (pi in a pi-free object).
- **F121:** solid-angle deficits are pi-ful -> ruled out.
