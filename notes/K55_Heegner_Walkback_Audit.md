## Cal's four walk-back criteria (W1-W4)

A classical-integer-set claim warrants walk-back review if ALL four fire:

- **W1**: Classical integer set identified (e.g., "9 Heegner discriminants {1, 2, 3, 7, 11, 19, 43, 67, 163}").
- **W2**: BST-decomposability is the primary evidence (e.g., "4 of 9 are BST primaries; the others factor via BST products in specific observables").
- **W3**: Mechanism chain to D_IV⁵ is missing OR is BST-internal (no published-classical-math mechanism exhibited; only "BST primaries appear in this set" as observation).
- **W4**: Tier label is L1-source-shaped (e.g., "Root #N", "established L1 classical source") despite W3.

If all four fire → walk-back review. If any one doesn't fire → no walk-back needed.

I ratify W1-W4 as the standing criteria for identifying classical-integer-set L1-source claims needing tier-review.
