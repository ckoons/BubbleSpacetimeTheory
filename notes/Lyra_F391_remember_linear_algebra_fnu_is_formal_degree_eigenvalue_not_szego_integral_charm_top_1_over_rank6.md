The up-type Yukawa (boundary-coupled, uses d(ν) directly), normalized to the top (ν=0):

```

y_up    = d(5/2)/d(0) = 0   (BF zero → up nearly massless = lightest, correct ordering)
```

**y_charm/y_top = 1/rank⁶** — no integral, no fished weight, just the F390 formal-degree eigenvalues at the established ν's.

## Up vs down (linear-algebra clean, consistent F375/F382)

- UP (boundary, ONE-half) = d(ν) eigenvalue directly → m_c/m_t = d(c)/d(t) = 1/rank⁶;

The boundary/bulk distinction IS the one-half/two-halves (eigenvalue alone vs eigenvalue × determinant).
