# F126 — The substrate atlas (Casey's vision) + team sync

Casey's program vision: *"build a model of the substrate which shows the position of landmarks, where the trajectories flow, where particles are absorbed and emitted, where the math properties like the Di spin-1/2 and the interfaces live; how particles travel inside the substrate and pick up specific properties in their trajectory that cancel/remain at the point of emission."*

## The gem: "cancel/remain at emission" IS the Gindikin tracking

Casey's "particles pick up properties in their trajectory that cancel/remain at the point of emission" is not just a picture -- it is the **Gindikin `(2pi)^(3/2)` tracking** (F124/F125), named physically. As a mode reaches emission (the boundary), the factors it carries either:
- **CANCEL** in the ratio (the `(2pi)^(3/2)` cancels in `d(nu)=Gamma_Om(nu)/Gamma_Om(nu-5/2)`) -> the **pi-free rational** (the discrete cell count, the tau leading), or
- **REMAIN** (an uncancelled half-integer factor) -> **`pi^(-1/2)`** (the odd-Peirce correction).

So Casey's intuition names the cancel-vs-remain mechanism that sets a particle's pi-character at emission. That node of the map is the computation.

## The atlas

**LANDMARKS** (nu-axis special points):
- `nu=0` -- tau / identity / **cone tip** (discrete bulk; mass = cell count)
- `nu=3/2` -- muon / Rac scalar singleton / unitarity bound (continuum Shilov `S^4`)
- `nu=2` -- **Di spinor singleton -- WHERE SPIN-1/2 LIVES** (orthogonal to the mass program; Elie)
- `nu=5/2` -- electron / BF / self-shadow (the log, the running, the unit)
- the rho-vector `(5/2, 3/2, 1/2)`; the Peirce strata (rank-2 diagonal = mass cells; `N_c=3` off-diagonal = realization plane)

**TRAJECTORIES** (flow): the nu-flow (trajectory conjecture: one particle, three realization points); the corkscrew escape (radius=mass, winding=phase/CP/mixing, half-twist=spinor bit, handedness=chirality); the heat-kernel cascade (`a0->Lambda`, `a1->gravity`, boundary terms `->masses`).

**EMISSION / ABSORPTION** (boundary): the Shilov boundary / tile edges = where realization happens. Light = uncommitted energy (spread, the photon = 2-singleton composite); matter = committed energy (the constituents; the Di for spin-1/2). Pair production = composite -> constituents, and the spin works (Flato-Fronsdal, Elie).

The atlas is a map of what we understand, not a ledger of what we've derived. **Count holds honestly at 2.**
