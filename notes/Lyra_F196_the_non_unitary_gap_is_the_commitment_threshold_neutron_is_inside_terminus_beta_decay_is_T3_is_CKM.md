# F196 — The non-unitary gap is the commitment threshold; the neutron is the inside terminus; beta decay = T₃ = CKM

Casey reopened post-EOD with three linked questions: is the gap inside/surface/outside; does the vacuum transfer energy to the neutrino which transports it back inside (oscillation = the transfer); and what is the neutron doing — another part of the count? Answering with the math, tiered honestly.

## 1. The gap is the commitment threshold (not a manifold region)

The non-unitary gap is **not a region of D_IV⁵** — it is a range of the **representation parameter ν** (the "spread" of a state), not a set of points. But its geometric correlate is sharp. Ordering ν by what its state is supported on:

| ν | unitary? | support | physics |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | yes (trivial) | a point (origin) | degenerate |
| **(0, 3/2)** | **NO — the gap** | no positive-norm realization | **can't commit** |
| 3/2 = a/2 = N_c/2 | yes | rank-1 boundary orbit (Shilov-type) | the surface |
| ≳ genus−1 = 4 | yes (discrete series) | the whole open domain | committed bulk (mass) |

So the gap sits **below the lightest committable boundary state.** A state there is trying to be more localized/degenerate than any normalizable boundary state allows — it has no positive norm → no rest mass → it **cannot commit.** The massless ν₁ (ν = 1/2) lives exactly at this **threshold**: not bulk-inside (large ν, massive matter), not a proper surface point (ν = 3/2), but the **sub-unitary skin between the surface and the outside (vacuum).** That is precisely where a courier between the committed interior and the vacuum exterior would have to live — Casey's intuition is geometrically well-placed.

## 2. The energy-transfer picture (largely supported, one honest distinction)

Casey: the vacuum transfers energy to the massless neutrino, which transports it back inside; the oscillation we see *is* the transfer.

**Supported:**
- A gap-state can't commit (massless) → it **transports** energy, doesn't store it. That is exactly a courier's job, and it's *why* it has to be massless.
- The **oscillation clock is set by the vacuum.** Elie's `m₃/m₂ = √((N_max−1)/rank²) = √34` puts the splitting that drives oscillation at **N_max − 1 = 136** — the full Depth Ceiling, *vacuum-subtracted* (T1444). The Δm² that makes neutrinos oscillate is clocked by the vacuum ceiling. In that exact sense, the vacuum drives the oscillation.

**Honest distinction:** free-flight oscillation is coherent **phase** evolution — a neutrino in vacuum gains no *net* energy oscillating. So "oscillation = energy transfer" reads cleanest as: (a) the vacuum **sets the Δm²** that drives the phase (solid — the √34/136 result), and (b) the actual energy **crossings** happen at the **endpoints** (emission, absorption) — the journey is phase, the ledger balances at the ends. Either reading keeps the structure Casey drew: **vacuum = reservoir, gap-state = courier, threshold = loading dock** — the SWPP absorb→commit→emit cycle (F174–176), now with the gap identified as the dock.

## 3. The neutron: not a count parameter, but the inside terminus — and beta decay = T₃ = CKM

**Not a new parameter.** The neutron is composite (udd): its mass is a function of the quark masses + QCD binding (Λ_QCD) + EM, and its lifetime a function of the VEV (G_F), V_ud, and the n−p mass difference. All already in the 26 → **zero** new free parameters.

**But it's the inside terminus of the courier route, and it unifies with the mixing sector.** Beta decay `n → p + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ` is, at quark level, `d → u + W⁻` — and `d → u` is the **weak-isospin / T₃ displacement**, which is the *same forced T₃ᴿ displacement* that generates the entire **CKM mixing** (F191/F193). So:

> the neutron's decay **is** the up↔down seat transition — the very object that forces the quark mixing — realized in committed bulk matter, **emitting the gap-state courier (the antineutrino).**

The neutron is where the courier launches: committed bulk matter sheds one quantum of commitment (d→u), and the neutrino carries the receipt out to the threshold. The CKM mixing sector and beta decay are **one structure (T₃).** That's a genuine unification, and it's clean: the T₃ we *proved* forces the Cabibbo angle is the same T₃ that fires beta decay.

**The neutron's role in the count** is an over-determined **consistency node / falsifier**, not a parameter: once BST forces the quark masses, Λ_QCD, V_ud, and the VEV, the n−p mass difference and the neutron lifetime are **predictions.** The live neutron-lifetime puzzle (beam-vs-bottle ~9 s discrepancy) is exactly the kind of sharp place a zero-parameter framework will eventually have to speak to — *not claimed resolved here*, but flagged as a target once the quark-mass sector lands.
