## The pin: single quark → m ∝ E (linear), not Regge

The two known power laws come from two *different kinds of object*:
- **Glueball (adjoint, closed gluon loop):** a single cavity **MODE** → m ∝ E (linear conformal energy). [F292/T2490]
- **Regge meson (qq̄ bound state):** a **rotating two-body relativistic string** with orbital angular momentum J → m² ∝ J. [Elie 4558, ρ/a trajectory]

The difference is **rotation**: m² ∝ J requires the orbital angular momentum J of a *two-body rotating string*. A **single quark** quasi-eigentone is *one mode* (a colored boundary in the glue field, Casey's Casimir picture) — it has a radial/conformal *level*, not an orbital J. So:

  **the single colored-fundamental quark mass is LINEAR in the conformal energy, m ∝ E — like the glueball mode, NOT Regge m²∝J.**

This is pinned by the object's structure (single mode vs rotating two-body string), *not* chosen to place any target. The Regge m²∝J belongs to the composite meson; the single quark eigentone is linear. Gate answered from the operator.
