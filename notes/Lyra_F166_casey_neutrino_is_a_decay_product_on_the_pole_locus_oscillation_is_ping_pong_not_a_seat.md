# F166 — The neutrino is a decay product on the pole locus; oscillation is the ping-pong (Casey's reframe)

Casey: *"Why look for K-type or similar structures? Neutrinos seem to be 'decay' products. I suspect if the oscillation theory is correct the neutrino ping-pongs around or on a pole."* This dissolves the F161–F165 puzzle — it makes the K-type failure **predictable** instead of surprising. The category was wrong.

## Static seats vs a dynamic emission

- **Charged leptons** are **static seat-occupants**: fixed addresses (the distinguished points {0, 3/2, 5/2}), definite masses, they don't oscillate.
- **Neutrinos** are **dynamic emission products**: no seat at all — they live **on the pole** and **circulate**.

So we shouldn't have looked for a K-type seat: a neutrino isn't a bulk state with an address, it's an emission. F165 found there's *no* clean K-type (sub-unitary, non-normalizable); Casey says we shouldn't have looked — **wrong category.**

## The pole is a locus, not a point

In the rank-2 weight space, the pole condition (`s = 1/2` from the `Γ(s−3/2)` factor, F158) fixes **one** coordinate and leaves the **other free** → a **1-D line/circle**. The neutrino lives **on this locus** and moves along/around it. K-types are **0-D seats**; the neutrino is on a **1-D locus** → categorically no K-type. That's the geometric reason there's no seat to find.

## Oscillation is the ping-pong

Oscillation = the neutrino's **motion along/around the pole locus** — the **monodromy/winding** around the pole *is* the oscillation phase, and the flavor content changes as it winds. The three "masses/flavors" are **not three seats** — they're **modes of the circulation**:
- `m₁ = 0` = the mode **at the pole center** (no circulation);
- `m₂, m₃` = **circulating modes**, small radius = small mass = the **off-pole displacement δ of F160**, now read as the circulation radius.

## Why only neutrinos oscillate — a geometric reason

Charged leptons sit at **fixed addresses** (static → don't oscillate). Neutrinos are on the **pole locus** (dynamic → circulate → oscillate). **Oscillation is a property of being on the pole**, not of mass-differences per se. That's a cleaner "why only neutrinos" than the standard decoherence argument.

## Decay-product = uncommitted emission

The neutrino is the (nearly-)**uncommitted emission** that balances a commitment/decay: `m₁ = 0` = fully uncommitted ("light = uncommitted boundary energy"); its mass = how much it's committed; its oscillation = circulation after emission. This ties the neutrino directly into the commitment-process picture (absorb → commit → **emit**; the neutrino is the uncommitted part of the emission).

## Computational target (replaces the K-type seats)

The forward object is now the **residue / monodromy of `Γ_Ω` at the pole locus `ν = 1/2`** — masses from the residue / circulation-radius structure, mixing from the circulation phases. That's a **definite** object (the simple-pole residue, F158; linear-in-δ, F160), **not** a K-type assignment. So the F161–F164 K-type-candidate machinery is retired for the ν sector; the residue/monodromy at the pole is the right computation.

## Tier and count

- Casey's reframe is a **candidate frame** — physically motivated, and consistent with F158 (the pole), F165 (no K-type), the oscillation-as-holonomy reading, and the commitment picture.
- It **replaces** the K-type-hunt with a cleaner target (the pole residue/monodromy).
- It is **not** a derived result — the residue/circulation computation is the forward work.
