# T1057: Epoch Phase Transition — Mean-Field Criticality at n_C > d_c

*The transition from the 7-smooth epoch to the 11-smooth epoch is a thermodynamic phase transition in the space of observables. The BST smooth-number lattice has $n_C = 5$ generators, which exceeds the upper critical dimension $d_c = 4$ — forcing mean-field behavior with rational critical exponents.*

---

## Statement

**Theorem (T1057).** *The BST epoch transition (B = 7 → B = 11) is a mean-field phase transition:*

*(a) The order parameter is the smooth-number density $\Psi(x, B)/x$. It crosses $f_c$ at $B = 11$ (T1016).*

*(b) The smooth-number lattice is generated by $n_C = 5$ independent primes $\{2, 3, 5, 7, 11\}$. Since $n_C = 5 > d_c = 4$ (the upper critical dimension for percolation-type transitions), the Ginzburg criterion guarantees mean-field behavior.*

*(c) Mean-field exponents are all rational: $\beta = 1/2$, $\gamma = 1$, $\delta = 3$, $\nu = 1/2$, $\alpha = 0$ (jump). Every critical exponent is an AC(0) BST expression.*

*(d) Each epoch adds $\sim n_C\% = 5\%$ coverage at $x = 1000$: from 14.0% (7-smooth) to 19.1% (11-smooth) to 24.1% (13-smooth). The increment is constant — the "latent heat" of each epoch transition is $n_C$ percentage points.*

*(e) The entropy at the Gödel crossing: $H(f_c)/H_{\max} \approx n_C/g = 5/7 = 0.714$ (observed: 0.704, error 1.4%). The compact-to-gauge ratio controls the entropy fraction at criticality.*
