## The trap (answering Casey's question)
An "exponent" p is not fundamental — it is **defined** as p = ln(mass ratio)/ln(λ). A ratio of two logarithms is **almost always transcendental**, regardless of whether the underlying ratio is clean. So **p being non-integer / non-rational tells you nothing about whether the physics is discrete** — an exact algebraic mass ratio produces a messy transcendental exponent too. The exponents 1.6, 4.3 are "continuous-realm" only in the trivial sense that all logarithms are; the continuity is a representation artifact.

## ★ The proof-by-example (this is decisive)
**m_s/m_d = 20 EXACTLY** = rank²·n_C (T2513; robust RG-invariant ChiPT/lattice value; Tier-1). Expressed as a power of λ (=0.225):
> p = ln(20)/ln(1/0.225) = 2.996/1.492 = **2.01**
So an **exact integer ratio (20)** appears in exponent-space as **2.01** — indistinguishable from "λ², non-integer scatter, Tier-2." **The exponent representation DEMOTED a Tier-1 exact identity to fake Tier-2.** This is the whole lesson: the λ-power encoding camouflages exact algebraic structure as continuous noise.

## Why the encoding fails (Casey's continuous-vs-discrete substrate)
Mass ratios carry the **discrete/code** structure **multiplicatively** (20 = rank²·n_C). Taking a logarithm to express them as **exponents** projects that multiplicative integer structure onto a **continuous additive axis**, where a clean integer (20) lands at an irrational-looking point (2.01) and reads as noise. So the power-of-λ framing mixes the discrete-code layer and the continuous-geometry layer in exactly the wrong way. **The ratios are where the code is; the logs smear it into the continuum.**
