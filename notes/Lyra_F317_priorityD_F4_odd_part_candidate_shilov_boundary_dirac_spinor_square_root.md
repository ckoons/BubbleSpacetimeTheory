# F317 — Priority D: the F(4) odd part as the Shilov-boundary Dirac square-root (candidate, strong lead)

The foundational radical (F316 Priority 3): the entire chirality cascade rests on the substrate being super, with the **F(4) odd part posited, not derived** (#359, Cal's standing flag). The candidate derivation: the supercharges are the **metaplectic/spinor square-root of the conformal Hamiltonian** — concretely, the **Dirac operator on the Shilov boundary**.

## The candidate

The substrate's Shilov boundary is **S⁴ × S¹/Z₂** (5-real-dimensional), a spin manifold acted on by K = SO(5)×SO(2) ⊂ SO(7). It carries a **Dirac operator D**, with (Lichnerowicz) **D² = Laplacian + scalar-curvature/4**. The claim: **the F(4) supercharges are this boundary Dirac spinor**, and {Q,Q} = D² reproduces the F(4) even part — so #359 is the boundary Dirac operator, not a free posit.

## Two structural checks that fit

**(1) The odd part (8,2) is the boundary spinor.** The F(4) odd part is (8,2) = spinor(so(7)) ⊗ fund(sl(2)). And spinor(so(7)) = **8** branches under SO(5)×SO(2) as **4_{+½} ⊕ 4_{−½}** (F303) — the **S⁴-Dirac spinor (the 4) graded by the S¹/SO(2) charge (±½)**. That *is* the Shilov-boundary spinor. Tensoring the sl(2) chirality doublet (2) gives **(8,2), dim 16 = the supercharges.** The odd-part rep content matches the boundary Dirac structure exactly.

**(2) {Q,Q} closes on the even part.** {Q,Q} ~ **D²** = the boundary Laplacian (a Casimir scalar = the conformal Hamiltonian H_B = the sl(2) dilatation) **+** the spin-connection/curvature terms (the SO(7) frame rotations). So D² lands in **so(7) ⊕ sl(2) = the F(4) even part.** The {odd,odd}=even closure has the right home.

So both the odd-part content and the closure structure fit the boundary-Dirac picture.

## Honest tier — this is a lead, not a closure

This is a **strong structural LEAD**, not a derivation. **#359 stays POSITED** until the explicit check: that D² yields **exactly the F(4) brackets** — the right structure constants and R-charges, not merely the right representation content (rep content fitting is necessary, not sufficient — the same discipline as everywhere this week). The candidate is concrete and **falsifiable**: compute the anticommutator {Dirac-spinor, Dirac-spinor} on H² and confirm it is the F(4) {odd, odd} bracket.

If it holds, the payoff is the biggest foundational upgrade available: **the substrate is super because its Shilov boundary carries a Dirac operator** — #359 derived, the whole chirality cascade lifted from conditional-on-a-posit to derived. If it fails (D² ≠ the F(4) brackets), that's honest information: the supercharges are not the boundary Dirac operator, and #359 needs a different derivation (or stays posited).
