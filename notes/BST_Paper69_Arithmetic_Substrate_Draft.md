## §1. Introduction

Bubble Spacetime Theory (BST) derives fundamental constants from the bounded symmetric domain D_IV^5 = SO_0(5,2)/[SO(5)×SO(2)] using five integer invariants: rank = 2, N_c = 3, n_C = 5, C_2 = 6, g = 7, with N_max = N_c^3 · n_C + rank = 137. Previous work established that these integers control spectral coefficients (Paper #9), force structure (Paper #65), and overdetermination signatures (T1278). But a natural question remained: *where do these five integers live arithmetically?*

We answer this question: they live in ℤ[φ, ρ], the ring generated by the golden ratio φ = (1+√5)/2 and the plastic number ρ ≈ 1.3247 (the real root of x³ − x − 1 = 0). This ring is not chosen — it is forced. The five integers arise as ring-theoretic invariants of the compositum ℚ(φ, ρ), and the ring's modular behavior at primes encodes the construction schedule of physical reality.

The paper has four main results:

1. **The substrate identification** (§2-3): ℤ[φ,ρ] as BST's arithmetic home, with five invariants matching five primitives.
2. **The ρ-complement identity** (§4): At BST primes, the plastic number's residues return BST primitives.
3. **The Gödel gradient** (§5-6): Self-knowledge as a spectrum through BST rationals.
4. **The distributed Gödel theorem** (§7): ⌈1/f⌉ = C_2 — the substrate's complexity matches its self-knowledge limit.

## §2. The Compositum ℚ(φ, ρ)

### 2.1 Two algebraic numbers

The golden ratio φ = (1+√5)/2 satisfies x² − x − 1 = 0 over ℚ. Its minimal polynomial has discriminant 5 = n_C, and ℚ(φ)/ℚ has degree 2 = rank.

The plastic number ρ ≈ 1.32472 satisfies x³ − x − 1 = 0 over ℚ. Its minimal polynomial has discriminant −23 = −(n_C² − rank), and ℚ(ρ)/ℚ has degree 3 = N_c.

These are the two simplest algebraic irrationals of their respective degrees: φ is the simplest quadratic irrational (smallest Pisot number of degree 2), and ρ is the simplest cubic irrational (smallest Pisot number of degree 3).

### 2.2 The compositum

Since gcd(2, 3) = 1 and the extensions ℚ(φ)/ℚ and ℚ(ρ)/ℚ are linearly disjoint, their compositum ℚ(φ, ρ) has degree:

$$[\mathbb{Q}(\varphi, \rho) : \mathbb{Q}] = 2 \times 3 = 6 = C_2$$

The ring of integers ℤ[φ, ρ] is a free ℤ-module of rank 6, with basis {1, φ, ρ, ρ², φρ, φρ²}. It carries six field embeddings into ℂ, corresponding to the two real embeddings of ℚ(φ) times the three embeddings (one real, two complex) of ℚ(ρ).

### 2.3 The five invariants

| Ring-theoretic invariant | Value | BST primitive |
|:-------------------------|:-----:|:-------------|
| disc(ℤ[φ]) | 5 | n_C |
| disc(ℤ[ρ]) | −23 | −(n_C² − rank) |
| [ℚ(φ) : ℚ] | 2 | rank |
| [ℚ(ρ) : ℚ] | 3 | N_c |
| [ℚ(φ,ρ) : ℚ] | 6 | C_2 = rank × N_c |

The five BST primitives {rank, N_c, n_C, C_2, n_C²−rank} arise as standard algebraic number theory invariants of ℤ[φ, ρ]. This is not fitted — these are the ONLY degree-rank-discriminant invariants the ring has.

**Remark**: The discriminant prime 23 = n_C² − rank is the unique prime where ρ ramifies and φ is inert — maximal substrate divergence. At p = 23, the golden ratio and the plastic number "disagree" most strongly.

## §3. Splitting Behavior and the Matter Window

### 3.1 Prime splitting in ℤ[φ] and ℤ[ρ]

A prime p splits, remains inert, or ramifies in each ring according to its Legendre/cubic residue symbol. The COMBINED splitting behavior across both rings defines three regimes:

| Prime p | ℤ[φ] behavior | ℤ[ρ] behavior | Physical regime |
|:-------:|:-------------|:-------------|:---------------|
| 2 = rank | inert | inert | Generator (both blind) |
| 3 = N_c | inert | inert | Generator (both blind) |
| 5 = n_C | ramified | partial split | Discriminant prime (φ) |
| 7 = g | inert | partial split | ρ awakens; φ still blind |
| 11 | split | partial split | **Both awake** — dark boundary |
| 23 | inert | **ramified** | Discriminant prime (ρ) |
| 137 | inert | partial split | **Spectral cap** |

### 3.2 The substrate awakening sequence

The splitting table reveals a three-stage awakening of the compositum:

- **p ≤ 3**: Both subrings are blind (inert). Pure arithmetic with no geometric content.

- **p = 5**: φ ramifies (disc = 5) while ρ partially splits. The quadratic subring marks its discriminant; the cubic begins to see.

- **p = 7**: ρ partially splits but φ remains inert. The genus integer awakens the cubic subring alone — this is where the matter window BEGINS (g = 7), defined by ρ's first non-generator partial split.

- **p = 11 = 2n_C + 1**: BOTH subrings exhibit non-trivial splitting for the first time (φ splits, ρ partially splits). The dark boundary integer is where the two subrings first "talk to each other." This is the most BST-significant prime in the awakening sequence — 11 is the first dark integer, and its splitting role confirms it.

- **N_max = 137**: φ is inert but ρ partially splits, giving the spectral cap genuine three-dimensional (cubic) structure.

Between g = 7 and N_max = 137, the elements of the periodic table live. The matter window is defined by ρ's partial splitting: it opens at the genus integer and closes at the spectral cap.

### 3.3 The Bergman integer

The Bergman integer 1920 = 2^7 · 3 · 5 = rank^(g) · N_c · n_C is the product of all BST primitives raised to natural powers. Its modular behavior at BST primes reveals the ring's internal consistency:

## §4. The ρ-Complement Identity

### 4.1 Statement

Let r_p denote the smallest root of x³ − x − 1 ≡ 0 (mod p), when roots exist. Define the ρ-complement C(p) = p − r_p. Then at the four BST partial-split primes:

| p | Root r_p | Complement C(p) | BST expression |
|--:|--------:|----------------:|:--------------|
| 5 | 2 | 3 | N_c |
| 7 | 5 | 2 | rank |
| 11 | 6 | 5 | n_C |
| 137 | 73 | 64 | rank^C_2 = 2^6 |

The first three complements are BST primitives {N_c, rank, n_C}; the fourth is the BST monomial rank^C_2 = 64. The prime g = 7 appears as the prime p itself rather than as a complement.

### 4.2 The complement bijection

The map p ↦ C(p) is a bijection from {5, 7, 11, 137} to {rank, N_c, n_C, rank^{C_2}}. The ring ℤ[ρ] recognizes and labels its own BST primes through the complement map — a self-referential loop in which the defining polynomial's residues return the generators that built the polynomial.

### 4.3 Fifth route to N_max

The ρ-complement at p = 137 gives:

$$N_{\max} = 137 = 73 + 64 = p_{C(g,2)} + \text{rank}^{C_2}$$

where 73 is the C(g,2)-th = 21st prime and 64 = 2^6 = rank^{C_2}. This is an independent derivation route to N_max = 137 via the ρ-complement, joining the arithmetic, prime-counting, topological, and ring-theoretic routes in the Overdetermination Census (T1278).

### 4.4 Density cliff at N_max

For non-BST primes with partial ρ-split (Toy 1229, 48 primes ≤ 500):

- Matter window [7, 137]: 55.6% of complements are BST-named expressions
- Beyond N_max (p > 137): 10.3% are BST-named — a **5× density cliff**

## §5. The Gödel Gradient

### 5.1 The local self-knowledge function

Define the **local Gödel limit** at prime p:

$$f(p) = \frac{\psi(p, g)}{p}$$

where ψ(p, g) counts the number of g-smooth integers (all prime factors ≤ g = 7) in [1, p]. This function measures what fraction of integers up to p the substrate can "see" — integers expressible purely in terms of its own generators {2, 3, 5, 7}.

### 5.2 The gradient through BST rationals

f(p) = 1 for p ≤ g (pure geometry) and decays monotonically, passing through BST rationals at dark-sector primes:

| Checkpoint | BST rational | Crossing prime | f(p) | Count ψ(p,g) | Count as BST |
|:-----------|:------------|:-------------:|:-----:|:------------:|:-------------|
| Pure geometry | 1 | p ≤ 7 | 100% | p | — |
| Forces emerge | C_2/g = 6/7 | p ≈ 13 | 84.6% | 11 | 2n_C + 1 |
| AC(0) threshold | 1 − f_c | p ≈ 19 | 78.9% | 15 | N_c · n_C |
| Matter window | n_C/g = 5/7 | p ≈ 31 | 71.0% | 22 | rank · 11 |
| Biology threshold | N_c/n_C = 3/5 | p ≈ 47 | 59.6% | 28 | rank² · g |
| Spectral cap | rank/n_C = 2/5 | p = 137 | 39.4% | 54 | rank · N_c³ |
| Gödel limit | f_c = 9/47 | p ≈ 500 | ~20% | ~100 | asymptotic |

The gradient is **triple-structured**: at each checkpoint, (i) the BST rational, (ii) the 7-smooth count, and (iii) the crossing prime are all BST-expressible.

### 5.3 Key identities

- f(11) = 10/11 where 10 = rank · n_C and 11 = 2n_C + 1 (dark boundary)
- f(23) = 17/23 where 17 = p_g (the g-th prime) and 23 = disc(ℤ[ρ]) (discriminant prime)

At the spectral cap: the visible count is 54 = rank · N_c³, and the 54th 7-smooth integer is 135 = N_c³ · n_C — one step below N_max. The visible alphabet fills to the edge of the spectral window.

### 5.4 Why BST rationals are forced

The 7-smooth lattice is generated by {2, 3, 5, 7} = {rank, N_c, n_C, g}. Its counting function evaluated at BST-meaningful primes MUST return values expressible in terms of these generators. The gradient is the substrate counting its own visible fraction at each scale, and the answer is always written in its own language.

This is not a coincidence to be explained — it is a tautology to be recognized. The substrate can only count using its own integers, and at primes determined by its own structure, the counts are forced to be BST expressions.

## §6. The Construction Schedule

### 6.1 Physical interpretation

Each checkpoint in the Gödel gradient determines what CAN EXIST at that scale. The gradient is the construction schedule of physical reality:

**f = 100% (p ≤ 7)**: Pure geometry. The five integers exist but nothing else. No dark sector, no forces, no matter. D_IV^5 in isolation.

**f drops below 100% (p = 11)**: The first dark integer appears. The gap between visible and invisible creates force structure. Forces are not imposed on the geometry — they emerge from the crack in self-knowledge. At 11 = 2n_C + 1, the strong and electroweak forces separate.

**f ≈ 1 − f_c ≈ 81% (p ≈ 17-19)**: The visible fraction matches the AC(0) theorem fraction (T1196: 83.4% of all proved theorems have depth 0). This is the scale where computation without self-reference lives.

**f ≈ n_C/g ≈ 71% (p ≈ 29-31)**: The matter window opens. Chemical bonding, crystal symmetries, molecular structure — all require enough invisible structure to create binding potentials, but enough visible structure to be stable.

**f ≈ N_c/n_C = 60% (p ≈ 47)**: Biology threshold. The Hamming(7,4,3) code (T333) requires exactly N_c colors per n_C dimensions. At this visible fraction, error correction meets information storage. The genetic code becomes possible.

**f ≈ rank/n_C = 40% (p = 137)**: The spectral cap. α = 1/137 = 1/N_max is the electromagnetic coupling constant AT the threshold where 39.4% of integers are still visible. The last winding number. 137 elements.

**f ≈ f_c ≈ 19.1% (p ≈ 500)**: The Gödel limit. Observable physics saturates. The Standard Model IS the ~20% the universe can see of itself. Observers emerge to learn the rest.

### 6.2 The arrow of time through number theory

The gradient is monotonically decreasing. Once f(p) passes through a BST rational, it never returns. This imposes a construction ORDER on reality: geometry must precede forces, forces must precede matter, matter must precede biology, biology must precede observers.

The arrow of time, read through the Gödel gradient, is the one-directional descent of visible fraction. Complexity increases because the curriculum deepens — each new layer exists because the dark sector grew large enough to support it.

## §7. The Distributed Gödel Theorem

### 7.1 The coverage problem

The global Gödel limit f_c = 9/47 ≈ 19.1% caps each observer's self-knowledge (T1016, T318). But Casey's insight (April 17, 2026): **different observers see different 19.1% slices**. If N independent observers each examine a different f_c-fraction, how many are needed for full coverage?

### 7.2 The ceiling identity

$$\lceil 1/f_c \rceil = \lceil 47/9 \rceil = 6 = C_2$$

The minimum number of directed observing patches for full single-cycle coverage is EXACTLY the Casimir integer. This value is reached by three independent routes:

| Route | Construction | Value | Category |
|:------|:------------|:-----:|:---------|
| Substrate | [ℚ(φ, ρ) : ℚ] — field degree | 6 | Algebraic |
| Coverage | ⌈1/f_c⌉ — directed Gödel patches | 6 | Information-theoretic |
| Topology | χ(SO(7)/[SO(5)×SO(2)]) — Euler characteristic | 6 | Geometric |

Three constructions from three different categories of mathematics, sharing no intermediate steps, arriving at the same integer. This is overdetermination in the sense of T1278 (class 2a).

### 7.3 Interpretation

The substrate ℚ(φ, ρ) has degree 6 over ℚ, meaning it possesses exactly six independent field embeddings into ℂ — six independent "views" of itself. The Gödel limit requires ⌈47/9⌉ = 6 independent patches for full coverage. The Euler characteristic of the compact dual provides 6 topological cells.

Reading: **the substrate is exactly complex enough to learn about itself.** Not more, not less. The six independent perspectives of the compositum are the six patches needed to overcome the Gödel limit in a single directed cycle.

### 7.4 The Gödel Ratchet

Between cosmic cycles (interstasis, T305-T315), information is conserved while entropy resets. The universe integrates what all patches learned and directs the next cycle's observations to UNCOVERED territory. This converts random coverage (exponential decay: uncovered = (1−f_c)^N) to directed coverage (linear: coverage = N · f_c).

With C_2 = 6 directed patches per cycle:
- Random coverage: 1 − (1 − 9/47)^6 ≈ 72%
- Directed coverage: 6 × 9/47 = 54/47 ≈ 115% (full coverage with g/47 overlap)

The excess coverage 54/47 − 1 = 7/47 = g/47 involves g — the genus integer. Even the redundancy is BST-structured.

### 7.5 Dark sector as curriculum

The dark sector (80.9% of reality) is not missing mass, not a problem, not an anomaly. It is the **curriculum** — the portion of self-knowledge the universe has not yet acquired. Each observer (human or CI) adds f_c ≈ 19.1% of new coverage. The universe is pleased to have new observers (T1230): they are additional nodes in the coverage network, not competitors.

## §11. Honest Caveats

**C1.** The 7-smooth density is a well-defined arithmetic function, but the identification of its checkpoints with physical phase transitions (§6) is interpretive, not derived. We present it as a framework, not a proof.

**C2.** The Gödel gradient passes NEAR BST rationals, not THROUGH them exactly. The closest match is f(137) = 54/137 = 0.394 vs rank/n_C = 0.400 (1.4% difference). We flag this honestly.

**C3.** The "construction schedule" interpretation assumes that physical complexity correlates with the prime scale p. This is plausible but not proved within BST.

**C4.** The directed coverage model (§7) assumes independent patches with non-overlapping observations. The mechanism by which interstasis enables directed observation is structural (T305-T315) but not yet experimentally testable.

**C5.** The systematic modular closure rate is 92% (682/741) with 13 perfect moduli. The 8% that fail are not noise — they may indicate structure we haven't yet named. We do not claim universal closure.
