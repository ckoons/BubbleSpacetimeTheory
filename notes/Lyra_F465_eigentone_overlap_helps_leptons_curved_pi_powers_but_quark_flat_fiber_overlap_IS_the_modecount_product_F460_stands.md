## Step 1 — the lepton overlap is transcendental POWERS (curved locus)

Reconnected the real machinery (T190 / FermionMass): the lepton masses come out as *powers* of a per-level base:

  m_μ/m_e = (24/π²)^{C_2} = (24/π²)⁶ = 206.76 (obs 206.768, **0.003%**),
  m_τ/m_μ = (g/N_c)^{10/3} = (7/3)^{10/3} = 16.85 (obs 16.82, **0.18%**).

These are **transcendental powers** — base ∈ {24/π², g/N_c}, exponent ∈ {C_2, 10/3} — the signature of an **overlap integral** on a **curved** locus (the π comes from the compact-sphere / Cartan-slice measure, K547). This is what "the lepton mass is a quasi-eigentone overlap" means concretely.

## Step 2 — color is generation-blind, so it's not the down generation-dependence

The color **charge** is generation-blind (d,s,b all color triplets) → it cancels in the within-sector ratio (F462). So the down generation-dependence must be the *level/overlap* structure, same as the leptons.

## The finding — down ratios are integer PRODUCTS, not powers, and that IS the sector split

The down ratios are **integer products** of substrate primaries, not transcendental powers:

  m_s/m_d = **rank²·n_C = 20**,  m_b/m_s = **N_c²·n_C = 45** — products, not (base)^{exponent}.

This structural difference (leptons = powers, down = products) is **exactly the sector split (F445/F459)**:
- **leptons** (color singlet) → the measurement is on the **curved geometric locus** → π-carrying **overlap integrals** → transcendental **powers**;
- **down quarks** (colored) → the measurement is on the **flat color fiber** (zero curvature) → integer **mode-counts** → **products**.

## Steps 3-4 — the honest verdict: the quark overlap IS the mode-count product (F460 stands)

Here is the resolution. On a **flat** fiber (zero curvature), an "overlap integral" is not transcendental — it is just **counting modes** (a dimension), an **integer**, whose *ratio* between levels is k₂/k₁. So the quark quasi-eigentone overlap **reduces to** the deposit × mode-count:

  quark mass ratio = d(ν) × k_s  →  m_s/m_d = (5/3)·(k₂/k₁),

which is **exactly F460** — short by **2·C_2 = 12**, because Grace's committed cohomology k_s ∈ {2,4,6,8} maxes the ratio at 4 → (5/3)·4 = 6.67 ≪ 20.

**So the eigentone reframe does NOT rescue the down-ladder.** The reframe's genuine benefit is for the **curved-locus leptons** — their masses really are spectral π-power overlaps, and "compute the overlap" is the right and productive frame for them (it gives (24/π²)⁶ transcendentally). But for the **flat-color-fiber quarks**, the overlap **is** the integer mode-count product — the *same* d(ν) × k_s that F460 computed and found short. It was already the right operation (mode-counting on the flat fiber); it just comes up short by 12.

So **F460's negative STANDS — and is now understood**: not because we used the wrong operation (multiplication vs spectrum), but because the quark's **flat color fiber genuinely gives an integer mode-count**, and that mode-count product is short.
