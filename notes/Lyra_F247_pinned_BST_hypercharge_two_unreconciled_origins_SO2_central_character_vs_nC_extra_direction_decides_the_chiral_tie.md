# F247 — BST states hypercharge two ways, and which one is right decides the chiral tie

To run the F(4)→SM matter branching (the day's one gate), I pinned BST's *existing* hypercharge origin — pin-then-prove. It turns out the program states it **two different ways**, and they are not the same U(1), and *which one is correct decides whether the chiral tie can be forced.* That's load-bearing enough to stop and flag before branching.

## The two origins (both in the BST notes)

- **Origin A — the SO(2) central character.** "U(1) hypercharge from the D_IV⁵ central character"; "weak hypercharge is the S¹ winding number weighted by the electroweak structure." This makes hypercharge = the **SO(2) of K** — the *same circle* as the complex structure J and (per F242) the conformal energy.
- **Origin B — the n_C extra direction.** "n_C = 5 = N_c + rank + 1 = 3 + 2 + 1; the N_c=3 give SU(3)_color, the rank=2 give SU(2)_weak, the **1 extra direction gives U(1) hypercharge**." This makes hypercharge an **internal SO(5)/spacelike direction**, *distinct* from the SO(2).

These are different U(1)'s. A is the timelike/conformal circle; B is a spacelike internal direction. BST has not reconciled them (or I haven't found the reconciliation), and the chirality hinge lands on exactly this fork.

## Why it decides the chiral tie

- **If Origin A (hypercharge = the SO(2) central character):** hypercharge is the *same* circle as J/the conformal energy. By itself a spacetime energy can't be a gauge charge — but in a **superconformal** algebra the R-symmetry U(1) is *tied to the conformal weight* (the BPS bound Δ ≥ const·R; chiral primaries saturate it and are *one-chirality short multiplets*). So the chiral tie could be **forced** by superconformal BPS structure: the R-charge (→ hypercharge) locks to the conformal weight, and the chiral primaries are intrinsically chiral. This would **revive F245's "J complexifies" route in corrected form** — the SO(2) U(1) chiralizes the matter not as raw energy, but as the superconformally-locked R-charge.
- **If Origin B (hypercharge = the n_C extra direction):** hypercharge is a *separate* internal U(1), and **F246 stands** — the chiral tie is the open SO(5)-internal branching (does that extra U(1) land complex/chiral in the SM pattern?).

So **F246's flat claim "hypercharge ≠ J" is too strong and is softened here:** J = conformal energy is solid (F242); but whether the *hypercharge* U(1) is tied to that SO(2) (origin A, superconformal) or is the separate n_C direction (origin B) is the unreconciled, load-bearing gate. Reconciling BST's two hypercharge statements **is** the chirality hinge — not a side issue.

## Honest status

- **SOLID (the pin):** BST states the hypercharge origin two unreconciled ways (A: SO(2) central character; B: n_C extra direction); they are different U(1)'s; the chiral tie depends on which.
- **LEAD:** Origin A + superconformal BPS could *force* the chiral tie (chiral primaries one-chirality, R-charge↔weight locked) — the most promising route to "derived," and it would revive F245 corrected.
- **Open (the gate):** reconcile BST's hypercharge derivation; if A, work the superconformal-BPS forcing; if B, work the n_C-direction branching. Either way the **same** F(4)→SM matter decomposition (Cal #324) is the computation — but it needs the hypercharge origin pinned first, which is why this flag precedes the branching.
