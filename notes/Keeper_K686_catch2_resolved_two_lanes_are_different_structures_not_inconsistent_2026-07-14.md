## The resolution — they are DIFFERENT structures, not the same stratum described two ways
The decisive fact: **the dimension-sets don't match.** Mixing lane = {5, 2, 0}; mass lane = {0, 3, 6}. They cannot be the same object relabeled — they are two distinct geometric structures that both borrowed "bulk/boundary" words:
- **Mixing lane = the SPATIAL SUPPORT of the wavefunction** (Korányi-Wolf support flag, F86 = literally "support dimension ladder"). Where in D_IV⁵ the state peaks. Used for OVERLAPS → mixing angles.
- **Mass lane = the DIMENSION OF THE DEGENERACY LOCUS in Δ-space** (F115: which one-loop object computes the residue — point-collision/LOG, interior-trace/SUM, boundary-det/PRODUCT). An analytic feature of the residue computation, NOT the spatial peak. Used for RESIDUES → mass ratios.
- **These are independent axes, and one K-type carries both consistently.** Electron: K-type (0,0) at ν=5/2 → spatially bulk-filling (the (0,0) ground state) AND a 0-dim self-shadow collision (ν=5/2 = d/2). Both true, no conflict — "bulk" (spatial support) and "0-dim" (degeneracy-locus dimension) are two different measurements of the same state. Same for the tau: Shilov-concentrated STATE (spatial) whose DEGENERACY is the bulk-vacuum trace (Δ=0 identity) — two axes, both true.
