The discrete-series Casimir is C₂ = n₁(n₁+5) + n₂(n₂+3) + n₃(n₃+1) with n₁ ≥ n₂ ≥ n₃ ≥ 0 (ρ = (5/2,3/2,1/2)).

> **{0, 3, 5, 6, 7, 9, 10, 12, 13, 14, 15, 17, 18, 20, 21, 22}**, MISSING **{1, 2, 4, 8, 11, 16, 19}**.

- The **first four non-zero rungs = {3, 5, 6, 7} = {N_c, n_C, C_2, g}** — the primaries (T2490).
- **Notable absence: 11 = c_2 is NOT a rung.** So the 0⁺⁺ glueball's *seat* anchor (c_2 = 11) is not a
  half-Casimir; the absolute-mass seat ladder and the discrete-series rung ladder are **different quantizations**

- The low gaps {1,2,4,8} (then 11,16,19) thin out as n grows; above the primaries the rungs densify.
