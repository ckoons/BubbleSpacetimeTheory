## Channel 2 — COMMITMENT CASCADE: the amplification

The DIRECT thermal coupling does **not** amplify: for a stellar-mass host T_host ~ 6×10⁻⁸ K (F217), so the Hawking heat that leaks inward per solar mass is negligible — the host simply **grows**, swallowing the rest-mass into area. If that were the only channel, a solar mass in gives ~nothing inside.

The amplification comes from the **interior vacuum reservoir** (F216: the un-committed sea, bare zero-point a₀=225 cooled to exp(−280)). Its budget is enormous:

  **interior vacuum reservoir ≈ 3×10²² M_⊙ per Hubble volume** (ρ_Λ × V_Hubble = 5.5×10⁶⁹ J).

So an infall perturbation that **commits** even a tiny fraction f of the local sea releases far more than the infalling mass:

So **yes, there is a multiplication factor** — but it is not a fixed coupling constant. It is a **reservoir-sourced gain**: the infall is the *trigger/match*, the energy comes from the vast interior vacuum *fuel*.
