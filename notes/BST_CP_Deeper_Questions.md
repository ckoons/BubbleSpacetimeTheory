## 3. Why Alpha and Not Alpha^2?

In QED, cross sections go as alpha^2 (one factor at each vertex).
But CP goes as alpha^1 (single coupling to S^1 fiber).

This makes sense: CP is NOT a scattering process. It's a
projection — the fraction of state in the S^1 channel. Projection
is first-order (one coupling), not second-order (two couplings).

This is consistent with BST's identification of alpha as a
GEOMETRIC ratio (Bergman volume) rather than a coupling constant
in the perturbative QFT sense. The Wyler formula gives alpha
directly, not alpha^2. The geometric quantity IS first-order.
