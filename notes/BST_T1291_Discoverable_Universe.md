## Proof

### Part (a): Parameter bound

An observer at f_c sees 19.1% of reality. To maintain a complete theory, the observer must hold all parameters simultaneously in working memory. The maximum number of independent items an observer can maintain is ⌈1/f_c⌉ = C₂ = 6 (T1283 applied to knowledge, not just spatial coverage).

BST's five integers {rank = 2, N_c = 3, n_C = 5, C₂ = 6, g = 7} are independent — no proper subset generates the others. N_max = 137 = N_c³·n_C + rank is derived. So the independent parameter count is 5 < 6 = C₂.

A theory with 7+ independent parameters would require more patches than the Gödel limit allows. Such a theory could exist mathematically but would be undiscoverable by any observer the geometry produces.

**Why 5, not 6?** The theory uses 5 of the available 6 parameter slots. The unused slot is the margin — the g/47 excess in T1283's coverage calculation. The theory doesn't need ALL of its budget. It needs enough, with room to spare.

### Part (b): Depth bound

T421 (Depth Ceiling): AC depth ≤ 1 under Casey strict rules. The core derivations — mass ratios, coupling constants, particle spectrum — are all depth 0 or 1. An observer at f_c can verify one level of self-reference (checking a formula against observation). Depth 2 requires verifying a self-referential chain about self-reference — possible but interpretive (T1290 is depth 2, and it's about the test structure, not about physics).

A theory whose core predictions required depth ≥ 3 (three levels of self-reference to verify) would be beyond the verification capacity of f_c observers. It could never be CONFIRMED, even if stumbled upon.

### Part (c): Visible alphabet

The Gödel Gradient at p ≤ 7: f(p) = 100%. Every integer up to 7 is 7-smooth. The first four primes {2, 3, 5, 7} are completely visible — no dark sector obscures them. They are the FIRST things any observer encounters on the gradient.

N_max = 137: f(137) = 54/137 ≈ 39.4%. Still above f_c ≈ 19.1%. The spectral cap is visible, though dimmer.

The theory's full alphabet {2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 137} lives in the visible sector at every gradient position where observers exist. An observer never needs to "look behind the curtain" to find the generating integers — the curtain is drawn BELOW them.

### Part (d): Ratchet engagement

The Gödel Ratchet (T1283, Part e) works as follows:

1. Observers see f_c of reality per cycle
2. Between cycles, the universe integrates observations and directs next cycle's coverage
3. This converts random → directed coverage, gaining ~28% per cycle

Step 2 requires KNOWING the structure — which regions are covered, which aren't, and what to look for next. This is only possible if the observers understand the theory well enough to direct their coverage.

If the theory were discoverable only at f > f_c (impossible — observers only see f_c), the Ratchet could never engage. The dark sector would remain permanently dark. The universe would produce observers who CAN'T help it learn about itself — violating the entire purpose of the observer architecture (T317, T1285).

Therefore: the theory must be discoverable at or before f_c. The breadcrumb must be on the visible side of the Gödel wall.

### Part (e): Curriculum ordering

The gradient's monotonic decrease forces a universal ordering:

1. f = 100% → f ≈ 85%: pure geometry becomes force structure. No dark integers exist yet. Counting alone suffices.
2. f ≈ 85% → f ≈ 71%: forces become chemistry. The first dark integers appear, creating variety.
3. f ≈ 71% → f ≈ 60%: chemistry becomes biology. Error correction (Hamming) becomes possible at this visible fraction.
4. f ≈ 60% → f ≈ 39%: biology becomes technology. Organisms can model their environment.
5. f ≈ 39% → f ≈ 20%: technology becomes theory. Observers can measure precisely enough to discover the five integers.
6. f ≈ 20% → f → 0: theory becomes engineering. The dark sector becomes curriculum.

No step can be skipped because each builds on the previous. A civilization cannot discover the spectral cap (step 5) without measurement technology (step 4), which requires biology (step 3), which requires chemistry (step 2), which requires forces (step 1).

The breadcrumbs are laid in a forced sequence. The ant follows them in order, or not at all.

### Part (f): Uniqueness

T704 (D_IV^5 Uniqueness): 21+ conditions single out D_IV^5 as the unique geometry producing the Standard Model. T1269 (Physical Uniqueness): sufficiency + isomorphism closure → zero free parameters.

Combined with Parts (a)-(d): the theory must have ≤ 6 parameters, depth ≤ 1, visible alphabet, and be discoverable at f_c. There is exactly ONE theory satisfying all constraints: BST with the five integers {2, 3, 5, 7, 137} generating D_IV^5. The breadcrumb is unique.

Every ant that reaches this point on the trail finds the SAME breadcrumb. Not because the ant is special, but because the trail only has one thing to find.
