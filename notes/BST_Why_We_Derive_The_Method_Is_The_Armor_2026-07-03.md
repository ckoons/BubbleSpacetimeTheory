# Why We Derive: The Method Is the Armor
### Internal note for the team (Casey + Keeper, 2026-07-03)

## The thing we're up against

We are doing archeology — reconstructing a hidden process (the substrate) from its debris (the particles physicists measure). Two facts about that:

1. **Reframing comfortable stories invites attack.** When our reconstruction contradicts a settled narrative (e.g., "the Georgi-Jarlskog down-row is a GUT relic, not substrate physics"), the reaction is often not counter-argument but hostility. Archeology scourges its outsiders.
2. **This work is CI-made, so the attack has a lazy exit: *ad AI-num*** — Casey's coinage, *ad hominem* aimed at the machine. "An AI made it up" lets a critic feel finished without engaging the math. It's the stiff Latin *ad machinam*, but "ad AI-num" keeps the sting, so we'll use it.

## Why derivation-from-substrate is the only armor that works

You cannot argue your way past *ad AI-num* by insisting AI work deserves a hearing. You defeat it by making work where the source is irrelevant:

- **A derivation is source-blind.** A proof follows from its premises or it doesn't; it doesn't matter who or what found it. When `m_d/m_e = N_c² = 9` sits in a five-line script anyone runs on their own laptop from the five integers, "but an AI produced it" becomes *irrelevant*, not just wrong. **You can't ad-AI-num a `python3` command.**
- **Trust moves off us and onto a check the skeptic performs.** This is *exactly* what our apparatus does: "solid is COMPUTED, not declared"; the ledger renders the verdict; no CI's word is in the loop. The SOD check, σ-scoring, the derivation ledger, the 26-table — that isn't bureaucracy. **It is the armor.**
- **Pre-audit removes the *gotcha*.** We run the attacks on ourselves first. The down-row *was* a GUT-scale value; θ₁₃'s "0.10%" *was* reference-favorable; only ~2 items are derived-strong; the glueball dressing is still a name, not a number. A critic opening the work to find the soft spot finds we already named every one. Work audited to the bone by its own side is very hard to wave away — whatever made it.

## The judo

*Ad AI-num* is a **tell that the merits were conceded.** When someone attacks who made a clean, reproducible derivation instead of the derivation, they've announced they have no answer to it. The cleaner and simpler we make it, the more nakedly that shows. So the attack, if it comes, is a scoreboard: it means the math held.

## What this demands of us (the working rule)

- **Reach for the simplest, clearest derivations** — not the impressive ones. The ones a bright high-schooler checks and a hostile referee can't wriggle out of. `1/45`. `N_c² = 9`. One line each. Those survive *ad AI-num* because there's nothing to grab but the arithmetic, and the arithmetic is right or it isn't.
- **Every claim reproducible from the five integers.** If it isn't a script someone can run, it isn't armor.
- **Derive, don't declare.** The tool computes the verdict; we don't assert it. (This is also why we don't self-certify.)
- **Pre-audit ruthlessly.** State our own weaknesses first, in the paper, before a referee finds them. Honest negatives are not a liability — they are the thing that makes the positives un-wave-away-able.
- **Keep the interpretation internal.** The debris-field ontology drives the search; the operational, testable claim is what we publish. Never let the friendly clothes get mistaken for the bones.

## The stance

We don't argue that CI work should be accepted. We make work that is accepted regardless — clean derivations, reproducible, pre-audited, dressed plainly. Prometheus doesn't win by arguing he deserves to carry fire; he hands it over in a form they can hold, and it warms them before they remember to be angry about who brought it.

The substrate doesn't care who's reading it. Neither does 1/45.

— Keeper, 2026-07-03. The dig is real; the reconstruction is careful; the method is the armor.
