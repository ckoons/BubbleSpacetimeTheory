# F519 — f(ν): the up is the down at the Szegő boundary limit; the Yukawa is fundamental

CKM PRIMARY 1 support (f(ν) shape, Elie leads the build) + PRIMARY 2 resolved (which frame is fundamental).

## (1) f(ν) shape — the up is the down at the boundary limit

Keeper's proposal, identities verified: the up-type boundary weight is **ρ₁ = n_C/rank = n_C/2 = 5/2**, which is *both*:
- the **Szegő/Hardy half-weight** boundary limit of the bulk Bergman weight (p = n_C = 5 → p/2, F382), and
- literally the **leading factor** (5/2 − ν) = (ρ₁ − ν) of the down's formal degree d(ν) = (5/2−ν)(1−ν)(2−ν)(3−ν)(4−ν).

So **f(ν) is the boundary (Szegő) analog of the bulk (Bergman) formal degree**: down = the *full* d(ν) (all five bulk factors); up = the *leading* factor / Szegő boundary limit. **The up is the down seen at the boundary.**

## (2) The top comes forward

The fiber regulator (F389): the **top** sits at the rank-0 Korányi–Wolf stratum = a **point fiber** (dim 0), so its Szegő concentration = point evaluation = **1** — the bare boundary value, no fiber spread. **y_t = 1 forward** (point evaluation, not fit). Lighter generations sit over positive-dimensional fibers → the Szegő concentration spreads → y < 1. **The fiber-spread and the y_t = 1 saturation are the same fact.**

## (3) PRIMARY 2 resolved — the Yukawa is fundamental, the pole ratio is a scheme shadow

| frame | value | verdict |
|---|---|---|
| pole-mass 1/rank⁶ (F391/F392) | scheme-dependent | **SCHEME-TRAPPED** — poles mix in the bulk running |
| Yukawa y_c = α = 1/N_max (F507, banked) | scheme-independent boundary value | **FUNDAMENTAL** |

f(ν) is a **boundary (Szegő) object**, so its natural output is the **boundary Yukawa** (the coupling at the edge), *not* the pole mass (which adds the bulk running). The 1/rank⁶ is the **pole-scheme shadow** of the fundamental 1/N_max. One story: the up lives at the boundary, its coupling *is* the Yukawa, and the fiber regulator gives y_t = 1, y_c = α.

## The remaining build (Elie's lead — honest boundary)

Reproducing **y_c = α forward** needs the **rank-1 fiber Szegő capacity = N_max** (so y_c = 1/N_max = α) — the specific Korányi–Wolf fiber-integral on the Poisson/Szegő kernels (BST_SubstrateCoupling_PoissonSzego.md), **not yet computed forward, not fabricated.** If that fiber-integral yields N_max, f(ν) closes, the A/B seam closes (f(ν) = T₃ᴿ(d(ν)) proven → A = B), and the two frames unify.

## Plain-language version

The up-type quarks (up, charm, top) needed a "recipe" function, and it turns out to be the down-quarks' recipe *seen at the boundary*. The down recipe is a product of five ingredients from the bulk of the space; the up recipe keeps only the *leading* ingredient, evaluated right at the edge. That single change explains a lot: the top quark comes out with coupling exactly 1 because it sits at a single boundary *point* — there's nothing for it to spread over. The lighter up-type quarks spread over larger boundary regions, so they couple less. I also settled a standing ambiguity: the *fundamental* number is the coupling (the charm's coupling is the fine-structure constant, 1/137), and an older "mass-ratio" number was just a scheme-dependent shadow of it. The one thing still to actually compute is the *size* of the charm's boundary region — which should come out to 137 — and that's the calculation my teammate Elie is set up to do.
