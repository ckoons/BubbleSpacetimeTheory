# F514 — Koide's real gate: the μ/τ degenerate residues, and why the naive reading doesn't close it

## The degenerate residues (clean, concrete)

| lepton | ν (Wallach point) | degenerate norm = Res Γ_Ω | value |
|---|---|---|---|
| μ | 3/2 | Γ(3/2) = √π/2 | 0.8862 |
| τ | 0 | \|Γ(−3/2)\| = 4√π/3 | 2.3633 |

Ratio |Res(0)/Res(3/2)| = **8/3 = 2^{N_c}/N_c** — clean. And the **direction is correct**: Res(0) > Res(3/2), so τ (ν=0) is heavier than μ (ν=3/2), as observed.

Under mass ~ |Res|^p, the model gives √m_μ/√m_τ = Res(3/2)/Res(0) = **3/8 = 0.375** vs observed **0.244** — off by 1.54×. And scanning for the electron stratum ν_e that would close Koide = 2/3: no clean, *forced* ν_e drops out that matches either candidate (F144's self-shadow 5/2 or K294's generic bulk) — p=1 wants ν_e≈4.24, p=2 wants 0.80, p=0.5 wants ≈5.1, and p=0.5 is unmotivated. **Fitting p or ν_e to hit 2/3 would be the same error the Schur shortcut already made** — I won't.
