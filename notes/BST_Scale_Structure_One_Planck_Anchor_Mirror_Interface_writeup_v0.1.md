# The Single-Scale Structure of D_IV⁵

*Scale as a Mirror Interface Object*

## Abstract

Bubble Spacetime Theory takes **exactly one** dimensionful input — the Bergman length ℓ_B, of order the Planck length. Every other physical scale is that single anchor multiplied by a dimensionless ratio built from the five integers {rank=2, N_c=3, n_C=5, C_2=6, g=7}. We show that the three regions of the D_IV⁵ Mirror (Casey #16) — discrete interior, the boundary, and the screened exterior — do **not** carry independent scales: they share the one anchor in three guises, connected by dimensionless ratios. The interior wears it as particle masses (Planck × `6π⁵α¹²` → m_e = 0.511 MeV); the exterior wears it as the host singularity's temperature (the Planck temperature), exponentially bled by `exp(−280)` to the dark-energy scale Λ. Quantum mechanics does not set a separate interior scale — QM *is* the interior's discreteness (compact isotropy → discrete K-types) — but the Planck anchor itself is the joint product of ℏ, G, and c, which meet geometrically at the boundary singularity. The single scale therefore lives **at the singularity** and is reflected by the Mirror into a discrete-interior face and a continuous-exterior face, making the scale itself an interface object on the same footing as masses and Λ.

## For the bright high-schooler (the story)

Most "theories of everything" need you to hand them several numbers with units — a length here, a mass there. BST asks for **one** number with units (essentially the Planck length) and nothing else. Everything else it computes as pure ratios — numbers like 4/79 or `exp(−280)` with no units at all.

So when you ask "what's the scale of the inside, the edge, and the outside of the universe?" the surprising answer is: **it's the same scale, worn three different ways.** The inside shows it to you as the masses of particles. The outside — if we're living inside a collapsed something — shows it as a blazing temperature at the edge, cooled almost to nothing by the time it leaks in to us (that leftover trickle is the dark energy). And the "edge" between them is the one place where everything piles up to infinity at once: gravity, quantum graininess, and heat all blow up together. That pile-up point *is* the one scale. The universe has one ruler; it just holds it up to different lights.
